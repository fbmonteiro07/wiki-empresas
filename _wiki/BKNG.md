## Snapshot
World's largest online travel agency by gross bookings. Two-sided marketplace connecting >4 million accommodation properties (≈90% of Booking.com room nights from independents, alt-accom and small chains; top-10 global chains only a low-double-digit % share) with travelers across five consumer brands: **Booking.com** (accommodations-led, the core), **Agoda** (Asia), **Priceline** (US), **KAYAK** (meta), **OpenTable** (restaurants) (10-K, 2026-02-18). Revenue is agency + merchant commissions on accommodations, plus a fast-growing payments/fintech layer, flights, attractions and advertising. Europe is ~half of room nights, Asia ~a quarter, US a low-double-digit % (Q4'25, 2026-02-18). Within internet/AI, BKNG sits as an incumbent travel aggregator whose franchise question is whether agentic LLMs disintermediate the OTA — management's view is the opposite (TAM expansion + new top-of-funnel demand).

## Debate / thesis
- **Bull (mgmt + analyst framing, 2026-02-18 / 2026-04-28):**
  - AI is a *net positive*, not a threat: incumbent scale + proprietary intent data drives conversion/personalization, and LLM travel discovery expands the digital-travel TAM (Fogel: digital share could go "35%… 45%… higher"). Frontier players moving toward performance-marketing/advertising rather than down-funnel = the Google-auction model that BKNG has thrived under (Fogel reply to BofA's Post, 2026-04-28).
  - Merchant-of-record is a moat: 4M+ properties, >100 payment methods / 50+ currencies, thousands of partner-services staff, and dense regulatory navigation (DSA/DMA/DAC7 etc.) that LLMs are unlikely to want to own (Fogel reply to Rothschild's Brignall, 2026-02-18).
  - Durable share gains + operating leverage: US accelerating 4 straight quarters and taking share; Connected Trip growing ~3x base; customer-service cost per booking down ~10% via GenAI ("a line item in the P&L where we have meaningful results" — Steenbergen). Aggressive buyback compounding per-share value.
  - External buy-side nod (dated, low-conviction): J.P. Morgan AM PM Felise Agranoff named BKNG an AI beneficiary outside tech — incumbent scale + market leadership to "deploy AI to improve content costs" and a "more virtual experience to book your travel," potentially inflecting profitability further (Barron's Tech Roundtable, 2025-03-26; price then ~$4,764 pre-split). Thesis-level only, no PT.
- **Bear / risks to thesis (from same calls + 10-K):**
  - Near-term geopolitics: Middle East conflict cut Q1 ~2pts (March room nights +1%, ~6pts hit) and is a ~3pt Q2 headwind; FY26 assumes the impact runs through end-June with a 2H recovery — an explicitly uncertain assumption ("anyone can put whatever you want in your model" — Steenbergen, 2026-04-28). Broader inflation/jet-fuel/airfare second-order effects are *not* in guidance.
  - Agentic disintermediation remains the structural overhang — proof points are still "very small sample," and Justin Post (BofA) flagged big AI players "moving away from transactions." Take-rate/marketing economics could compress if traffic shifts to LLMs.
  - Alt-accom growth decelerating (Barclays' Young, 2026-02-18) — FY25 ~10%, Q4 ~9% — vs. Airbnb-style pure-plays; supply +8% to 8.6M listings is the build, not yet the result.
  - Marketing is "highly variable" and management is willing to deleverage opportunistically (Q4'25 marketing +24bps of GBV) — leverage is a choice, not a guarantee.
  - **Asia competitive pressure from Trip.com:** Bernstein (Boris Van / Ting Ming Neo, 2025-10-27) frames Trip.com — not BKNG — as the structural OTA winner in SEA/Korea on China scale, lower-cost China ops and competitive pricing (Trip.com's mkt-+-G&A burden materially below BKNG/EXPE on FY23 data). BKNG's **Agoda** is positioned only as the defender: per Bernstein, Agoda has *raised promotions in South Korea and SEA to grab/defend share against Trip.com*, with steady state not expected until ~2028E. Read-through: intensifying intra-Asia couponing is a margin/take-rate headwind on the high-growth Asia segment, not just a top-line tailwind. (Caveat: this is a Trip.com-bull report; BKNG appears solely as the incumbent being attacked.)

## Catalysts / what to watch
- **Q2 FY2026 print (late July/early Aug 2026, est.):** the cleanest read on Middle East trajectory vs. the end-June recovery assumption; watch room nights vs. +2–4% guide and whether the ~3pt headwind decays.
- Middle East de-escalation / transit-corridor (Europe–Asia) normalization — the single biggest swing factor on FY26.
- US room-night growth durability (5th consecutive acceleration?) and direct-mix progress.
- **Genius relaunch** — mgmt repeatedly flagged "strengthening Genius this year" + Connected-Trip integration ("Connected Trip with Genius is superpower" — Fogel); product rollout to watch.
- Penny / Booking.com agentic booking rollout cadence and conversion data scaling beyond small samples.
- Capital return pace post 25-for-1 split (record Q1 buyback); dividend at $10.50/qtr post-split.
- Transformation Program delivery: $500–550M in-year 2026 savings funding the $700M reinvestment.
- Agoda vs. Trip.com promotional intensity in SEA/Korea — couponing escalation/de-escalation as a tell on Asia segment margins (Bernstein, 2025-10-27).

## Risks
- Geopolitical/macro shocks to travel demand (Middle East now; precedent: COVID, Russia-Ukraine, Israel-Hamas) — 10-K and both calls.
- Agentic/LLM disintermediation of the OTA top-of-funnel; dependence on Google as a top-of-funnel demand source.
- Intensely competitive industry (10-K) vs. Airbnb, Expedia, Google, Trip.com, metas and direct supplier channels; Bernstein flags Trip.com as a structural share-taker in Asia leveraging China-scale cost advantage (2025-10-27).
- Regulatory load — EU DSA/DMA/DAC7, AI Act, payments/privacy across 200+ countries (Fogel, 2026-02-18); ongoing EU competition scrutiny.
- High merchant/payments mix raises payment-processing cost and operational/fraud exposure.
- FX translation swings (FY26 reported rates flattered ~2pts by FX; cc the cleaner read).
- Seasonality (10-K) and ADR/length-of-stay softness at the lower-end US consumer segment.
