## Snapshot
DISCO is the near-monopoly maker of **precision back-end semiconductor tools** — wafer **dicing** (blade & laser saws), **grinding** (back-grinders / wafer-thinning) and **polishing** — plus the **precision processing tools / consumables** (dicing blades, grinding & polishing wheels) those machines consume. It sits at the end of the wafer-fab flow, between wafer completion and assembly/packaging, and is structurally levered to **advanced packaging and HBM**: DISCO holds an estimated **~70-80% share** of the dicing/grinding equipment used to thin HBM wafers to ultra-thin 30-50µm for stacking (WebSearch / Bitget overview, Jan-2026). Two product categories: **Precision Machines** (dicing saws, laser saws, grinders, polishers) and **Precision Processing Tools** (consumable blades/wheels) plus support services (DISCO IR, 2026). FYE March; **~90% overseas sales** (89.9% in Q2, Quartr 2025-10-29). FY ended Mar-2026 was the **sixth consecutive record year** — net sales ¥436.9bn (+11.1% YoY), driven by generative-AI GPU/HBM demand (Quartr / Japan IR, 2026-04-22). See cross-theme: [cowos-packaging](themes/cowos-packaging.md). **Fiscal-year labeling caveat:** DISCO/Japan-IR call the year ended Mar-2026 "FY2026"; Quartr labels it "FY2025." Same period — flagged because aggregators disagree.

## Position in the value chain
DISCO buys precision components (spindles, optics, motion stages, abrasives) and converts them into the dominant **dicing / grinding / polishing** back-end toolset, selling to foundries, OSATs and memory makers for the **advanced-packaging and wafer-thinning** steps. It is the choke-point supplier for HBM stack thinning and 2.5D/3D package singulation — its read-through to the AI trade is direct (CoWoS/HBM volume), not just cyclical-WFE beta. DISCO is **process-agnostic across interconnect roadmaps** — it told Jefferies it is "involved in all new packaging systems," so the tool-pull holds whether the winning package is CoWoS, FOCoS or Intel EMIB-T (Jefferies, 2025-12-17). Sales recognize on equipment acceptance, so the quarterly path is lumpy; the monthly shipment release is the cleaner demand read.

## Debate / thesis
- **Bull:** DISCO is the cleanest pure-play on **advanced packaging + HBM wafer-thinning** — the back-end choke-point with ~70-80% share of HBM dicing/grinding equipment (WebSearch/Bitget, Jan-2026). Six consecutive record years, ~69% gross margin, ~40%+ OP margin, ~79% equity ratio — best-in-class semicap economics. Demand corroborated independently from the supply side: **@jukan05** flagged "DISCO dicing/grinding... tightening" alongside TEL price increases and full-capacity/double-shift fabs (repo briefing, 2026-06-12). HBM bit-growth + 2.5D/3D package proliferation is a multi-year volume tailwind, and AI GPUs need ever-thinner wafers (30-50µm) — squarely DISCO's franchise.
- **Broadening packaging-roadmap optionality:** DISCO told Jefferies it is **"involved in all new packaging systems"** and is seeing **"a lot of R&D activity in advanced packaging outside Taiwan,"** explicitly citing **Intel EMIB-T** — won't comment on specific projects, but if a US/Korea/Taiwan customer's new package realizes into production, "that is good for DISCO" (Jefferies, 2025-12-17). Read-through: DISCO captures dicing/grinding tool-pull whether the winning interconnect is CoWoS, FOCoS or EMIB-T, de-risking single-platform (CoWoS) dependence as Google/Meta evaluate EMIB-T for TPU v9 / next-gen MTIA amid CoWoS capacity constraints (Jefferies, 2025-12-17).
- **NAND / HBF call option:** Morgan Stanley is **OW on DISCO**, preferring it (with Advantest) among Japan SPE names for **HBF (high-bandwidth flash) exposure vs. UW Lam Research** — citing DISCO's "dominant position in the HBM grinder market" and expecting it to benefit significantly from any expansion of HBF-related system demand, as AI inference drives an incremental ~US$29bn NAND TAM by 2029e (MS, 2025-09-10). DISCO is also flagged among Japanese SPE with relatively high NAND-tool exposure (alongside Kokusai, TEL, Micronics Japan), so a NAND capex recovery is upside beyond the HBM/CoWoS core. MS placed DISCO in the **"most attractive" risk-reward quadrant** of its NAND coverage (MS, 2025-09-10).
- **Bear:** **Rich multiple** — Simply Wall St explicitly warns the stock "already trades on a rich earnings multiple" despite strong growth (Apr-2026); the AI/HBM up-cycle is priced in. Quarterly revenue is **acceptance-lumpy** (OP −33% QoQ in Q1), making prints volatile and easy to disappoint on timing. As a back-end tool maker it carries full **AI-capex-cycle beta** — an HBM/advanced-packaging air-pocket hits volumes directly. The NAND/HBF leg is a call option, not a base case: MS itself notes NAND-maker capex appetite "remains low" with suppliers prioritizing DRAM/HBM, and HBF solutions only available ~early 2027 (MS, 2025-09-10). Single-country (Japan) manufacturing base, ~90% overseas sales → FX (yen) and export-control/China exposure. Heavy customer concentration in a handful of foundry/OSAT/memory names.
- **Where the sell-side/buy-side stands:** **Thin English coverage**, but two attributable sell-side prints now in repo. **Morgan Stanley OW** (Japan SPE top pick alongside Advantest for HBM-grinder / HBF leverage; no explicit JPY PT in the NAND note) (MS, 2025-09-10). **Jefferies** corroborates broadening advanced-packaging activity and process-agnostic positioning (specialist-sales channel, 2025-12-17). Supply-chain read-through bullish (@jukan05, 2026-06-12); tape bullish (stock +9% on FY print). Treat valuation as the live debate, not direction.
