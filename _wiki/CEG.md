## Snapshot
The largest private power producer in the world and the largest clean-energy generator in the US post-Calpine (10-K FY25, 2026-02-24). A fleet of ~55 GW of capacity — nuclear, natural gas, geothermal, hydro, wind and solar — capable of supplying ~27M homes and ~10% of the country's clean energy. The core is the largest nuclear fleet in the US: ~22 GW generating 183 TWh of zero-emission electricity in 2025. Calpine (closed 2026-01-07) added ~23 GW across 72 gas/geothermal/battery assets, strong in Texas, California and the Northeast — making CEG also the largest natural gas and geothermal generator in the country (10-K FY25). Beyond generation, it is one of the largest competitive retail suppliers, serving ~2.5M accounts and three-quarters of the Fortune 100. The structural thesis: selling firm 24/7 carbon-free power to data-center/AI demand via long-term PPAs.

## Current state (latest quarter)
**Q1 FY26 (call 2026-05-11):** adjusted operating EPS $2.74, a beat of the $2.54 consensus and +$0.60 y/y; GAAP EPS $4.49. FY26 guidance reaffirmed at **$11.00–$12.00/share** (midpoint $11.50), with a base-earnings growth rate "above 20% through 2029". Repurchased ~1.2M shares at ~$285 ($335M) under the **$5B** buyback authorization. Free cash flow guided at **$8.4B for 2026–2027** and **$11.5–$13B for 2028–2029** (~45% growth at the midpoint). ~5,000 MW of new resources (nuclear uprates, gas, battery) submitted to the PJM interconnection queue. Hyperscaler 2026 capex cited as "almost 75% higher than last year" (Dominguez, Q1 FY26).

FY25 context (from the `_2026-03-31` outlook stub): Q4 FY25 adj EPS $2.30; revenue $6.07B; FY26 guidance initiated at the 2026-03-31 business outlook, the company's first update of the combined entity post-Calpine. The combined fleet serves 275M MWh of electricity and 800 Bcf of natural gas per year across 40 states (Q1 FY26).

**Operating drivers (Q3 FY25, call 2025-11-07):** nuclear capacity factor of 96.8%, ~4% above the industry average. Crane Clean Energy Center (formerly Three Mile Island) restart progressing well, "no new challenges emerging"; CEG asked FERC to transfer the Eddystone CIRs to accelerate capacity credit to 2027 (response expected Jun–Jul 2026, Q1 FY26). ~900–1,000 MW of nuclear uprates identified — Byron/Braidwood in the plan (already accretive), LaSalle/Limerick/Calvert Cliffs (190 MW) still out of EPS until 2030 (Q1 FY26). A 1,000 MW demand-response initiative via Grid Beyond — Dominguez compares it, in PJM's ELCC calculation, to "an entire nuclear plant" (Q3 FY25).

**Contracted hyperscaler PPAs (third-party detail, JPM):** the two marquee deals quantified — **Clinton Clean Energy Center (IL):** 20yr PPA with **Meta** for the full **1,121 MW** output, starting **June 2027** (announced 2025-06-03); **Crane/TMI Unit 1 restart:** 20yr PPA with **Microsoft** for **835 MW**, starting **2028** (announced 2024-09-23) (JPM, 2025-11-10 / 2025-11-04). JPM characterizes recent DC PPAs as typically **front-of-the-meter** (data center buys from the grid like any other customer) rather than behind-the-meter, to avoid the perception of removing capacity from other ratepayers — relevant to how CEG's deals are structured and to the affordability scrutiny from state regulators, who increasingly require exit/termination penalties so retail ratepayers don't absorb the cost (JPM, 2025-11-10).

## Debate / thesis
_Sell-side coverage is thin in this corpus: zero equity calls and no briefings roll-up for CEG (see INDEX). Bull/bear below derived mostly from the transcripts; analysts present on the calls: Morgan Stanley, Wolfe, Wells Fargo, Barclays, Jefferies, JPMorgan, Evercore, Scotiabank, Seaport, Melius. The two JPM AI-power thematics cited below reference CEG only as a third-party datapoint (no CEG rating or price target)._

- **Bull:** data-center demand described as "hotter than ever" and "past the seventh-inning stretch"; ~$0.75T of capital projected for data centers — "twice the size of the 3 largest listed US utilities" (Dominguez, Q3 FY25). CEG sells the product no one else has ready: firm, clean, 24/7 power, available **now** vs. new nuclear whose price "is far from set". The macro backdrop supports this: JPM models US power consumption growth accelerating to **~2%/yr through 2030** (vs. ~1% over the prior decade) on data-center load, while new supply lags — **~100 GW** of new generation needed to meet DC demand, gas-turbine lead times of 3–4 years and nuclear builds historically ~10 years and over budget, making electric generation a key bottleneck. JPM ranks nuclear as the **preferred technology** for its scale, carbon-free profile and 24/7 baseload — exactly CEG's existing fleet — and notes **>23 GW** of nuclear projects signed with hyperscalers since Oct-2024 (JPM, 2025-11-10 / 2025-11-04). McHugh: heat rates and spark spreads expanding, with greater price pressure in the outer years than in the near term (Q3 FY25). Uprates are capital efficient — no incremental O&M/fuel, "great investments", organic IRRs >10% (Dominguez/Smith, Q1 FY26). Calpine adds $2/share of EPS one year after close (Smith, Q1 FY26). PJM market reform (white paper "Powering Reliability Through Market Design") shifts revenue toward the energy market and co-optimizes reserves — seen as a positive (Dardis, Q1 FY26).

- **Bear:** the big hyperscaler deals keep **slipping** — promised "before the Q4 call" on Q3 FY25, still not announced as of Q1 FY26; some conversations "paused" awaiting PJM regulatory clarity (Dominguez, Q1 FY26). ERCOT forwards weak despite the data-center pipeline — CEG admits load "is not in the system yet, it's being built" and considers the market undervalued only from 2028–2029 (Q1 FY26): the thesis depends on timing. "Bring-your-own-generation" and new entrants (oil/gas, new techs) threaten the long-term earnings visibility of the fleet — Storozynski (Seaport) presses directly on how many units CEG will manage to contract (Q3 FY25); JPM corroborates that hyperscalers are "increasingly looking into building their own generation" (BYOG) as a response to the generation bottleneck (JPM, 2025-11-10). Lockup of 25M shares held by the Calpine sellers expires 2026-06-30 (another 25M on 2027-06-30) — a technical overhang (Smith, Q1 FY26). Option B of the PJM white paper (differentiated reliability treatment by load) raises "real legal questions" and still needs to be digested (Dardis, Q1 FY26).

- **Where the sell-side/buy-side stands:** no PTs/ratings in the co-located sources — no equity call and no archived briefing for CEG (INDEX, CEG section: "Equity calls (0)", "Briefings (email): —"); the JPM AI-power reports carry no CEG rating/PT. Coverage here is purely from transcripts; the tone of the analysts on the calls is constructive but focused on when (not if) the deals close. One archived rating: **Morgan Stanley (Stephen Byrd) screens CEG Overweight with ~38% upside to PT** (price $279.46, bull/bear skew 2.1), naming it in both the "AI Infrastructure + Powering AI" and "AI Adopters with Pricing Power" thematic screens (MS "Revisiting Our 10 Thematic Predictions for 2026", 2026-04-06). _Caveat from the same note: Byrd flags that **merchant power companies whose margins track power prices "may face challenges given greater policy efforts to reduce customer energy bills"** (GRID Act / state DC moratoria) — a risk to CEG's merchant-nuclear economics distinct from the contracting-timing bear above._ [../relat%C3%B3rios%20bons/MSR_000333CH_040626_2536.html](../relat%C3%B3rios%20bons/MSR_000333CH_040626_2536.html)

**Behind-the-meter / BYOG read-through (expert calls, 2026):** the threat to CEG's contracted-fleet visibility — "bring-your-own-generation" and new entrants — is corroborated by expert work. A behind-the-meter power expert (ex-GE Vernova) argues **BTM is a permanent solution, not a bridge** (grid reconnection raises public rates; ISOs "pick their battles"), and a midstream desk's DC expert puts **BTM at ~40% of power deals** (Wells Fargo "Aman Chakri BTM expert", 2026-05-27; WFC "midstream Friday wrap", 2026-06-05). A DC broker frames the counter for firm nuclear: the **"big four" hyperscalers get whatever they want, while Oracle is the IG-credit swing** and neoclouds need backstops — i.e., counterparty credit, not just power, gates contracting; the broker still sees **~30% of his pipeline as true grid-islanded BTM** (JPM DC broker call, 2026-05-04). Read as sharpening CEG's "deals keep slipping / BYOG threatens long-term visibility" bear, balanced against Bernstein's survey (98% prefer grid) already in the theme.

**Macro / capex read-through (JPM "AI Capex 2.0", 2026-06-16):** JPM now models **138 GW of US data-center capacity growth through 2030** (up from 122 GW in its Nov-2025 forecast), within **$5.5tn of total AI capex through 2030** (up from $5.1tn) — reiterating that **power remains the most critical constraint** on DC growth. Per EPRI, data centers could reach **9–17% of total US electricity demand by 2030 (from ~3–4% today)**. The report's data-center/utility deal table corroborates CEG's marquee contract — the **Constellation–Meta Clinton Nuclear PPA, 1.1 GW, 20-yr beginning June 2027** (announced 2025-06-03) — alongside a wave of nuclear/gas hyperscaler PPAs. JPM frames hyperscaler "all-of-the-above" power procurement (BTM gas, BYOG, build-to-transfer) as the workaround to a grid-interconnection queue of up to 7 years and regulated-utility capex ($208bn in 2025, ~30% on generation) that can't keep pace — a constructive backdrop for CEG's firm, clean, 24/7 nuclear fleet (JPM "AI Capex 2.0", 2026-06-16).

## Risks
- **Data-center contracting slippage** — the growth thesis depends on closing long-term PPAs that keep getting delayed by regulatory dependence (PJM/FERC) and third parties in interconnection (Q3 FY25, Q1 FY26).
- **Weakness in ERCOT forwards** and the timing of load arrival (10-K FY25; Q1 FY26).
- **PJM regulatory/legislative risk** — capacity-market design, colocation/BYOG rules and differentiated load treatment (Q1 FY26).
- **Regulatory/affordability scrutiny** — state regulators focused on hyperscalers paying their fair share; DC tariffs increasingly include early-termination penalties and front-of-meter structures to protect retail ratepayers (JPM, 2025-11-10).
- **Calpine integration/divestitures** — assets to sell for DOJ/FERC approval; natural-gas exposure brings fuel-price volatility and potential future carbon cost (10-K FY25; Q3 FY25).
- **Nuclear operating risk** — concentrated fleet; safety/outages; relicensings depend on NRC approval.
- **Calpine share overhang** (lockups Jun-2026/Jun-2027).
- **New nuclear cost/execution** — CEG remains cautious; significant capital only after a durable PPA + firm prices with proven constructibility (Q3 FY25).
