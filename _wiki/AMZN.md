## Snapshot
Three reportable segments: **North America** (Q1'26 $104.1B rev, +12%, 7.9% OI margin), **International** ($39.8B, +11% ex-FX), and **AWS** ($37.6B, +28%, $14.2B OI). AWS is ~21% of revenue but the overwhelming majority of operating income and the entire valuation debate. On top of the retail flywheel (1P/3P marketplace, FBA, Prime, fast/quick commerce) sit three high-margin attach businesses — **Advertising** ($17.2B Q1'26, +22%), **Prime Video**, and now an AI-infrastructure complex (Trainium/Graviton custom silicon, Bedrock, Anthropic anchor). New optionality: **Amazon LEO** (satellite broadband, commercial launch Q3'26) and **Amazon Supply Chain Services** (3P logistics / LTL). The stock is positioned by the Street as the "infrastructure backbone of AI" — OpenAI + Anthropic both running on AWS (JPM AI Capex & Financing, 2025-11-10: "best positioned of the hyperscalers to generate tangible returns from AI investment").

- **Trainium economics / the silicon long (SemiAnalysis "AWS Trainium3 Deep Dive," 2025-12-04):** Most detailed third-party validation of the custom-silicon thesis. Trainium3 (GA + Trainium4 announced at re:Invent) is a "step-function" upgrade — N3P node (from N5), 144GB HBM3E 12-hi (Hynix/Micron, up from sub-par Samsung on Trn2), +70% memory bandwidth, doubled MXFP8 FLOPs + new MXFP4 support, ~1,000W TDP. New all-to-all **switched** scale-up fabric: NL32x2 air-cooled ("Teton3 PDS") is the volume SKU in '26; NL72x2 liquid ("Teton3 MAX") is AWS's answer to Nvidia's GB200 NVL72 — AWS is the **first non-Nvidia vendor to ship a switched scale-up rack** (ahead of AMD MI450X). Core economics: Trainium gives up marketed FP8/FP4 density vs Nvidia/AMD, but systems are "dramatically cheaper" because AWS **avoids the margin stacking embedded in Nvidia servers** → materially lower upfront cluster capex per logical chip; lower operating TCO driven mostly by chip TDP (Trn2 ~500W, Trn3 ~1,000W vs ~1,200W GB200 / 1,400W GB300). Caveat: no native FP4 support → higher TCO per marketed FP4 FLOP than Nvidia. AWS is open-sourcing its software stack (PyTorch backend, NKI compiler, then XLA/JAX) to build a CUDA-style ecosystem; LNC=8 (the mode mainstream researchers want) not supported until mid-2026. AWS's air-cooling-first datacenter design (vs peers' DLC pivots) is a time-to-market + fungibility advantage, benefiting Project Rainier ramp. Bottom line: a credible NVDA challenger on perf-per-TCO, alongside Google TPUv7 and AMD MI450X — "Nvidia stays King as long as Jensen keeps accelerating." Supply-chain read-through: Marvell **lost the Trainium3 socket to Alchip** (poor Trn2 execution); Alchip leads Trn4 backend (UALink + NVLink-Fusion tracks). UBS (2026 overview) sees MRVL retaining only "some revenue for Trainium 3" via the Celestial AI acquisition — i.e. MRVL is no longer the lead designer.
- **Anthropic anchor — asset and dependency (JPM "Anthropic Buckle Up," 2025-09-18):** AMZN is Anthropic's **primary cloud and training partner**, $8B invested to date, distributing Claude to tens of thousands of customers via Bedrock. **Project Rainier = 2.25 GW dedicated supercomputer**, ~5x Anthropic's prior cluster, trained on Trainium — AWS cites 30–40% better price-performance vs comparable GPU instances, which could underpin Anthropic's price competitiveness (relevant given Claude API costs run ~8x Google/OpenAI). ## Risks
- **Capital intensity / ROIC**: $200B FY26 capex (~$150B AWS-driven per JPM 2025-11-10), depreciation drag on AWS margin, suppressed near-term FCF; potential shift toward IG debt issuance / sale-leaseback financing (Q1'26 call; JPM AI Capex & Financing, 2025-11-10; 10-K capex-cycle disclosures).
- **AI demand concentration**: backlog skewed to a few frontier labs; Anthropic (Project Rainier 2.25 GW, $8B AMZN invested) is both the franchise asset and a single-point dependency, plus reported AI-shopping tension between the two (JPM, 2025-09-18 / 2025-11-10).
- **Component/memory cost inflation** and supply constraints squeezing infra unit economics (Q1'26 call); Trainium3 HBM3E sourcing shift to Hynix/Micron and N3P yield are gating items (SemiAnalysis, 2025-12-04).
- **Competition**: Azure (MSFT AI ARR >$37B, +123% — Bernstein 2026-04-30) and GCP (backlog surge, TPU rack sales) contesting AI cloud; Trainium still trails Nvidia on FP4 and CUDA-ecosystem maturity (SemiAnalysis, 2025-12-04); agentic commerce threatening retail/ad funnel.
- **Regulatory/antitrust, FX, energy/power availability, geopolitical** (10-K FY25 risk factors; LEO/supply-chain execution risk); labor-automation (up to ~75%) carries social/regulatory exposure (JPM, 2025-11-10).
