## Snapshot
AXT manufactures compound-semiconductor substrates — principally indium phosphide (InP), gallium arsenide (GaAs), and germanium. InP exceeded 50% of quarterly revenue as of Q1 2026, making AXTI the most direct public-market expression of the InP supply constraint that has become the bottleneck for AI-datacenter photonics (CPO / optical transceivers). China controls 70%+ of global InP output; AXT + Sumitomo together hold ~80% of non-China InP capacity. The stock re-rated from a ~$500M market cap to a reported $7.24B on the optical-supply thesis, but is increasingly framed by FinTwit as a higher-beta "trading vehicle," with [LITE](LITE.md)/[COHR](COHR.md)/IQE preferred for lower China-regulatory-risk core exposure.

## Current state (latest quarter)
No full quarterly financials in the ported corpus. InP was >50% of Q1 2026 revenue (@ParadisLabs, 2026-06-12/13). Earlier, AXTI pre-announced an FQ4 2025 downside: revenue guided ~$23MM vs. Street ~$28.75MM (~20% miss), "primarily due to fewer export-control permits for indium phosphide being issued by China's Ministry of Commerce than previously expected"; management said underlying demand was "very strong" (Vital Knowledge, 2026-01-08) — the first concrete confirmation that MOFCOM permitting is an active revenue gate, not a theoretical risk.

## Debate / thesis
- **Bull — InP supply oligopoly + demand surge:** AI inter-rack bandwidth is upgrading 400G→800G and CPO is going mainstream. AXT + Sumitomo control ~80% of non-China InP. China eased some InP export restrictions in June 2026 (first 2026 batch approved, relieving H2 CPO schedules), while CSPs (led by Google) are personally visiting non-China substrate makers to secure long-term supply — entrenching pricing power (@jukan05, @aleabitoreddit, 2026-06-15/16/17). Every major optical name ([LITE](LITE.md), [COHR](COHR.md), [AAOI](AAOI.md)) needs InP substrates it cannot quickly switch away from due to validation cycles.
- **Bear — China regulatory exposure + trading-vehicle discount:** AXTI is already obtaining permits to ship to US companies' ex-US sites (e.g., COHR Sweden), showing the permitting regime is a live gating factor (@crux_capital_, 2026-06-12). @ParadisLabs, after taking profits, explicitly demoted AXTI from core hold to trading vehicle, preferring [LITE](LITE.md) (lower China-supply beta), [COHR](COHR.md) (InP-permit torque), and IQE (asymmetry) (2026-06-12/13). The ~14x MC run sets a high bar.
- **Debate — 6-inch InP transition:** [COHR](COHR.md) is ~1 year early to 6-inch InP (4x capacity expansion underway), which could displace AXTI substrate demand if validated at scale. @insane_analyst raised yield concerns and noted "there is a reason Lumentum and AXTI both avoiding 6-inch" — implying AXTI's conservatism is either prudence or a capability gap (2026-05-07, 2026-06-12).
- **Where the sell-side stands:** No formal equity-research ratings or PTs for AXTI in the ported corpus. Jefferies Asia TMT Sales (Rankie Wong) mentions AXTI in roundup subjects (sales commentary, no rating/PT). Vital Knowledge covers AXTI episodically (see the Jan 8 preannouncement). FinTwit consensus: higher-beta InP play, trimmed after the run.
- **Jefferies optical expert call — AXTI named directly in the InP supply chain (Jefferies · expert "Rick Li," 2026-07-14).** A Jefferies-hosted optics-expert call lists AXTI explicitly among the related names in the InP/optical-transceiver bottleneck (alongside AVGO, Marvell, Sumitomo Electric, Innolight, DSBJ) — independent sell-side corroboration of the core thesis, not just FinTwit. The call reiterates that **InP substrate stays indispensable regardless of modulation path** (EML, SiPh, or the emerging TFLN at 3.2T all still need an external InP CW laser), supporting AXTI's structural relevance beyond any single technology transition. One nuance to flag: the call also notes a first Chinese **200G EML** entrant (**DSBJ, targeting MP 2H26**) and that **China controls ~70% of *global* InP supply** — since DSBJ is a downstream EML-chip maker likely sourced on domestic Chinese substrate, this doesn't directly erode AXTI/Sumitomo's ~80% *ex-China* share, but it is a watch-item for how fast China's own InP-substrate supply chain verticalizes behind its EML entrants (Jefferies optical expert call, 2026-07-14).

## Catalysts / what to watch
- **China InP export-license cadence** — whether eased restrictions hold and ex-US shipment permits (e.g., COHR Sweden) clear.
- **6-inch InP validation** at [COHR](COHR.md) and whether AXTI follows or stays conservative.
- Next print: revenue/EPS/GM and the GaAs/Ge contribution beyond "InP >50%."
- Any US-government response to China InP export controls; CSP long-term supply agreements.

## Risks
- China MOFCOM permitting directly gates revenue (proven by the FQ4'25 miss).
- 6-inch InP displacement risk if [COHR](COHR.md) scales first.
- "Trading vehicle" beta / large MC run-up sets a high valuation bar.
- Customer/supply-chain concentration in a regulatory-sensitive material.
