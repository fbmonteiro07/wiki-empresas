## Snapshot
Vertiv is the leading pure-play in **critical digital infrastructure** for data centers, communication networks, and commercial/industrial environments — it powers, cools, deploys and services the electronics that process and store data (10-K, 2026-02-13). Portfolio spans **power** (AC/DC power management, single- and three-phase UPS, low/medium-voltage switchgear, busbar/busway, rack PDU), **thermal management** (air- and liquid-cooled — chillers, CDUs, rear-door, direct-to-chip), **integrated modular/prefab** (SmartRun overhead busway, OneCore converged white-space), **racks/enclosures** (Great Lakes), and a large **service & spares** annuity. Reports three geographic segments: **Americas** (~68% of Q1'26 sales), **APAC** (~19%), **EMEA** (~12%). End markets: data centers (hyperscale/cloud, colo, neocloud, enterprise), comms networks, C&I — with hyperscale/cloud + neocloud (CoreWeave, Nebius) the AI growth engine. The directest listed AI-datacenter power/cooling pure-play, and NVIDIA's named **800 VDC** ecosystem partner.

## Position in the value chain
Vertiv sits in the middle of the AI-datacenter physical-infrastructure stack: it buys power and thermal components (power semis/SiC, transformers, compressors, copper busbar, controls) and integrates them into the **gray-space-to-white-space** systems — power train, thermal chain, racks, prefab modules, plus lifecycle services — that hyperscalers, colos and enterprises plug compute into. It is a direct **AI-datacenter pure-play** and, via the **NVIDIA 800 VDC collaboration**, co-designs the next-gen power architecture (centralized rectifiers, high-efficiency DC busways, rack-level DC-DC) for Rubin Ultra-era AI factories (Vertiv–NVIDIA PR, 2025-10; Q3 FY25, 2025-10-22).

**Market sizing / share.** BofA sizes the global DC equipment market at $400bn+ in 2024 → >$500bn 2025E, +23% CAGR to >$900bn by 2028, of which **Electrical equipment ~$18bn and Thermal ~$10bn in 2024**; within those, **Schneider leads electrical (~22% share) and Vertiv is the thermal share leader (~17%)** (BofA DC primer, 2025-09-15). BofA's content/MW work shows the architecture shift is content-accretive: NVL72 raises infrastructure content/MW to ~$3.1mn from ~$2.8mn for DGX SuperPOD (electrical +9%, thermal +18%, driven by CDUs/power-shelves), with Kyber/800V roughly in line with NVL72 (BofA, 2025-09-15). JPM frames the addressable pools as growing faster than the installed base: **server PSU TAM 3-4x to ~$38bn by 2028 (~64% CAGR); liquid-cooling TAM to ~$30bn by 2028 (~51% CAGR)** — CDU/sidecar (~$21bn) the largest liquid-cooling chunk — implying ~$450mn PSU / ~$360mn liquid-cooling TAM per incremental 1GW by 2028, with Vertiv named a key power and thermal beneficiary (JPM AI Datacenters & Power, 2025-11-04). VRT itself guides the DC market to **10-13% blended unit growth** (15-17% hyperscale/colo, 4-6% enterprise) and ~100GW added globally 2023-29; JPM's own model (~25% GW CAGR, installed base doubling to ~160GW) calls that "conservative" (JPM AI Capex & Financing, 2025-11-10).
