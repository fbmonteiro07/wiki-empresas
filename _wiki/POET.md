## Snapshot
POET Technologies develops an Electrical-Optical Interposer (EOI) platform — wafer-scale optical integration targeting co-packaged optics (CPO) and near-package optics (NPO) for 800G/1.6T datacenter transceivers. The company is pre-revenue at meaningful scale (~$1M annual run-rate per FinTwit, May 2026) but secured a landmark $50M initial order from Lumilens in May 2026 (scalable to $500M over five years), framed as a ~50x run-rate de-risking event. POET sits in the photonics/CPO basket alongside [AAOI](AAOI.md), SIVE, ALMU, and LWLG, with persistent short pressure and a live valuation debate vs. Taiwan-listed FOCI (3363).

## Debate / thesis
- **Bull — CPO optionality + Lumilens de-risks the story:** The **$50M Lumilens order (scalable to $500M / 5 yrs)** for EOI wafer-scale integration — engineering samples late 2026, production targeted 2027 — gives POET its first material revenue anchor (2026-05-15). Stock rose +22–27% on the news. The deal implicates the broader chain: SIVE (lasers), Semtech (receivers), Foxconn/Liteon (module assembly). The $400M raise at a premium gives a long runway. POET's InP exposure is directly relevant to datacenter laser demand (SemiAnalysis InP thread, 2026-05-05).
- **Bear — valuation vs. FOCI; revenue still negligible:** @aleabitoreddit (213k–372k views) argues FOCI (3363, ~$3.1B MC) is "literally the bottleneck for CPO volume ramp" and NVDA/TSM's primary CPO supplier — yet POET traded at a comparable/higher MC (~$3.14B at the $50M announcement) with a fraction of FOCI's revenue and ramp visibility (2026-05-18). @crux_capital_ trimmed his position on the Lumilens pop. The $50M's ~50x rev-rate nature underscores how tiny current revenues are.
- **Structural — CPO timeline risk:** Jefferies (Beavington, 2026-06-12) flagged a 2–3 year CPO OIO pushout to 2030–31 due to interposer overheating. If CPO slips, POET's NPO/CPO addressable market is delayed; near-term pluggable 800G/1.6T is the bridging opportunity.
- **Where the sell-side stands:** No formal sell-side PT/rating found in the ported corpus. Coverage is FinTwit-driven, with persistent short pressure on the small-cap optics basket (@crux_capital_, June 2026).
