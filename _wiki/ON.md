## Snapshot
onsemi is a US-listed **IDM intelligent-power and intelligent-sensing** vendor — power discretes/modules (Si MOSFETs/IGBTs, **SiC** "EliteSiC", **GaN incl. vertical GaN**), analog/mixed-signal, and **CMOS image sensors**. Three reportable segments: **Power Solutions Group (PSG, 47% of FY25 revenue), Analog & Mixed-Signal Group (AMG, 38%), Intelligent Sensing Group (ISG, 15%)** (10-K, 2026-02-09). FY2025 revenue **$6.0bn** (Q4 FY25 call, 2026-02-09). By end-market: **Automotive 51%, Industrial 28%, Other 21%** (Other = AI data center, computing, consumer, networking) (10-K, 2026-02-09). The franchise is a cyclical auto/industrial power + image-sensor business — bottoming after seven quarters of auto decline — now layering a **structural AI-datacenter-power (800V DC) ramp** that is re-rating the analog/power group.

## Position in the value chain
onsemi is **vertically integrated in SiC** — it brings substrate/boule growth largely in-house to assure supply, control cost and differentiate device performance (10-K, 2026-02-09) — then fabs/packages power devices, analog and image sensors and sells into **automotive/EV, industrial (incl. energy storage), and AI data-center power**. The incremental driver is **800V DC**: management quantifies content at **~$115k per 800V rack vs ~$9.5k at standard 120kW**, with AI-datacenter revenue set to **double YoY in 2026** off a **>$250m FY25** base (Q1 FY26 call, 2026-05-04). Citi frames onsemi as uniquely differentiated via **vertical GaN** (GaN leads sub-650V at the rack; SiC better >1,000V infrastructure) (Citi, briefing 2026-06-15). The same SiC franchise carries **EV/industrial cyclical exposure** — SiC GM sits below corporate average on underutilization. See [800v-dc-power](themes/800v-dc-power.md) · [ai-datacenter-power](themes/ai-datacenter-power.md).

## Debate / thesis
- **Bull:** Cyclical trough is in — auto grew YoY for the first time in seven quarters and gross margin is climbing through the downturn (38.2% Q4 FY25 → 38.5% Q1 FY26) on FabRite footprint cuts (12% capacity reduction FY25), so incremental volume drops through at high margins toward the 53% target (Q1 FY26 / Q4 FY25 calls). The structural kicker is **AI data-center power**: >$250m FY25, doubling in 2026, with ~$115k of content per 800V rack and a uniquely complete "wall-to-core" power tree plus **vertical GaN** (first revenue 2027) and the Qorvo **SiC JFET** acquisition feeding AC-DC PSU content (Q1/Q3 FY25 calls; Citi 800V note, briefing 2026-06-15). Citi has onsemi on a **90-day upside Catalyst Watch** into the **Sept 16 Analyst Day**, expecting a raised target model + renewed SiC momentum (Citi/Atif Malik, briefing 2026-06-15). Citi formally **raised the PT $100→$120 (20x 2028E EPS, up from the 3-yr avg 17x)** and lifted C26/C27/C28 EPS **+1%/+12%/+9% to $3.07/$4.64/$6.00** on price hikes + extended analog recovery; Citi sees ON (with TXN) as having the **largest GM-expansion opportunity (>10pp)** given ~65% internal wafer manufacturing, and is the **only name in Citi's coverage developing vertical GaN** (sampling now, shipment 2H27). On energy-storage (an 800V-industrial beneficiary), ON's **ESS business grew ~40% CAGR over five years and holds ~60% market share** (Citi "800V power", 2026-06-15). FCF $1.4bn FY25 (24% margin) funds a $6bn buyback (~100% of FCF returned) (Q4 FY25 call).
- **Bear:** It is a **share game, not a TAM game** — Citi's 800V *top pick is TXN* ("DC power share gains 2H26… gaining share vs Infineon/MPWR/Renesas"), with onsemi only Neutral; the GaN-at-rack vs SiC-at-infrastructure split means onsemi competes against TXN's quadrupled high-voltage GaN capacity (Citi, briefing 2026-06-15). UBS's house view is the cleanest articulation of the margin bear case: **"the margin picture will remain structurally challenged unless demand inflects substantially (a 'v-shaped recovery') or management more aggressively rationalizes capacity"** — global demand still soft with inventory only beginning to clear after a year of doldrums, and **Chinese indigenization** ramping as competitive pressure (UBS/Arcuri, 2025-12-22). UBS's own model bears this out — op margin only reaches ~26% by FY27 vs the 40% target, and its FCF/share sits ~32% below the Street (UBS/Arcuri, 2025-12-22). Channel checks push the 800V earnings impact out **2–3 years** — "every DC construction PM… no plans to do this anytime soon," and **China can flood SiC/GaN** like it did InP (@bubbleboi, briefing 2026-06-15; @trendforce, briefing 2026-06-17). The legacy book remains the drag: **SiC GM below corporate average on underutilization**, ~5% of FY25 revenue exiting in 2026, China EV/passenger weakness, and a heavy multi-year restructuring tab (>$0.9bn of charges across FY25 + Q1 FY26). A German auto Tier-1 procurement expert added a demand-side negative — onsemi demand "definitely not increased that much" vs peers, held back by a **lack of new innovations / new product lines and an inability to meet OEM requirements** (Expert call, 2026-06-08). Jefferies' analog analyst is structurally negative on the discrete-power exposure that defines ON's PSG segment — SiC "is a pretty bad industry... as long as you play in silicon carbide, it's going to be a negative for your margin," and silicon IGBTs a "terrible, horrible industry" being killed by China — vs silicon MOSFETs / analog / MCUs which he likes; this is the cleanest external articulation of why ON's power-discrete book caps margins through the cycle (Jefferies "Analog Semis," 2026-06-03). The AlphaSense Infineon primer quantifies the operating-leverage downside: **ON's FY25 EBIT margin collapsed to 1.4% from a 30.8% FY23 peak** — the most severe compression in the analog/power peer set — "illustrating the intense operating leverage inherent in discrete power semiconductor manufacturing" (AlphaSense "IFX Primer", 2026-06-22).

## Risks
- **Auto/EV + industrial cyclicality:** 79% of revenue is auto+industrial; China passenger-vehicle softness, EV mix, tariff-driven order hesitancy (10-K 2026-02-09; Q2 FY25 call).
- **800V share & timing:** TXN/IFX/STM/MPWR/Renesas competing for the same content; channel checks flag a 2–3yr lag to earnings (Citi/@bubbleboi, briefing 2026-06-15).
- **SiC underutilization:** SiC GM below corporate average; gross margin highly utilization-sensitive (68%→77% recovery still well below optimal) (Q1 FY26 / Q2 FY25 calls).
- **Margin / capacity-rationalization risk:** UBS sees margins structurally capped absent a V-shaped demand recovery or harder capacity cuts — its op-margin path tops out near 26% by FY27 vs the 40% target (UBS/Arcuri, 2025-12-22).
- **China supply flood:** Chinese SiC/GaN vendors and broader indigenization challenging IDM incumbents; potential oversupply/pricing pressure (UBS/Arcuri, 2025-12-22; @trendforce, briefing 2026-06-17; 10-K trade-restriction risk factors).
- **AI-demand / responsible-AI risk factor:** 10-K explicitly flags that AI-power products "may not capture market share as expected" and AI deployment/returns could be materially lower than expected (10-K 2026-02-09, Item 1A).
- **Manufacturing concentration & restructuring execution:** interdependent multi-facility network — a single-site disruption is disproportionate; large ongoing realignment charges (10-K 2026-02-09).
- **Customer/distributor concentration:** one distributor ~12% of revenue Q1 FY26 (10-Q 2026-05-04).
