## What it is / why it matters

The jump from 48V/415V AC distribution to **800V DC** inside the data center is the single biggest power-architecture change in 20+ years, and it is being forced by NVIDIA's roadmap rather than chosen. As GPU racks climb from ~130kW (Blackwell) toward 450kW (Rubin Ultra) and 600kW+ (next-gen "Feynman"/Kyber), the only way to push that power without drowning the rack in copper is to raise the voltage and drop the current (Citi 800V expert, 2026-06-16; UBS 800V expert, 2026-05-14). The transition reshuffles a multi-decade-stable supply chain: it kills low-frequency transformers and legacy UPS, mints a new market for solid-state transformers (SSTs), and re-rates the power-semi and connector content per megawatt. For a buy-sider this is both a content-growth story (analog/power semis, connectors, electrical OEMs) and a disruption story (who adapts vs. who gets disintermediated). Trigger to watch is simple: **track the chip ramp and you track the 800V ramp** (Citi, 2026-06-16).

## State of play (latest)

- **Timeline pulled forward, not delayed.** NVIDIA's Kyber/Rubin Ultra rack is the inflection point; expert consensus is the 800V DC standard arrives with that rack in 2H26 (AlphaSense expert call, 2026-06-03). FinTwit flagged it was "pulled ahead to Q3 2026" with NVDA Vera Rubin + GOOG next-gen DCs as first adopters (@aleabitoreddit / Commercial Times, via NVDA briefing 2026-06-15). JPM spec sales models **15–20% adoption** for 800V near-term (JPM AI-power pitch, 2026-06-11).
- **It rolls out in phases, not a switch.** Three flavors coexist (Citi, 2026-06-16): (1) **bipolar ±400V DC sidecar** — CSP-driven (Google/Meta "Mt. Diablo"), easiest because it sits inside the ~600V regulatory envelope and reuses EV/auto power electronics, retrofittable in brownfield; (2) **monopolar 800V DC sidecar** — NVIDIA-driven, needs supply-chain requalification; (3) **full 800V SST** architecture delivering DC straight to the rack — the eventual end-state, but not mainstream until **late 2028–2029** (Citi 2026-06-16; UBS 2026-05-14; AlphaSense puts SSTs ~24–36 months out, 2026-06-03).
- **Power delivery on the board is changing too.** Conversion moves from today's multi-stage (800→50→12→1V) toward vertical power delivery this year and integrated voltage regulators (IVR) over the next few years, shrinking VRM-to-processor distance to cut losses; ~11 semi suppliers are fighting across the conversion stages with no clear winner yet (Citi, 2026-06-16).
- **Content per MW is the bull metric.** Monolithic Power is the purest play (>50% of sales data-center, guided 65% y/y DC-power growth); TI ~15% of sales; ADI, Infineon, Microchip all guiding 40–100% DC-power growth this year (Citi, 2026-06-16). The debate is whether >20% DC sales mix re-rates the analog group's multiple into the 30s — Citi says yes, "we've seen this movie before in networking."
- **Adoption sequencing firming up (Citi).** Citi now frames the phased ramp with dates: CSP-driven ±400V HVDC sidecar first; NVDA-led monopolar 800V sidecars ramping with **Rubin Ultra in late 2H27 (supporting up to ~1 MW)**; full 800V SST architecture for >1 MW from **2029** (greenfield). Power-per-rack quadruples ~120–130kW today → ~250kW in 2026 (Vera Rubin NVL144) → ~600kW in 2027 (Rubin Ultra NVL576/Kyber) → >1 MW in 2028 (Feynman) (Citi "800V power", 2026-06-15). On-board conversion phases: 3-stage (800→48/54→12→sub-1V) first ~2H27 on Rubin Ultra; 2-stage direct (800→12/6→sub-1V) longer-term ~late 2028 on Feynman; Rubin/Rubin Ultra stay on *lateral* power delivery on cost; vertical power modules carry ≥3x the content of lateral discretes, MPWR already shipping vertical modules to Google in 2025 (Citi, 2026-06-15).
- **Power-delivery TAM the new sizing metric.** Citi sizes the rack-level GPU power-delivery market (800V→sub-1V) at a **>70% CAGR, ~$2B (2025/26) → ~$12B by 2028**, Stage-2 sub-1V conversion ~60% of content; incumbents Infineon/MPWR/Renesas with TXN the relative share-gainer ramping 2H26 (Citi, 2026-06-15). Separately, the broader Industrial-classified HVDC grid-to-DC opportunity (ESS+SST+SSCB) is only ~$2B / ~2% of the $100B Industrial semi TAM — "limited over the next 24 months." JPM frames it bigger across the whole stack: power-management content TAM ~$20B (2025) → ~$45B (2028) at ~+30% CAGR (~$1mn/MW); AI power interconnect ~$2B → $4B+ (40%+ CAGR, ~$150 content/XPU); WBG power devices ~$1B (2025) → $5B+ (2030) at 30%+ CAGR, long-term $25K–$35K/MW split ~evenly SiC/GaN; in-rack power scales ~8x from 132kW to 1 MW over 5 years; 800V cuts amperage ~15x vs 54V (JPM "800V", 2026-06-09).
- **GaN at the rack, SiC at infrastructure (chemistry split).** GaN preferred for sub-650V conversion at server/rack level (leverages mature 650V EV ecosystem; suits ±400V bipolar where single-device stress ≤650V); SiC for >1,000V single-stage infrastructure conversion (rectifiers, power shelves, SSTs, DC protection). TXN among few high-voltage (>900V) GaN vendors, internal GaN capacity **quadrupled since Oct'24**; ON the only coverage name developing vertical GaN (sampling now, shipping 2H27). Gartner DC GaN+SiC transistor market ~$130M (2025) → ~$650M (2030), ~38% CAGR (Citi, 2026-06-15). Power-delivery supplier landscape is fragmented (~11 vendors at GTC 2026) on standardized footprints, capping pricing power and integration-premium capture (Citi, 2026-06-15).
- **Efficiency is the prize.** Reducing conversion stages saves ~4–7% end-to-end efficiency (each conversion is 1–3%); NVIDIA's pitch to customers is "take the 7% and buy more chips" (AlphaSense expert, 2026-06-03; UBS, 2026-05-14). Lab studies cited move AC ~78% efficient to ~94% in the DC end-state.
- **SST pricing, the UPS kill, and the DC-breaker choke point (Delta SST expert).** A second 800V expert call (ex-Amazon DC-infra head, now Delta) puts hard numbers on the architecture economics: **SSTs price at ~1.4–1.6x conventional transformers today**, falling with scale/competition; the full 800V architecture **eliminates the UPS** (removing the double-conversion loss) and legacy isolation transformers, going utility-drop straight to the rack PSU — a **1–3% efficiency gain worth >$100M/yr opex on a gigawatt campus**, which is what's pulling finance/investors into the switch. SST lead times are **~10 months at the earliest**. The 800V trigger is the chip: **~180–200 kW/rack** is the point where "you can't get enough copper into the rack." Adoption pilots went 3 MW (last year) → 10 MW (this year) → a **"huge jump in 2027."** The chief bottlenecks are **DC breakers — "ABB is king"** (DC arc doesn't self-extinguish; only a few makers) — and **in-rack PSUs** (thousands of orders placed). Delta is building **three US factories** (one integration/skid, two components) to localize; hyperscalers prefer single-OEM ("one throat to choke") but take whatever's available now (AlphaSense/Delta "800V SST expert", 2026-05-20). Also flagged the **plus/minus-400V → 800V switch** (POs being cancelled for ±400V in favor of 800V) and **high-voltage substation-transformer shortage** as an extra tailwind to SST/direct-DC.
- **Infineon "grid-to-core" content (IFX primer).** Infineon commands a **~43% share of AI-server power semiconductors, rising to ~47% in 2026**, with AI-power revenue guided to **double from €750m (FY25) → €1.6bn (FY26) → €3.3bn (FY28)** at **55–60% gross margins** (vs. ~40% corporate). Its 800V positioning spans the full stack — **SiC-based solid-state transformers at the grid, PSUs, and rack DC-DC** ("grid-to-core"), and it joined **NVIDIA's MGX AI-Factory ecosystem to support 800 VDC** power architectures; AI rack power requirements shift **10–20 kW → 100 kW → 800 VDC** (IFX Primer, AlphaSense, 2026-06-22). Pulled forward the **€5bn Dresden Smart Power Fab to summer 2026**; backlog surged to €25bn.
- **Silicon MOSFETs / VRMs are the data-center analog inflection (Jefferies analog).** The 800V/AI-power pull is creating an analog "upcycle" — the **voltage regulator module (VRM) in the data center is driving massive silicon-MOSFET growth** for Infineon, where MOSFETs behave "almost like an analog business" (>30% op margin, little new capacity vs. GaN). Data-center silicon-MOSFET consumption goes from **~¼ of automotive volume (2024) → roughly equal (2026) → bigger (2027)**; STMicro participates on the **optical** side (DC optical revenue guided ~$1–2bn). The structural difference vs. the 2021 cycle: AI-data-center demand is **structural, not cyclical**, so the content/MW re-rating has a longer runway (Jefferies "analog semis IFX/STM", 2026-06-03).

## Key debates

- **Bull / where the money is:** Content per MW rises across the stack and re-rates the analog/power-semi group; new SST + DC-protection markets open from zero. TI is Citi's top idea (PT $345, best-positioned on capacity + GaN/WBG), with a positive catalyst watch on ON Semi (vertical GaN, Sept analyst day) (Citi, 2026-06-16). Connectors (Amphenol, TE) win on the copper math — at current architecture a 1MW rack needs ~200kg of busbar copper, "astronomical" and physically displacing compute space, so higher voltage is unavoidable (Citi, 2026-06-16). SST optionality is a free call on GEV/electrical OEMs (Bernstein power names, 2026-06-18).
- **Bear / what could break it:** It's slower than the hype. A "very well-known enterprise/hyperscaler crossover" has hard-committed to **no DC until 2032**; sidecar is a floor-space eater feeding a 36-month chip on 15-year power amortization; SST players "don't even have proof of concept yet" with no field hours (AlphaSense expert, 2026-06-03). Real gating items are DC breakers/protection (DC arc doesn't self-extinguish — only 2–3 makers), insulation/clearance, EMI, and an OCP/UL safety spec not even published yet (UBS, 2026-05-14; AlphaSense, 2026-06-03). Incumbents (Eaton/Schneider) have little financial incentive to disrupt overflowing AC order books (AlphaSense, 2026-06-03).
- **Copper "tax" nuance:** absolute copper content actually *decreases* per rack at 800V vs. 48V (current falls, busbar cross-section shrinks); the offset is the Rubin Ultra TDP step-up, leaving content roughly flat-to-modestly-up gen/gen — not the runaway copper bull some assume (UBS expert Q&A, 2026-05-14).
- **Timeline / inflection points:** sidecar production must be ready 2H26; SST proof-of-concept this year → scale manufacturing mid-2027; GEV piloting an SST with a hyperscaler in fall 2026; full SST mainstream 2028–2029; long tail to 1,500V thereafter (UBS, 2026-05-14; Bernstein, 2026-06-18; Citi, 2026-06-16).
