_Wiki · generated 2026-06-19 · cross-company theme · sources: equity calls (`../_equity_calls/Semis`), briefing roll-ups (`../_briefings/by-ticker`), earnings transcripts, company pages. Company pages: [../00_INDEX.md](../00_INDEX.md)._

## What it is / why it matters
CoWoS (chip-on-wafer-on-substrate) is the 2.5D advanced-packaging technology that stitches GPU/ASIC logic dies and HBM stacks onto a silicon interposer — it is the physical gate on every leading-edge AI accelerator, and through 2027 it is as binding a constraint as 3nm wafers themselves. The buy-side reason to care: CoWoS (plus its 3D sibling SoIC and the substrate/OSAT layers around it) sits *upstream* of the entire AI compute P&L. TSMC owns the bulk of the capacity, so its expansion cadence rate-limits NVDA/AVGO/AMD shipments; but the more interesting move is that value is now visibly migrating *out* of TSMC toward OSATs (ASE, Amkor), substrate vendors, and — as a wildcard — Intel's EMIB panel-level packaging. A former TSMC sales exec put the punchline bluntly: CoWoS was "a technology of the future" that TSMC nearly killed for a decade because it wasn't profitable, and is now "something everybody's depending on" (TSMC former exec, 2026-04-14).

- **TSMC gets paid twice across a single HBM unit's journey — the structural rationale for why TSMC protects CoWoS even as it outsources volume (SemiAnalysis "Scaling the Memory Wall", 2025-08-11).** The supply-chain flow behind HBM integration: a **base-die designer** (Nvidia, Broadcom, or a hyperscaler) hands the design to **TSMC, which fabricates the base-die wafer (N12/N3)**; that wafer is then **consigned to the HBM manufacturer** (SK Hynix/Samsung/Micron) for TSV formation, DRAM-die stacking, and packaging; the finished HBM stack ships back to the accelerator designer, which **consigns it to TSMC a second time** for CoWoS packaging alongside the logic die (p.41-42). Net: **TSMC is paid twice across one HBM unit's journey** — once for the base-die wafer, once for the interposer packaging — a structural rent that helps explain why TSMC has historically been reluctant to fully cede CoWoS share to OSATs even while routing overflow volume to ASE/Amkor for capacity reasons. TSMC is acknowledged as the **advanced-packaging leader for HBM integration**, but memory makers retain real leverage via **TSV-formation and DRAM-stacking IP** that TSMC does not itself possess (p.43) — the packaging-side counterpart to the "TSMC fabricates the base die because memory makers mostly lack leading-edge logic capability" framing on [hbm-memory.md](hbm-memory.md).
- **Why hybrid-bonding adoption in HBM should be expected to run slow — the TSMC advanced-logic precedent (SemiAnalysis "Scaling the Memory Wall", 2025-08-11).** SemiAnalysis argues the **routing-density case for CoWoS itself is unambiguous**: HBM3E-class integration requires routing **>1,000 wires between the XPU and HBM**, a density that exceeds what conventional PCB or substrate technology can support, which is why interposer-based CoWoS became mandatory rather than optional (p.3). On the timeline for the *next* packaging transition (hybrid bonding), the report reasons by analogy to **TSMC's own adoption curve for hybrid bonding in advanced logic** — which took years to move from pilot to volume — to argue that **HBM's hybrid-bonding adoption will likewise be slow** (p.13). This is a useful counter-anchor to the more bullish 2026-06-23 HB-timeline reads elsewhere on this page (Redburn's Conor/Sherman checks have Besi already the sole TSMC-qualified bonder with SOIC lines expanding aggressively into 2027-28): the SemiAnalysis precedent-based argument implies the *memory-side* hybrid-bonding leg (as opposed to the logic/SoIC leg) could lag the more optimistic capacity numbers, consistent with the W2W/optical-DRAM-as-alternative-path hedge Redburn separately flags on [hbm-memory.md](hbm-memory.md).

## Key debates
- **Bull / where the money is:** Shortage is "the name of the game" and persists into 2027+ across 3nm wafers, memory and advanced packaging (Gokul, Computex, 2026-06-11). The cleanest expressions are the *picks-and-shovels* layers that capture spillover regardless of who wins the GPU war — OSATs (ASE/KSE), bonder/inspection equipment (BESI for hybrid bonding, plus AMAT/KLAC on advanced-packaging tools), and substrate (Unimicron, Japanese vendors Ibiden/Shinko). KSE and the OSATs were "quite constructive" on raising capex; clean-room construction itself is now a constraint (Gokul, 2026-06-11).
- **Bear / what could break it:** Value migration away from TSMC is the structural risk to the capacity owner — CoWoS-R/-S increasingly farmed to ASE/Amkor, and Intel EMIB as a genuine second source. Demand-double-counting also lurks: customers' forecasts historically sum to multiples of the real end market, which TSMC itself discounts (former exec, 2026-04-14). And specific programs slip — Rubin cut to ~2.0–2.2M units this year on HBM4 issues (from 2.8–2.9M); AMD MI450 CoWoS-L pulled down for 2026 on the 2nm ramp delay before a strong 2027 (Gokul, 2026-04-13 / 2026-05-27).
- **Intel packaging as trojan horse.** EMIB (and EMIB-T for the rumored 4-die Rubin Ultra SKU) is the credible non-TSMC route — SK Hynix is doing joint 2.5D/EMIB R&D with Intel amid the CoWoS crunch (NVDA briefing jukan05/dnystedt, 2026-05-11/12). The flagship test case is **Google TPU V9 "Puma Fish" (MediaTek)**: plan A is Intel "EMEM"/EMIB with ~25–28 silicon bridges (yield unproven at that complexity), plan B is TSMC CoWoS-L; Google has pre-paid Intel to reserve 2028 substrate capacity, decision expected late-26/early-27 (Gokul, 2026-05-27). One bull case sizes Feynman I/O-die packaging at ~$4.8B with "Intel w/ EMIB the only game in town" if 4-die wins (NVDA briefing bubbleboi). Counter-view: Jeff Pu argues 3DIC, not CoPoS/4-die, breaks the area limit (NVDA briefing, 2026-05-16); JPM frames the Intel piece as "mostly packaging, not TSMC replacement" (Samik Chatterjee, 2026-06-09).
- **CoPoS pushed out.** Panel-level CoPoS now looks like 2029–2030, not 2028 — TSMC keeps extending CoWoS (14X reticle in 2028; AMD MI500 already targeting ~14–15X). NVDA Feynman on CoPoS is "touch and go" (Gokul, 2026-05-27). ~60–70% of CoPoS equipment is shareable with CoWoS; the delta is glass/TGV/panel tooling.
- **The three-way CoWoS-vs-EMIB-T-vs-Glass-Core-Substrate debate gets a fourth angle — Corning's optical route (2026-06-29/30 updates).** The debate is no longer just CoWoS-vs-EMIB-T: Morgan Stanley (Amir Amerian sales, with Derrick Yang/Charlie Chan/Meta Marshall) reports TSMC discussed **Glass Core Substrate** technology on June 11 in Japan, naming **Ibiden and Innolux as development partners** — but Derrick Yang stresses this signals proof-of-concept completion only, not a mass-production commitment. Adoption timing is seen as more likely **2H28** (vs the market's assumed "CoPoS" 2029 timeline). The technology would replace organic CCL substrate with glass + through-glass-vias (TGV), aimed at closing TSMC's performance gap vs Intel's EMIB-T for very large chips — positive for glass supplier **GLW** and panel makers **Innolux/BOE**, negative for CCL/T-glass suppliers (**Nittobo**). Notably, **BOE** (OW-rated by MS) is pursuing a different strategy — building the entire Glass Core + Substrate stack itself, targeting GPU/ASIC makers beyond the China ecosystem, explicitly to pressure TSMC into adopting BOE as a supplier alongside Ibiden/Innolux (MS/Amir Amerian, 2026-06-30). Separately, **Susquehanna (Mehdi Hosseini, 2026-06-29)** flags Corning's new **Glass Bridge optical connector** (30μm+ core pitch, <2dB coupling loss) and "GlassWorks AI" end-to-end optical platform as positioning **GLW as an alternative supplier to TSMC's CoWoS-L (COUPE) platform** for CPO commercialization — i.e., a non-TSMC-dependent path for AI-era advanced packaging/optical integration, adding a fourth competitive vector (optical/CPO-native packaging) alongside CoWoS, EMIB-T, and Glass Core Substrate. See the "Customer-side rebuttal" item in State of play (AlphaSense/former AMD director, 2026-06-30) for the demand-side pushback on the whole displacement thesis.
