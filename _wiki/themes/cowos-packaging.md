# Theme — CoWoS / Advanced Packaging

_Wiki · generated 2026-06-19 · cross-company theme · sources: equity calls (`../_equity_calls/Semis`), briefing roll-ups (`../_briefings/by-ticker`), earnings transcripts, company pages. Company pages: [../00_INDEX.md](../00_INDEX.md)._

## What it is / why it matters
CoWoS (chip-on-wafer-on-substrate) is the 2.5D advanced-packaging technology that stitches GPU/ASIC logic dies and HBM stacks onto a silicon interposer — it is the physical gate on every leading-edge AI accelerator, and through 2027 it is as binding a constraint as 3nm wafers themselves. The buy-side reason to care: CoWoS (plus its 3D sibling SoIC and the substrate/OSAT layers around it) sits *upstream* of the entire AI compute P&L. TSMC owns the bulk of the capacity, so its expansion cadence rate-limits NVDA/AVGO/AMD shipments; but the more interesting move is that value is now visibly migrating *out* of TSMC toward OSATs (ASE, Amkor), substrate vendors, and — as a wildcard — Intel's EMIB panel-level packaging. A former TSMC sales exec put the punchline bluntly: CoWoS was "a technology of the future" that TSMC nearly killed for a decade because it wasn't profitable, and is now "something everybody's depending on" (TSMC former exec, 2026-04-14).

## State of play (latest)
- **Capacity trajectory raised repeatedly through 1H26.** JPM (Gokul) now models total CoWoS capacity at ~**115K / 175K / 220K wpm by end-26 / 27 / 28E**, with total CoWoS *consumption* +86% this year and +30% next (Gokul/JPM CoWoS update, 2026-05-27). Jefferies (Rankie Wong) independently moved its forecast to **180K / 220K wpm by end-27E / 28E** (NVDA briefing, 2026-06-05). Earlier waypoints: Gokul had 145K/175K end-26/27 in mid-May, and 115K/155K/175K on 2026-04-13 — the numbers have only inched up.
- **The non-TSMC share is the real story.** The portion of CoWoS *not* done at TSMC is being raised hard — to roughly **35K wpm (~25–30% of supply) by end-27, up from only ~3–5% / 10–12K today**, mostly ASE + Amkor, a ~4x jump in OSAT full-stack process in 2027 (Gokul, 2026-05-27). ASE skews to AMD (Venice CPU) + some NVDA; Amkor to NVDA (CoWoS-R for Vera) + some Broadcom.
- **SoIC is the fastest-ramping leg.** 3D hybrid-bonding capacity goes from ~10–14K today to **~40K end-27 → ~65K wpm end-28E** (Gokul, 2026-05-27). Driver is migration of high-end AI accelerators to SoIC — NVDA Feynman, AMD MI, Google TPU V9, Trainium 4 — plus a slice for silicon-photonics/CPO integration. TSMC's new AP7 fab phases are being dedicated to SoIC while CoWoS is increasingly outsourced.
- **Substrate is the next bottleneck.** Substrate (ABF/CCL) supply can grow only ~15%/yr against >15% demand; spot ABF +30%, contract +5–10%, with first LTAs locking ~40–50% price hikes off end-25 base (Gokul/JPM Computex team — Jerry, 2026-06-11; NVDA briefing dnystedt, also flags Ajinomoto pondering 30% hike). Because EMIB-style packaging has no interposer, value that TSMC used to capture accrues directly to substrate vendors (Gokul, 2026-05-27).
- **TSMC is building packaging in Arizona.** The US buildout now contemplates ~5 CoWoS assembly/test factories alongside the wafer fabs, plus the first TSMC R&D fab outside Taiwan (TSMC former exec, 2026-04-14; [TSM page](../TSM.md)).
- **Fubon nudges CoWoS up again + sizes SoIC/CoPoS.** Fubon raises end-27 CoWoS capacity to **180kwpm (from 170k)**; +40kwpm in 2028 → **220kwpm end-28**, plus **20-30kwpm CoPoS** by end-28 (R&D line at VisEra, process complete 2H27-1H28, MP targeted 2H28; "top-priority project" for Feynman/Coupe 2.0). SoIC capacity to **45/65/90kwpm by end-FY26/27/28** — driven by silicon photonics, Broadcom TPU V9/V10 (V9 = two 3D-stacking chips, 2x N2P + 2x N3P dies; V10 = four 3D chips, all-N2, 400G SerDes), plus AWS and NVDA entering SoIC by 2028 (Fubon "Semi 2027 outlook", 2026-06-05).
- **OSAT capacity inflecting hard (corroborates value-migration).** Fubon revises ASE FoCoS up to **45kwpm end-27 (from 30k)** to serve NVDA Vera CPU (via SPIL), AMD Venice CPU, and Broadcom switch chipsets/transceivers; ASE LEAP revenue $4.2bn (FY26) → $8.3bn (FY27), >40% of ATM revenue by FY27 (Fubon, 2026-06-05). Redburn flags OSAT FY26 capex expectations up **+122% YTD**, with ASE utilization recovered to 80% (1Q26) and tightness emerging in leading-edge 2.5D (Redburn, 2026-06-15).
- **Hybrid bonding (SoIC) — Redburn: Logic > Memory.** Redburn raises BESI FY26-30 revenue 9-20% on two new HB logic legs: (1) China — Huawei "LogicFolding"/Tau-scaling logic-on-logic stacking (EUV-restricted path to density), a **cumulative 73-bonder** opportunity to 2030; (2) **CPO** — reliability gains tip scale-up networking to CPO by 2028/29, **50 bonders to 2030** for TSMC's COUPE (PIC-EIC SoIC bonding); HB rev 60% CAGR to €1.085bn FY30, PT raised €270→€350 (Redburn "BESI", 2026-06-15). BESI Investor Day 18 June flagged as the catalyst on memory-HB throughput/yield.
- **The memory-HB wildcard (bear nuance).** Redburn keeps a probability-adjusted haircut on BESI's HBM D2W bonder market (cumulative 247 units to 2030, lower share than logic): Korean makers lean on local supply (SEMES, Hanwha Semitech); and two alternatives threaten the D2W TAM — **Wafer-to-Wafer (W2W)** bonding (8x throughput; imec/EVG showed 200nm W2W at sub-40nm overlay — could bond DRAM *pairs* and halve D2W bonds, viable mainly at ≤2-high given yield penalties on ~95% DRAM yields) and **optically-networked commodity DRAM** (3-4x pin speed but ~300ns latency keeps HBM on the critical path — judged a long-dated revolution, not medium-term) (Redburn "BESI", 2026-06-15).
- **CoWoS→CoPoS the back-end inflection (Jefferies Asia, 2026-04-07):** interposer chips/package degrade fast as accelerators scale — **Blackwell ~15-16 chips per CoWoS interposer → Rubin ~12 → Rubin Ultra only ~5-7** — forcing a move to chip-on-panel-on-substrate (a square panel format). Early CoPoS machine orders already showing at **C Sun**. TSMC plans to localize the CoWoS/packaging supply chain from ~10% to ~30% share (read-through caution on Shibaura Mechatronics). Jefferies flags Shibaura's 6500W (2x throughput at +50% price) means TSMC needs *fewer* Shibaura tools (Jefferies "Asia tech", 2026-04-07).

Second SoIC bonder vendor likely **Shibaura then ASMPT**; CoWoS-S (TPU/Broadcom) capacity not being expanded — TSMC pushing those customers to OSATs (Amkor, ASE) (JPM "TSMC preview/Gokul", 2026-04-02).

## Key debates
- **Bull / where the money is:** Shortage is "the name of the game" and persists into 2027+ across 3nm wafers, memory and advanced packaging (Gokul, Computex, 2026-06-11). The cleanest expressions are the *picks-and-shovels* layers that capture spillover regardless of who wins the GPU war — OSATs (ASE/KSE), bonder/inspection equipment (BESI for hybrid bonding, plus AMAT/KLAC on advanced-packaging tools), and substrate (Unimicron, Japanese vendors Ibiden/Shinko). KSE and the OSATs were "quite constructive" on raising capex; clean-room construction itself is now a constraint (Gokul, 2026-06-11).
- **Bear / what could break it:** Value migration away from TSMC is the structural risk to the capacity owner — CoWoS-R/-S increasingly farmed to ASE/Amkor, and Intel EMIB as a genuine second source. Demand-double-counting also lurks: customers' forecasts historically sum to multiples of the real end market, which TSMC itself discounts (former exec, 2026-04-14). And specific programs slip — Rubin cut to ~2.0–2.2M units this year on HBM4 issues (from 2.8–2.9M); AMD MI450 CoWoS-L pulled down for 2026 on the 2nm ramp delay before a strong 2027 (Gokul, 2026-04-13 / 2026-05-27).
- **Intel packaging as trojan horse.** EMIB (and EMIB-T for the rumored 4-die Rubin Ultra SKU) is the credible non-TSMC route — SK Hynix is doing joint 2.5D/EMIB R&D with Intel amid the CoWoS crunch (NVDA briefing jukan05/dnystedt, 2026-05-11/12). The flagship test case is **Google TPU V9 "Puma Fish" (MediaTek)**: plan A is Intel "EMEM"/EMIB with ~25–28 silicon bridges (yield unproven at that complexity), plan B is TSMC CoWoS-L; Google has pre-paid Intel to reserve 2028 substrate capacity, decision expected late-26/early-27 (Gokul, 2026-05-27). One bull case sizes Feynman I/O-die packaging at ~$4.8B with "Intel w/ EMIB the only game in town" if 4-die wins (NVDA briefing bubbleboi). Counter-view: Jeff Pu argues 3DIC, not CoPoS/4-die, breaks the area limit (NVDA briefing, 2026-05-16); JPM frames the Intel piece as "mostly packaging, not TSMC replacement" (Samik Chatterjee, 2026-06-09).
- **CoPoS pushed out.** Panel-level CoPoS now looks like 2029–2030, not 2028 — TSMC keeps extending CoWoS (14X reticle in 2028; AMD MI500 already targeting ~14–15X). NVDA Feynman on CoPoS is "touch and go" (Gokul, 2026-05-27). ~60–70% of CoPoS equipment is shareable with CoWoS; the delta is glass/TGV/panel tooling.
