# Theme — China AI Buildout & Export Controls

_Wiki · generated 2026-06-25 · cross-company theme · sources: equity calls, briefing roll-ups, earnings transcripts, research library, Twitter/X corpus (ported from research-wiki). Company pages: [../00_INDEX.md](../00_INDEX.md) · themes index: [00_THEMES.md](00_THEMES.md)._

## What it is / why it matters

The dual dynamic shaping semicap and foundry exposure to China: **export controls limiting what US/Dutch vendors can sell** while **China accelerates a domestic AI hardware stack** (Huawei Ascend + SMIC) that is structurally chip-count-inflationary, supporting domestic foundry/WFE demand even as US equipment names lose China revenue. China was 30–35% of WFE revenue for US semicap names (AMAT >30%, LRCX higher) pre-restrictions. Export controls cap that mix but simultaneously drive a China domestic buildout (Huawei Ascend roadmap, SMIC 7nm ramp, Hua Hong, NAURA/AMEC) that is **chip-count-inflationary** — Huawei's cluster architecture uses ~114× more chips vs Nvidia to achieve comparable aggregate compute. Net impact on global WFE and foundry capex is ambiguous; it depends on whether domestic China capex offsets the US-vendor revenue loss.

Cluster comparison vs Nvidia: Atlas 950 vs NVL144 = **113.8× NPU count, 6.8× cluster FP8, 63.0× interconnect bandwidth, 15.4× memory capacity** (0.06× per-chip perf); Atlas 960 vs NVL576 = 107.6× NPUs, 6.5× cluster FP8, 22.7× interconnect, 12.2× memory (Bernstein, Huawei Roadmap, 2025-09-22).

- **MATCH Act — the key export-control bill to watch (Gavekal, Assessing/Chips Insight, Jun 12 2026):** the Multilateral Alignment of Technology Controls on Hardware Act (introduced Apr 2 2026, bipartisan, 27+ co-sponsors) would (i) expand SME controls to **components and after-sales servicing** and cover **all immersion DUVs** "regardless of overlay," (ii) give allies (Netherlands, Japan) 150 days to align or face extraterritorial controls, (iii) impose Entity-List-style comprehensive controls on a dozen+ Chinese champions (**YMTC, CXMT, SMIC, Hua Hong** + affiliates). The most damaging piece is expanded curbs on **ASML immersion DUV** — ASML holds **>90% of the global immersion DUV market** (Nikon shipped 11 in 2024 vs ASML's **131 in 2025**), and without DUV access China cannot expand advanced capacity beyond stockpiles. Passage as standalone is unlikely (compressed election calendar); more plausible path is folding language into the year-end NDAA. **Micron is the primary lobbying force**; **Lam, KLA, Applied Materials oppose** (AMAT was fined **$252mn** by Commerce for 2022 China shipments; both AMAT/LRCX have billions in China exports stuck in a BIS license backlog). China SME self-sufficiency for mature-node mass production is only **~15–20%**.
- **China's retaliation toolkit — rare earths & critical minerals (Gavekal, Jun 12 / Jun 29 / May 18 2026):** if MATCH passes in any form Beijing retaliation is "almost guaranteed" via critical-minerals controls. Specific chokepoints: **yttrium** (heavy rare earth; yttrium-oxide is a protective ceramic coating in SMEs — Beijing held up shipments to US SME firms, releasing only a small batch in Mar 2026); **indium phosphide (InP)** substrates for optical transceivers (China = **70% of refined indium**; ~70% supply gap in 2025–26; controls hit **AXT**, #2 InP producer and a **Coherent** supplier, which makes all InP via its China subsidiary). On Jun 29 Beijing added **MP Materials and USA Rare Earth** to its export-control list (retaliating for the Pentagon adding 65 Chinese firms to the 1260H list) — both still depend on Chinese magnet-making equipment/know-how, so the designation could crimp Washington's rare-earths reshoring.
- **Corporate America's China dependence (Gavekal, Beijing Shopping List, May 14 2026):** the 16–17 CEOs on the Trump-Xi summit trip were seeking market access, supply-chain stability, or relief from Chinese penalties. China revenue as % of total: **Qualcomm 46%** (FY25), **Tesla 19%** (1Q26), **Apple 18%** (Greater China 1Q26), **Coherent 11%**, **Meta 11%**, **Nvidia 9%** (FY26), **Micron 7%** (mainland FY25), **Illumina 5.6%**. Beijing kept **blocking H200 imports** (approved by Trump Dec 2025) to protect Huawei Ascend while still admitting Blackwell; a B300 8-GPU server hit **~RMB7mn (~$1.03mn)** in China, up from ~RMB4mn late-2025 and ~2× the US price, on acute domestic compute shortage.
- **Export-control enforcement loopholes (Gavekal, Jun 4 2026):** BIS May 31 2026 guidance clarified that shipping controlled AI chips to **overseas subsidiaries** of Chinese firms also needs a license (closing a loophole created by the May-2025 rescission of the AI Diffusion Rule). Two loopholes persist: Chinese firms renting **overseas cloud** (increasingly from US-based providers), and some Chinese chip designers still fabbing restricted parts at **TSMC/Samsung** via intermediaries. China also elevated its **outbound-investment regime** to a State Council-level regulation (effective Jul 1 2026) tying tech-export/dual-use/data controls to ODI and adding extraterritorial countermeasures (>$3trn accumulated ODI stock).
- **Huawei "Tau (τ) Scaling Law" & SMIC process reality (Gavekal, May 28 2026):** at a May 25 Shanghai keynote Huawei's He Tingbo proposed **τ-scaling** (time/system-level optimization over geometric shrink) plus **LogicFolding** (stacked active logic tiers via ultra-fine hybrid bonding) — Gavekal frames it as a rebrand of existing "More-than-Moore"/STCO ideas, with LogicFolding "heavily built on Cadence" IP and China still reliant on foreign 3D-IC EDA (Synopsys/Cadence/Siemens = **>70%** of China's EDA market). SMIC's most advanced "5nm" **N+3 achieves density closer to TSMC 6nm** via DUV multi-patterning at high cost/low yield; commercial EUV is "at least 10 years" away. YMTC's Xtacking hybrid-bonding cited as the precedent for a successful Chinese leapfrog.

## Key debates

- **Bull / China-neutral / offset:** domestic AI buildout (Huawei Ascend + SMIC ramp) inflates WFE demand globally; local semicap vendors (NAURA, AMEC, Piotech) gain share; foundry (SMIC, Hua Hong) expand. Net WFE not much lower; US names shift product mix to leading-edge (research-wiki synthesis).
- **Bear / export-control:** US vendors permanently lose the 25–35% revenue bucket; China domestic tools are 1–2 generations behind; SMIC/Hua Hong can't produce HBM or leading-edge logic below 7nm at scale; the domestic-substitute timeline is long (research-wiki synthesis).
- **Timeline / inflection points:** Huawei packaging roadmap to 1µm pitch by 2027 (SA, May 2026); local AI production capacity tripling in 2026 (Bernstein, 2025-09-22); tariff damage delayed to late CY25/CY26 by long delivery times (Bernstein, 2025-05-05); DeepSeek sovereign-compute buildout now underway (SA, 2026-06-10).
