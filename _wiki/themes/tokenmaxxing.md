## What it is / why it matters
"Tokenmaxxing" was the 2025 reflex of enterprises (and their employees) maxing out token consumption to look AI-forward; the 2026 sequel is the pullback — firms now demanding **control** over token spend, routing cheaper tasks to cheaper models, and testing open-source/DeepSeek substitutes. The buy-side question this resolves: **does economic value keep accruing to frontier models (Anthropic/OpenAI pricing power, and the GPU capex that underwrites it), or does model substitution and cost discipline cap the monetization curve?** The on-disk evidence (Jun 2026) is two-sided: spend is still climbing fast and the frontier still captures ~90% of revenue, but a cost-control backlash is real on the margin and is starting to move enterprise behavior, vendor pricing (MSFT Copilot → usage), and the read-through to hyperscaler demand.

Read-through to monetization: the model-economics fight (frontier vs open-source, inference vs training) maps directly onto who captures token revenue (Bernstein "Cloud in the Quarter", 2026-06-22).
