## What it is / why it matters
Hyperscaler capex (MSFT, AMZN, GOOGL, META, ORCL) is the single biggest **demand signal** for the entire AI hardware chain — GPUs/ASICs, memory, optical, power, semicap. Tracking the trajectory (and the gap between **consensus**, our **house model**, and the **street-high**) is how we size the cycle and judge whether the supply names are pricing too much or too little. This page is the synthesis layer over the AI_Demand_Capex dashboard.

## Key debates
- **Bull / where the money is:** demand visibility keeps pulling guides up (GOOG 2026 capex guided $75B→$175-185B→$180-190B over four prints; MSFT/AMZN/META all raising). Backlog (GOOG Cloud $462B, ~2x q/q, Q1 2026) underwrites the spend (Google official model, Transcript Dashboard).
- **Bear / what could break it:** ROIC/FCF — GOOG FCF went negative in Q1 2026 ($-26.6B) as capex/rev hit ~32% (Google official model, Cash Flow tab). The debate is digestion/air-pocket risk if monetization lags the build.
- Watch the **consensus-vs-house-vs-street-high spread** as the positioning signal per name.
- **NEW (2026-07-01) — buyer-only vs buyer-and-seller hyperscaler model:** Meta launched "Meta Compute" (established internally Jan-2026, publicly surfaced 2026-07-01) — an external cloud business to **sell** surplus AI computing capacity, with a roadmap to build 10s of GWs by 2030 and hundreds of GWs long-term (Citi · Ronald Josey, 2026-07-01). This is a genuine structural shift: Meta was previously purely a compute **buyer/renter** (from CoreWeave, Nebius, Oracle, Google Cloud, AWS); it now becomes a potential **competitor** to the neoclouds it has been a customer of (Barclays · Ross Sandler, 2026-07-01, flagging Meta's $20bn+ TCV deals with CoreWeave/Nebius plus multi-year Oracle/Google Cloud/AWS deals as the at-risk backlog). CoreWeave has ~1/3 of its backlog tied to Meta — headline risk to neoclouds if Meta stops signing new deals (Citi Tech Research call — Placeres/Josey/Radke/Rollins/Terry, 2026-07-01). Counter-evidence the "excess capacity" framing may be overstated: Google reportedly capped Meta's use of Gemini AI/Google Cloud due to Google's own infra constraints (UBS · Christina Dwyer, 2026-07-01), and Meta was simultaneously signing new capacity (Crusoe, ~1.6GW, ~2026-06-18) while capacity-constrained at Google Cloud in the same window — read as next-gen GPU compute still in shortage, with only legacy H100/A100 capacity being resold, e.g. xAI's Colossus 1 sold to Anthropic for $1.25bn/month, 220,000+ GPUs (JPMorgan · Wei Zheng Chan desk, 2026-07-01). Market reaction: semis fell 6%, memory fell 11% intraday on the capex-demand debate reignited by the headline, while Mag 7 was +1.6% and META itself +8.8% same day — JPM's house view leans toward positioning/de-grossing rather than a fundamental narrative shift (JPMorgan · Ellen Wang, Market Intelligence, 2026-07-01).
