# Theme — HBM / Memory Super-Cycle

_Wiki · generated 2026-06-19 · cross-company theme · sources: equity calls (`../_equity_calls/Semis`), briefing roll-ups (`../_briefings/by-ticker`), earnings transcripts, company pages. Company pages: [../00_INDEX.md](../00_INDEX.md) · themes index: [00_THEMES.md](00_THEMES.md)._

## What it is / why it matters
Memory has gone from the cyclical victim of the semi complex to its tightest, most profitable corner. HBM — the stacked DRAM that feeds AI accelerators — is **sold out for all of CY26 on price and volume, including HBM4** ([MU page](../MU.md); Mehrotra, Micron Q1 FY26, 2025-12-17), and that scarcity has spread back into conventional DRAM and NAND because every HBM die displaces ~3x the wafer of a commodity DDR5 die. The result is a pricing melt-up that has run the memory names parabolic (MU +30% in a week, $748→$971; Susquehanna 2026-05-30 via [MU briefing](../_briefings/by-ticker/MU.md)) and re-rated the equity story from "trough multiple on cyclical earnings" toward an earnings-durability framework underwritten by multi-year contracts. The buy-side question this theme resolves: **is this a structurally longer up-cycle (LTAs + supply discipline + AI memory-intensity), or the textbook commodity top that always follows astronomical ASPs?** The on-disk evidence (May–Jun 2026) is two-sided and the debate is no longer direction but duration.

## State of play (latest)
- **HBM sold out CY26; pricing into 2027-28.** Micron's entire CY26 HBM supply, HBM4 included, is committed on price and volume; HBM TAM ~$35B (2025) → ~$100B by 2028 (~40% CAGR), pulled in two years vs prior ([MU page](../MU.md), Q1 FY26, 2025-12-17). HBM blended ASP +~30% for next year (Samsung/Hynix) = ~10% like-for-like + mix from HBM4, but HBM4 ramp pushed to 2H so more of the blended ASP effect spills into 2027; HBM price negotiations only begin July after the June quarter (JPM/Jay, [memory-China call](../_equity_calls/Semis/2026-05-28_JPM_memory-China.md), 2026-05-28).
- **DRAM/NAND pricing still ripping.** 2Q26 DRAM ASP +50-60% q/q, NAND +75-100% q/q; 3Q26 DRAM +~20% q/q (memory ests, briefing 2026-05-29, [MU](../_briefings/by-ticker/MU.md)). Micron Q2 FY26 already printed DRAM pricing "mid-sixties %" q/q and NAND "high-seventies %", 75% gross margin, with guidance to ~81% GM next quarter ([MU page](../MU.md), 2026-03-18). JPM models full-year server-grade DRAM +~25%, with 3Q DRAM/NAND +~60% q/q and upside to 20% if LTAs aren't signed in 2Q (JPM/Jay, 2026-05-28).
- **TrendForce revises its (previously most-conservative) DRAM forecast *up* (TrendForce, 2026-06-30).** 3Q26 PC DRAM contract growth lifted to **+15-20% QoQ** (from the +8-13% Avril flagged 2026-06-23), 4Q26 to **+3-8%** (from +0-5%), and **3Q26 server DRAM +13-18% QoQ** — i.e. even the bearish desk now models pricing above its own late-June base case; TrendForce reiterates DRAM shortage / elevated hardware prices **through 2028** on **cleanroom-limited** wafer capacity as the binding bottleneck (TrendForce via @jukan05 / @trendforce, 2026-06-30). _(Supersedes the 06-23 TrendForce +8-13% Q3 / +3-8% Q4 base case cited below under 'DRAM price cadence.')_
- **LTA structure — customers proposed the 2028+ deals first; SK Hynix keeps a floor with no ceiling (KIS via @jukan05, 2026-06-29).** With commodity-DRAM operating margins already ~80% / gross margins >90%, prices can't rise endlessly on supply/demand alone — yet it was **customers, not suppliers, who first proposed 3-5yr LTAs covering 2028+**, knowing new-fab output only contributes from 2028 (a shortage-runs-long signal). Structural detail: **SK Hynix's LTA has a price floor with no ceiling, whereas Samsung/Micron structures differ** — the first time in memory history suppliers have locked a contract structure that sustains high margins over an extended period (KIS via @jukan05, 2026-06-29).
- **HDD pulled into the same tractor beam.** With NAND tight, Seagate/WDC are running ~90% sequential incremental gross margins and, unlike NAND, managing price up "more on a steady basis" to give predictability — and no longer giving back generation-change discounts in a supply-constrained market (Samik/JPM, [memory-HDD preview](../_equity_calls/Semis/2026-06-12_Samik_memory-HDD-preview.md), 2026-06-12). Memory cost pass-through is now a visible gross-margin headwind across the hardware complex (Calix, Motorola, Flex — same call).
- **Demand fuel beyond HBM: CPU + KV-cache.** NVIDIA Vera standalone CPU ~500k units this year, ~3M in 2027; AI-CPU demand could lift total server DRAM bit demand >15-20%, and Vera/in-house CPUs use higher-content LPDDR5X SO-DIMM/SoCAM, draining low-power DRAM supply (JPM/Jay, 2026-05-28; mechanism corroborated [Arcuri/Micron](../_equity_calls/Semis/2026-05-15_Arcuri-Micron_memory.md), 2026-05-15). Agentic inference grows context ~30x/yr, inflating the KV-cache across the full memory hierarchy — HBM → expansion DRAM (SoCAM/DDR) → a brand-new NAND-flash context tier (245TB SSDs) (Sathya Kumar/Micron via UBS, 2026-05-15). The low-power-DRAM squeeze is now acute: SoCAM/LPDDR5X CPU demand may exceed the smartphone DRAM market next year, and Samsung/Hynix say they can fill their fabs "even if we don't supply any volume to smartphone customers" — leaving CXMT to backfill Chinese smartphone LPDDR (JPM/Jay, 2026-05-28). The DRAM shortage now reads worse than the CPU shortage: **NVIDIA halved the LPDDR5 content in Vera Rubin racks** and servers are shipping "significantly nerfed" on DRAM — a measure of pent-up demand that snaps back (NVIDIA re-doubles memory) once supply loosens (MS/Moore, [MS memory & semicap](../../relat%C3%B3rios%20bons/2026_06_22_ms_on_memory_and_semicap_22_jun_26.html), 2026-06-22).
- **China supply ramp accelerating — the "partial quick fix" (Stratechery, 2026-06-23).** Citing the WSJ memory-crunch overview, Stratechery flags China as the closest thing to a quick fix for the shortage, though at best partial: **CXMT** (China's top DRAM maker) is seeking a **~$4B Shanghai IPO**, is building new fabs, and reported **Q1 2026 revenue +700% y/y** (products still trail the big three); **Yangtze Memory** (NAND) is building three new factories that would **more than double its capacity by end-2027**. Because DRAM (unlike logic) is fungible, OEMs can route Chinese chips to Asia-bound product and big-three chips to the West — **HP and other top PC makers are in talks to use CXMT memory in products bound for Asia**, and Apple's Tim Cook said "everything needs to be on the table" on China policy. Thompson's structural take: by under-building during their oligopoly, the big three (Samsung/SK Hynix/Micron) created the opening for Chinese competition that gets better simply by winning this volume (Stratechery, 2026-06-23).
- **The Asia-trip + TrendForce update: HBM pricing is *too low* and the allocation priority has flipped (Conor + Avril, 2026-06-23).** Two independent 2026-06-23 sources converge on the same structural read: HBM has been *under*-priced vs conventional DRAM, so makers are quietly de-prioritizing it. **General-server DRAM is now allocation #1, HBM #2 — a reversal of the last four years** (Redburn "Conor O'Mara Asia Trip Takeaways", 2026-06-23). The price math: server DDR5 already sits **>$2/Gb** while **HBM3E is only ~$1.3–2/Gb** (the $1.3 a 2025-fixed price, the $2 a recent upside order) and **HBM4 is currently ~$2.05/Gb** — below conventional, despite HBM4 being ~3x the die size of conventional DRAM; TrendForce's analyst ("Avril") argues HBM4 **must reach ~$5/Gb in 2027** (her conservative number — implied "should be six or seven" but discounted for the small buyer base) just to incentivize makers to keep reserving HBM wafer allocation (StoneX/TrendForce "Memory expert call — Avril", 2026-06-23). Conor's supply-chain checks frame it from NVIDIA's angle: **NVDA HBM pricing ~$17–18/GB (2026) → $30–32/GB (2027), +70–80%**, with everyone else (AMD, Broadcom, AWS) priced *higher* since NVIDIA's volume earns the lowest price; HBM4 LTAs not even finalized two weeks ago despite Samsung already shipping, with the expected increase "**90–100% vs last year's LTA**" (Redburn, 2026-06-23; StoneX/TrendForce, 2026-06-23). The 2027 industry HBM supply forecast was **revised down 60B → 50B Gb-equivalent — not on demand weakness but because conventional DRAM is more profitable**, so makers optimize mix away from HBM (there is a TSV/backend "die penalty" on HBM) (Redburn "Conor O'Mara Asia Trip Takeaways", 2026-06-23).
- **HBM supply +~60% in 2026, but 2027 hinges on Rubin Ultra density (Avril, 2026-06-23).** HBM supply growth is **~60% in 2026 with Rubin density *flat*** (moderate demand); **2027 growth is a question mark pending NVIDIA's Rubin Ultra density decision** — Rubin Ultra is "supposed to double density" but NVIDIA may cut it if HBM supply is short. The reserved incremental HBM wafer capacity for next year is only +60K. End-2026 HBM wafer capacity: **Samsung 180K, SK Hynix 200K (dominant), Micron 100K (risk of dropping to 80K if HBM4 qualification issues persist)** — and Samsung's HBM4 progress raises the possibility it **overtakes SK Hynix as the #1 HBM supplier in 2027** (StoneX/TrendForce "Memory expert call — Avril", 2026-06-23). Micron's weak HBM4 is paradoxically a *near-term positive*: it lets Micron reserve more wafer for higher-return conventional DRAM, helping its staggering +42% bit-supply growth (StoneX/TrendForce, 2026-06-23).
- **DRAM tight through 2028; clean-room space is the binding constraint, EUV the 2027 add-on (Avril, 2026-06-23).** TrendForce frames the supply ceiling explicitly: **through end-2028 the biggest limitation is clean-room space, not equipment or demand** — even a greenfield announced today yields no output before **H2 2029**. 2027 layers on a *second* constraint: **EUV/advanced-litho availability, as DRAM makers now compete with TSMC and foundries for tools** (corroborated by Conor's separate EUV-bottleneck read). DRAM exiting-capacity (K wafers/mo): 2025 Samsung 740 / SK Hynix 610 / Micron 365 / Nanya 65 (mostly legacy DDR4) / CXMT 300; by end-2027 Samsung +45 → ~785, SK Hynix reaches **710 (narrowing the gap with Samsung)**, Micron 440. Key fab cadence: Samsung **P5L phase-1 not ready until mid-2028** (P5L phase-2 just announced, earliest output ~2030); SK Hynix **Yongin FAB1 (200–250K) ready end-2027 but chip-out only 2028** on the 2-quarter wafer cycle; Micron Idaho 1&2 (~50–60K each, longer US lead times) + the PSMC fab acquisition (~40K, Micron-equipment transition, output H2-2027) (StoneX/TrendForce "Memory expert call — Avril", 2026-06-23).
- **LTAs — TrendForce vs Conor, two framings of the same instrument (both sources, 2026-06-23).** TrendForce: LTA discussions began Q2 2026 (first wave US CSPs + NVIDIA + Apple), terms typically **3 years (2026–28)** with a **floor price** and in some cases a **ceiling defined as ≤15% QoQ increase from Q3 2026**; **~20–30% of discussions done, expected 60–70% by end-2026**; prepayments/down-payments as a commitment mechanism (forfeited if the customer walks), and LTAs are **primarily allocation guarantees, not price-fixing**. HBM LTAs are moving from annual to **at-least-every-two-quarters renegotiation** because 2026 HBM was negotiated in 2025 at too-low levels (StoneX/TrendForce "Memory expert call — Avril", 2026-06-23). Conor's sharper buy-side framing: **LTAs are call options** — fixed volume + floor price + no cap = a call-option payoff that is worth a lot in the up-cycle and **zero in the down-cycle when hyperscalers break the contract** (as they always have); 5-year volume contracts, **1-year prepaid** per his checks (Edison's expert said 2-year), escrow unclear; max operating cash hyperscalers will bear for memory is ~30% (currently only mid-teens-to-20%), so no pushback yet — "they don't like the prices but they're in no position to push back" (Redburn "Conor O'Mara Asia Trip Takeaways", 2026-06-23).
- **DRAM price cadence — the bull/bear range across three desks (both sources, 2026-06-23).** Conor's experts: **+20–25% QoQ in both Q3 and Q4** (Sherman/Redburn says +30%/+40%; Edison heard +40–50%) vs buy-side expectations of only ~20% Q3 / 5–10% Q4 — "whichever one is right, memory prices keep going up" for at least six months; the first downturn signal would be **large DRAM capacity additions in 2H27** (Redburn "Conor O'Mara Asia Trip Takeaways", 2026-06-23). TrendForce is the conservative outlier — published **+8–13% Q3 / +3–8% Q4** vs "market chatter" of +30–40% which Avril calls neither likely nor healthy (DRAM already rose +45–50% in Q4'25, +100% in Q1, +60% in Q2) — but her "gut feeling" is **Q3 upside >20%, Q4 >10–15%**; Apple already agreed to a **>20% mobile-DRAM increase in Q3**; **Micron flagged as the price outlier** (capacity-constrained → pushes hardest for highest ASPs; if it succeeds Korean makers follow). The 2027 wildcard is **LPDDR5 becoming the tightest product** as NVIDIA Vera/next-gen CPU and smartphones (Apple) compete for it — potential for QoQ acceleration to resume (StoneX/TrendForce "Memory expert call — Avril", 2026-06-23). Customer pushback is split: large US CSPs/NVIDIA/Apple "are not complaining about price — their concern is allocation"; Asian consumer brands (PC OEMs, smartphone makers) push back but lack pricing leverage (StoneX/TrendForce, 2026-06-23).
- **BofA's "AI 2030" — memory the key constraint, MU PO $500→$950 on a sum-of-parts (BofA, 2026-05-13).** Now 6 weeks old and superseded on the headline PT (June desks at $1,625–1,750), but BofA adds two pieces of durable framing the June notes don't. (1) **The SOTP construction:** MU $950 = **$710/sh from traditional cyclical DRAM/NAND (3.1x CY27E P/B, high-end of MU's 0.8–3.1x historical range)** + **$190/sh from the HBM business (27x CY27E PE, in-line with AI-compute peers)** — the explicit split between commodity-cyclical and HBM-secular value, useful against the bear's "it's all one commodity" framing. (2) **The "structural higher floor" thesis:** memory *capacity and bandwidth* are now the binding inference bottleneck (vs compute historically) because compute scales once per model while **memory bandwidth scales per user, per query, per agent**, and context windows are expanding faster than compression (NVFP4 KV-quant) can offset. BofA sizes **HBM TAM $35B (CY25) → $168B (CY30), +37% CAGR**, HBM content/accelerator **187GB → 464GB (~20% CAGR)**, with HBM staying ~14–20% of accelerator spend; Vera Rubin uses **288GB HBM4/accelerator**. DRAM sufficiency ratio stays >110% (no material oversupply) through CY28; supply elasticity structurally lower as MU's >$25B FY26 capex goes mostly to *cleanroom construction* (meaningful capacity only CY28+) (BofA "AI 2030", 2026-05-13). [BofA "AI 2030" (2026-05-13)](../../relat%C3%B3rios%20bons/Vivek_State_of_the_union.html)
- **NAND/SSD read-through from the cache-hit re-pricing (FundaAI, 2026-06-24).** Light read-through, not the subject: FundaAI argues DeepSeek V4's targeted Cache-Hit price cut (to as low as ~¥0.025/Mtok) is backed by **KV cache moving to SSD at scale** (DSA compresses KV cache to 10% of V3.2; DualPath solves the SSD→GPU read-bandwidth bottleneck), pushing agent-scenario cache-hit rates to 95%+ — "a positive for SSDs, and **NAND demand could benefit materially**" in large-scale agent workloads (Jevons: cheaper per-token storage → retain history longer/wider → total usage expands). On the binding constraint, FundaAI's compute-bottleneck section lists **HBM/DRAM/KV-cache/CXL as what "determine inference throughput and long-context carrying capacity,"** with multi-agent fan-out pushing total KV-cache pressure "against the HBM and memory walls" and lifting the value of CXL memory pools (FundaAI "Deep|LLM 26H1 Update Part 2", 2026-06-24). [FundaAI "Deep|LLM 26H1 Update Part 2" (2026-06-24)](../../relat%C3%B3rios%20bons/LLM_Part_2_funda.html)
- **Enterprise sourcing pain — the demand signal from the buyer side (MS expert, 2026-04-10).** A Morgan Stanley hardware-sourcing expert call (enterprise buyer, not a CSP) framed the shortage from the bottom of the queue: enterprise customers wanting DRAM/NAND are "bottom of the list" behind HBM/AI/CSP allocation. **Server prices ran +100% (up to +300%) vs Q4-2025**; storage finished-good cost +25% to +150%; lead times blew out from 4-6 weeks to 4-42 weeks. A purchase order is "like a deli ticket" — it secures queue position, not price; some OEMs invoke force-majeure to re-price after the PO. Suppliers are "sold out for 26 component-wise" with 2027 "going fast"; the buyer hopes for normalization to "15-20% q/q" increases in H1-2027 but treats 2027 as a "big unknown" — "once prices go up, they don't come down." Confirms the tightness narrative is visible all the way down to the enterprise end-buyer, with cost pass-through not yet rippling up the SaaS stack (MS "memory sourcing expert", 2026-04-10).

## Sizing & sequential growth (the numbers)

Memory capex is the dollar engine of the super-cycle: after a flat 2022-24 (~$55-61B), it re-accelerates to **$101B (2026E) → $123B (2028E)** as DRAM/HBM lead — memory back to **~46% of total semi capex** (vs ~32% at the 2023 bottom). Micron's own capex echoes it: ~$14B FY25 → $20B → **>$25B FY26**.

## Key debates
- **Bull / where the money is — duration, not direction.** The cleanest bull is UBS Arcuri on MU: PT $535→$1,625, "path to >$100 EPS," C27/28/29E $155/$167/$117, LTAs locking ~30% of industry DDR bits and ~$400bn FCF C27-29E ([MU briefing](../_briefings/by-ticker/MU.md), 2026-05-26/31 — flagged "most controversial UBS note recently"). Supporting voices: DB (Weathers, Buy) "DRAM tightness extends to 2028+" (2026-06-17); GS (Schneider) "ongoing tightness through CY27, significant upside to Street" (2026-06-09); Citi 8x C27 EPS from 5x (2026-05-19); Susquehanna $1,750 (2026-05-30). Mechanism: HBM's ~3:1 die displacement + node-driven bit decline + multi-year cleanroom/EUV lead times keep supply short while LTAs/SCAs convert the equity into an earnings-durability story (JPM, 2026-05-28). Suppliers are now optimizing for **durability over sequential momentum** — most will be at ~80% gross margin by year-end and are "securing the durability of earnings rather than seeking additional sequential upside" (JPM/Jay, 2026-05-28). GS (Junie Yoon) makes the cleanest "longevity is underestimated" case: DRAM names at 5-6x earnings disbelieve durability, but a 9x earnings multiple is "conservative" vs semis peers with similar margins/moats at high-teens-to-20x — and the bear-case sensitivity (DRAM ASP **−30% in 2027 then −30%/−50% in 2028 still leaves DRAM GM ~40%**) is the downside protection that justifies an earnings (not P/B) multiple; lower crash-risk than past cycles because hyperscaler demand visibility lets suppliers spend capex efficiently rather than overbuild (GS "memory", 2026-06-08). UBS Arcuri's incremental tell into the Micron print: the Street is fixated on DDR, but **HBM pricing is "what is really surprising to the upside,"** and the **LTAs (not pricing) are the re-rate catalyst** — investor demand is "rabid" (40+ call requests/day, "panicked about missing these moves," 4 death threats after the PT-to-$1,600 note went viral) (UBS "Arcuri call", 2026-06-15).
- **Supply discipline is the load-bearing assumption.** Capex has *not* yet ramped meaningfully because it takes 12-18 months to source tools and find fab space; the industry mis-forecast a mid-26/early-27 *down*-cycle and is only now turning the crank (Micron ex-VP, [procurement call](../_equity_calls/Semis/2026-06-10_Micron-exVP_procurement.md), 2026-06-10). The 2028 governor is EUV: ASML's ~90 tools by end-CY27 are insufficient once Micron/Samsung/Hynix each add ≥100k wafers/month — Samsung/Hynix need ~20-25 tools each, Micron ~10-15, TSMC already ~40 (JPM/Jay, 2026-05-28). DRAM↔HBM mix is fungible overnight (no equipment change), so makers keep starving commodity DRAM to feed HBM — the "die penalty" that sustains the shortage (JPM, 2026-05-28; ex-VP, 2026-06-10). UBS (Arcuri) reframes the EUV governor more benignly — ASML isn't a physical bottleneck until ~**110 EUV slots (~$225B WFE)**, beyond which it must expand floor space, and it's already getting the same 8-10-quarter visibility as US toolmakers, so supply "is not going to be the limitation"; UBS raised 2028 WFE to $250B and floats 2029 into the $300B range, with memory not entering sustained oversupply until **~mid-2028 at the earliest** (UBS "Arcuri call", 2026-06-15). MS semicap (Shane) sizes WFE at **$149B (2026) → $191B (2027, +28%) → mid-teens growth (2028)**, flags 2026 DRAM WFE revisions "even more aggressive" (~+60% to ~$50B), but underscores remarkable **NAND capital discipline** — a NAND-WFE "resurrection" is a 2027 story, not a 2026 pull-in (MS "memory & semicap", 2026-06-22).
- **Bear / what could break it — the commodity snap-back.** The sharpest bear on disk is the **ex-Micron VP of Procurement** (2026-06-10): the market is pricing peak earnings as sustainable — "a highly catastrophic assumption." HBM differentiation is *temporary* (once a 2nd/3rd vendor qualifies at each customer "it already has become a commodity"); Micron's cost structure is worse than Samsung's and ~level with SK Hynix; and "your $900 stock right now will become a $50 stock again," possibly normalizing "within this calendar year or early next" as Micron/SK/Samsung bring disruptive new-fab capacity online ~a year out. He frames the AI buildout as a multi-layer bull-whip ("beer game") removed from end demand that can't amortize ~$6-700B of infra spend. The XP GPU specialist independently calls HBM "comoditizado" — once multiple vendors are NVIDIA-qualified the timing advantage decays to a commodity ([XP/GPU-memory](../_equity_calls/Semis/2026-05-27_XP_GPU-memory.md), 2026-05-27).
- **Anthropic implied to be building an in-house ASIC (Avril, 2026-06-23).** TrendForce reads the Anthropic–Micron deal — **server DRAM + enterprise SSD in 2027 plus HBM in 2028** — as direct evidence that **Anthropic is developing its own in-house ASIC** (you only need HBM if you have your own accelerator); Anthropic is pursuing the **same combined deal with SK Hynix and Samsung** (StoneX/TrendForce "Memory expert call — Avril", 2026-06-23). On the China supply side, both sources play down the near-term threat: **CXMT is sold out, with its +20K end-2026 increase mostly an HBM *attempt*** that won't ramp in volume (technology too old; output only good for low-grade consumer LPDDR5/DDR5), and Conor hears a "good chunk" of CXMT capacity is earmarked for **HBM inside China** for the domestic AI ramp — not a US oversupply threat (Redburn "Conor O'Mara Asia Trip Takeaways", 2026-06-23; StoneX/TrendForce, 2026-06-23). NAND: global wafer capacity *declines 3% in 2026* as Samsung/SK Hynix convert NAND to the more-lucrative DRAM (+6% 2027, +11% 2028); **YMTC is the more concerning Chinese supplier** (uses domestic tools to expand, strong in consumer-grade QLC) but its **QLC is only ~13% of output**, capping it to the consumer segment vs enterprise-SSD-critical Kioxia/Solidigm (StoneX/TrendForce, 2026-06-23).
- **Second-source qualification & China.** Customers force-qualify Chinese DRAM (CXMT) on cheap LPDDR Micron/Samsung/Hynix won't supply at current ASPs — "once they're in, they're in," a lasting share leak at the low end (ex-VP, 2026-06-10). CXMT filed its IPO at ~50% net margin, ASP +~80-85% q/q, but can't hit server or Apple grade; bottleneck is cleanroom + equipment, not policy — and US discussion of banning all equipment sales to China could cap it (JPM/Jay, 2026-05-28). HBM4E adds a wildcard: customer-supplied custom logic base die would *de*-commoditize HBM — but the ex-VP doubts it scales on warranty/yield/liability grounds (2026-06-10). Redburn's bus tour corroborates the *de*-commoditization vector: HBM is moving to **increasing base-die customization** to deliver a system-level (not commodity-chip) solution — "new agreements bind the financial and technological fortunes of the AI supply chain more closely together," suppressing double-ordering and supporting through-cycle margins (Redburn, 2026-06-17).
- **DRAM > NAND on the supply-risk axis (MS/Bernstein).** The buy-side preference has swung toward NAND/SanDisk, but MS argues that's backwards on a 2-3yr view: fundamentals are **"10 out of 10 for DRAM, ~8/10 for NAND,"** and some NAND **2028 LTA lock-in prices are *down* vs 2027** (materially so for enterprise TLC) — softened by built-in escalators — whereas DRAM long-term negotiations "go very well"; MS prefers Micron/DRAM and reads DRAM as the primary AI bottleneck (MS "memory & semicap", 2026-06-22). The NAND↔HDD substitution math (Bernstein expert, ex-WDC NAND VP): the TCO crossover where NAND takes HDD share sits at **~1.5-3x $/GB** (vs the prior ~5x); the gap blew out to ~15-25x in the run-up (vs ~4-5x in Aug-2025), so the swing back toward HDD is "somewhat inevitable" as builders re-tier away from the AI "gold rush" all-flash sloppiness. NAND's peakiness is structural: AI needs the **latest NAND node** (only ~30-35% of capacity), and the under-invested NAND base can't lift that fast — but the new LTAs are only ~25% financially guaranteed (e.g. SanDisk's $11B on $42B), so a −50% market price translates to ~−25% effective ASP, and "tearing up" means renegotiating for more years, not walking (Bernstein "HDD/NAND", 2026-05-04).
- **New structural risk to the HBM *market size* (Redburn): W2W bonding + optically-networked DRAM.** Redburn's BESI work surfaces two technologies that could shrink the HBM TAM itself, not just commoditize it. **Wafer-to-Wafer (W2W) hybrid bonding** (imec/EVG showed 200nm W2W at sub-40nm overlay) could bond DRAM *pairs* before dicing/KGD-sort, halving the per-stack bond count — though yield penalties on ~95% leading-edge DRAM yields limit it to ~2-high. More radically, **optically-networked commodity DRAM** (a large LPDDR pool reached via optics, 3-4x HBM pin speed) could "reduce the size of the HBM market altogether" — but ~300ns light↔electrical conversion latency keeps HBM on the critical path, so Redburn judges it a long-dated revolution, with HBM staying for low-latency delivery and optical DRAM as a prefetch tier (Redburn "BESI", 2026-06-15). Net: another reason the bull case rests on HBM *durability* assumptions.
- **CPU/server-DRAM demand leg quantified (UBS).** UBS's server-CPU TAM work underwrites the DRAM bit-demand uplift: server-CPU units 23M (2025) → **63-70M by 2030 (30% CAGR)** on agentic AI, with AI-CPU (head-node + standalone) climbing 3M → 33M units; standalone CPUs outside the rack go 0 → 13M — each pulling LPDDR5X SO-DIMM/SoCAM content. AI-CPU ASPs rise on core count ($2,420 → $3,769) (UBS "CPU TAM", 2026-05-21). Consistent with JPM's "AI-CPU could lift server DRAM bit demand >15-20%."
- **Timeline / inflection.** Near-term catalyst is Micron's F3Q26 print (June 24): Street rev ~$34.7B vs guide $33.5B, FY27/28 EPS pegged ~$111/$116 ([MU briefing](../_briefings/by-ticker/MU.md), 2026-06-12). The structural inflection both camps point to is **~mid-2027 to 2028**, when new fabs land — the bull says EUV scarcity caps that supply; the bear says it breaks the price. UBS converges on this: sustained memory overcapacity "not until the middle of 2028 at the earliest," so WFE wouldn't roll until ~2029 (UBS "Arcuri call", 2026-06-15). The cleanest single read across the complex now (MS): DRAM fundamentals 10/10, NAND 8/10, with the buy-side so crowded ("every hand in the room owns memory") that dips get bought — and Apple's Tim Cook calling memory pricing a "100-year flood" (WSJ) is the demand-side exclamation point (MS "memory & semicap", 2026-06-22).
