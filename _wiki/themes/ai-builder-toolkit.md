## What it is / why it matters
The software bear case focuses on fewer human seats; the second-order bull case is that agents create more code, tests, dependencies, state, queries and design variants. The investable distinction is the **monetization transmission**: prefer systems where machine activity is already metered or makes an existing governance/context layer more valuable, rather than buying application software simply because valuations fell (Citrini Semis, 2026-07-20).

## State of play (latest)
- **GitLab (GTLB) — governance/context tollbooth, but monetization is early.** Orbit maps code, tests, permissions, incidents, dependencies, customer behavior and infrastructure requirements; Orbit Remote can let external agents query that graph through MCP/REST and consume GitLab Credits. GitLab Flex lets customers move committed spend between seats and credit products. The key caveat: Orbit Remote is beta, Orbit Local is free, GitLab’s own Duo Agent queries are zero-rated, and no public per-query multiplier exists (Citrini Semis, 2026-07-20).
- **MongoDB (MDB) — the cleanest automatic usage meter.** Agents repeatedly read/write session state, tool outputs, permissions, application data, intermediate results and prior actions. Atlas monetizes the hot working set—memory, CPU, throughput and availability—so agent activity can transmit directly into consumption revenue. Citrini cites Atlas growth reaccelerating from ~26% to 29%+, crossing a $2B annualized run-rate, with 29% growth in the April quarter (Citrini Semis, 2026-07-20).
- **Elastic (ESTC) — permission-aware enterprise context plane.** Elastic creates its own searchable indices and ranks information by semantic relevance, keywords, metadata, permissions, recency and operational signals. Citrini cites FY26/Q4 growth of 17%/16%, FY27 guidance of ~14%, cRPO/RPO growth of 20%/28%, >one-third of $100k+ customers using Elastic AI capabilities, and ~19% guided operating margin (Citrini Semis, 2026-07-20).
- **Figma (FIG) — more generated output raises the value of design systems.** Agents can create the first draft, but brand rules, consistency, accessibility, localization, compliance, human judgment and iterative testing remain governance problems. Citrini cites ~46% revenue growth and ~139% NRR as evidence that fundamentals strengthened despite the “AI replaces design tools” narrative (Citrini Semis, 2026-07-20).

## Key debates
- **Bull / where the money is:** AI expands machine-generated work faster than it compresses human seats; databases and search indices monetize the increased activity automatically, while repositories and design systems become the policy/context layers that agents must consult (Citrini Semis, 2026-07-20).
- **Bear / what could break it:** GitLab still has to prove a seat-to-consumption transition; new AI apps may default to Postgres ecosystems such as Supabase/Neon/pgvector instead of MongoDB; hyperscalers and labs may internalize agent memory; and application-software estimates can still fall as coding agents spread (Citrini Semis, 2026-07-20).
- **Scorecard:** MDB has the most direct existing meter; ESTC monetizes the indexed copy of enterprise information; GTLB has the largest monetization-model transition; FIG depends on design-governance demand outrunning automated pixel generation (Citrini Semis, 2026-07-20).
