# Theme — Optical / CPO (Co-Packaged Optics & AI Interconnect)

_Wiki · generated 2026-06-19 · cross-company theme · sources: equity calls (`../../../equity_calls_transcripts/Semis`), briefing roll-ups (`../_briefings/by-ticker`), earnings transcripts, company pages. Company pages: [../00_INDEX.md](../00_INDEX.md) · themes index: [00_THEMES.md](00_THEMES.md)._

## What it is / why it matters
AI interconnect is the bandwidth plumbing that decides how big a model you can train and how efficiently you can serve it — and it is in the middle of a copper-to-optics phase change. The mechanism is simple physics: every time you double interconnect bandwidth, the reach a high-fidelity electrical signal can travel halves, so as scale-up fabrics get both faster (800G → 1.6T per port) and physically larger (e.g. Rubin Ultra NVL576 = eight NVL72s in one fabric), copper runs out of room and the signal has to go photonic (Mike/Rothschild-Redburn, 2026-05-19). That pulls optics from a niche outside-the-box product into the heart of the rack, opening an *incremental* TAM that these vendors' products were never inside before — which is why a basket that "used to trade in the teens" now trades 25–30x CY28 (Meta Marshall/MS, 2026-05-07). The buy-side reason to care: the spend is being levered three ways at once — scale-out (between racks), scale-up (inside the rack), and scale-across (between data centers/buildings) — and the binding constraint through 2028 is not demand but **laser/InP supply** plus manufacturing ramp. This is a picks-and-shovels, supply-gated up-cycle, and the cleanest expression is the light source, not the module.

## State of play (latest)
- **Demand is not the debate; supply is.** Across every call the refrain is identical: orders strengthen weekly, the question is capacity. Chinese transceiver leaders said by end-Q1 they were "confident to fulfill the entire 2027 demand" and are struggling only to expand capacity; the upstream chokepoint is the **EML laser / CW laser shortage**, plus DSPs and even passive components (Andy/MS optical panel, 2026-05-07). Most Chinese transceiver names *missed* Q1 on delivery despite linear demand — a supply miss, not a demand miss, with procurement inventory building on balance sheets (Andy/MS, 2026-05-07).
- **800G → 1.6T transition is the volume event, and it's bigger than 400G.** Ciena expects to exit 2026 at or near #1 share in 800G coherent pluggables (was late to 400G, ~30% share); the 800G market is larger than 400G because port counts hold while price-per-plug rises, and 1.6T high-performance modems are already shipping (Ciena is sole vendor with a 1.6T modem vs the market's 1.2T) (Ciena/Brodi, 2026-06-12). Cisco/Acacia is pushing a 1.6T PAM4 receiver — its first organic inside-the-DC receiver — that 650 Group calls a multi-billion-dollar, "millions of units" opportunity on vendor-diversity demand (650 Group, 2026-06-04).
- **InP is the structural chokepoint and it does not clear.** Lumentum guides InP-laser demand growing ~85% CAGR to end of decade while world InP capacity only 2x–4x over three years — so the imbalance persists; LITE + COHR are "completely sold out this year, next year and most of 2028," with supply response gated by multi-year fab conversions (Mike/Rothschild, 2026-05-19). Lumentum confirms take-or-pay LTAs locked into 2028 and "increasingly confident" into 2029; it is auctioning incremental units above committed take-or-pay at healthier pricing (Lumentum/Michael, 2026-05-07).
- **NVDA is forcing the CPO pace; AVGO/Cisco are measured.** NVDA pushes CPO hardest because it needs the manufacturing *volume* in place ahead of the inevitable scale-up transition; Broadcom and Cisco have native switch silicon in-house and don't need to be early, so expect a higher CPO mix at NVDA next year than at AVGO/Cisco even for equivalent products (650 Group, 2026-06-04). Cisco's first CPO is expected in Silicon One on a ~102T/51.2T switch for scale-out (650 Group, 2026-06-04).
- **NVDA–Corning partnership reframed the CPO supply chain.** Corning entered a commercial + technology + equity deal with NVDA (NVDA warrant up to ~$3.2B of stock; a direct multi-billion-dollar prepayment) to 10x US connectivity capacity and +50% fiber, explicitly aimed at "what's next going inside of the box" CPO — underpinning Corning's plan to build a **$10B photonics business by 2030** (Corning IR, 2026-05-12). NVDA struck a parallel laser purchase-agreement with Lumentum; LITE reads Jensen "buying fibers" as a tell that NVDA is preparing to own scale-up cluster wiring (Lumentum/Wupen, 2026-05-07).
- **Scale-across has inflected from a standing start.** 650 Group sizes scale-across going from ~$500M last year to a ~$10B market; Ciena is seeing a second major hyperscaler place scale-across coherent-pluggable orders, where RLS cuts fiber-pair count ~64x and 800G halves the modem count vs 400G (~50k vs ~100k modems for a 22-petabit link) (650 Group, 2026-06-04; Ciena, 2026-06-12).
- **CPO reliability has crossed the threshold that tips it into scale-up — the 2028/29 inflection now has a catalyst.** Redburn (Mike Harrison's "Tripping the Light Fantastic", 1 May 2026) had framed pluggables' serviceability edge as offsetting CPO's power efficiency; the update is that **recent CPO link-failure-rate improvements (Meta data) tip the balance toward wider scale-up adoption by 2028/29**. The enabling step is in packaging: the optical engine's **Photonic IC (PIC) and Electronic IC (EIC) must be hybrid-bonded via direct Cu-to-Cu** to kill parasitics at the boundary and hit **200 Gbit/s per lane** — TSMC's COUPE platform does this in SoIC (Redburn "BESI", 2026-06-15). This is the supply-chain mechanism behind the optics-into-the-rack thesis, and it routes incremental TAM to TSMC SoIC + hybrid-bonding equipment (BESI).
- **Pump lasers are a *second*, distinct InP constraint — and "scale-across" turned it into a growth leg.** Lumentum holds **70–80% of the pump-laser market** (and similar on ITLAs), is "the kingmaker in terms of allocation," and is **behind demand by ~50–60%** — even more acute than EMLs — driving **mid-double-digit % price increases** on pumps and a guided **3–4x output/revenue over 4–6 quarters**; scale-across orders now come from **Nokia, Cisco, Molex and Ciena**, not just Meta/Ciena (Hurlston, JPM LITE mgmt call, 2026-05-07; LITE IR call, 2026-05-11). Pumps share LITE's Santa Fe fab with phase-one CPO, forcing Caswell UK faster into CPO service (JPM LITE mgmt call, 2026-05-07).
- **Optical's supply discipline looks *less* tight than HDD on capacity — by design.** Unlike the HDD duopoly (explicitly not adding unit capacity), optical vendors are **signing LTAs up to five years and adding InP unit capacity** (brown- and green-field) — the multi-pronged capacity ramp (vs HDD's one- or two-product ramp) is the swing factor, and customers are now trying to **lock in supply** rather than chase demand visibility (BofA optical supply-chain call, 2026-06-12).
- **6-inch InP yield is the COHR-vs-LITE fork.** Coherent reports **every product on 6-inch (EMLs, CW, photodiodes) yielding better than prior gen**, with 6-inch >50% of InP volume from the Sep-Q and a path to **>42% GM**; Lumentum stays hesitant on 6-inch (its 4-inch yields are already good, so relative uplift is smaller). COHR is guiding **a quarter ahead on 2x InP cal-26 + another 2x cal-27** (JPM "Networking recap", 2026-05-08; BofA CIEN supply-chain call, 2026-06-12).
- **400G-per-lane copper is settled — and it's a CPO release valve.** The standards bodies have resolved that **400G-per-lane can still run copper** (Broadcom pushing full-copper scale-up at 400G/lane), so **CPO concern is "both overblown and much further into the future"**; co-packaged-copper benefits the connector vendors (Molex, Amphenol, TE, TTM) and lets CPO ramp "more naturally" rather than being forced. New laser entrants (AAOI, MACOM, Semtech, Lightmatter, Nokia) pile in as the CPO laser TAM scales **low-hundreds-of-millions → ~$1B**, raising a real **glut risk** as everyone adds capacity (650 Group, 2026-05-26).
- **CPO/SiPh is a named SoIC growth driver into 2027–28 — capacity is being built for it.** Fubon: silicon-photonics chips become "a big SoIC application in 2027," and TSMC SoIC capacity scales **45k → 65k → 90k wpm by end-FY26/27/28**; CoPoS (20–30k wpm by end-2028, a TSMC priority project at VisEra, MP targeted 2H28) is being readied partly for NVDA's Feynman platform features (Coupe 2.0/LPU). On the optical/networking-interconnect side, **Broadcom's transceiver demand "will grow tremendously in 2027" driven by hyperscalers such as Meta**, handled by **ASE (FoCoS revised up 30k → 45k wpm by end-2027)**; V10 TPU adopts **400G SerDes** (Fubon "Semi 2027 outlook", 2026-06-05). Redburn corroborates: TSMC accelerating AP7 SoIC capacity, CPO a "continuation in growth for SoIC."

- **Scale-up switching + scale-up CPO are the near-term "explode" trade — merchant goes zero-to-big in two years (BTG/LightCounting expert "Bob", 2026-06-25).** LightCounting's read: **scale-up switching, then scale-up CPO right behind it, are "very much going to explode in the near term"** — starting with [ALAB](../ALAB.md) **Scorpio X ramping now**, followed by scale-up CPO ramping **particularly with the [MRVL](../MRVL.md)/Celestial AI acquisition toward the end of next year** — i.e. these go from zero to very large merchant numbers over the next couple of years. Most early CPO designs use **external laser sources supplied by [COHR](../COHR.md) and [LITE](../LITE.md)** ([AVGO](../AVGO.md) "supposedly" has high-power lasers but isn't seen in near-term designs); for CPO one external laser feeds multiple ports, so it needs **very-high-power lasers where the supplier count is essentially two (possibly three)** and ASPs are higher — reinforcing the own-the-light-source thesis. The SiPh piece routes to MRVL (Celestial AI) plus privates Ayar Labs / Lightmatter; VCSEL-based scale-up (no external laser) is so far a China / near-package-optics interest, not adopted by NA hyperscalers (BTG "LightCounting Optics/CPO Expert Call", 2026-06-25).
- **CPO is reliability-driven now, but NVDA is still pushing ahead of end-demand (BTG/LightCounting, 2026-06-25).** Corroborates the Meta reliability-inflection thesis: [AVGO](../AVGO.md)'s earlier CPO designs got little traction ("seeding the market," small deployments), but **Meta lab-testing of AVGO's latest switches found them far more reliable than pluggable-optics systems**, making CPO interesting for *reliability* (critical to AI training), not just power. The caveat: **[NVDA](../NVDA.md) has not yet published reliability data for its own CPO**, so it remains "more of a push from NVIDIA" — NVDA is preparing the supply chain for volume (the rationale behind its COHR/LITE investments) **"but we don't think the demand matches up"** with the supply being lined up just yet; large-customer pull "we don't necessarily see just yet" (BTG "LightCounting Optics/CPO Expert Call", 2026-06-25).
- **1.6T DSP: design wins ≠ share — AVGO competitive but not at the volume sockets yet (BTG/LightCounting, 2026-06-25).** First-gen 1.6T DSPs ran too hot, so **all suppliers had to redo 3nm designs**; MRVL was first to market but didn't ship in meaningful volume, letting AVGO catch up — **AVGO and MRVL now essentially in lockstep on 1.6T time-to-market**. But near-term 1.6T demand is **primarily Google (uses MRVL) and NVDA (uses its own internal DSP, 5nm shipping now, 3nm coming this year)**, so despite a competitive product **AVGO has little near-term 1.6T share**; AVGO should gain share rapidly as 1.6T broadens (incl. Tomahawk-6 switches) beyond Google/NVDA. On MRVL: surprised that besides Celestial AI scale-up CPO, MRVL guided its **legacy in-house PAM4 ~200G/lane optical engine to ~$150M revenue next year (same number as Celestial)** — MRVL now offering a broad portfolio (Celestial, VCSEL arrays, micro-LEDs) and letting customers choose, which may be why NVDA treats MRVL as ecosystem rather than a Broadcom-like competitor (BTG "LightCounting Optics/CPO Expert Call", 2026-06-25).
- **AI networking TAM raised to ~$316bn (from ~$240bn) on optics/switch ramp (BofA "AI 2030: Stronger for Longer", 2026-05-13).** BofA's CY30 AI-networking TAM goes to **~$316bn from ~$240bn** (AI switching $129bn, SmartNIC $78bn, connectivity/other $110bn of which optical $88bn at +42% CAGR / copper $22bn at +61%), inside a ~$1.7Tn total AI-DC-systems TAM; CPO transceiver TAM is modeled small but rising ($0.2bn 2026 → ~$1.1bn 2028). The note lifts MRVL PO to $200 (from $125), COHR to $400 (from $365), maintains LITE $1,100 — flagging **COHR initial scale-out CPO UHP lasers shipping 1H27** as an NVDA-supply-chain tailwind (BofA "AI 2030: Stronger for Longer", 2026-05-13).
- **Tower Semiconductor fireside chat details the foundry-side heterogeneous-integration roadmap (SIG/Mehdi Hosseini, 2026-07-01).** SIG hosted TSEM management on transceiver-manufacturing dynamics: **pluggables remain the primary Si-Ph/Si-Ge demand driver at 800G** (no negative demand impact from the CPO narrative, consistent with the theme's existing "coexistence, not winner-take-all" read), with content per transceiver rising as the industry migrates 800G→1.6T; **LPO and pluggables could be among the first form factors to reach 400G/lane**. **NPO transceiver adoption is expected to begin by YE2026 and accelerate into 2027** alongside 1.6T pluggable traction — a specific timing data point for the scale-up transition this theme has been tracking qualitatively. Management still frames **true optical CPO commercialization as "several years away"** and expects pluggables to persist, with CPO capturing only part of total transceiver growth (consistent with the page's existing bear case that CPO-in-scale-out saves only ~2-3% of cluster power). On the laser side: TSEM's opportunity is **heterogeneous integration of III-V (InP) lasers onto its silicon-photonics platform** — sourcing InP wafers with epitaxial layers already applied from **IQE** (not bare substrate from Sumitomo) — targeting pluggables and NPO initially, independent of CPO adoption; the **III-V-on-CMOS integration challenge has been resolved**, with new customer relationships (Scintil on the laser side, Oarele, Salient) rather than existing ones, and **management named Nvidia as the party driving the customer roadmap**. On wafer size, management called **200mm "perfectly adequate and the better platform on cost"** for current Si-Ph/Si-Ge products (nitride-carried laser layers work fine at 200mm); 300mm is reserved for larger-form-factor/integrated-receive products and its added capacity **awaits a METI (Japan) grant approval**. On the path to 400G/lane, TSEM cited **two competing approaches: an all-silicon route via a [COHR](../COHR.md) partnership (Tower SiPho + Coherent's design)** and a **heterogeneous InP route via an OpenLight partnership**, plus investment in lithium niobate/polymers. Separately, TSEM flagged that **resolving fiber-array-unit (FAU) challenges — work associated with [GLW](../GLW.md) — would be positive for Tower and accelerate scale-up**, though Tower itself has not announced FAU participation (SIG "Tech Talk: Inside the Transceiver Manufacturing Supply Chain," Mehdi Hosseini, 2026-07-01).
- **Conor's Asia trip surfaces a SiPh equipment-spend play and new optical-component names (Conor, 2026-06-23).** Redburn's Asia trip introduced **silicon-photonics chips as a new SOIC use case** (confirming the optics-into-the-rack / TSMC COUPE thesis from the equipment side) and several off-coverage names as ways to play CPO/SiPh equipment spending: **Ferrotec** makes **thermoelectric (TEC) modules for optical** with ~**70% share** (Lumentum ~20%, also supplies Coherent/II-VI and InnoLight/Eoptolink) at ~**30% margins** — a coherent-optics-leveraged business growing faster than the group; **Kolon Industries (Korea)** makes the **MPO specialty chemical for CCL** (current customers iTech/EMC/TUC), revenue doubled to ~160B, under Doosan qualification (could 4x to 300–400B if qualified); and lab-equipment house **M.A.Tech** (top-10 customers, +51% growth) surfaced **Hamamatsu Photonics (Japan)** — an **IR/CCD detector** maker — as a probe/test supply-chain idea (alongside FormFactor/MPI on probing and Keysight/Anritsu on test) (Redburn "Conor O'Mara Asia Trip Takeaways", 2026-06-23). These are plain-text off-coverage names (no wiki page).

## Key debates
- **Bull / where the money is — own the light source.** In a CPO module the laser is ~30–40% of BOM vs only 10–15% in a pluggable, because CPO lasers are far less commoditized (more IP, higher complexity) — so the differentiated money is in lasers, not module assembly (Mike/Rothschild, 2026-05-19). CPO TAM ~$15B by 2030 (Rothschild), which Coherent itself now calls "conservative"; scale-across ~$7B; OCS and scale-up Ethernet each ~$6B (Mike/Rothschild, 2026-05-19). Corning's content/GPU rises from low-hundreds of dollars toward 1.3–1.5x in scale-up, and a fully-optical scale-up world needs ~10x the fiber of today's 16 fibers/GPU (Corning, 2026-05-12).
- **Bear / what could break it.** (1) **Capacity flooding** — any rampant-growth cycle invites overbuild fear, and China oversupply + valuation re-rating is already driving a "weaker pure-play optics" narrative (LITE, AAOI, FN, CIEN, IQE) vs "stronger" copper+optics names (SMTC, GLW, ALAB, CRDO, MRVL) (@crux_capital_, company-specific briefing 2026-06-19). (2) **Multiples** — the basket re-rated from teens to 25–30x CY28; MS flags least control of output = most caution, ranking by destiny-control: Lumentum > Corning > Coherent > Ciena (Meta Marshall/MS, 2026-05-07). (3) **CPO-in-scale-out is modest** — replacing scale-out transceivers with CPO saves only ~2–3% of cluster power and you lose the field-replaceability of a pluggable, so scale-out CPO penetration should lag scale-up materially (Mike/Rothschild, 2026-05-19).
- **CPO vs pluggables vs LPO — coexistence, not winner-take-all.** The Feb panic that pluggables were "older generation / going to be very bad" has not shown up in demand — pluggables see no negative impact, even stronger demand; shortage in everything (Andy/MS, 2026-05-07). The likely split: CPO penetration much higher in scale-up than scale-out; pluggables/IMDD persist for scale-out and DCI; LPO and linear re-drivers fill copper-to-optics edges. Teradyne sees SiPh and EML coexisting (Jevons-paradox adoption as SiPh commoditizes) rather than one replacing the other (Teradyne/Arcuri, 2026-05-04).
- **OCS — Google is the anchor, externalization is the catalyst.** OCS keeps signals optical via MEMS mirror arrays (Lumentum) or liquid-crystal (Coherent), avoiding power-hungry O-E-O conversions. Google historically built OCS in-house and is now sourcing externally — a natural buyer for Lumentum's MEMS arrays; LITE targets $400M OCS in 1H-FY27 and ~$1–2B in 2027, gated by test-time/throughput not demand, with the roadmap "fracturing" into high-Radix (training), low-Radix (inference) and scale-up variants (Lumentum/Michael+Wupen, 2026-05-07). Mike/Rothschild has more confidence in LITE's field-tested MEMS than COHR's less-proven liquid-crystal (2026-05-19).
- **Hybrid-bonding equipment is a new, levered way to play CPO-into-scale-up.** Redburn forecasts a cumulative **50 BESI hybrid bonders to serve CPO applications by 2030, accelerating from 2H28** — because the EIC-PIC optical-engine bond uses D2W hybrid bonding (heterogeneous die favor D2W over W2W), feeding TSMC's COUPE/SoIC roadmap. Redburn raises BESI FY26-30 revenue estimates +9–20% and EBIT +13–27% (CPO + China LogicFolding + mainstream OSAT recovery; OSAT capex expectations +122% YTD, ASE utilization 59%→80% by 1Q26), Buy, PT €350 (Redburn "BESI", 2026-06-15). Read-through: CPO scale-up is now showing up in *equipment* order books, not just module/laser forecasts.
- **Timeline for optics inside the rack.** Scale-out CPO is the near wave (now); scale-up arrives in stages — uplinks/multi-rack first (OCP/October-26 timeframe for industry awareness), then true inside-the-rack GPU-to-GPU "realistically the very end of this decade," because copper stays cheaper and known until you're forced off it (650 Group, 2026-06-04). The disaggregation bet matters: hyperscalers are forcing Broadcom/Marvell to build switch chips (next-gen Tomahawk) that support an open CPO ecosystem, which is what lets Ciena/Nubis (Vesta CPO, Nitro linear re-driver) play against captive solutions (Ciena, 2026-06-12).
