## Snapshot
nVent is an electrical connection & protection pure-play, spun off from Pentair in 2018. Two reportable segments post-portfolio reshuffle: **Systems Protection** (FY2025 $2,592.9M, +42.2%; brands HOFFMAN enclosures, SCHROFF, TRACHTE control buildings, plus switchgear/bus systems from the Avail Electrical Products Group; liquid cooling + air cooling) and **Electrical Connections** (FY2025 $1,300.2M, +9.9%; brands CADDY, ERICO, ILSCO; bus systems, cable management, power connections). FY2025 continuing net sales **$3,893.1M, +29.5% (+12.6% organic)** (10-K filed 2026-02-17). The company sold its Thermal Management business to Brookfield for **$1.6B** net cash (2025, now discontinued ops) and redeployed into infrastructure: **EPG/Avail ~$1.0B (closed 2025-05-01), Trachte ~$0.7B (2024), ECM ~$1.1B (2023)**. It sits squarely in the **AI data-center power & cooling** chain — DC sales hit ~$1B in 2025 (vs ~$600M in 2024), now the primary growth engine.

## Position in the value chain
nVent converts commodity inputs (steel, copper, aluminum, electronic components) into engineered electrical infrastructure: connection/protection hardware, **busbar/power distribution (EPG switchgear + bus systems, PDUs)**, **liquid cooling (CDUs, modular cooling)**, and protective **enclosures/control buildings**. It sells primarily to **AI data centers (hyperscalers), power utilities, industrial and broader infrastructure** customers — increasingly direct to the AI buildout, where it supplies both white space (~80% of DC mix) and gray space. The data-center power + liquid-cooling franchise is the highest-growth, highest-AI-beta node of the chain (NVIDIA partner network; CDU roadmaps to 2030). For market context, BofA sizes the all-electrical-equipment DC market at ~$18bn in 2024 (Schneider ~21%, Vertiv ~17%, Eaton ~16%, ABB ~9% — Exhibit 6) and the all-thermal market at ~$10bn (Vertiv ~20% leader — Exhibit 7) — NVT is not large enough to break out in either share pie, but is a Tier 1 player in the narrower, nascent ~$1.2bn CDU sub-segment (BofA DC Primer, 2025-09-15). See themes: [800v-dc-power](themes/800v-dc-power.md), [ai-datacenter-power](themes/ai-datacenter-power.md).

## Debate / thesis
- **Bull:** AI data-center power/cooling super-cycle with multi-year visibility. DC sales ~$1B in 2025 (from ~$600M in 2024); backlog tripled to $2.3B at YE25 (~50% of 2026 guide) and grew to $2.6B in Q1 (Q4 2025 call 2026-02-06; Q1 2026 call). Liquid-cooling penetration "<30% of data centers" (mgmt) leaves long runway; NVIDIA partner network designation + CDU roadmaps to 2030 (Q3 2025 call 2025-10-31). **NVT is a Tier 1 CDU vendor** — one of only four (with Delta, Schneider/Motivair, Vertiv) BofA scores at ≥$100M CDU revenue with multiple variants — and offers in-rack + in-row across liquid-to-air and liquid-to-liquid (RackChiller CDU800 launched Nov 2020; CDU service capabilities being built out, a key barrier to entry in a conservative, uptime-first buyer base) (BofA DC Primer, 2025-09-15). In a still-nascent CDU TAM (~$1.2B in 2024), incumbency on/around the rack defends share vs. new-entrant disruption (BofA DC Primer, 2025-09-15). Portfolio remix into infrastructure (EPG/Avail switchgear+busbar, Trachte control buildings) raises structural growth and AI beta. Net leverage 1.5x leaves M&A/buyback firepower.
- **Bear:** Order growth is lumpy and concentration on AI DC projects raises whisper of a digestion air-pocket; backlog quality and bid-win variability flagged in 10-K. Electrical Connections margin hit -390 bps on copper inflation in Q1 — pricing lag risk. Tariff headwind ~$80M FY26 (after ~$90M prior year, ~$170M cumulative two-year). Heavy acquisition-led growth (EPG/Trachte/ECM) carries integration and goodwill-impairment risk. Liquid-cooling competition intensifying — BofA counts ~30 vendors / 100+ CDU variants now in market (BofA DC Primer, 2025-09-15). **800V-DC read-across risk:** when NVIDIA named its 800V-DC architecture *power-system* partners, the list was Eaton / Schneider / Vertiv only — NVT was not on it; its NVIDIA tie sits on the CDU/Computex side, not the power-system side (BofA DC Primer, 2025-09-15). Per BofA, the AC→DC shift simplifies UPS (−10–20% cost) but is largely offset by higher-voltage switchgear + rectifiers, leaving electrical content/MW roughly flat — i.e., the architecture pivot reads as share-mix risk more than a content tailwind for NVT (BofA DC Primer, 2025-09-15).
