## Snapshot
Argan is an EPC (engineering, procurement, construction) holding company for energy infrastructure, operating primarily through its Gemma Power Systems subsidiary. Three segments: **Power** (~78% of revenue — gas-fired combined-cycle plants, plus solar/batteries, biofuel, biomass), **Industrial** (~20% — field services and fabrication of piping/pressure vessels/tanks) and **Teledata** (~2% — communications/data infrastructure, including data centers and federal installations) (Q1 FY27, 2026-06-04). The Power backlog is worth ~80% of historical consolidated revenue (10-K FY26, 2026-03-26: 80.1% in Fiscal 2026). It is a niche small-cap: management repeats that only "a handful of firms" can execute gigawatt-plus combined-cycle work under fixed-price contracts (Q1 FY27).

## Debate / thesis
- **Bull:** structural demand for firm generation — electrification, onshoring, EVs and data centers strain the grid; combined-cycle gas is "the ideal solution" and few competitors execute gigawatt-plus jobs on a fixed-price basis (Watson, Q1 FY27). Long growth runway: management says it is targeting $2B of revenue "down the road" and capacity for 10-12 simultaneous jobs (currently at 8) (Watson, Q1 FY27). Track record of executing above contract — early substantial completion at Trumbull and Midwest drove a Power margin of 23.6% in Q1 (29% in Q4 FY26) (Watson/Baugher). A debt-free balance sheet + $421M net liquidity supports "several billion more" of backlog and provides a bankability/bonding advantage (Watson, Q1 FY27). Industrial is growing optionality: record revenue, $225M backlog and a multi-year runway in data-center tanks (Watson, Q1 FY27).
  - **Execution track record quantified (deck):** Gemma **has not had a lost (defaulted) job since Argan acquired it in 2006** — a ~19-year clean execution record that underpins the fixed-price + bonding moat (InvestorDay, 2025-04-08). Gas-fired projects span a **3-4-year build cycle**, and management frames industry demand as continuing "the next decade and beyond" (InvestorDay, 2025-04-08).
  - **Demand TAM — management's own framing (deck):** US electricity consumption seen growing **+4%/yr through 2027** (IEA, Feb-2025); AI/data-center power demand at a **~70% annual CAGR through 2027** (Morgan Stanley, Mar-2024); US EV load projected to rise **~20x to 468,000 GWh by 2040 (from 24,000 GWh in 2023)** (PwC, 2024); **$525B of US factory commitments since 2021** (semis/chips/batteries/solar) (NYT, Mar-2024) (all via InvestorDay, 2025-04-08). Supply-side support for replacement EPC: **12.3 GW of capacity set to retire in 2025 (up from 7.4 GW in 2024, a 65% jump) and ~78 GW of planned retirements over the next 10 years** (EIA Feb-2025 / NERC Dec-2024, via InvestorDay 2025-04-08).
- **Bear:** **backlog does not grow linearly** — it fell from $2.9B → $2.8B; large new jobs depend on development milestones outside Argan's control (air/water permits, gas access, turbines, financing) and the "10-18 months" window is repeated call after call (Watson, Q1 FY27). Margin is binary and back-end-loaded: management declines guidance, noting that quarterly margins have ranged from **11.4% to 25%** over the last 2 years and that new projects still carry "many open risks" — visibility on final margin is low (Baugher, Q4 FY26; Watson, Q1 FY27). Renewable demand has "softened" — solar/battery exposure is less attractive (Watson, Q1 FY27). Concentration: a small-cap dependent on a few large customers and on fixed-price contracts with liquidated damages (10-K FY26).

Observable coverage only via analysts present on the calls: CJS Securities (Chris Moore), Goldman Sachs (Ati Modak), Lake Street Capital (Rob Brown), JPMorgan (Michael Fairbanks). The tone of the questions is constructive, focused on capacity, the cadence of new jobs and margin sustainability — there is no PT/rating recoverable from the on-disk sources.
- **Expert-call read-through — ex-insider color (4 calls, Jun-2025 → Jan-2026).** _Caveat: the Gemma-president call and both Kindle Energy calls are the same individual (Gemma president 2018 → Sep-2024, now MD of EPC at Kindle Energy, a Blackstone developer/owner) — one voice, not three sources. The mid-2025 calls are structural background and predate the current $2.8B backlog/FY27 prints._
  - **Competitive set named + pricing power quantified:** the fixed-price power-EPC field is down to Bechtel, Burns & McDonnell, Black & Veatch, Kiewit, Zachry and Gemma (AECOM, Fluor, CB&I and Shaw all exited), and **only Gemma and Kiewit still take full lump-sum EPC risk with liquidated damages** — the structure banks require. Owners now pay for proposals (can run ~$1M) and sign LOIs with multi-million breakup fees; where they used to get 3-4 bids they get one; the EPC price for the same scope/contract structure is up ~2/3; and equipment lead times (turbines, HRSGs, steam turbines) have stretched typical builds from 29-31 to 42-44 months (expert call — former president, Gemma Power Systems, 2025-06-16).
  - **Margin trajectory (current, Jan-2026 vintage):** Power-EPC gross margins have moved from ~16-17% historically to ~20%, and are "poised to increase" toward ~22.5-25% as EPCs price to market, load more contingency (~7.5% historically → ~10-12%) and negotiate longer schedules/easier LD terms that shift risk back to owners — vs. solar EPC at only 8-10% and "very, very competitive" (expert call — MD of EPC, Kindle Energy [ex-Gemma president], 2026-01-15).
  - **Capacity skepticism — the counter to "10-12 jobs":** the practical ceiling is **~4 simultaneous complex CCGT projects** (Gemma's historical peak; a CCGT needs a 45-50-person staff team + 700-1,000 craft) — "if you told me it was 10-12 gas projects, I'll tell you you're crazy"; the 10-12 figure must include renewables/simple-cycle/smaller work, and adding projects risks "diluting the core team" and margin degradation (expert call — MD of EPC, Kindle Energy [ex-Gemma president], 2026-01-15).
  - **Backlog-quality caveat — SLEC/Sandow Lakes (mid-2025 vintage, since overtaken by execution):** as of Jun-2025 the expert's Kindle team had **declined any financial contribution to the Sandow Lakes (SLEC) project**, saying it had a full NTP issued but no financing and (he claimed) no air permit or interconnect agreement yet, with owner Xebec (LA real-estate) having no power-gen build experience — yet Gemma is structured **cash-positive with no downside**: limited-NTP up-front payments plus termination-for-convenience cost recovery ("they can't lose money"). Same expert — an AGX shareholder — called the stock "unrealistic/overrated" (expert call — MD of EPC, Kindle Energy [ex-Gemma president], 2025-06-27). _Note: SLEC 1.2 GW is in execution per the Q1 FY27 call — read this as dated color on backlog-conversion risk, not a current project fact._
  - **Pipeline + management color:** Oglethorpe Power (GA) has ~$1B of work across two plants out for bid via Sargent & Lundy, with Gemma at an estimated "60%-or-better" chance of prevailing over Kiewit and Black & Veatch; and CEO Watson is driving genuinely new Gemma↔Roberts collaboration (shared board meetings, early constructability input — under the prior regime Roberts BD was ordered "don't call Gemma"). Roberts has also restricted its ASME code-vessel shop to fabricating only for its own construction projects (expert call — former regional BD manager, The Roberts Company, 2025-06-30).
- **Expert-call read-through — gas-plant build pipeline + the lumpiness gate.** A DC-build satellite-tracking call quantifies the gas-generation construction backdrop behind AGX's gigawatt-scale Power backlog: **~25 GW of natural-gas capacity starting construction in 2026 (~8 GW online '26, ~11 GW '27), then ~35 GW of starts in 2027 and ~38 GW in 2028** — a multi-year EPC tailwind. But it also corroborates AGX's own "backlog swings" bear point: most slippage is **delays, not cancellations**, gated by **labor (5 craft workers retiring per 1 entrant), permitting and 3–5-yr turbine lead times** — exactly the development-milestone gates AGX cites between job awards (Jefferies "DC build / Vulcan-IIR", 2026-06-22). Read as sector corroboration (no AGX rating), supportive of the "handful of firms can do fixed-price GW-scale gas EPC" thesis.

## Risks
- **Fixed-price contracts** with liquidated damages and re-performance obligations: cost overrun/delay becomes a direct loss on the project (10-K FY26).
- **Reliance on surety/bonding** on a project-by-project basis; a change in the surety's risk assessment could limit the ability to compete (10-K FY26).
- **Customer concentration** — a meaningful share of annual revenue comes from a few customers; the mix varies year to year (10-K FY26).
- **Timing/cancellation:** contracts can be postponed or cancelled after award; start depends on third-party development milestones (10-K FY26; Watson, Q1 FY27).
- **Inflation/supply chain and trade policy** (tariffs) on equipment for fixed-price projects; mitigated by early equipment purchasing, but not eliminated (10-K FY26).
- **Margin and backlog lumpiness** — low visibility, no margin guidance (Baugher, Q4 FY26).
