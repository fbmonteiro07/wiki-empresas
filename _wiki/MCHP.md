## Snapshot
Broad-line embedded-control semi: 8/16/32/64-bit mixed-signal microcontrollers, analog, FPGA, networking/connectivity, and data-center/memory products, sold as a "Total System Solution" into industrial, automotive, aerospace & defense, communications, data center and consumer. FY26 (ended Mar-31-26) net sales **$4,713.1M, +7.1% y/y**, split MCU **$2,355.4M (50.0%)**, Analog **$1,329.0M (28.2%)**, Other (incl. FPGA, DC, licensing) **$1,028.7M (21.8%)** (10-K FY26, 2026-05-21). ~75% of sales foreign (China 18%, Taiwan 15%); ~35% of FY26 sales from own US fabs, balance outsourced (10-K FY26). The story is mechanical cyclical torque: FY24 sales were **$7,634.4M** at the peak — MCHP cut almost in half through the destocking trough and is now climbing back, with operating leverage doing the heavy lifting.

## Position in the value chain
MCHP buys wafers/substrates from external foundries (advanced nodes) and runs its own US fabs (~35% internal), then ships microcontrollers + analog + FPGA + connectivity into late-cycle industrial / auto / aero-defense / data-center customers. The torque sits at both ends: a thin sliver of incremental volume off the destocked base drops through at ~95% incremental gross margin (FQ2 FY26), while **substrate/foundry tightness ("70-80% of nodes constrained," Q4 FY26)** now caps how fast it can convert a book-to-bill well above 1.0.

## Current state (latest quarter)
- **Q4 FY26 (ended Mar-31-26, reported 2026-05-07):** Net sales **$1.311B, +10.6% q/q, +35.1% y/y**, above the high end of guide; cons ~$1.26B. Non-GAAP EPS **$0.57** ($0.07 above midpoint, cons ~$0.50). Non-GAAP GM **61.6%** (incl. $46.6M underutilization), op margin **30.6%**, adj EBITDA $466.8M (Q4 FY26 call).
- **Recovery cadence:** four straight up quarters off the trough — $1.075B (Q1) → $1.14B (Q2) → $1.186B (Q3) → $1.311B (Q4), with GM marching 54.3% → 56.7% → 60.5% → 61.6% (FY26 transcripts). The latest reported quarter (FQ3 FY26 in the 10-Q, ended Dec-25) showed net sales $1,186.0M vs $1,026.0M PY, GM 59.6% (10-Q 2026-02-05).
- **Inventory:** company DOI **185 days (-16 q/q)**, distributor **26 days** (historic low); sell-through +11.4%; April was the largest booking month in ~4 years, book-to-bill well above 1.0 (Q4 FY26 call).
- **Supply-gated upside:** "We are constrained on substrates… 70% to 80% of process technology nodes are constrained" (Sanghi, Q4 FY26). Lead times extending; delinquencies rising "not at crisis level."
- **Post-earnings momentum confirmed at JPM TMC (JPM Harlan Sur, Semis Spring Series, 2026-05-29):** Sur relays that at JPM's TMC conference MCHP reiterated **April bookings were the highest month in nearly four years, with momentum continuing into May**; **distribution inventory at just 26 days** with restocking now underway and many direct customers who hadn't ordered in 12-18 months coming back. Critically on pricing, MCHP is **holding pricing to preserve design momentum and rebuild customer relationships** after prior-cycle missteps — but did **not rule out a broader set of price increases into 2H or next year** if input costs keep rising and tight corridors of the portfolio (which they are seeing) persist. JPM keeps MCHP an **overweight top pick** among broad-based names; flags calendar-27 EPS for the broad-based trio (TI/ADI/MCHP) already revised **+23% over the past two earnings seasons** with more revision runway as restocking (the "second leg") is still ahead.
- **Balance sheet:** total indebtedness **$5.54B** (10-K FY26); net debt/adj EBITDA **3.54x** (from 4.18x prior qtr); buybacks paused for deleveraging; quarterly dividend **$0.455/sh** declared 2026-05-07 (10-K FY26). Capex tiny (~$92M FY26; ~$100M FY27 guide).
- **Guide — FQ1 FY27 (Jun-26):** net sales **+11% q/q (±1%)** ≈ $1.44-1.47B (cons ~$1.34B); non-GAAP EPS **$0.67-0.71**, ~19% above Street (Q4 FY26 call; briefing 2026-05-08).

## Debate / thesis
- **Bull:** Textbook deep-cyclical recovery with extreme operating leverage — incremental GM ~95% / incremental op margin ~84.6% q/q at the early stage (Q2 FY26 call); LT model 65% GM / 40% op margin vs 61.6%/30.6% today (Q4 FY26). Distributor inventory at historic lows (26 days), book-to-bill >1.0, supply tightness building pricing/allocation power. New growth legs in data center (FY26 18% of mix; PCIe Gen6 switches, retimers, CXL controllers — plus SSD/flash controllers, where MS frames MCHP and Marvell as the two major US datacenter SSD-controller suppliers and closest peers, AI NAND overview MS) and aero/defense (16%, NATO + aircraft ramp). **UBS (Arcuri) reit Buy, PT $85** (raised from $65), +31% upside off ~$66 spot — thesis is the 9-step plan executing with both internal and channel inventory glut "nearly worked through," so revenue still has room to grow as inventory normalizes while GM leverage compounds as underutilization and inventory charges fall away (UBS 2026 overview, 2025-12-22). UBS sits well above the Street: FY27E rev **$5,687M (+24% y/y)** vs cons ~$5,455M, FY27E EPS **$2.84** vs cons $2.52 (+13%); FY28E rev **$7,048M**, EPS **$4.36** vs cons $3.39 (+17%); GM modeled to 65% by exit-FY27, op margin to ~42%, FCF building $1.0B FY26 → $1.5B FY27 → $2.3B FY28 (UBS 2026 overview, 2025-12-22). The FQ4 guide grew 11% with EPS ~19% above cons "validates the re-rating call" (briefings 2026-05-08, 2026-05-19).
- **Bear:** Stock already re-rated hard (+35.1% y/y revenue, shares ran into the print). MS (Moore) reit **EW, $94** — frames the DC disclosure as in-line and the price hikes as "defensive, not offensive," and flags MCHP's DC sub-segment as "not dissimilar to peers in size/growth but **lagging TXN and ADI**" (UBS Arcuri's own caveat, briefings 2026-05-08, 2026-06-02). Leverage still elevated (3.54x), China 18% of sales into intensifying local competition (UBS flags "idiosyncratic share loss / market destruction from China indigenization" as the key risk, UBS 2026 overview 2025-12-22), and the recovery is now supply-gated — "Can I grow a business 50% in one quarter? No" (Sanghi). Automotive was the laggard end-market through the cycle.
- **AI-power-semi sizing (BofA "Watts to Tokens" full model, 2026-05-25):** BofA's bottoms-up AI analog-semi model has MCHP as a **small but stable participant — revenue $249m (CY25) → $901m (CY30), share ~3.2% → ~3.4%** (roughly holding share, well behind TXN/ADI/IFX/ON). MCHP is named among the key vendors in **Analog ICs** (controllers/PMICs/supervisors/digital-power) and **MCUs** (distributed power orchestration across rectifiers/sidecars/IBCs/VRMs) plus clocking/timing (PCIe oscillators/switches through Gen7) — i.e. control-plane/networking content rather than the SiC/GaN power-conversion sockets that drive the fastest-growing device pools; MCHP is not in BofA's SiC/GaN winner set. This corroborates the JPM read below that MCHP's DC content is control-plane, not 800V power. (BofA "Watts to Tokens" full model, 2026-05-25)
- **Data-center exposure (JPM):** JPM's AI-power primer (Gartner basis) sizes MCHP's data-processing/compute content at **~$926m CY2025 (~14.9% of revenue)** — a higher revenue share than most analog peers, but composed of **MCUs (~$328m), wired-connectivity/app-specific (~$252m), voltage-reg/reference (~$66m) and PCIe/networking** — i.e. control-plane + networking silicon, not the SiC/GaN power-conversion sockets that anchor the 800V thesis (MCHP is not among JPM's named 800V power players) (JPM "First Principles," 2026-06-25).
- **MCU-cycle context (Citi):** Citi confirms **MCHP announced a broad-based price increase starting in June** (alongside IFX/TXN/NXPI/ON pricing actions). Citi's cycle work frames the upside still ahead specifically for MCU-heavy names like MCHP: the **microcontroller cycle has lasted only ~4 quarters** (vs ~7 for analog), with industry **MCU revenue still ~20% below prior peak and units ~23% below**, and MCU pricing flat/stable since 2022 — i.e. MCU is the laggard category yet to inflect, leaving restocking runway (Citi "800V power", 2026-06-15). An auto Tier-1 expert described Microchip demand as **"consistent"** with no clear share loss, affecting the product lines / new products it launches (Expert call, 2026-06-08).
- **Where the Street stands (briefings):** **GS Buy, PT $115** (26x normalized EPS $4.50) — expects another beat-and-raise quarter with ~1% revenue upside; **FY27 (Mar) non-GAAP EPS est ~7% above Street** (Goldman Sachs "2Q Preview: Expect broad upside...", 2026-07-05); UBS reit Buy $85 (Arcuri); JPM "Recovery Inflection"; Citi Buy (Malik); Barclays constructive; **MS EW $94** (Moore) (2026-05-08). Read-through anchor for the analog complex (ADI/ON/NXPI/STM/IFX/POWI) — peers raising DC guides on the same inflection (2026-06-02). No Outlook desk notes pulled this session — see Sources.
- **LTA case-study read-through (Bernstein, historical — NOT a forward call on MCHP):** Bernstein's memory-LTA series (part 2, Newman, 2026-07-09) uses Microchip's **Preferred Supply Program (PSP)** as the canonical cautionary example of how *weak* legacy semiconductor LTAs failed — a **12-month rolling non-cancelable/non-reschedulable (NCNR) backlog with no cash commitment**, relaxed to 6 months in Aug-2023 and discontinued for new orders in Feb-2024 (reputational, never actually enforced). In the 2020-22 COVID-era shortage, double/triple-ordering inflated an artificial backlog; once demand normalized the LTAs collapsed under an inventory glut and MCHP (like ADI) intentionally **undershipped**, with **net sales down >42% (Q3 FY25 vs Q3 FY24)** at the trough — consistent with the ~half-peak decline (FY24 $7,634M peak) already in the Snapshot. Bernstein's lesson — weak contract terms + a transient, mean-reverting shortage — is invoked as the **anti-template** against which the New Memory LTAs (SNDK/MU) are judged to have "real teeth" (upfront back-end-weighted collateral, investment-grade counterparties, structural AI demand). This is a **backward-looking case study, not a new rating, PT or estimate on MCHP** (Bernstein "New Memory LTAs part 2", 2026-07-09).

## Risks
- Supply-side cap on the recovery (substrate/foundry constraints on 70-80% of nodes) limiting near-term shipment upside (Q4 FY26).
- Elevated leverage ($5.54B debt, 3.54x) and convertible/senior debt maturities; dividend competes with deleveraging (10-K FY26).
- China 18% of sales amid intense local competition; "idiosyncratic share loss / market destruction from China indigenization" the lead bull-case risk (UBS 2026 overview, 2025-12-22); ~75% foreign-customer concentration and tariff exposure (10-K FY26).
- Distributor channel: no long-term purchase agreements, terminable with little notice; Arrow ~12% of sales (10-K FY26).
- Demand round-trip risk if the macro/auto recovery stalls before utilization (and underutilization charges) normalize.
