## Snapshot
Veeco is a **smaller-cap semiconductor process-equipment maker** ($664M FY2025 revenue, -7% y/y; 10-K, 2026-02-25) that holds leading positions in a handful of niche front-end and advanced-packaging process steps rather than competing across broad WFE. Reports as **one segment**, sold into four end-markets: **Semiconductor $477M / 72%**, Compound Semiconductor $60M / 9%, Data Storage $39M / 6%, Scientific & Other $89M / 13% (FY2025; 10-K). Core franchises: **Laser Spike Annealing (LSA)** and next-gen **Nanosecond Annealing (NSA)**; **Ion Beam Deposition (IBD/Spectre)** for EUV mask blanks, pellicles and advanced-DRAM bitline films; **MOCVD/MBE** epitaxy (GaN-on-Si, InP) for compound semi/photonics; and **single-wafer wet processing + lithography** for advanced packaging. Levered to the right secular vectors — **gate-all-around (GAA) anneal, HBM/advanced DRAM, AI-driven advanced packaging, silicon-photonics InP lasers, and HAMR HDD** — but with meaningful China mature-node and customer-concentration drag. CEO Bill Miller, CFO John Kiernan.

**The dominant fact for the equity: VECO is being acquired by Axcelis (ACLS) in an all-stock merger.** Each VECO share converts to **0.3575 Axcelis shares**; combined company ~58.4% Axcelis / 41.6% Veeco fully diluted; both shareholder bases approved (2026-02-06). As of the Q1 print only **one regulatory approval is outstanding — China SAMR antitrust** — with close expected **H2 2026** (10-K Note 17, 2026-02-25; 10-Q Note 10, 2026-05-05). VECO trades as a merger-arb / Axcelis-proxy, not a standalone fundamental story.

## Position in the value chain
Veeco is a specialty process-equipment supplier sitting between precision-component/optics vendors and leading-edge chip, HDD, advanced-packaging and compound-semi manufacturers. It does not span broad WFE — it owns a few high-value niche process steps (anneal, ion-beam, epi, wet) that gate performance at advanced nodes. Smaller-cap; most levered to advanced packaging, HBM and GAA anneal.

## Current state (latest quarter)
**Q1 2026 (2026-05-05):** Revenue **$158M**, within guide but down y/y; **BIS export-license change removed ~$8M of LSA revenue** from a China customer (CFO Kiernan). Non-GAAP GM **~36%**, non-GAAP EPS **$0.14** (GAAP near-breakeven / ($0.01) net loss, pressured by ~$2M merger costs and weaker mix — 10-Q). Mix: Semi $109M (69%), Compound Semi $19M (12%), Data Storage $10M (6%), Scientific $20M (13%). Cash & ST investments **$383M**. China collapsed to **$20M / 13% of sales** vs $71M a year ago (-72% y/y; 10-Q) as mature-node demand rolled off, offset by Rest-of-APAC $90M (+51% y/y).

**Guidance:** Q2'26 revenue **$170–190M**, GM 38–40%, non-GAAP EPS $0.20–0.32. **FY2026 reaffirmed: revenue $740–800M (+11–16% y/y), non-GAAP EPS $1.50–1.85, GM 41–43%** — second-half weighted, with semi ~mid-teens growth, compound semi ~+50%, and data storage roughly doubling to ~$80M (Q4'25 / Q1'26 calls). FY2025 exit backlog was **$555M, +35% y/y** (Q4'25 call, 2026-02-25). Biggest 2026 swing factor flagged on the call: **>$250M of orders received for MOCVD + wet + IBD tools** for customers' indium-phosphide laser manufacturing (silicon photonics), shipping from Q3'26 and ramping hard into Q1'27, with **Spectre IBD capacity set to expand ~10x** by early 2027 (CEO Miller, 2026-05-05).

## Debate / thesis
- **Bull (standalone fundamentals):** Multiple eval-to-production "inflection points" stacking into 2026–27 — LSA at all three Tier-1 logic customers + a leading HBM supplier, a **second Tier-1 DRAM LSA eval** with potential pilot/HVM orders in 2027, NSA at leading-edge logic for **GAA low-thermal-budget anneal**, and IBD300 at DRAM for bitline films (Q1'26 call). Advanced packaging **more than doubled y/y** on AI; data storage fully booked through 2026 into 2027 on HAMR; silicon-photonics InP order book (>$250M) is a genuine new growth leg. Management frames a >$5B pro-forma SAM and a "very positive WFE environment" into 2027. Internal: **Citi (Atif Malik) Buy, reiterated — 2027E upside on silicon photonics** (briefing, 2026-05-06).
- **Bull read-through from the Aixtron sell-side — Veeco as the MOCVD share-gainer:** Jefferies' downside/risk case on Aixtron explicitly flags **Aixtron losing compound-semi share to Veeco in SiC, GaN and microLED**, with the incumbent's pricing power and margins challenged in that scenario (Jefferies AIXTRON note, 2026-02-10) — a constructive competitive read for Veeco's MOCVD/MBE franchise. The same note frames the structural optoelectronics tailwind Veeco is exposed to: **hyperscaler/datacom capex pull-forward** (Coherent and Lumentum FYQ2'26 capex came in ~35% above expectations, both front-loading into H1'26) lifting InP-laser/optical-tool demand into 2026–27, plus an **800V-HVDC GaN inflection in H2'26/H1'27** ahead of NVIDIA's Kyber rack (H2'27 launch) — supportive of Veeco's >$250M InP-laser MOCVD/wet/IBD order book and ~+50% compound-semi guide. Caveat: the note is about Aixtron and treats Veeco only as competitor and comp, so the read-through is indirect.
- **Bear:** Standalone economics are soft — FY2025 revenue -7%, GM compressed to 40% on mix + tariffs, Q1'26 a GAAP loss. **China was 27% of FY2025 and is in structural decline** (mature-node + BIS license drag). **Heavy customer concentration** — top-10 customers = 68% of receivables; one customer = 17% of FY2025 sales (10-K). Cyclical, sole-source-supplier-dependent, tariff-exposed cost base. As a "second provider" in epitaxy (vs Aixtron) and a niche player vs AMAT/LAM/KLAC, pricing power is limited. Most of the 2026 growth is **second-half weighted and eval-dependent** — execution/timing risk is high.
- **Where the sell-side/buy-side stands:** Effectively a **merger-arb / Axcelis-proxy** equity — the spread and the China-SAMR approval drive the stock more than the print. Internal coverage is thin: Citi (Malik) Buy reiterated (2026-05-06); a "Tom O'Malley updates post-print" mention (briefing, 2026-05-19); and a conference appearance (Jefferies Chicago Semis, Aug 24-25). No internal equity-call notes on the name. On comps, Jefferies' semi-cap table puts **Veeco at 20.0x CY26E / 15.1x CY27E P/E, 2.4x / 2.0x EV/Sales, 16.5x / 9.6x EV/EBITDA, 18.7% CY27E EBIT margin** — the cheapest of the three MOCVD-equipment names (vs Aixtron 30.6x/21.0x P/E, 18.8x/13.3x EV/EBITDA; AMEC far higher) and well below front-end large caps (AMAT/LAM/ASML at ~27–36x CY26E EV/EBITDA) (Jefferies AIXTRON note, 2026-02-10).

## Catalysts / what to watch
- **China SAMR antitrust approval** — the last gate to the Axcelis merger; close guided H2 2026 (drop-dead Sep-30-2026, auto-extendable to Jun-30-2027 for regulatory; VECO termination fee $77.5M, Axcelis $108.7M — 10-K Note 17).
- **Q2 2026 print (~early Aug 2026):** GM recovery toward 38–40%, China trajectory, and progress on the second-half-weighted FY guide.
- **Silicon-photonics InP ramp:** >$250M order book — shipments from Q3'26, Spectre IBD ~10x capacity ramp into early 2027. Watch datacom optics capex (Coherent/Lumentum front-loading H1'26) as the leading indicator (Jefferies AIXTRON note, 2026-02-10).
- **800V-HVDC GaN inflection:** power-semi epi-tool orders expected H2'26/H1'27 ahead of NVIDIA Kyber (H2'27); a swing factor for Veeco's GaN MOCVD exposure and the Aixtron share contest (Jefferies AIXTRON note, 2026-02-10).
- **LSA/NSA eval conversions:** second Tier-1 DRAM LSA → pilot/HVM in 2027; NSA at leading-edge logic for GAA; IBD300 DRAM bitline decisions (extended into 2026).
- **Data storage / HAMR:** first 2026 shipment in Q2; order book into 2027.

## Risks
- **Merger execution / China SAMR (10-K Note 17, 10-Q Note 10):** deal could be delayed or blocked at China antitrust; standalone valuation reverts if it breaks. Management distraction / business disruption during the interim period.
- **China end-market (10-K):** 27% of FY2025, structurally declining on mature-node roll-off + BIS export-license restrictions (cost ~$8M in Q1'26).
- **Customer concentration (10-K):** top-10 = 68% of receivables; one customer 17% of FY2025 sales; small base of large manufacturers.
- **Cyclicality + eval dependence:** WFE-cyclical, second-half-weighted FY26, growth gated on eval-to-production conversions.
- **Supply chain / tariffs (10-K, Q2'25 call):** sole-source components; outsourcing risk; ~100bps tariff GM headwind; cost inflation on imported materials.
- **Competitive position:** niche player vs AMAT/LAM/KLAC; "second provider" in epitaxy vs Aixtron — limited pricing power. (Note the converse risk read: Jefferies frames Veeco as the share-*gainer* against Aixtron in SiC/GaN/microLED — Jefferies AIXTRON note, 2026-02-10.)
