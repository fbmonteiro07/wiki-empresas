## Snapshot
Micron is one of three scaled DRAM makers (with Samsung and SK Hynix) and a top-tier NAND supplier, selling DRAM/NAND/NOR under the Micron and Crucial brands (FY25 10-K, 2025-10-03). DRAM is ~79% of revenue, NAND ~20-21% (Q2 FY26, 2026-03-18). The franchise is reorganized around four data-center-centric BUs: Cloud Memory (CMBU), Core Data Center (CDBU), Mobile & Client (MCBU), and Auto & Embedded (AEBU). The thesis hinges on memory being repositioned "from a system component to a strategic asset" for AI — Micron supplies the full memory hierarchy that agentic inference consumes: HBM next to the accelerator, LPDDR/DDR5/SoCAM expansion memory on the CPU, and high-capacity QLC/TLC data-center SSDs for the growing KV-cache/context tier (Sathya Kumar via UBS Arcuri call, 2026-05-15). It is the #2-3 HBM player behind SK Hynix; only credible third source on HBM4 given Samsung's product issues.

## HBM technology / share (deep dive)
- **MU's HBM positioning:** Micron leapfrogged in HBM — it skipped standard HBM3 entirely — on the back of a focus on TSVs and the power-delivery network, underpinning its claimed ~30% lower power consumption (claim "yet to be verified") (SemiAnalysis, 2025-08-12). Micron and Samsung use Non-Conductive Film (NCF) vs Hynix's proprietary MR-MUF molded underfill. For MU and SK, yield loss on HBM is more than offset by pricing → HBM is margin-accretive; Samsung's worse yields ironically tighten total supply.
- **HBM4 base die — the key watch item:** the HBM4 base die moves to a modern logic process (TSMC N12 and N3 variants). Pure-memory makers Hynix and Micron lack leading-edge logic design capability and will lean on TSMC + IP partners; only Samsung can build the advanced base die in-house. Custom base dies arrive with HBM4 for NVIDIA/AMD, broadening to other accelerators at HBM4E (~2027); they strengthen vendor lock-in because each is tied to a vendor's proprietary TSV layout (SemiAnalysis, 2025-08-12). Caution flag: Jefferies expert (Inspectrum, 2025-12-17) says **Micron is redesigning its HBM4 base die** to reach speed parity — the two Korean players (esp. Samsung) carry higher confidence on higher speeds, i.e. MU has work to do on the new base die.
- **HBM share (UBS estimates, 2026 overview):** MU HBM bit shipment 0.9 / 4.6 / 7.4 bn Gb (2024/25/26E); HBM as % of MU total DRAM bits 1.5% / 6.2% / 8.4%; MU HBM bit-share of the market ~21-22% in 2026E (vs SK Hynix the leader). MU HBM front-end capacity 30 / 65 / 100 k-wpm (4Q, '24/'25/'26E). Samsung's 2025 HBM share ~20%, "will go up next year" (Jefferies/Inspectrum, 2025-12-17). UBS: MU's HBM business alone supports **$5 / $7+ of EPS in 2026/2027** (~17-18% of EPS), justifying a higher multiple on the segment.

## NAND / storage
- **AI finally reaches NAND (MS, 2025-09-10):** incremental ~$29B AI-inference NAND TAM by 2029; AI NAND ≈34% of global NAND market by 2029. Industry shifts from oversupply (since 2022) to **shortage from 2H26** — ~2% base case, up to ~8% in the bull case as nearline (NL) SSD takes share from NL HDD amid HDD capacity constraints. QLC eSSD is the optimal storage tier for AI (high-capacity, randomized I/O). MS prefers SNDK / Korea names directly; read-through to MU is positive but MU is not MS's NAND top pick. Constructive for MU's ~20% NAND mix into the back half of CY26.

## Debate / thesis
- **Bull:** This is a durable up-cycle, not a normal one. DB (Melissa Weathers, Buy) raised PT 2026-06-17: "DRAM tightness extends to 2028+; agentic AI drives memory intensity." GS (Schneider) sees "ongoing tightness through CY27; significant upside to Street estimates" (briefing 2026-06-09). The marquee bull is **UBS Arcuri (Buy, PT $1,625, raised from $535)** on a "path to >$100 EPS," LTAs locking ~30% of industry DDR bits and ~$400bn FCF C27-29E (briefings 2026-05-26 / 2026-05-31). Citi (Atif Malik, Buy, PT $840) re-rated to 8x C27 EPS from 5x (2026-05-19); MS reiterates bull case PT $1,050 (2026-06-04). Mechanism: HBM's 3:1 DDR5 die trade-off + node-driven bit decline + multi-year cleanroom lead times keep supply structurally short while LTAs/SCAs convert the equity into an earnings-durability story (JPM memory-China call, 2026-05-28). The UBS 2026 deck adds two structural legs: HBM cannibalizing DDR wafer capacity keeps DDR undersupplied (DDR GM may surpass HBM GM in early C2026), and the longest memory upcycle in 30 years. CPU upside is incremental fuel — NVIDIA Vera standalone CPU ~500k units this year, ~3M next year, could lift total server DRAM demand >15-20% (JPM/Gokul, 2026-05-28).
- **Bear:** Memory is still a commodity and the cycle always turns. The sharpest bear voice on disk is the **ex-VP Procurement (Micron)**, 2026-06-10: the market is pricing peak earnings as a sustainable level — "a highly catastrophic assumption." His view: HBM differentiation is temporary (once a second/third vendor is qualified at each customer it commoditizes like DRAM), Micron's cost structure is worse than Samsung's and roughly at SK Hynix, and "your $900 stock right now will become a $50 stock again" once new fabs (Micron/SK/Samsung) bring disruptive capacity online ~a year out — possibly normalizing "within this calendar year or early next." Two technical bear reads-through support the "differentiation is temporary" thesis: MU is redesigning its HBM4 base die for speed parity and lacks in-house leading-edge logic for custom base dies (Jefferies/Inspectrum + SemiAnalysis) — Samsung's foundry is a structural edge there. He frames the AI buildout as a bull-whip ("beer game") several layers removed from end demand that can't amortize ~$6-700B of infra spend. Second-order: customers are being forced to qualify Chinese suppliers (CXMT) on cheap DRAM Micron won't supply — "once they're in, they're in." UBS's own desk called the Arcuri PT "the most controversial UBS note recently" (briefing 2026-05-31).

- **F3Q26 print — REPORTED June 24, 2026 (massive beat-and-raise).** Actuals $41.5B rev / 84.9% GM, F4Q26 guide $50B / ~86% / $31 EPS, 16 SCAs disclosed — see Current state. ## Risks
- ASP volatility / cyclicality — 10-K flags "significant volatility in average selling prices" and cash-flow/operating-result volatility that can pressure the credit rating (FY25 10-K).
- Commodity normalization — capacity from Micron/SK/Samsung new fabs landing ~CY27-28 could break the price structure (ex-VP, 2026-06-10).
- Cost-structure disadvantage vs Samsung (geographically dispersed footprint); roughly at SK Hynix (ex-VP, 2026-06-10).
