## Snapshot
Micron is one of three scaled DRAM makers (with Samsung and SK Hynix) and a top-tier NAND supplier, selling DRAM/NAND/NOR under the Micron and Crucial brands (FY25 10-K, 2025-10-03). DRAM is ~79% of revenue, NAND ~20-21% (Q2 FY26, 2026-03-18). The franchise is reorganized around four data-center-centric BUs: Cloud Memory (CMBU), Core Data Center (CDBU), Mobile & Client (MCBU), and Auto & Embedded (AEBU). The thesis hinges on memory being repositioned "from a system component to a strategic asset" for AI — Micron supplies the full memory hierarchy that agentic inference consumes: HBM next to the accelerator, LPDDR/DDR5/SoCAM expansion memory on the CPU, and high-capacity QLC/TLC data-center SSDs for the growing KV-cache/context tier (Sathya Kumar via UBS Arcuri call, 2026-05-15). It is the #2-3 HBM player behind SK Hynix; only credible third source on HBM4 given Samsung's product issues.

**F3Q26 (reported 2026-06-24 AH) — a massive beat-and-raise.** Revenue **$41.5B** (+346% y/y, +74% q/q) vs Street ~$34.7B and JPM $35.6B; non-GAAP gross margin **84.9%** (all-time high, +350bps vs Street 81.4%); DRAM ASPs +63% QoQ, NAND ASPs +87% QoQ (JPMe). Stock **+19% AH to a new ATH** (JPM / Harlan Sur; BofA / Vivek Arya; Barclays / Tom O'Malley; MS / Joseph Moore; UBS / Tim Arcuri; Citi / Atif Malik, 2026-06-25). **Guidance — F4Q26 (August):** revenue **$50B** (+18-23% QoQ per Fubon), GM ~**86%**, EPS **$31** — far above prior Street consensus $24.1 and the buyside bogey of ~$28 (Fubon/Jefferies / Richard Hsia; JPM / Harlan Sur, 2026-06-25).

**16 Strategic Customer Agreements (SCAs) signed (from 1 last quarter) — the marquee development** (MU F3Q26 call, 2026-06-24; Barclays / Tom O'Malley, 2026-06-25): non-cancelable, take-or-pay; mostly 5-year terms (CY26–CY30), auto customers 3-year. 4 large customers, 3 medium, remainder smaller auto. Memory market outlook from the Q3 deck: DRAM shipments 2026 +20-25% (above prior), NAND +~20% (in line); tight beyond 2027 (@ParadisLabs, 2026-06-24).

**Prior quarter — Q2 FY26 (reported 2026-03-18) — a blowout.** Revenue $23.9B, +75% q/q, +196% y/y (4th straight record). DRAM $18.8B (+74% q/q), pricing up "mid-sixties percent" sequentially; NAND $5.0B (+82% q/q), pricing up "high-seventies percent." **Capacity-constrained — hardest to add wafer (StoneX/TrendForce "Memory expert call — Avril", 2026-06-23):** Micron is "the only company having extreme difficulty adding wafer capacity in 2026," holding ~flat at **~365K wafers/month** (vs Samsung ~740K, SK Hynix ~610K). 2027/28 growth comes from **Idaho 1 & 2 (~50-60K each, longer US construction lead times)** plus the **acquired PSMC Taiwan fab (~40K) transitioning to Micron equipment with real output H2 2027**; reaching ~440K by end-2027. Critically, Micron **grows faster than its Korean peers in 2028 (+21% YoY)** off the new greenfield. On HBM, end-2026 wafer cap **~100K, with risk of a drop to ~80K if HBM4 qualification issues persist** — though the analyst frames a weak HBM4 as paradoxically good near-term, freeing wafer back to higher-margin conventional DRAM (where Micron's Taiwan fabs run 24/7, driving a "staggering 42%" supply bit-growth) (same source).

**Price outlier — pushes the highest ASPs (StoneX/TrendForce "Memory expert call — Avril", 2026-06-23):** the analyst flags **Micron as the likely price outlier** in QoQ negotiations — constrained capacity means it naturally pushes for the highest ASPs, "and if Micron succeeds, the two Korean makers will follow through" for market-share-competition reasons. (Her base-case DRAM price view is the market's most conservative: Q3 +8-13% / Q4 +3-8%, with gut-feel upside to Q3 >+20% and Q4 +10-15% — vs Conor's Redburn checks of 30-40% QoQ in both Q3 and Q4.)

**Anthropic–Micron deal (StoneX/TrendForce "Memory expert call — Avril", 2026-06-23):** the announced partnership is a **combination of server DRAM + enterprise SSD (2027) plus HBM (2028)**; the HBM leg "means Anthropic wants to have its own in-house ASIC." Anthropic is pursuing the same deal structure with SK Hynix and Samsung.

**Structural setup (Q1 FY26, 2025-12-17):** entire CY26 HBM supply — including HBM4 — sold out on price and volume. HBM TAM ~$35B (2025) → ~$100B by 2028 (~40% CAGR), pulled in two years vs prior. CY26 industry DRAM and NAND bit-shipment growth capped at ~20% by supply; Micron grows in line. 1γ DRAM and G9 NAND become majority bit mix by mid-CY26. First five-year Strategic Customer Agreement (SCA) signed — multiyear, specific volume/price commitments, distinct from the old one-year LTAs; in discussion with multiple customers. JPM corroborated at its TMC conference that MU emphasized supply tightness "well beyond CY26," extended undersupply through CY27, secured its first **five-year DRAM SCA** with multiple additional customers in active negotiation (incl. NAND SCAs), and that **HBM4 production is ramping ~2x faster than the HBM3E ramp**, with 1γ DRAM (EUV-based) and G9 NAND on track to be the majority bit mix by mid-2026 (JPM "Conf feedback", 2026-05-22). UBS (Arcuri) corroborates MU "sold out on HBM3E/HBM4 volumes in C2026," with industry DDR/NAND undersupply running into C1Q27 / C4Q26 respectively (UBS 2026 overview); DeepResearch TSM cross-checks Micron HBM3E "fully booked for 2026" (Sep-23-2025).

**Pricing / cycle (UBS 2026 overview, Arcuri):** the current memory upcycle "could turn out to be the longest of the last 30 years" — HBM consumes enough wafer capacity that traditional DDR wafers are flat-to-declining, structurally undersupplying DDR. UBS models DRAM contract pricing +35%/+30% q/q in C4Q25/C1Q26 and NAND +20%/+20%, with customers likely accepting double-digit sequential ASP increases through C2026. Key call: MU's DDR (ex-HBM) gross margin likely **surpasses HBM GM for the first time in early C2026** as DDR tightens. Into the June print, Arcuri's incremental tell was that the market is fixated on DDR but **HBM pricing is "what is really surprising to the upside"** — and that the LTAs (not pricing) are the re-rating catalyst; MU itself told him after the PT raise "we think the stock should trade at a higher multiple and you're finally the one that opened this debate" (UBS "Arcuri call", 2026-06-15). JPM separately models blended HBM ASP **+~30% for 2026** (~10% like-for-like + mix from HBM4) — but with HBM4 ramp pushed to 2H, more of the ASP effect spills into 2027; HBM price negotiations only begin July after the June quarter (JPM "Memory", 2026-05-28). Jefferies expert checks (Inspectrum, 2025-12-17) add HBM contract prices are signed and better than feared — HBM4 ASP a ~60% premium vs prior assumption of HBM3E declining ~20%; HBM industry bit production ~+50% in 2026 (≈20bn→30bn 1Gb-eq). 2026 WFE intentions skew to DRAM (the strongest segment) over NAND, with NAND capacity roughly flat (Jefferies/TEL, 2025-12-17).

## HBM technology / share (deep dive)
- **MU's HBM positioning:** Micron leapfrogged in HBM — it skipped standard HBM3 entirely — on the back of a focus on TSVs and the power-delivery network, underpinning its claimed ~30% lower power consumption (claim "yet to be verified") (SemiAnalysis, 2025-08-12). Micron and Samsung use Non-Conductive Film (NCF) vs Hynix's proprietary MR-MUF molded underfill. For MU and SK, yield loss on HBM is more than offset by pricing → HBM is margin-accretive; Samsung's worse yields ironically tighten total supply.
- **HBM4 base die — the key watch item:** the HBM4 base die moves to a modern logic process (TSMC N12 and N3 variants). Pure-memory makers Hynix and Micron lack leading-edge logic design capability and will lean on TSMC + IP partners; only Samsung can build the advanced base die in-house. Custom base dies arrive with HBM4 for NVIDIA/AMD, broadening to other accelerators at HBM4E (~2027); they strengthen vendor lock-in because each is tied to a vendor's proprietary TSV layout (SemiAnalysis, 2025-08-12). Caution flag: Jefferies expert (Inspectrum, 2025-12-17) says **Micron is redesigning its HBM4 base die** to reach speed parity — the two Korean players (esp. Samsung) carry higher confidence on higher speeds, i.e. MU has work to do on the new base die.
- **HBM share (UBS estimates, 2026 overview):** MU HBM bit shipment 0.9 / 4.6 / 7.4 bn Gb (2024/25/26E); HBM as % of MU total DRAM bits 1.5% / 6.2% / 8.4%; MU HBM bit-share of the market ~21-22% in 2026E (vs SK Hynix the leader). MU HBM front-end capacity 30 / 65 / 100 k-wpm (4Q, '24/'25/'26E). Samsung's 2025 HBM share ~20%, "will go up next year" (Jefferies/Inspectrum, 2025-12-17). UBS: MU's HBM business alone supports **$5 / $7+ of EPS in 2026/2027** (~17-18% of EPS), justifying a higher multiple on the segment.

## NAND / storage
- **AI finally reaches NAND (MS, 2025-09-10):** incremental ~$29B AI-inference NAND TAM by 2029; AI NAND ≈34% of global NAND market by 2029. Industry shifts from oversupply (since 2022) to **shortage from 2H26** — ~2% base case, up to ~8% in the bull case as nearline (NL) SSD takes share from NL HDD amid HDD capacity constraints. QLC eSSD is the optimal storage tier for AI (high-capacity, randomized I/O). MS prefers SNDK / Korea names directly; read-through to MU is positive but MU is not MS's NAND top pick. Constructive for MU's ~20% NAND mix into the back half of CY26.

## Debate / thesis
- **Bull:** This is a durable up-cycle, not a normal one. DB (Melissa Weathers, Buy) raised PT 2026-06-17: "DRAM tightness extends to 2028+; agentic AI drives memory intensity." GS (Schneider) sees "ongoing tightness through CY27; significant upside to Street estimates" (briefing 2026-06-09). The marquee bull is **UBS Arcuri (Buy, PT $1,625, raised from $535)** on a "path to >$100 EPS," LTAs locking ~30% of industry DDR bits and ~$400bn FCF C27-29E (briefings 2026-05-26 / 2026-05-31). Citi (Atif Malik, Buy, PT $840) re-rated to 8x C27 EPS from 5x (2026-05-19); MS reiterates bull case PT $1,050 (2026-06-04). Mechanism: HBM's 3:1 DDR5 die trade-off + node-driven bit decline + multi-year cleanroom lead times keep supply structurally short while LTAs/SCAs convert the equity into an earnings-durability story (JPM memory-China call, 2026-05-28). The UBS 2026 deck adds two structural legs: HBM cannibalizing DDR wafer capacity keeps DDR undersupplied (DDR GM may surpass HBM GM in early C2026), and the longest memory upcycle in 30 years. CPU upside is incremental fuel — NVIDIA Vera standalone CPU ~500k units this year, ~3M next year, could lift total server DRAM demand >15-20% (JPM/Gokul, 2026-05-28).
- **Bear:** Memory is still a commodity and the cycle always turns. The sharpest bear voice on disk is the **ex-VP Procurement (Micron)**, 2026-06-10: the market is pricing peak earnings as a sustainable level — "a highly catastrophic assumption." His view: HBM differentiation is temporary (once a second/third vendor is qualified at each customer it commoditizes like DRAM), Micron's cost structure is worse than Samsung's and roughly at SK Hynix, and "your $900 stock right now will become a $50 stock again" once new fabs (Micron/SK/Samsung) bring disruptive capacity online ~a year out — possibly normalizing "within this calendar year or early next." Two technical bear reads-through support the "differentiation is temporary" thesis: MU is redesigning its HBM4 base die for speed parity and lacks in-house leading-edge logic for custom base dies (Jefferies/Inspectrum + SemiAnalysis) — Samsung's foundry is a structural edge there. He frames the AI buildout as a bull-whip ("beer game") several layers removed from end demand that can't amortize ~$6-700B of infra spend. Second-order: customers are being forced to qualify Chinese suppliers (CXMT) on cheap DRAM Micron won't supply — "once they're in, they're in." UBS's own desk called the Arcuri PT "the most controversial UBS note recently" (briefing 2026-05-31).
- **Where the sell-side stands:** Near-unanimous bullish — DB Buy (raised PT, 2026-06-17), GS PT up (2026-06-09), MS PT $1,050 (2026-06-04), UBS Arcuri Buy $1,625 (2026-05-26/31), Citi Buy $840 (2026-05-19), Susquehanna $1,750 (2026-05-30), JPM OW (TMC recap, 2026-05-21). MU +30% on the week and +9.9% in a single session into June (briefings). The debate is no longer direction but duration — the bull case explicitly underwrites tightness "beyond CY26" / into 2028; the bear case is that this is a textbook top.

- **Longevity-of-cycle re-rating (GS, Junie Yoon):** GS's core argument is that the **longevity of the cycle is underestimated** — Hynix/Samsung (and by read-across MU) trading at 5-6x earnings says the market doesn't believe the durability; GS's own 9x target is "conservative" vs semis peers with similar margins/moats trading high-teens-to-20x. The downside-protection math: even a **bear case of DRAM pricing −30% in 2027 then −30% in 2028 (or −50% in 2028) still leaves DRAM gross margin ~40%** — supporting an earnings-based multiple. The lower sharp-downturn risk vs prior cycles is because demand visibility (esp. hyperscaler) lets suppliers spend capex efficiently rather than overbuild (GS "Memory", 2026-06-08).
- **DRAM > NAND, MU the cheaper 2028 play (MS, Joe Moore):** MS frames the fundamentals "10 out of 10 for DRAM, maybe 8/10 for NAND" and is **more bullish on Micron than SanDisk** — DRAM is the primary AI bottleneck (NVIDIA halved LPDDR5 content in Vera Rubin racks because there isn't enough memory; servers shipping "significantly nerfed" on DRAM, so the DRAM shortage is worse than the CPU shortage). MS views Micron as "a much cheaper stock on 2028 numbers than SanDisk" after SNDK's big move; the LTA disclosure gap (Micron honoring customer confidentiality) may persist but doesn't worry MS. Apple CEO Tim Cook (WSJ) called memory pricing a "100-year flood" (MS "Memory & semicap", 2026-06-22).

- Additional SCA signings / disclosure of structure (floor pricing, cancellation terms) — the re-rating catalyst.
- Capacity: Idaho fab 1 first wafers mid-CY27 (pulled in); Idaho 2 by 2028; New York groundbreaking early CY26; Singapore HBM packaging contributing CY27, NAND fab CY28.
- NAND shortage inflection from 2H26 (MS, 2025-09-10) — eSSD/NL SSD displacement of HDD as a CY26-27 pricing catalyst for the NAND book.
- EUV / tool availability as a 2028 supply governor (JPM: ASML ~90 tools end-CY27 insufficient — Samsung/Hynix each need ~20-25, Micron ~10-15, TSMC already ~40; Micron only recently adopting EUV at 1γ, with EUV reserved for new fabs post-Idaho (Idaho ph.2 / Hiroshima / New York), and if MU can't get EUV by 2028 it would prioritize 1γ for HBM — JPM "Memory", 2026-05-28). UBS frames the same governor more benignly: ASML not a bottleneck until ~110 physical slots (~$225B WFE); ASML is getting the same 8-10-quarter visibility as US toolmakers, so supply is not the limiter (UBS "Arcuri call", 2026-06-15).

## Risks
- ASP volatility / cyclicality — 10-K flags "significant volatility in average selling prices" and cash-flow/operating-result volatility that can pressure the credit rating (FY25 10-K).
- Commodity normalization — capacity from Micron/SK/Samsung new fabs landing ~CY27-28 could break the price structure (ex-VP, 2026-06-10).
- Cost-structure disadvantage vs Samsung (geographically dispersed footprint); roughly at SK Hynix (ex-VP, 2026-06-10).
- HBM4 base-die execution — MU redesigning the base die for speed parity and dependent on TSMC/IP partners for advanced logic; Samsung's in-house foundry is an edge (Jefferies/Inspectrum 2025-12-17; SemiAnalysis 2025-08-12).
- Geopolitics/regulation — export restrictions, tariffs, trade measures; China DRAM substitution via CXMT gaining low-end LPDDR share (10-K; JPM 2026-05-28).
- HBM share/competition — SK Hynix leadership (NVIDIA multiyear tech partnership), Samsung HBM4 qualifying and ~20% share rising (Jefferies/Inspectrum 2025-12-17); customers dual-source by design.
- Demand elasticity in price-sensitive PC/smartphone/consumer segments at elevated ASPs (Mehrotra, 2025-12-17).
