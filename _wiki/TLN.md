## Snapshot
Talen is a merchant independent power producer (IPP) repositioning itself as the "powering-AI" infrastructure play in PJM. ~13.1 GW of generation, of which **2.2 GW is nuclear** at the single Susquehanna plant (PPL zone, PA); the balance is a dispatchable fossil fleet — CCGTs, intermediate/peaking gas (Montour, Martins Creek) — principally in Mid-Atlantic, Ohio and Montana (TLN 10-K, 2026-02-26). It sells energy, capacity and ancillaries into wholesale markets and is converting merchant assets into contracted infrastructure via long-term data-center PPAs. Anchor contract: the **front-of-the-meter ~1.9 GW Amazon/AWS PPA at Susquehanna** (revamped/doubled and moved FOTM in June 2025; AA-credit counterparty). The investor framing is the "Talen flywheel" — contract assets, acquire accretively, recycle capital, repeat — driving free-cash-flow-per-share growth (Q4 FY25, 2026-02-26).

## Current state (latest quarter)
Q1 FY26 (2026-05-05): **$473M adjusted EBITDA, $350M adjusted FCF** — EBITDA more than doubled and FCF roughly quadrupled YoY on the Freedom & Guernsey acquisitions (~2.8 GW CCGT, closed late Nov 2025), higher spark spreads, capacity/RMR revenues live since June 2025, and the ongoing AWS ramp (Cole Muller, Q1 FY26). Fleet generated ~16 TWh at a 55% capacity factor; Susquehanna Unit 1 refueling outage completed efficiently and re-synced to grid (Terry Nutt, Q1 FY26).

2026 guidance reaffirmed: **adj. EBITDA $1.75–2.05bn, adj. FCF $0.98–1.18bn** — and explicitly excludes the pending Cornerstone acquisition (3 plants, OH/IN; ~$500M run-rate EBITDA, >$4/sh incremental FCF). Cornerstone close expected "as soon as this summer" pending FERC §203 (HSR cleared March; Indiana order pending) (Q1 FY26).

Balance sheet / capital returns: net leverage 3.1x at 3/31, target <3.5x. Pre-funded Cornerstone with **$4bn senior unsecured notes at a blended ~6.25%**, and retired $1.2bn of 8.625% senior secured notes — ~$40M/yr interest savings (~$1/sh FCF), cutting secured debt from ~60% to ~30% of total and lifting ratings (Cole Muller, Q1 FY26). **$2bn buyback authorization through 2028**; $100M repurchased in Q1; mgmt frames buying "at scale over time." Prior buybacks: ~$2bn / ~24% of float at ~$149 avg over 2.5 yrs (Terry Nutt, Q4 FY25).

Preliminary outlook (incl. Cornerstone, marks to 3/31): **~$34/sh FCF in 2027, ~$36 in 2028 base; ~$41 in 2028 with 70% of FCF to buybacks** — a 30% lift vs. January, implying ~11% FCF yield. Mgmt flags ~$5/MWh of further spark-spread appreciation already realized since 3/31 not in the numbers (Cole Muller, Q1 FY26).

## Debate / thesis
- **Bull (mgmt + the structural setup):** Demand growth is real and supply is static — "value of steel in the ground." Q1 PJM weather-adjusted deliveries +3% YoY; PPL zone peak load projected +70% over five years, AEP +30% (Q4/Q1 FY26). Susquehanna's ~1.9 GW AWS PPA gives an AA-credit, long-dated contracted base (35% of gross margin contracted long-term; each incremental 1 GW PPA adds ~15pts, next deal could reach ~50%). FCF/sh compounding via the flywheel, with multiple un-modeled upside levers (spark spreads, AWS acceleration, new 1 GW PPAs, accretive M&A — each "10%+" to FCF/sh). Capacity tailwind: last BRA would have cleared >$500 absent the $330 cap, and the price collar is being extended for two more auctions (Mac McFarland / Terry Nutt, Q4 FY25).
- **Bear / risks to thesis:** Single-asset nuclear concentration (one plant, Susquehanna) and merchant commodity exposure — spark spreads and a widening, possibly non-fundamental West Hub–PPL zonal basis swing FCF/sh meaningfully (Chris Morice, Q1 FY26). Regulatory overhang: PJM co-location tariff still unsettled (FERC instructed PJM on allowable configs Dec 2025; PJM filed Feb 23, 2026), and the Reliability Backstop / RVP design, cost allocation and "fair share" of data-center load remain open (10-K; Q4/Q1 FY26). The **Montour zoning denial** punctured the data-center development narrative — mgmt now refuses to discuss the pipeline publicly, reducing visibility. New-build economics don't clear on a merchant basis ("the gap is pretty large," Cole Muller, Q1 FY26) — growth needs either a 15-yr RVP award or a hyperscaler offtake. Leverage rising into Cornerstone; financing pre-funded ahead of regulatory close (carry cost, execution risk).
**Deck modeling detail (Investor Update, 2025-09-09 — original Cornerstone-excluded outlook bridge; FCF/sh figures since superseded by the Q1 FY26 marks above, but the unit economics below remain the cleanest disclosed):**
- **AWS PPA = ~$18bn of nominal cash flows over the 17-year contract at the *minimum* ramp** — the long-dated annuity behind the "contracted infrastructure" reframe (Investor Update, 2025-09-09).
- **AWS volume ramp (minimum):** ~**240 MW mid-2026 → 360 MW mid-2027 → 480 MW mid-2028**, scaling to **840–1,200 MW by 2029** and the full **1,680–1,920 MW by ~2032**; AWS can accelerate to the full 1,920 MW with notice (Investor Update, 2025-09-09).
- **AWS PPA incremental Adj FCF/sh:** ~**$2.00 in 2029, ~$2.50 in 2032** on top of base generation — implying total Adj FCF/sh of **$4.00–5.75 in 2029 and $7.00–8.25 in 2032** (vs ~$1.55 base impact in 2026). PPA carries a shortfall "make-whole": if AWS draws no power, the floor payment is **~50–65% of the full impact** — a hard downside cap on the anchor contract (Investor Update, 2025-09-09).
- **Power-price torque (as of 7/31/25):** fleet **~50% hedged 2026E, ~25% 2027E**; a +$10/MWh move adds **~$230mm of margin in 2026 / ~$385mm in 2027** (−$210mm / −$350mm on the downside) — quantifies the merchant beta the bears flag (Investor Update, 2025-09-09).
- **Capacity torque:** a **$50/MWd swing in the '27/'28 BRA = ~$90mm** of 2027 capacity revenue; across both '27/'28 and '28/'29 it is **~$160mm** to 2028. MAAC capacity volumes ~8,670–8,775 MW, priced ~$301–329/MWd (Investor Update, 2025-09-09).
- **'27/'28 BRA setup:** PJM parameters embed **~6 GW of incremental demand vs only ~4 GW of expected incremental supply** — mechanically tighter than the '26/'27 auction; company assumes '27/'28 clears at the ~$329/MWd cap (Investor Update, 2025-09-09).
- **Fleet granularity (pro-forma 13,191 MW):** Susquehanna **2,228 MW, 90%-owned**, running **93% CF in 2026E / 96% in 2027E** and ~**32% of total fleet generation**; pending Guernsey **1,836 MW (OH, COD 2023)** and Freedom **1,045 MW (PA, COD 2018)** are among the lowest-heat-rate plants in the PJM stack. Coal generation is **required to cease at Brunner Island (1,429 MW), Keystone and Conemaugh by Dec 2028** — a known capacity roll-off to model (Investor Update, 2025-09-09).
- **Balance-sheet starting point:** pre-Cornerstone deck modeled **~$3.8bn of acquisition debt at 6.3–7.3%**, pro-forma net debt **~$6.56bn**, net leverage **<3.5x by YE2026**; ~**45.7mm share** count assumed throughout the per-share bridge (Investor Update, 2025-09-09).

- **Where the sell-side/buy-side stands:** No PTs/ratings captured on disk (no briefings, no equity calls). Sell-side engagement on the calls skews constructive — Q&A pressure is on (i) whether new gen must be 1:1 with new load (mgmt: no, it's a "fifty-hour" adequacy problem solved by batteries/CTs, not baseload), (ii) the basis dislocation, and (iii) buyback cadence and Cornerstone timing (Q1 FY26 Q&A). Treat all PT/consensus figures as unavailable in-file.

**Behind-the-meter read-through (expert calls, 2026):** the BTM/co-location question at the center of TLN's thesis is sharpened by two expert calls. A behind-the-meter power expert (ex-GE Vernova) argues **BTM is a *permanent* solution, not a bridge** (grid reconnection raises public rates and ISOs "pick their battles"), and a midstream desk's DC expert puts **BTM at ~40% of power deals**, with post-grid-connect BTM later running ~15–30% capacity for peak-shaving (Wells Fargo "Aman Chakri BTM expert", 2026-05-27; WFC "midstream Friday wrap", 2026-06-05). A DC broker frames ~**30% of his pipeline as true grid-islanded BTM** and notes counterparty credit (big-four vs. Oracle/neocloud) gates contracting as much as power (JPM DC broker call, 2026-05-04). Read-through: supportive of TLN's "value of steel in the ground" but a reminder that the FOTM/co-location regulatory path (TLN's forced pivot) remains the swing factor.

**Macro / regulatory read-through (JPM "AI Capex 2.0", 2026-06-16):** JPM cites Talen's Susquehanna directly as the cautionary case on behind-the-meter co-location regulatory risk — **FERC's 2024 rejection of the first-of-its-kind Talen Susquehanna/AWS BTM co-location agreement**, after which Talen **pivoted to a front-of-meter arrangement to avoid further FERC scrutiny** (the structure embedded in the current ~1.9 GW AWS PPA). JPM's deal table logs both **Talen–Amazon Susquehanna expanded PPA, 1.92 GW, 20-yr ending 2042 (6/11/25)** and the original **960 MW (3/4/24)**. Backdrop is supportive: JPM now models **138 GW US DC capacity growth through 2030** (up from 122 GW) within **$5.5tn AI capex**, with **power the binding constraint** (7-yr interconnection queues, 3–5 yr gas-turbine lead times) — hence hyperscaler "all-of-the-above" / BYOG / build-to-transfer workarounds. PJM-region detail in the report aligns with TLN's PPL-zone leverage (JPM "AI Capex 2.0", 2026-06-16).

## Catalysts / what to watch
- **Cornerstone close** — FERC §203 + Indiana order, expected this summer; triggers 2026 guidance reset (+~$4/sh FCF, ~$500M EBITDA run-rate).
- **PJM RVP / Reliability Backstop** rules — PJM signaled timing pulled to **this fall**; pay-as-bid construct and 15-yr awards are the key unlock for new-build financing.
- **FERC co-location tariff** finalization (PJM filing 2026-02-23) — governs how large load attaches to existing generation.
- **Next 1 GW+ PPA signing** — the swing factor to ~50% contracted gross margin; mgmt says delivery wouldn't start before ~2028 regardless of signing date.
- **Buyback acceleration** post-Cornerstone as leverage trends below 3.5x; capacity auction (BRA) outcomes under the extended price collar.

## Risks
- Single nuclear plant (Susquehanna) — outage/operational concentration; refueling cadence drives quarterly swings.
- Merchant commodity exposure: power, spark spreads, fuel; West Hub–PPL zonal basis widening (mgmt calls it temporal, but it's in the marks).
- Regulatory: PJM RVP/co-location/cost-allocation uncertainty; "fair share" of data-center cost still undefined.
- Concentration in one off-taker (AWS) for the anchor contracted cash flows.
- Leverage/financing: rising debt into Cornerstone, pre-funded at ~6.25%; interest-rate and capital-markets access risk on future PPAs requiring credit support.
- Development execution: Montour-style zoning/permitting setbacks; new-build only viable with offtake or RVP award.
