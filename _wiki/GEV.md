## Snapshot
GE Vernova is the GE spin-out (April 2024) holding the legacy electric-power franchise across three segments: **Power** (gas turbines, nuclear/SMR, hydro, steam), **Electrification** (grid transmission, HVDC, transformers, switchgear, power conversion/storage, grid software), and **Wind** (onshore + offshore). It is the central pure-play on the AI-driven electricity build-out: data-center load needs both new generation (Gas Power) and grid hardware (Electrification). FY26 revenue guided to ~$44.5–45.5B (Q1 FY26, 2026-04-22). Power is the cash/earnings anchor, Electrification is the emerging growth story, Wind is a managed drag. Largest installed base of gas/steam turbines + generators of any OEM globally (Q1 FY26 transcript), giving a structural services annuity. FY26 revenue now guided to **$45.5–46.5B** and FY26 FCF to **$11.5–12.5B** (Q2 FY26, 2026-07-22 — both raised; see Current state).

## Risks
- **Order/demand cyclicality** — long-cycle backlog exposed to a capex/AI-spend reversal; orders peak narrative live. Barclays notes the ~4 GW of "surge" capacity (7E France + Greenville weekend shifts) can be removed quickly if demand softens (Powering AI — Gas Turbines, 2026-01-21).
- **Supply chain / capacity constraints** — castings & forgings (Howmet/PCP/CPP) are the true bottleneck; manufacturing capacity constraints flagged as a risk factor (10-K 2026-01-29). Turbine blades/vanes require rare-earth/superalloy inputs (yttrium, rhenium, cobalt, nickel) with single-crystal casting overlap with aerospace — GEV is stockpiling yttrium with the US govt (Powering AI — Gas Turbines, 2026-01-21).
- **Fixed-price / large-project execution** — liquidated damages and unanticipated expense if specs/schedules missed, explicitly cited for Wind component delivery (10-K 2026-01-29).
- **Offshore Wind** — US offshore activity halted Dec 22; Haliade-X program loss-making; Vineyard/Dogger Bank execution risk into 2027 (Q4 FY25; 10-K).
- **Tariffs (Section 232)** — $250–350M net '26 impact; Belfort-imported turbines and Prolec exposed (Q1 FY26).
- **Customer/geographic concentration & commodity prices** — macro deterioration, decarbonization-policy shifts (10-K risk factors).
- **Geopolitical** — Middle East exposure; LNG-to-power projects in Vietnam (4.8 GW) where one project is being weighed against renewables (Q1 FY26).
- **Competitive capacity build / new entrants** — Siemens Energy ramping to 30+ GW avg FY28-30 and entrants (Doosan, FTAI, ProEnergy, Boom) adding aero/large-frame supply could erode GEV's scarcity-driven ~20–30% US price premium over time (Powering AI — Gas Turbines, 2026-01-21).
