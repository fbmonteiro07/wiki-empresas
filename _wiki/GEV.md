## Snapshot
GE Vernova is the GE spin-out (April 2024) holding the legacy electric-power franchise across three segments: **Power** (gas turbines, nuclear/SMR, hydro, steam), **Electrification** (grid transmission, HVDC, transformers, switchgear, power conversion/storage, grid software), and **Wind** (onshore + offshore). It is the central pure-play on the AI-driven electricity build-out: data-center load needs both new generation (Gas Power) and grid hardware (Electrification). FY26 revenue guided to ~$44.5–45.5B (Q1 FY26, 2026-04-22). Power is the cash/earnings anchor, Electrification is the emerging growth story, Wind is a managed drag. Largest installed base of gas/steam turbines + generators of any OEM globally (Q1 FY26 transcript), giving a structural services annuity.

| **Main buyer(s)** | Utilities/IPPs (~80% of gas) + data-center/hyperscalers (~20% and the growth driver — DC orders $2.4B in Q1 alone) |

## Debate / thesis
- **Bull:** The stock is an **earnings-revisions machine** — "GE is going up off the back of revisions… last year up 100% because consensus was up 100%" and PMs are buying it valuation-insensitive on pace of EBITDA growth (GEVernova callback, 2026-04-22). The biggest narrative shift over the last 3–6 months is **Electrification** flipping from "peaking" to accelerating: management doubled its TAM from $150B (Strazik, London, mid-March) to $300B and pulled forward the $60B+ Electrification backlog / $200B total backlog target to '27 (callback, 2026-04-22). Gas Power supply-chain bottleneck (Howmet/PCP/Doncasters/CPP castings) appears to have eased — GEV "wrote a check," is getting capacity first vs. Siemens/MHI, commands a **~20–30% US price premium** vs. ENR, and may raise the 24 GW production target (GEVernova preview, 2026-04-17). Data-center power thesis backed by Jefferies (James Snyder) initiating **Buy, 2028E EPS $6.35** (briefing 2026-06-01). Hard-asset/AI-grid positioning — Tiger top-10 holding ~$1.03B (briefing 2026-05-16). US installed only 13 GW of gas in all of 2025 vs. GEV signing ~18 GW over two quarters — demand is "rather dramatic" (callback, 2026-04-22).
  - **Scarcity / pricing power confirmed (Barclays Powering AI — Gas Turbines, 2026-01-21):** large-frame turbines are effectively **sold out for 2028 delivery, with only ~10 GW left for 2029, and GEV expects to be largely sold out for 2030 by end-2026** — the capacity-constrained backdrop that lets GEV push price and protect high-margin aftermarket. Barclays frames the 16→20 GW (3Q26) → 24 GW (2028) ramp as **prudent / "surge"-heavy**: 2 GW is older 7E tech in France with no capex, 2 GW is F/HA out of Greenville via weekend shifts under the new labor contract, and only 4 GW is genuine new-machine capacity (400 machines, 200 in '25 + 200 in '26) ramping into '28 — i.e., ~4 GW of "surge" could be pulled quickly in a downturn. **Slot-reservation deposits are now ~25% (vs. 5–10% in prior cycles)**, with OEMs vetting project funding/permitting/gas/interconnect/EPC before converting SRAs to firm — these down-payments are what drove GEV's Q1 FCF surge. Concrete pricing read: KU/LG&E paid GEV a **$25mm reservation fee** to lock a slot + firm pricing for the 645-MW Brown 12 CCGT (~$2,100/kW, 2030) vs. ~$1,400/kW for its 640-MW Mill Creek 5 (2027) — illustrating both rising $/kW and the new slot-fee regime. GEV is also working with the US government to **stockpile yttrium** (turbine-blade superalloy input), de-risking the casting bottleneck.
  - **DC pipeline pull (Barclays Powering AI — 45 GW US DC Pipeline, 2025-10-31):** of ~45+ GW of large US data-center projects tracked (>$2.5tn of US spend at ~$55mm/MW), GEV equipment anchors several — **Homer City Energy Campus (PA): 7 GEV HA-class turbines** for a 4.4 GW coal-to-gas DC campus (first power '27); **Crusoe ordered 29 GEV aeroderivative turbines (~1 GW, ~35 MW/unit)** for Stargate-adjacent sites, with ~840 MW still unallocated across its WY/TX pipeline; Stargate 1 (Abilene) deploys 5 GEV LM2500 aeros. Read-through to multi-year GEV gas + grid demand.
- **Bear:** **Order-peak risk** — Q4'25's 24 GW / $22.2B order quarter is the comp to beat; post-analyst-day, hedge funds called the desk arguing "this is the peak, there was pull-forward" (callback, 2026-04-22). Buy-side survey skews cautious: most expected Q1 orders only "slightly above consensus, not a blow-out," and only ~12% saw Section 232 as "impactful" — i.e., bull case largely embedded (preview, 2026-04-17). **Wind "is the gift that keeps on giving"** — surprise Q2 EBITDA loss guide of $200–300M (desk modeled ~$50M); US halted all offshore wind activity Dec 22 (Q4 FY25, 2026-01-28). **Pricing-metric ambiguity** — the 10–20 pts $/kW figure ignores mix (peakers/aeros distort it); desk "not quite sure what's going on" (callback). Conversion of SRAs to firm orders gated by **permitting/EPC/interconnect**, not money — 2030 slots filling ahead of 2029 (callback). Section 232: F-class turbines imported from Belfort, France would face 25% on full value; tariff net impact guided $250–350M for '26 (Q1 FY26 + preview).
  - **Capacity / lead-time bear nuance (Barclays Powering AI — Gas Turbines, 2026-01-21):** GW headlines overstate near-term power delivery — large-frame turbines take **another ~18–24 months from shipment to commercial operation** (a 7HA.03 2x1 combined-cycle unit, ~1,282 MW, runs ~24 months), so the majority of OEM capacity brought online in 2028 may not generate power until **2030+**. Gas-turbine demand is also **not all AI** — international (Asia/ME/Africa), coal-retirement replacements, O&G, peaking-to-firm-renewables, and aftermarket are large; aeroderivative supply directly competes with aerospace jet-engine capacity (GE/GEV/BKR field-use carve-outs notwithstanding). And it's an OEM-wide ramp: Siemens Energy (17→22 GW avg FY25-27, 30+ GW avg FY28-30, some backed by fixed off-take) plus new entrants (Doosan 12 units/yr ≈4.5 GW by '28 incl. 5 xAI units; FTAI 100+ aero conversions/yr) chip at GEV's scarcity premium over time.
- **Expert-call read-through (turbine-market dynamics):** A gas-power expert (Norbert Neuster, ex-Cummins Power Systems) frames the turbine setup that underpins GEV's pricing power — **gas is the only viable non-grid option for 2026–28 builds** (SMR/geothermal "not ready") and turbine OEMs are "everybody's sold out, lead times really, really long." On the OEM field: **MTU/Rolls-Royce is "back with a vengeance,"** recovering lost share — i.e., GEV's scarcity premium faces a recovering #3; **Caterpillar is shifting build slots from diesel to gas** (gas accrues aftermarket hours/revenue), and new entrants (Generac/Weichai on diesel; Doosan/Kael/Yanmar on larger engines) are arriving — all consistent with GEV's "order-peak / new-entrant" bear nuance. He expects equipment pricing to **come back down over the next 2–3 yrs** as supply catches up (Wells Fargo "Norbert Neuster turbine/genset expert", 2026-04-10). Separately, a DataCloud-Cannes recap relayed a cynical industry answer to **why GEV can't sign long-term framework agreements with regulated utilities** — a regulated utility that locks in low turbine pricing "looks bad" vs. forward-looking peers on EPS, and risks a prudency clawback if costs later fall (Cantor/Obin DataCloud, 2026-06-08) — color on the gating of GEV's 30–35 framework-agreement pipeline.
- **Where the sell-side/buy-side stands:** Jefferies (Snyder) **Buy**, data-center power buildout thesis (briefings 2026-06-01, 2026-06-12). JPM (Rishabh Gupta) **OW on the India T&D arm**, PT raised to Rs5,050 — read-through to parent grid demand, not the US listing (briefings 2026-05-19/20). Barclays (Julian Mitchell) covers GEV within a **NEUTRAL** U.S. Energy Services & Technology sector view; no explicit GEV PT in the Powering AI thematics, which lean constructive on the scarcity/pricing setup (Powering AI — Gas Turbines, 2026-01-21). Buy-side 2030 EBITDA expectations have re-rated hard: 44% see $18–20B, ~20% see >$20B vs. an August bull pitch of just $14B — the stock is priced off 2030 and estimates keep climbing (preview, 2026-04-17). UBS 800V-power equity call (2026-05-14) ties GEV into the data-center power-density theme.

## Catalysts / what to watch
- **Q2 FY26 print (~late July 2026):** the order bogey — beat the Q4'25 24 GW / $22.2B; mgmt guided 10–15 GW new contracts in Q2 and April orders already exceeded all of Q1 in dollar value (>$5.6B per callback). Watch $/kW mix (a large aero order would flatter it).
- **Gas Power framework agreements:** 30–35 multi-year hyperscaler/utility frameworks in negotiation; none closed to date — first signing would be a catalyst (Q1 FY26).
- **Mid-2026:** Gas capacity steps to ~20 GW annualized; productivity from 280+ new machines / 1,800 new US workers should show in Q3. By Barclays' read GEV should be **largely sold out for 2030 delivery by end-2026**, with only ~10 GW of 2029 slots left — watch for an upward revision to the 24 GW production target (Powering AI — Gas Turbines, 2026-01-21).
- **Nuclear/SMR:** OPG Darlington Unit 1 basemat install underway; Clinch River (TVA) NRC construction license expected H2'26; up to $40B US/Japan SMR funding for GE Vernova Hitachi (GBA likely first, 10 units).
- **Electrification product ramps:** EMS (2nd order already in April), MV-UPS stability block (potential orders H2'26), solid-state transformer (first hyperscaler delivery fall '26 → 6-month test → potential orders H1'27).
- **Section 232 wind/solar tariff outcome** — could give US onshore orders clarity in H2'26.

## Risks
- **Order/demand cyclicality** — long-cycle backlog exposed to a capex/AI-spend reversal; orders peak narrative live. Barclays notes the ~4 GW of "surge" capacity (7E France + Greenville weekend shifts) can be removed quickly if demand softens (Powering AI — Gas Turbines, 2026-01-21).
- **Supply chain / capacity constraints** — castings & forgings (Howmet/PCP/CPP) are the true bottleneck; manufacturing capacity constraints flagged as a risk factor (10-K 2026-01-29). Turbine blades/vanes require rare-earth/superalloy inputs (yttrium, rhenium, cobalt, nickel) with single-crystal casting overlap with aerospace — GEV is stockpiling yttrium with the US govt (Powering AI — Gas Turbines, 2026-01-21).
- **Fixed-price / large-project execution** — liquidated damages and unanticipated expense if specs/schedules missed, explicitly cited for Wind component delivery (10-K 2026-01-29).
- **Offshore Wind** — US offshore activity halted Dec 22; Haliade-X program loss-making; Vineyard/Dogger Bank execution risk into 2027 (Q4 FY25; 10-K).
- **Tariffs (Section 232)** — $250–350M net '26 impact; Belfort-imported turbines and Prolec exposed (Q1 FY26).
- **Customer/geographic concentration & commodity prices** — macro deterioration, decarbonization-policy shifts (10-K risk factors).
- **Geopolitical** — Middle East exposure; LNG-to-power projects in Vietnam (4.8 GW) where one project is being weighed against renewables (Q1 FY26).
- **Competitive capacity build / new entrants** — Siemens Energy ramping to 30+ GW avg FY28-30 and entrants (Doosan, FTAI, ProEnergy, Boom) adding aero/large-frame supply could erode GEV's scarcity-driven ~20–30% US price premium over time (Powering AI — Gas Turbines, 2026-01-21).
