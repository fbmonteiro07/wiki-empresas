## Snapshot
Family of Apps (Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, Threads, Messenger) reaches 3.5B+ daily users; ~98% of revenue is advertising. Reality Labs (Quest, AI glasses, Horizon) is the secondary, loss-making segment. The stock is now a hyperscaler/AI-capex name as much as a digital-ads name: META is building frontier models inside Meta Superintelligence Labs (MSL), spending on NVDA GPUs + AMD + Broadcom custom silicon (>1GW own ASIC rolling out per Q1'26 call), and signing multi-year cloud deals. Chain position: ad platform + AI hyperscaler / GPU + ASIC buyer + power/data-center demand driver.

- **Capex $19.8B in Q1; FY26 capex guide RAISED to $125–145B** (from $120–135B), driven mostly by **higher memory/component pricing**, not more gigawatts (Q1 FY26 call; corroborated by Justin Post, BofA, 2026-06-11 equity call). FY26 total opex guide **unchanged $162–169B**; OI expected above FY25. _Prior step-up for reference: 2025 capex guide was raised to ~$71B midpoint (from $69B), up ~$32B YoY, with mgmt flagging "notably larger" dollar growth into 2026 led by infra/cloud/depreciation (Jefferies, 2025-11-10)._
- **Q2 FY26 revenue guide $58–61B** (~2% FX tailwind). Tax rate 13–16% remaining quarters.
- **AI/products:** Muse Spark (first MSL model) shipped, now powering Meta AI across the family + standalone app; double-digit % lift in Meta AI sessions/user. **Muse Spark NOT yet in the ads/recommendation stack** — so the 33% growth is pre-AI-product (emphasized by bull on the BofA call, 2026-06-11). Backstop: franchise still throws off >$130bn OCF at ~41% op margin (JPM "AI Capex 2.0", 2026-06-16).
- **Digital-ad checks — Meta Business Agents the big swing (UBS Ben Legg + Scott Linzer, 2026-06-17/22):** Legg is increasingly excited about **Meta Business Agents** (formerly Meta Business AI) as the **biggest 5-year tailwind for Meta** — rebranded at a London conference into an "AI business manager" for micro-businesses: a SaaS layer (planned **$15 and $50/month tiers, currently free**) that ingests Meta/Google ads, Stripe, CRM, calendar data + runs ads/WhatsApp on top, plus a "live link when your brand is mentioned in conversations" inventory opportunity (3.5B DAU × 20–30 convos/day). - **Custom silicon (JPM "AI Capex 2.0", 2026-06-16):** Meta MTIA on a multi-generation roadmap; **Broadcom planning multiple gigawatts of MTIA deployments beginning in 2027**, and Meta intends to train next-gen models on MTIA — corroborates the >1GW own-ASIC ramp.
- **Storage-procurement profile (Former Strategic Procurement Manager at Meta expert call, 2026-01-28):** a rare look at Meta's data-storage supply chain. Unlike AWS/GOOG/MSFT (who buy HDDs as a commodity for cloud-for-customers at the lowest price point), **Meta builds mostly for its own requirement** and balances SSD vs HAMR on TCO. Today Meta's **nearline (warm) storage is ~75-80% HDD / 20-25% SSD (QLC NAND); by 2030 the expert expects ~40% SSD / 60% HDD, on an installed base ~4x today's** (archival/cold storage stays HDD-only). HDD lead times have compressed to ~6-12 months with narrower forecast windows (12-18mo vs prior 3-5yr) as the HDD/SSD substitution and AI demand cloud visibility; Meta over-orders (~1M units vs ~800k needed) to hedge supply, redeploying surplus across sites. Vendor tiering: **WD preferred over Seagate** (higher capacity + NAND partnerships → consolidated SSD+HDD vendor), Toshiba for non-AI storage. QLC SSD priced ~3-4x HDD today, expected to fall to ~2-2.5x by 2028; TLC NAND stays tight/premium 3-4 more years. On DRAM, Meta buys LPDDR/DDR5/GDDR for graphics/HPC but views DDR as unattractive for AI (HBM is the mechanism); the expert does not see broad hyperscaler double-ordering as realistic today given how tight/expensive memory is (Former Strategic Procurement Manager at Meta Platforms expert call, 2026-01-28). Corroborates the memory/component-cost driver behind the capex-guide raise.
- **Model gap quantified + Meta IS Anthropic (FundaAI "Deep|LLM 26H1 Part 2", 2026-06-24):** FundaAI's frontier-lab review reframes META's AI position two ways. (1) **Meta is one of Anthropic's largest enterprise customers by token consumption — Claude Code is the company-wide coding standard, NOT its own Muse Spark** — direct evidence the in-house model isn't yet good enough for Meta's own engineers, and a cost line that flows to Anthropic/AWS. Internal "switch back to in-house models / reduce reliance on Anthropic" discussion persists but FundaAI sees near-term implementation as unlikely given the gap. (2) On the models: post-Alexandr-Wang MSL shipped flagship **Muse Spark (codename Avocado, 2026-04-08)** with multimodal understanding "aligned to frontier models but coding clearly behind"; next-gen flagship **Watermelon (3T params)** targets coding catch-up; video model **Mango (vs Sora)** slipped from H1'26 to 2026-09. FundaAI's summary: Meta's flagship "has not reached frontier level in either multimodal or coding." Sharpens the existing bear "model gap" point with current (not Llama-4-era) names (FundaAI, 2026-06-24). [Source](../relat%C3%B3rios%20bons/LLM_Part_2_funda.html)
- **Barclays "AI's S-Curve Is Steepening" (2026-06-01):** includes META in its hyperscaler-capex aggregate (GOOGL/AWS/META/MSFT/ORCL) that Barclays now sees reaching **~$1.16T by 2028E** (25%+ above Street), as the AI capex peak gets pushed beyond 2028 (~36 GW of next-gen compute); META carried **OW/Positive, PT not changed in this note** ($635 ref) — incidental inclusion in the capex framework, no META-specific thesis change (Barclays, 2026-06-01).

## Risks
- **Capex/ROIC:** capital intensity (~$125-145B FY26) with no contractual revenue backlog vs. peers; incremental ROIC questioned hard by buy-side (BofA call). FY27 free-cash-flow estimates likely too high.
- **Ad-revenue concentration:** ~98% of revenue is advertising; macro/demand and time-spent competition are existential (10-K 2026-01-29 risk factors; Nowak's "competing for how you waste your time"). Tariff channel: e-commerce/DTC (largest advertiser segment, China-heavy) is the most acute exposure (Seufert/Stratechery, 2025-04-24).
- **Regulatory/legal:** US youth-safety trials in 2026 ("may ultimately result in a material loss" — Susan Li, Q1 FY26); EU privacy/DSA headwinds; UK under-16 social ban (briefing 2026-06-16). 10-K flags privacy, antitrust, content regulation.
- **Talent/execution:** model gap vs. OpenAI/Anthropic (Llama 4 #39 on LM Arena, JPM 2025-07); morale strain post-RIF; key AI departures (Rothschild 2026-06-18).
- **RL drag:** ~$4B/qtr operating loss; wearables thesis unproven at scale.
- **Founder control:** super-voting structure limits shareholder recourse (BofA call).
