## Snapshot
Vistra is the largest competitive (merchant) power producer in the US: ~41GW+ of generation across ERCOT, PJM, New England, NY and California, spanning natural gas (combined-cycle/peakers), nuclear, coal-to-gas conversions, solar and battery storage, plus a leading competitive retail electricity franchise serving millions of residential and C&I customers. The model is **integrated** — generation + commercial hedging + retail — which is the core of the bull case: retail and gen offset each other across weather/price regimes (Q1 FY26, 2026-05-07). Two reportable segments: Generation (~$4.29B FY25 adj. EBITDA) and Retail (~$1.62B FY25), per Q4 FY25 (2026-02-26). The stock is now an AI-power / data-center demand play sitting at the intersection of merchant power and hyperscaler capex.

## Current state (latest quarter)
**Q1 FY26 (2026-05-07):** ~$1.494B adjusted EBITDA, a record calendar-Q1, +~20% YoY and +~85% vs Q1 2024 (Kris Moldovan). Generation $1.426B (strong realized revenue, higher PJM capacity revenue, Lotus contribution); Retail $68M — a deliberate YoY decline against an exceptionally mild ERCOT winter (2nd-warmest Q1 since 1950) and tough comp vs a record 2025. **Capital returns / framework (Q1 FY26 + Q4 FY25):** ~$525M of buybacks in the first 4 months of FY26 plus a ~$75M Q1 dividend = ~$600M returned YTD; 10b5-1 program is designed to accelerate on weakness (it leaned in during Jan-Feb price pressure). Since Nov-2021: ~169M shares retired at ~$37 avg (Q1 FY26). Remaining authorization ~$1.475B. Line of sight to **>$10B cash generation over 2026-2027**: ~$3B to equity holders, ~$4B to accretive growth (Cogentrix, Permian gas, PJM nuclear uprate, Oak Hill 2), leaving ~$3B unallocated, all at ~2.3x net debt/EBITDA by YE27 (Moldovan).

**FCF/share bridge (Q4 FY25, 2026-02-26):** >$12.50 adj. **Strategic build-out:** Cogentrix (5,500MW gas, ~$730/kW net of tax benefits, mid-single-digit FCF/share accretion in 2027, high-single-digit avg 2027-29) on track to close 2H26 (Q4 FY25). ~3.8GW of nuclear now under 20-yr PPAs — Amazon at Comanche Peak (1,200MW, energization 2027, full ramp 2032) and Meta across Perry/Davis-Besse/Beaver Valley (2,176MW operating + 433MW uprates, uprates online 2031-2034). Jefferies characterizes the Comanche Peak nuclear deal as **premium-priced** but notes it landed "with little fanfare," and that the IPPs' DC-announcement cadence has slipped to late-2025/early-2026 (Jefferies 3Q25 DC Leasing, 2025-11-10). ~3.2GW more nuclear (Beaver Valley + Comanche Peak) still uncontracted. ~4,500MW of organic development in process, majority online by 2028 (Q1 FY26). Post-Cogentrix CCGT fleet = 26GW at ~60% utilization — the key operating-leverage lever (Q4 FY25).

## Debate / thesis
_Sell-side coverage in the disk sources is **thin** — one morning-briefing datapoint (MS, constructive) plus one attributable published rating (Jefferies, **HOLD**, see below); no equity-call notes beyond questioner attributions. Bull/bear below is drawn primarily from the two most recent transcripts, the one briefing line, and the Jefferies DC-power note. Flagging this explicitly._

- **Bull:** Structurally improved, *durable* demand — first prospective 4-yr sustained US load-growth period since the era ending 2007; US consumption hit ~4,200 TWh in 2025, +2.5% YoY (Q4 FY25). ERCOT load growth of 5-6% to 2030 and PJM 2-3% (Q1 FY26 framing) drive higher utilization of an existing fleet at only ~60% utilization — operating leverage without greenfield risk. Jefferies' sector model corroborates the macro: 100 GW of incremental US data-center demand through 2035 (60/40 1H/2H weighted), driving a 2.3% load-growth CAGR (vs 0.6% the prior decade), with a "rising tide lifts all boats" read-through for gas, solar and battery — though new nuclear is "a less direct beneficiary" given build lag (Jefferies 3Q25 DC Leasing, 2025-11-10). Integrated model proven across weather (gen offset mild-weather retail drag in Q1 FY26; thermal was ~93% of ERCOT delivery during the tightest hours of Winter Storm Fern). Contracting de-risks the model: ~half of total adj. EBITDA expected from highly stable sources once signed PPAs ramp (Q4 FY25) — "not trading growth for stability, achieving both" (Burke). Aggressive, value-accretive buyback (~$20B value created since 2021) into a high FCF yield. Hyperscaler capex tailwind: >$700B in 2026, ~+50% YoY (Q4 FY25); Jefferies flags +$0.5T of CY26 tech capex revisions across AMZN/META/GOOG/MSFT (3Q25 DC Leasing). Briefing datapoint: MS (Arcaro) flagged the **Helix Digital Infrastructure ($10B, with KKR/NVDA/KIA)** initiative as a new theme worth ~$23/share from an initial $1B (morning, 2026-06-12).
- **Bear:** Mgmt itself is more cautious than the market on *pace* — load growth is "real but not at the extremely elevated levels in the rapid time frame forecast by many third parties"; meaningful data-center tightening doesn't begin until "late 2027 or early 2028" (Q4 FY25). ERCOT forwards have come off on mild weather; Burke concedes forwards don't even reflect his own 5-6% load view, i.e. earnings hinge on weather/volatility that has been muted since the Aug-2023 peak. **IPPs are losing the DC-contracting race to regulated utilities and behind-the-meter "upstarts":** Jefferies rates VST **HOLD** (ref $188.28, 2025-11-10) and prefers regulated/development-led names NRG, NI and ETR for data-center power exposure, noting the IPPs had a "more subdued" 3Q earnings season while regulated utilities (WEC, DTE, NI) announced 1GW+ campuses on the strength of large-load tariffs and faster speed-to-market (Jefferies 3Q25 DC Leasing). Behind-the-meter — where VST does not lead — is increasingly the channel of choice (3rd-party estimates ~20GW by 2030). Regulatory overhang in PJM: colocation tariff rules, reliability backstop auction (RBA, possibly Sept), connect-and-manage and load-interconnection queues all unresolved — a peer (Constellation) flagged a customer "pause"; VST says no slowdown but admits deals are "complicated" and slow. Growing **affordability backlash** is a fresh political/regulatory risk: post the record PJM capacity clear and the Nov-2025 NJ/GA elections, residential-bill impact from data centers is a near-daily news theme, pushing regulators toward tariffs that must show demonstrable customer benefit (not just "no harm") — structurally favoring regulated utilities over merchant IPPs (Jefferies 3Q25 DC Leasing). Counter-party / duration-mismatch risk is also rising (NVDA chips amortize in ~5 yrs vs 20+ yr asset underwriting; some contracts signed with opaque SPVs rather than the hyperscaler directly). Retail FY26 EBITDA moderates toward a ~$1.4B medium-term run-rate vs the FY25 record (one-time supply/Energy Harbor tailwinds don't repeat). Battery economics in ERCOT have "returned virtually nothing" (Burke, Q1 FY26). Big inorganic appetite = integration and capital-allocation execution risk.
- **Where the sell-side/buy-side stands:** Two attributable reads on disk, mixed. **Jefferies / Julien Dumoulin-Smith** — **HOLD** (ref $188.28, 2025-11-10); constructive on the load-growth macro but prefers NRG/NI/ETR over VST in DC power, viewing IPPs as having ceded share to regulated utilities and BTM upstarts. **MS / David Arcaro** — constructive, surfacing the Helix Digital Infrastructure theme as incremental ~$23/share upside (briefing, 2026-06-12). Other active questioners on the calls (not ratings) include Wells Fargo (Pourreza), Wolfe (Fleishman), JPM (Tonet), UBS (Appicelli), BNP (Sutton), Seaport (Storozynski), Scotiabank (Wiesel). No formal PTs available in co-located sources.

**Behind-the-meter read-through (expert calls, 2026):** the BTM-vs-grid debate that frames VST's "IPPs ceding DC share" bear point sharpened in two expert calls. A behind-the-meter power expert (ex-GE Vernova) argues **BTM is a *permanent* solution, not just a bridge** — connecting hyperscalers to the grid raises rates on the public and ISOs "pick their battles," so the grid-reconnection thesis that favors merchant IPPs may not fully materialize (Wells Fargo "Aman Chakri BTM expert", 2026-05-27). A midstream desk's hosted DC expert puts **BTM at ~40% of power deals**, with post-grid-connection BTM assets later running only at **~15–30% capacity for peak-shaving** (WFC "midstream Friday wrap", 2026-06-05). Counter-tension from the broader cohort: Bernstein's 50+ operator survey still has **98% preferring grid** and BTM a 5–10% niche (already in this theme). Net: the data points cut both ways on whether VST's grid-connected merchant fleet or BTM upstarts win the marginal DC contract.

**Macro / capex read-through (JPM "AI Capex 2.0", 2026-06-16):** JPM's deal table corroborates VST's marquee nuclear contract — the **Vistra–Meta PPA across Beaver Valley, Davis-Besse and Perry, 2.6 GW, 20-yr (announced 1/9/26)**. The macro is supportive of VST's load-growth thesis: JPM now models **138 GW of US DC capacity growth through 2030** (up from 122 GW) within **$5.5tn AI capex**, with **power the binding constraint**. ERCOT-specific (VST's core market): JPM notes **interconnection queues continue to grow in ERCOT**, with incremental gas additions in ERCOT/SPP/MISO and **Texas a key BTM market given the lack of FERC oversight** — a double-edged read (more demand, but BTM/regulated competition for it). Gas remains the largest planned generation addition through 2031 (most online ~2029 on 3–5 yr turbine lead times), consistent with VST's Cogentrix/Permian gas build and its "late 2027/early 2028" tightening view (JPM "AI Capex 2.0", 2026-06-16).

## Catalysts / what to watch
- **Cogentrix close (2H26)** — triggers the FY26 guidance + FY27 midpoint update; ~$700-750M potential FY27 EBITDA add from Cogentrix + Meta operating PPAs (Q4 FY25 disclosure math).
- **Next nuclear/gas contract announcement** — ~3.2GW Beaver Valley + Comanche Peak uncontracted; mgmt says appetite "as high as ever" (Q4 FY25). Beaver Valley colocation a watch item. Jefferies notes the IPP announcement timeline has slipped to late-2025/early-2026 (3Q25 DC Leasing) — execution on this cadence is the key proof point vs the regulated-utility share gains.
- **PJM regulatory cadence** — colocation tariff compliance filings, the reliability backstop auction (potentially Sept), connect-and-manage rulemaking, price-collar extension (Q4/Q1 calls). Affordability politics (NJ/GA Nov-2025 elections, PJM capacity-price backlash) raising the bar on large-load tariffs (Jefferies 3Q25 DC Leasing).
- **ERCOT batch 0 / load-interconnection process** — VST thinks total ERCOT growth is ~30-40GW by 2030 (~10-15GW data centers), well below the 300-400GW queue noise (Q1 FY26).
- **Helix Digital Infrastructure ($10B w/ KKR/NVDA/KIA)** — emerging theme per MS (briefing, 2026-06-12); watch for formal disclosure.
- Continued buyback acceleration if shares stay weak; possible 3rd IG ratings upgrade "as early as later this year" (Q4 FY25).

## Risks
- Commodity/power-price and basis exposure; merchant earnings depend on volatility — muted weather since Aug-2023 has pressured ERCOT forwards (Q1 FY26).
- Weather concentration (ERCOT) — mild winters compress retail; extreme events (Fern, Uri-type) cut both ways.
- Nuclear operational/outage risk and regulatory (NRC license, PTC downside protection relied on in hedging) (Q4 FY25).
- PJM/FERC/ERCOT regulatory uncertainty on colocation, interconnection, capacity-market design — could delay or reshape data-center contracting. Affordability backlash + counter-party/SPV credit and duration-mismatch risk increasingly central to large-load tariff design (Jefferies 3Q25 DC Leasing).
- Competitive/share risk: regulated utilities and behind-the-meter providers taking DC-power share from IPPs; Jefferies prefers NRG/NI/ETR to VST in the space (HOLD, 2025-11-10).
- Execution/integration risk on Cogentrix and the organic build pipeline; capital-allocation discipline (mid-teens levered return threshold).
- Retail margin normalization toward ~$1.4B (Q4 FY25); battery/storage returns weak in ERCOT.
