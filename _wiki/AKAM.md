## Snapshot
Akamai is the original CDN, now mid-pivot from a legacy content-**Delivery** business (in secular decline) toward two growth pillars: **Security** (Web App Firewall, API Security, Guardicore Segmentation / micro-segmentation, Zero Trust) and **Cloud Compute / Cloud Infrastructure Services (CIS)** built on the Linode acquisition plus a new Akamai Inference Cloud (distributed CPU + NVIDIA GPU at the edge). The platform spans 4,300 locations across 700 cities in 130 countries — the distributed footprint is the central value proposition for both security (intercepting attacks at the edge) and low-latency AI inference (AKAM Q1 2026 call, 2026-05-07). FY2025 revenue $4.208B, +5% YoY; by solution: Security $2.243B (~53%), Delivery $1.257B (~30%, declining), Cloud computing $708M (~17%, of which CIS $314M growing ~36%) (AKAM Q4 2025 PR, 2026-02-19). The core debate: can Security + CIS growth (and now AI-infrastructure mega-deals) outrun the structural erosion of legacy Delivery and re-rate a business that has long been valued as ex-growth.

## Current state (latest quarter — Q1 2026, call 2026-05-07)

- **Headline:** Q1 2026 total revenue **$1.074B, +6% YoY** as reported (+4% cc). Non-GAAP EPS **$1.61, down 5% YoY** (pressured by colocation buildout, higher depreciation and headcount tied to CIS investment). Non-GAAP operating margin **26%** (AKAM Q1 2026 call, 2026-05-07).
- **Segment growth (the whole story):**

Structured as dedicated capacity (take-or-pay, recognized ratably "like a subscription"), revenue starts ramping in **Q4 2026 (~$20–25M)** and builds across 2027+ (AKAM Q1 2026 call, 2026-05-07).

## Debate / thesis
- **AI-software read-through — "CDN for tokens" reframes the CDN TAM (Vercel CEO Guillermo Rauch, Stanford MS&E435, 2026-06-29):** Rauch argues token/inference traffic needs CDN-style routing/caching/observability/security ("AI Gateway = CDN for tokens"). Directionally an extension of Akamai's core competency (edge delivery + security + compute) — but the agentic-deploy/AI-gateway land-grab is currently led by Vercel and Cloudflare, so this is a TAM opportunity AKAM must show it can capture rather than be bypassed by. See [tokenmaxxing theme](themes/tokenmaxxing.md).
- **Bull:** The story has shifted from a legacy delivery network to a credible **AI-infrastructure platform**, and the inflection is now visible in real bookings, not narrative. CIS is inflecting at +40–45% YoY with the $1.8B/7-yr and $200M wins validating distributed AI demand; mix-shift (CIS + Security growing, Delivery stabilizing) should drive faster total growth and eventually margins — management guides to **double-digit total growth in 2027** (BofA on AKAM, 2026-05-13; AKAM Q1 2026 call, 2026-05-07). The distributed-edge architecture (4,300 locations) is a genuine differentiator vs centralized hyperscalers/neoclouds for low-latency inference, and Akamai wins on latency + ability to secure data-center space/power on good credit (AKAM Q1 2026 call, 2026-05-07). Security is a structural beneficiary of AI: CEO Leighton says he's "never seen the CISOs more agitated" — AI-powered attacks and unpatchable legacy devices are driving urgency, with WAF re-accelerating and API/Guardicore still low-penetration (AKAM Q1 2026 call, 2026-05-07). Strong FCF generation funds the buildout and buybacks (FY2025 OCF $1.519B / 36% margin) (AKAM Q4 2025 PR, 2026-02-19).
- **Bear:** The pivot dilutes near-term economics — Q1 2026 EPS fell 5% YoY, FY2026 operating margin flat at ~26% with no expansion, and capex spiked to 40–42% of revenue, so this is an **"invest now, earn later"** story whose payoff sits in 2027+ and depends on execution (AKAM Q1 2026 call, 2026-05-07). The CIS mega-deals are lower-margin (management said it's willing to do large deals at potentially **sub-30% operating margin**) and depreciation-heavy, so the model becomes more capital-intensive and reliant on a small number of large frontier-model customers — concentration risk. Legacy **Delivery is in secular decline** (−7% YoY, ~30% of revenue) and management itself frames it as a cash cow to "harvest" to fund compute, not a growth engine — even agentic/AI traffic isn't expected to "make a huge swing in the amount of bits" delivered absent video generation (AKAM Q1 2026 call, 2026-05-07). The compute pivot also pits AKAM directly against hyperscalers and neoclouds ("every deal is competitive") with a far smaller balance sheet, and may eventually require external capital if mega-deals keep landing (AKAM Q1 2026 call, 2026-05-07).
- **Where the Street stands:** **BofA (Tal Liani) UPGRADED to Buy from Neutral, PO raised to $175 from $130** (vs $149.56 ref price, ~18% upside), 2026-05-13, on the AI-infrastructure pivot becoming visible. BofA models revenue growth accelerating from **7% in 2026 to 11% in 2027**; estimates: 2026E rev $4,552.6M / EPS $6.93; 2027E rev $5,073.3M / EPS $7.78; 2028E rev $5,598.8M / EPS $9.03. BofA's view: CIS scale validates AI positioning, mix-shift offsets delivery decline, and the new deal adds recurring capacity-based revenue — "investment today, earnings tomorrow" (BofA on AKAM, 2026-05-13).

## Risks
From the FY2025 10-K (AKAM 10-K FY2025) plus the debate above:
- **Slowing / flat / limited revenue growth** has hurt and may continue to hurt profitability and the stock (AKAM 10-K FY2025).
- **Cloud-computing execution dependency** — an increasing share of revenue depends on successfully building the compute platform, developing AI capabilities, and attracting a customer base that traditionally partnered elsewhere; "we may not be successful in our artificial intelligence initiatives" (AKAM 10-K FY2025).
- **Competition** — hyperscalers/larger rivals can expand points of presence, secure server components (incl. memory), co-location space and power on preferred/priority terms — constraining supply and raising AKAM's costs (AKAM 10-K FY2025).
- **DIY / insourcing** — large customers shifting to in-house delivery or security would hurt the business (AKAM 10-K FY2025).
- **Capital intensity & indebtedness** — heavier capex and convertible debt increase vulnerability to adverse conditions and could limit future financing if cash generation weakens (AKAM 10-K FY2025).
- **New-solution development risk** — failure to develop/acquire attractive new solutions could hurt results (AKAM 10-K FY2025).
- **Global operations, tax, regulatory and integration (acquisitions)** risks; defects/disruptions in products and IT systems; dependence on third-party software (AKAM 10-K FY2025).
- **Customer concentration** in CIS mega-deals (a few frontier-model customers driving disproportionate compute revenue/capex) (AKAM Q1 2026 call, 2026-05-07).
