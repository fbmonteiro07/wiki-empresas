## Snapshot
Arm is the upstream IP-licensing layer of the compute industry: it designs CPU/GPU/NPU architectures and processor IP and collects (i) up-front license fees and (ii) a per-unit royalty on substantially every chip shipped using its designs — typically a % of chip ASP or a fixed fee per unit, rising as more Arm IP is included (FY26 20-F, Item 4). FY26 revenue $4.92B, +23% YoY, split License & other $2.31B (47%) / Royalty $2.61B (53%); operating margin ~18% GAAP, ~49% non-GAAP in Q4 (FY26 20-F; Q4 FY26 call, 2026-05-06). The franchise is in two transitions that define the thesis: (1) royalty mix-up from ARMv9 + Compute Subsystems (CSS) lifting per-chip rates, and (2) a move *downstream* into its own silicon — the **Arm AGI CPU** launched at Q4 FY26 with Meta as lead partner. ~57% of revenue comes from top-5 customers (incl. Arm China and SoftBank); SoftBank controls Arm (~controlled-company / FPI status). Mobile app processors are ~43% of royalty (FY26 20-F).

## Position in the value chain
Arm sits **upstream of nearly every SoC** in the world — virtually all smartphones, a majority of tablets/digital TVs, and a rising share of data-center CPUs run an Arm-architected core. It ships no volume silicon historically (AGI CPU is the first, production end-CY26); revenue is a **license + recurring per-unit royalty** model with a long tail (chips ship for years). The swing variable is the **server / data-center CPU TAM** — hyperscaler custom CPUs (AWS Graviton, Google Axion, Microsoft Cobalt, NVIDIA Vera/Grace) are Arm-architected, and agentic AI is lifting CPU:GPU ratios toward ~1:1.

## Current state (latest quarter)
**Q4 FY26 (call 2026-05-06):** revenue $1.49B (+20%); License & other $819M (+29%); Royalty $671M (+11%); non-GAAP EPS $0.60 (record); FY26 $4.92B (+23%), non-GAAP EPS $1.77. Vs Street (Capstone briefing, 2026-05-06): license beat ($780M), royalty slightly light ($691M); stock +13% in session. **Q1 FY27 guide:** rev $1.26B ±$50M (~+20%), non-GAAP EPS $0.40 ±$0.04.
- **Data center royalty more than doubled YoY** again; Arm targets a **>$100B data-center CPU TAM by 2030** as agentic AI needs >4x today's CPU capacity (Q4 FY26 call). Haas: "by the end of the decade, the largest market share by CPU type will be Arm."
- **Own silicon — Arm AGI CPU:** launched with **Meta as lead partner**; **~$2B of customer demand across FY27+FY28** (>2x the analyst-day figure), 50+ ecosystem partners (AWS, Broadcom, GCP, MSFT, NVDA, Oracle, Samsung, TSMC). FY27 AGI revenue held at ~$1B on supply constraints; first-gen GM ~30%+ (dilutive to the ~49% non-GAAP IP model). FY31 framing: ~$15B AGI + ~$10B IP, combined EPS >$9. Meta's Arm-based server CPU is **under development with Socionext** as ASIC design partner (JPM ASIC Design Services teach-in).
- **CSS the royalty engine:** CFO Child (Q3 FY26 call) — CSS could be "upwards of 50% of royalties within a couple of years"; CSS royalty rates now >10% of ASP (Q1 FY26). Royalty stacking confirmed from the SoftBank side: **v9 royalty ≈ 2x v8, and CSS ≈ 2x v9** (Jefferies Techknowledge / SoftBank takeaways, 2025-12-17). Same note flags a live monetization dispute — SoftBank says Arm is charging MediaTek the **full CSS license even where MediaTek uses only part of the IP** (CSS billed as a packaged solution); MediaTek denies using CSS.
- **Royalty/licensing guidance + cloud-share milestone (expert callback):** post-Q4 FY26, ARM is guiding **both royalty and licensing +20% YoY in fiscal Q1 (June qtr) and across FY27** (Q4 royalty was +11% on a tough MediaTek-9400 comp + low-end smartphone weakness); **cloud/AI royalty up ~2x YoY**, and Arm says its **cloud (data-center) share has reached ~50%** the way it measures it, up from near-zero at end-2020 (Grace/Vera at NVDA the big driver). Q4 licensing +29% on a major Indonesian sovereign-wealth-fund deal + two new CSS deals (one smartphone "sounds like MediaTek," one data-center networking); **ACV +22% YoY**. FY27 licensing guide includes **~$100M for the AGI chip**; AGI backlog "basically doubled in six weeks" since launch but is **memory-constrained** (N3 chip, customers can't place a TSMC PO without HBM allocation Arm can't help secure), so "AGI revenue will be more about supply than demand for the foreseeable future." Arm sees a hyperscaler roadmap to a **700-core chip** (~4x today's 284-core), with per-core ASP "not really that different" from x86 but Arm's core counts growing faster — and Arm internally pegs the **server-CPU TAM at $200-300B by 2030** on a demand basis (vs the ">$100B" it says publicly) (Expert "SiTime/ARM call," 2026-05-07).
- **Sizing the $20B NVIDIA "standalone-Arm" number (UBS equity call):** Arcuri decomposes NVIDIA's $20B figure — most of it is **systems + DRAM, not chips**; the **true chip-equivalent is ~$5B (~1M Vera units)**, since Vera standalone ASP is **~$3-4k** vs **~$9k of DRAM per system** (CPU ≈ 20-30% of system value). On the same Apples-to-Apples basis, NVIDIA's **$200B systems TAM ≈ ~$100B chip TAM** by 2030 — i.e. roughly in line with Arm's public ">$100B," not a separate upside leg. UBS's own bottoms-up model pegs the **2030 server-CPU market at ~$175B** (vs AMD/Lisa Su's **$125B** and Arm's "2-300B of demand"); Arm told UBS offline that **~$175B is the bogey that "marries DRAM supply with demand"** — the binding constraint is DRAM, not CPU demand (UBS "Arcuri" equity calls, 2026-05-21 / 2026-05-18).
- **Arm AGI CPU — Gen-1 specs + economics (expert CPU-roadmap call):** first-gen part is a **~300W TDP** design (vs typical servers ~400W) with **~80 cores** — low vs AMD's ~180-core Bergamo; reads as a Gen-1 product, and there is **no third-party benchmark yet** vs an AMD Turin. Per-core royalty on hyperscaler CPUs is **only ~$0.50-1.00/core = low-single-digit % of chip price**, which is precisely why Arm wants its own silicon — but own chips **cannibalize those royalties at the margin**. SoftBank's ambitions reportedly run **beyond CPUs into accelerators** (Masa asking Haas to take a bigger role; Arm/SoftBank hold **Graphcore IP**). And as Broadcom (which largely doesn't build CPUs) wins more ASIC share, every Broadcom/MTIA deployment **still needs a host CPU** — a structural pull for merchant Arm CPUs (expert "CPU roadmap" equity call, 2026-04-22).
- **IP-licensing base — CSS KPIs (SemiAnalysis):** over **1 billion Neoverse cores** deployed across datacenter CPUs/DPUs to date, with **21 CSS licenses signed across 12 companies**; datacenter royalty revenue **"more than doubled Year-over-Year."** Arm itself projects **CSS to represent over 50% of royalty revenue within the next couple of years** — figures as disclosed/projected by Arm and relayed by SemiAnalysis, not independently modeled by the desk (SemiAnalysis, Wong & Patel, "CPUs are Back," 2026-02-09).
- **"Phoenix" — Arm's first full chip design (SemiAnalysis):** SemiAnalysis frames Arm's own-silicon push (codenamed **Phoenix**) as a genuine business-model shift — from pure IP licensor to full chip vendor designing an entire CPU end-to-end (cores through packaging), putting Arm into **direct competition with its own CSS licensee customers**. **Meta is the first Phoenix customer**; **Cloudflare is reportedly a prospective customer**. Separately, **Arm (majority-owned by SoftBank) is also designing a custom CPU for OpenAI as part of the Stargate OpenAI/SoftBank venture.** Phoenix specs per SemiAnalysis: standard Neoverse CSS design/layout similar to Microsoft Cobalt 200; **128 Neoverse V3 cores**, Arm CMN mesh across **two half-reticle dies on TSMC 3nm**; **12 channels of DDR5 at 8400 MT/s**; **96 lanes of PCIe Gen6**; configurable TDP **250W-350W**; coherent shared memory to attached XPUs over PCIe6 via an Accelerator Enablement Kit. SemiAnalysis: "With this, Meta now has their own Arm CPU to match the likes of Microsoft, Google and AWS." (SemiAnalysis, Wong & Patel, "CPUs are Back," 2026-02-09.) _Note: this appears to be the same/overlapping own-silicon program the wiki has been tracking as "Arm AGI CPU" (Meta-led, Q4 FY26 launch) — SemiAnalysis uses the codename Phoenix; not yet reconciled spec-for-spec (e.g. 128 vs ~80 cores reported by the expert CPU-roadmap call) so both labels are carried here pending clarification._
- **Arm Venom — Phoenix's successor, 2027 (SemiAnalysis):** debuts 2027 with **228 future Neoverse V4 cores**, likely derived from Arm's C1 Ultra mobile core; built on **TSMC N2** as a **single reticle-sized compute die** surrounded by multiple memory/PCIe Gen7 I/O dies, "in a layout similar to Nvidia's Vera CPU"; **Socionext has design involvement**. Key architecture change: moves to **LPDDR6** memory from traditional DDR5 DIMMs (following Nvidia's design choices), improving package density and tighter AI-XPU integration; next-gen Accelerator Enablement Kit (AEK) over **PCIe7** for XPU attach with coherent memory expansion (SemiAnalysis, Wong & Patel, "CPUs are Back," 2026-02-09).
- **Qualcomm re-enters datacenter CPUs — SD2, 2027 (SemiAnalysis):** Qualcomm (Nuvia team, acquired 2021; Gerard Williams III ex-Apple) returns to the datacenter CPU market from 2027 — its prior SD1 chip never launched after **Arm's 2022 lawsuit against Qualcomm**. In 2025 Qualcomm officially announced its return, developing/supplying CPUs for Saudi Arabia's HUMAIN AI project; **SD2** (SemiAnalysis working name) will support **Nvidia's NVLink Fusion** as a coherent interconnect to AI GPUs. Qualcomm's 2025 acquisition of **Alphawave Semi** shores up SerDes/chiplet interconnect; on its Q4 2025 earnings call Qualcomm confirmed **SD2 ships for revenue in 2027**, calling the datacenter CPU market "a multi-billion dollar opportunity." Risk flag: **Gerard Williams III and other original Nuvia team members left Qualcomm in January 2026**, "casting a shadow over the future of their CPU program," though SemiAnalysis believes "the impact will be limited until beyond 2028" (SemiAnalysis, Wong & Patel, "CPUs are Back," 2026-02-09).
- **Companion-CPU lever + Google Arm mix (Citi Asia checks):** the Arm storyline is most levered to **companion CPUs** (the fixed CPU:GPU ratio inside AI servers, ~99% Arm in GB-class racks). Citi's Asia checks found the **CPU mix at Google up to ~60% Arm** (Axion, higher than expected) and **Graviton ~60% of AWS's general-purpose mix** — i.e. x86 volumes structurally lower. NVIDIA's ~$20B standalone-Arm-CPU figure (Vera, ramping Q4) is "chips not memory" and likely bundles NVLink/head-node CPUs given the ASP is far above peers; the agentic-AI/RAG portion of the TAM stays more x86-native near-term (Citi "Asia recap," 2026-05-22).
- **Server-CPU TAM doubling (sell-side framing):** BofA (Arya, 2026-02-23) lifted server CPU TAM to **~$60B by CY30E from ~$27B in CY25** (+17% CAGR; AI servers ~70%/~$41B at +27% CAGR), with Arm value share to **~20-25%+ by CY30E from ~5% in CY24** (raised from prior 15-20% outlook); AMD ~40%, INTC down to ~40% from 63% CY24. ## Debate / thesis
- **Bull (Bernstein, Rasgon):** PT raised **$300 → $500, Outperform** (2026-06-17) on a **server CPU TAM raise to $223B**; Arm targeting **$22B revenue by 2030**, FY31E EPS lifted $9.83 → $11.79. CPU:GPU ratio moving from ~8:1 (training) to ~1:1 (agentic/inference). Axion is actively displacing x86 host CPUs in Google TPU 8t/8i — "50% less power, 80% improvement over x86" (Arm CEO at GCP Next, 2026-05-11). PC optionality: NVDA RTX Spark / N1X Arm PC with Microsoft (briefings, 2026-06-01; ARM +12% pre-mkt).
- **AGI CPU silicon optionality (BofA, Arya, 2026-02-23):** with a full CPU **chiplet**, Arm's per-server economics expand **~30x** — from royalty of ~$50-100/chip @ ~98% GM to full silicon of ~$1.5k-2.0k/chip @ 50-60% GM. At a modest **~3% server CPU share by CY28E** (~$1.2B incremental sales) Arm captures ~$0.30/~10% EPS accretion; ~5% share by CY30E (~$3B) = ~$0.70/15-20% accretion — not yet in models. Caveat: the ~$200M/qtr (~$800M/yr) SoftBank licensing/outsourced-R&D line **may go away** once the chiplet matures (one-time, embedded in CPU ASP) — Arm needs ≥3% share just to offset it. 'Arm Everywhere' event held SF, Mar 24 2026 (expected merchant-CPU unveil).
- **Own-silicon channel conflict, sharpened (SemiAnalysis "Phoenix"):** the debate around Arm's own-CPU push is now explicit and named. Bull case: Phoenix is a genuinely new, far-higher-ASP revenue stream on top of the royalty stack, validated by real wins — **Meta as first customer**, **Cloudflare reportedly prospective**, and (per SemiAnalysis) **Arm/SoftBank also designing a custom CPU for OpenAI inside the Stargate venture** — i.e. Arm capturing hyperscaler/neocloud demand it would otherwise only clip a royalty on. Bear case: Phoenix puts Arm into **direct competition with its own CSS licensee base** — the same AWS/Google/Microsoft/Nvidia customers who already design their own Arm-architected CPUs (Graviton, Axion, Cobalt, Vera) are Arm's own royalty payers; a full chip vendor pitching those accounts risks **channel conflict and margin cannibalization** at precisely the point where CSS is supposed to become >50% of royalty revenue. SemiAnalysis frames this as a structural business-model pivot (IP licensor → full chip vendor, cores-to-packaging), not an incremental SKU (SemiAnalysis, Wong & Patel, "CPUs are Back," 2026-02-09) — consistent with the wiki's existing "AGI CPU" channel-conflict risk flagged from the FY26 20-F.
- **Bear:** royalty came in *light* vs Street in Q4 FY26 even as license beat — the highest-multiple part of the story (per-unit royalty compounding) is lumpy. The AGI CPU pivot trades Arm's pristine IP margins for ~30% hardware GM and puts Arm into **direct competition with its own licensees** (explicit new risk factor, FY26 20-F). Customer concentration is acute: top-5 = ~57% of revenue, Arm China alone ~16%; **PRC ~18% of revenue** with US/UK–China trade & national-security overhang, and Arm does **not control Arm China** (FY26 20-F risk factors). Mobile is ~43% of royalty into a soft smartphone unit backdrop (memory-driven; Q3 FY26 flagged 1–2% total royalty risk). Competition from x86 and free **RISC-V** (some customers are RISC-V backers). UBS's Arcuri is pointedly skeptical on the bull-case royalty math: investors model **50c-$1 per core**, but Arcuri argues the big CPU winners pay far less — **NVIDIA pays "substantially below 50 cents per core, way below,"** as do the large hyperscaler Arm-CPU customers — so the back-of-envelope core-count × royalty math is "too high"; he also discounts Arm's own AGI-CPU demand because the bulk of customers (he believes Meta + OpenAI) "don't do what they say they're going to do," warranting a bigger discount factor (UBS "Arcuri call," 2026-06-15). An expert CPU-roadmap call sharpens the quality-of-earnings critique: a lot of Arm's profit comes from **SoftBank licensing / outsourced-R&D**, so "the quality of earnings is not the best" vs peers, and the AGI CPU is a **Gen-1 part (~80 cores, ~300W) with no independent benchmark yet** vs an AMD Turin — recall Intel "took forever and then gave up" trying to enter mobile, so the merchant-CPU push will likely take until ~CY28-30 to show in the P&L (expert "CPU roadmap" equity call, 2026-04-22).

## Risks
- **Own-silicon pivot (AGI CPU):** new competitive/brand/operational/financial risk; channel conflict with licensees; ~30% GM dilutive to IP model (FY26 20-F). Note the SoftBank licensing line (~$800M/yr) may not survive the chiplet transition (BofA, 2026-02-23).
- **Customer concentration:** top-5 ~57%, Arm China ~16–21%, mobile app processors ~43% of royalty (FY26 20-F).
- **China:** PRC ~18% of revenue; no control over Arm China; US/UK–PRC trade & national-security exposure (FY26 20-F).
- **Architecture license substitution:** customers can take an architecture license and build their own cores, or shift to **RISC-V** / x86 (FY26 20-F). x86 still ~70-80% of server CPU value today; RISC-V winning low-end industrial (UBS, 2025-12-22).
- **Cyclicality / smartphone units:** royalty tied to end-market chip shipments; memory-driven handset unit softness (Q3 FY26 call); mobile ~50% of revenue exposed to tariff-related demand destruction (UBS, 2025-12-22).
- **Governance:** SoftBank-controlled company + FPI exemptions limit minority influence (FY26 20-F).
