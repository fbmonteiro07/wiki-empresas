## Snapshot
Coherent is a vertically-integrated photonics maker that has re-rated into one of the cleanest large-cap plays on AI optical interconnect. The story is the Data Center & Communications segment, now ~75% of revenue (Q3 FY26, 2026-05-06), spanning datacom transceivers (800G/1.6T), DCI/scale-across coherent transceivers (ZR/ZR+), optical circuit switching (OCS), and an emerging co-packaged optics (CPO) franchise. The legacy Industrial segment (lasers, engineered materials, semicap) is the smaller, cyclical balance. Coherent's structural edge is owning the indium phosphide (InP) supply chain — EMLs, CW lasers, photodiodes — which is the binding constraint for the entire transceiver industry. Per the 10-K (FY25, 2025-08-15) the legacy reporting structure is three segments (Networking, Materials, Lasers); management now guides on the DC&Comms vs Industrial split. CEO Jim Anderson (ex-Lattice), CFO Sherri Luther.

## Current state (latest quarter)
**Q3 FY26 (2026-05-06):** Record revenue of $1.8B, +7% q/q and +21% y/y reported; **+9% q/q and +27% y/y pro forma** (ex-A&D and the divested Munich division). Non-GAAP gross margin **39.6%**, +57bp q/q and +105bp y/y; non-GAAP operating margin 20.3%; non-GAAP EPS **$1.41, +55% y/y**. DC&Comms +>40% y/y; within it, data center +13% q/q / +37% y/y (second consecutive quarter of double-digit sequential growth) and communications +16% q/q / **+60% y/y** on DCI/scale-across. Industrial down modestly but semicap bookings inflecting. Backlog at a record; **orders now extend into CY2028 and LTAs to end of decade**; Q2 FY26 data center book-to-bill exceeded 4x (2026-02-04).

Balance sheet de-levered hard: net debt leverage **0.5x**, down from 1.7x in Q2 and 2.1x a year ago; cash jumped to $3B after **NVIDIA's $2B equity investment** (announced 2026-03-02). Capex stepped up to $290M (vs $154M prior) and guided higher again — pure capacity-add. **Q4 FY26 guide:** revenue $1.91–2.05B, GM 39–41%, non-GAAP EPS **$1.52–1.72** — an implied acceleration. Anderson framed the June quarter as "a new inflection point" and reiterated FY27 growth > FY26.

Operational drivers: 6-inch InP ramp running ahead of plan — internal InP capacity to double by CY-end (now a quarter early), then **more than double again by CY27** (a ~4x over two years). 6-inch yields exceed 3-inch on all three device types; 6-inch produces >4x devices at <half the cost (Sherman TX, Järfälla Sweden, + new Zurich site for early CY27). First 6-inch-content transceivers shipped in Q3, already aiding revenue and GM. 800G to grow in CY26 with 1.6T ramping rapidly (EML + SiPh first, 200G VCSEL-based later) — Coherent (as II-VI) began 1.6T mass shipment in 2Q25, in line with Chinese leaders Innolight/TFC (Bernstein China Next Winners, 2025-10-27). OCS TAM raised to **>$4B**, bottleneck cleared, ramping across two sites. CPO TAM put at **>$15B** ("conservative"), initial scale-out revenue H2 CY26, scale-up H2 CY27; JPM independently sizes the scale-up CPO laser/light-source TAM at **>$9B** for the Broadcom/Coherent/Lumentum group, and views scale-up CPO (vs scale-out, which likely stays pluggable) as the more probable near-term adoption path — a greenfield opportunity rather than a displacement risk (JPM Hardware & Networking, 2025-12-15).

- **InP ramp / margin path (incremental).** Coherent is guiding **a quarter ahead on its 2x InP ramp for cal-26**, then **another 2x in cal-27**; callback messaging is **high-teens data-center revenue growth for the June quarter** with no pushback on further acceleration into FY27 (something around **30%+ FY27 revenue growth**); **6-inch becomes >50% of total InP volume from the September quarter**, when it starts to materially contribute to gross margin (JPM "Networking recap", 2026-05-08). Coherent affirms a path to **>42% gross margin** via 1.6T mix, 6-inch wafers ramping, more internal sourcing, and industrial-side pricing — a series of small levers, not one (BofA CIEN supply-chain call, 2026-06-12).
- **6-inch yield edge vs LITE.** Coherent reports **every single product on 6-inch — EMLs, CW lasers, photodiodes — is yielding better than the prior generation**, not just in aggregate; the divergence vs Lumentum's more hesitant 6-inch stance is partly base-rate (Coherent's prior-gen yields were lower, so relative improvement is larger) (BofA CIEN supply-chain call, 2026-06-12). Demand visibility is "quite strong" with **limited double-ordering** from customers (BofA CIEN supply-chain call, 2026-06-12).
- **NVDA ELS read-through.** Because Lumentum still sounds hesitant on selling the full ELS package to NVDA, the likely medium-term outcome is **NVDA securing Coherent and Fabrinet as its two ELS suppliers** (BofA CIEN supply-chain call, 2026-06-12). As a **large merchant customer of Lumentum's lasers**, Coherent's order behavior is a demand tell — it is "asking for more, not less" (Hurlston, JPM LITE mgmt call, 2026-05-07).

## Debate / thesis
- **Capstone model (house view, built on Jefferies/Blayne Curtis):** revenue $6.3B→$9.1B→$16.6B→$19.6B (CY25-28E, +19/+44/+83/+18%) on the 800G/1.6T DC ramp (DC/Comms mix 68%→88%); pro-forma EPS $4.35 / $8.27 / $19.21 / $23.60; GM ~39%→41%, EBITDA margin ~23%→33%. (Capstone official model — based on Jefferies, 2026-05-26)
- **Bull:** Demand "exceptionally strong with no signs of attenuation," visibility into CY2028, multi-year LTAs that bundle customer upfront capex + demand commitments (Anderson, 2026-05-06). InP vertical integration is the chokepoint — China controls 70%+ of global InP output and Coherent is ~1 year early to 6-inch (@ParadisLabs, 2026-06-13; briefing). NVIDIA's $2B equity stake + multi-year CPO supply deal to end of decade validates the franchise; NVDA 13F also disclosed a 7.79M-share holding and Druckenmiller/Duquesne took a stake (briefings, 2026-05-16/18). Multiple incremental growth vectors layering on (OCS, CPO, Multi-Rail ~$2B+ TAM H1 CY27, thermal/Thermadite + TEGs H2 CY27). Margin runway to the **>42% investor-day target** driven by 6-inch cost, yield and pricing optimization (Luther, 2026-05-06; MS, 2026-06-12). **JPM initiated COHR at Buy/OW** (ref $260.64) as a key beneficiary of broad optical growth and ranks it **top of the optical basket — COHR > LITE > CIEN > GLW > FN** — on diversified revenue drivers, "self-help" margin leverage, AI revenue mix lifting from **~22% (CY25) toward 29% then ~41%**, and ~12% revenue growth. The key differentiator vs Lumentum: COHR's **hybrid (InP + SiPh/VCSEL) manufacturing model is structurally defensive against InP-capacity overbuild**, whereas the SiPh shift (cheaper CW lasers displacing higher-ASP EMLs) is a sharper headwind for a pure-InP merchant model; JPM also flags COHR's VCSEL franchise as underappreciated for eventual scale-out adoption (JPM Hardware & Networking, 2025-12-15). COHR is also tagged among the best-positioned names across the DCI/coherent-pluggable, RLS and amplifier opportunities (JPM Hardware & Networking, 2025-12-05). Sell-side PTs: **Citi (Papa Sylla) Buy $420** — highest bull case, FY28E EPS ~$16, +52% vs Street; **Jefferies (Blayne Curtis) Buy $375**; **Barclays (T. O'Malley) OW $350**; **JPM (Samik Chatterjee) OW**, "buy the dip" (2026-05-07/06-11 briefings).
- **Bear:** Coherent is seen as **less in control of its own capacity than Lumentum** — "it's just taken a little bit longer to ramp" (Meta Marshall, MS, 2026-05-07). On a relative-quality view, Lumentum's CPO lasers look better-specified and Coherent's **liquid-crystal OCS is less field-tested** than Lumentum's MEMS arrays (Mike, Rothschild/Redburn, 2026-05-19). Gross-margin fall-through is underwhelming given the 6-inch ramp and exits — incremental GM only ~44% y/y; "margins leave more to be desired" (O'Malley/Barclays; O'Malley question, 2026-05-06). Citi explicitly **prefers LITE over COHR on a 12-month view** on faster growth (LITE +42% FY26 sales vs COHR +9%; 3-yr CAGR 12% for COHR) and faster GM expansion, and notes LITE — ~50% share of AI EML lasers — is a **key merchant EML supplier to Coherent**, underlining COHR's external dependence on a competitor for a core transceiver input (Citi LITE Initiation, 2025-08-29). Bernstein ranks Coherent (incl. legacy II-VI) the **#2 global transceiver supplier in 2024 behind Innolight**, in a market increasingly dominated by Chinese players (Innolight, Eoptolink, Hisense, Accelink) — a long-run share/competition risk (Bernstein China Next Winners, 2025-10-27). InP substrate is a structural China-supply risk — the CEO reportedly traveled to China with a Trump delegation seeking InP (briefing, 2026-06-15). Stock fell ~11% on 2026-06-10 on CPO-delay concerns (JPM framed as overblown). Valuation: post-NVDA the space trades ~25x CY28 EPS for COHR/LITE — well off its historical teens (MS, 2026-05-07).

## Risks
- **InP / materials supply (10-K, 2025-08-15):** dependence on rare-earth minerals, noble gases and InP substrates; China controls majority of InP output — geopolitical/export-control exposure to a core input. Note ongoing reliance on merchant EML supply from Lumentum (~50% AI EML share) for part of the transceiver portfolio (Citi, 2025-08-29).
- **Execution on capacity ramp:** 6-inch InP yield/qualification risk; OCS production bottlenecks (one cleared Q3, others possible); competitor (Lumentum) seen as further ahead on capacity control.
- **SiPh substitution:** rising silicon-photonics adoption favors cheaper, smaller CW lasers over higher-ASP EMLs — a market-level revenue headwind (sharper for pure-InP merchant models than for COHR's hybrid model, but a watch item) (JPM Hardware & Networking, 2025-12-15).
- **CPO timing & competitive position:** scale-up CPO is H2 CY27 and unproven at volume; copper-vs-optics penetration debate (10–50% by 2030 per Corning internal range); peers question liquid-crystal OCS durability.
- **Competition / share:** Chinese transceiver vendors dominate the global market and COHR ranks #2 behind Innolight (2024) — long-run pricing/share pressure (Bernstein China Next Winners, 2025-10-27).
- **Valuation / multiple:** re-rated to ~25x CY28; any demand-digestion or supply over-build undercuts the equity story.
- **Customer concentration / China end-market:** hyperscaler-concentrated demand; export-control and complex regulatory exposure across a multi-country manufacturing footprint (10-K).
