## Snapshot
Bloom Energy manufactures solid-oxide fuel cells (SOFC) that generate electricity on-site from natural gas or hydrogen, sold primarily to commercial/industrial customers including data centers. The narrative centers on data-center power demand: hyperscalers and AI-infra builders (e.g., Nebius) are signing power-supply deals with BE as grid capacity constraints intensify, validating the "AI factory power" bottleneck thesis alongside [GEV](GEV.md), [VRT](VRT.md), [PWR](PWR.md), and [ETN](ETN.md).
_Note: many "BE" tags in sell-side digests are false positives for BE Semiconductor (BESI) — Bloom-specific coverage is thinner than headline counts suggest._

## Debate / thesis
- **Bull:** Data-center power crunch is a durable demand tailwind for on-site fuel cells, and BE is the most capable SOFC vendor at scale today. Hyperscaler/AI-infra deals validate the thesis — BE struck a deal to help power Nebius's AI buildout (Vital Knowledge, 2026-05-20/21), and @ParadisLabs named BE as a Google $80B supply-chain / data-center power beneficiary (2026-06-03, 2026-06-05). Druckenmiller/Duquesne disclosed a $38.6M position (2026-05-18). BE hit a 52-week high in May 2026 alongside NVDA/TSM/AVGO (@stockmktnewz, 2026-05-15).
- **Bear:** Rothschild Redburn rates BE **Sell** (2026-05-21). Alternative SOFC supply scaling — Delta Electronics (TWN: 2308) demoing a steel-based SOFC using Ceres IP, shipping 30–50 MW in Taiwan, framed as "overflow capacity with inferior specs vs BE today" but a "mild negative for BE moat narrative" (Jason Chips / Computex, 2026-06-07). Jefferies ran a Bull/Bear debate event (2026-06-11) rather than a directional note — signaling unresolved Street debate. Gas-infrastructure bottlenecks gate site addressability — STACK/Oracle NJ site delayed to 2029 on gas-pipeline/permitting constraints, with BE stepping in (SemiAnalysis corpus, ~Jun 2026).
- **Where the sell-side stands:** Rothschild Redburn **Sell** (2026-05-21). SIG/Susquehanna (Chuck Minervino) constructive on guidance trajectory across three 2026 notes, no PT captured. Bernstein (Sunaina Ocalan) previewed an Energy Transition initiation (2026-06-18) describing BE as "counter-consensus" with GEV the top pick — actual rating/PT not in the ported corpus. Barclays energy desk (Stan Solomon) mentions BE in roundups, no formal rating.
