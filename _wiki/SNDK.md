## Snapshot
SanDisk is a pure-play **NAND flash** maker and storage-solutions vendor — flash components plus SSDs, memory cards and drives sold under the SanDisk and WD brands (FY25 10-K, 2025-08-21). It is the **standalone flash business spun out of Western Digital**, which separated on **Feb 21, 2025**; trades on Nasdaq as SNDK with only a short post-spin filing history (one 10-K, six 10-Qs as of Jun-2026). It runs a **single reportable segment** but reports three end-markets: **Data Center, Edge (PC/mobile), and Consumer** (10-K; Q3 FY26 transcript, 2026-04-30). Bits come from the long-standing **Kioxia "Flash Ventures" JV** (BiCS NAND fabs in Yokkaichi & Kitakami, Japan; JV extended to Dec-2034). It competes with Samsung, SK Hynix and YMTC in flash (FY25 10-K). The thesis: NAND is being repriced from a commodity into a strategic AI-storage input — enterprise/QLC SSD for the data-center capacity tier and KV-cache, plus on-device-AI premium storage at the edge. The demand math that underpinned the re-rate: MS sizes an **incremental ~US$29bn AI-NAND TAM by 2029**, with AI NAND reaching **34% of the global NAND market / 431EB (~20% of bit shipments) by 2029**, driven by CSP AI-inference storage (MS AI NAND overview, 2025-09-10).

## Position in the value chain
SanDisk sits in the **middle** of the flash chain: it co-develops and co-manufactures BiCS NAND with **Kioxia** (Flash Ventures JV) plus buys equipment/wafers, turns it into flash + SSD/storage solutions, and sells to **datacenter/enterprise (hyperscalers), client OEMs and consumer** channels. Short post-spin history (standalone since Feb-2025) means limited standalone comparables — model off the WDC-era flash segment plus six quarters of SNDK filings. In NAND, **QLC eSSD** is the structural growth vector: it is "the most optimal storage solution" for the randomized-I/O needs of AI training/inference (pre-trained models, checkpoints, KV-cache off-loading), displacing both TLC NAND and nearline HDD (MS AI NAND overview, 2025-09-10). SNDK still trails Micron and Hynix/Solidigm in eSSD share; the **BiCS 8** transition + controller investment is management's path to close that gap.

## Current state (latest quarter)
**Q3 FY26 (reported 2026-04-30, quarter ended Apr 3, 2026) — a blowout.** Revenue **$5.95B**, +97% q/q, +251% y/y, well past the $4.4–4.8B guide. **Non-GAAP gross margin 78.4%** (vs 51.1% prior q; guide 65–67%); **non-GAAP EPS $23.41** (vs $6.20). Adjusted FCF $2.955B (49.7% margin); cash $3.735B. Crucially, **bits were flat y/y and down high-teens q/q** — the move is entirely **price/mix**, not volume (Q3 FY26 transcript). **Data center $1.467B, +233% q/q** (TLC enterprise SSD + QLC "Stargate" readiness); Edge $3.163B, +118%; Consumer $820M, −10%.

**Business-model shift:** five multiyear supply agreements (NBMs) signed — 3 in Q3, 2 in Q4 — with **$42B minimum contractual revenue** (Q3 contracts) and **>$11B financial guarantees** (incl. ~$400M prepayments), covering **>1/3 of FY27 bit supply** (Goeckeler/Visoso, 2026-04-30). Kioxia JV extended to **Dec-2034**; $1B Nanya investment for DRAM supply. JPM corroborated the LTA layer at its TMC conference and added that SanDisk **extended its undersupply call from end-CY26 to end-CY27**, with the three signed LTAs carrying **floor-and-ceiling pricing and pre-pay-style walkaways with financial guarantees**, and that BiCS 8 (200L+, TLC+QLC) is executing well (JPM "Conf feedback", 2026-05-22). The NBM enforceability mechanics (Bernstein expert, ex-WDC NAND VP Rob, 2026-05-04): the ~$11B guarantee on ~$42B obligations is ~25% — so in a sharp downturn a customer tearing up the deal still leaves SanDisk capturing ~25%, i.e. **a −50% market price would translate to only a ~−25% effective ASP decline** for SanDisk; in practice "tearing up" means renegotiating for more years at a lower price, not walking (Bernstein "HDD/NAND", 2026-05-04).

**Guidance — Q4 FY26:** revenue **$7.75–8.25B**, GM **79–81%**, **EPS $30–33** (158M shares). New **$6B buyback** authorized; remaining $650M term loan repaid; capex held to ~4% of revenue. CY26 data-center growth raised to **mid-70s%** (from 60s%). The trajectory off the spin is steep: Q4 FY25 GM 26.4% / EPS $0.29 (2025-08-14) → Q3 FY26 GM 78.4% / EPS $23.41 in three quarters.

## Debate / thesis
- **Bull:** AI is turning NAND structural. The disk transcripts frame NAND as "the only economically viable solution" for trillion-parameter model capacity (Goeckeler, 2026-04-30), with KV-cache alone an estimated **75–100 incremental exabytes in 2027, doubling thereafter** (Q2 FY26, 2026-01-29). The NBM/LTA layer ($42B backlog, >$11B guarantees, >1/3 of FY27 bits locked) is the re-rating mechanism — management argues it makes earnings "durable, structurally higher… less cyclical." Sell-side validation: **MS (Joseph Moore, OW) raised PT to $1,750 from $1,100** on 2026-06-02/03 ("+59% raise… match published estimates"; briefing 2026-06-03). **GS** went "full bullish mode on memory stocks," citing MU + SNDK (briefing 2026-06-01). SNDK is repeatedly named in the GOOG $80B AI-supply-chain map for NAND (ParadisLabs, briefings 2026-06-03). Tape: hit ATHs alongside the memory complex through May (briefings 2026-05-07).
- **Bear:** This is still NAND, and the print is running entirely on price while bits shrink — the classic late-cycle tell. The cross-read from the [HBM/memory theme](themes/hbm-memory.md) and the on-disk Micron ex-VP call (2026-06-10) is that astronomical memory margins normalize hard once new fab capacity lands ~a year out; the AI buildout is a bull-whip several layers from end demand. A second structural worry: the **eSSD-vs-HDD cannibalization that powers the AI-NAND TAM is maturing** — Bernstein argues peak NAND penetration in datacenter storage has likely passed, with the NAND/HDD ASP ($/GB) premium having stabilized at ~6-7x for business-critical HDD (down from 8.8x but no longer compressing), so the easy share-gain leg is mostly behind (Bernstein on HDD, 2025-10-23). FinTwit flagged the crowding: JEF called SNDK the template for "an extremely crowded stock running into the print with high expectations" (briefing 2026-05-07), and Burry/Krinsky's "Party Like It's 1999" analog cited **SNDK +581% as 1999's 2nd-best NDX name** (briefing 2026-05-07) — i.e., a momentum-blowoff comp. Hedgeye's "$25K → $1.06M in 12 months (43x)" is celebrated as a top-signal, not a fundamental (briefings 2026-05-06/07).
  - **"Too promotional," prefer Kioxia (Jefferies, Conor, 2026-04-07):** Jefferies prefers Kioxia *over* SanDisk because SanDisk "is out there telling you everything" (K-B cash ~75-100 EB targets, HBF, etc.) so any disappointment hurts the stock, whereas conservative Kioxia only surprises up. Jefferies also flagged that the SanDisk CEO publicly **dismissing YMTC as "not a real risk" is "really, really stupid"** — YMTC is ~13% NAND share (Q3'25) heading to ~19% by year-end, has the dominant hybrid-bonding/stacking patent pool, and a clear 400-layer roadmap, so it is a genuine NAND-share threat (the China DUV "Match Act" restrictions hit DRAM, not NAND — YMTC has its own X-stacking IP) (Jefferies "Asia tech", 2026-04-07).
  - **HPF risks being "dead on arrival" vs Kioxia Super IO (Redburn, Conor, 2026-06-23):** Conor prefers Kioxia over SanDisk specifically on the latency angle for agentic AI. SanDisk's **HPF** (its ultra-low-latency / high-IOPS answer to Kioxia's **Super IO / XL Flash**, in an R&D project with Global Unichip) **is not due until 2028** — by which point Kioxia's SLC-based Super IO will have been shipping ~2 years (10M units this year vs 3M last, scaling to 100M next, with NVIDIA as partner). His blunt read: **"if Super IO succeeds, HPF is dead on arrival."** (Note: distinct from SNDK's HBF accelerator-attached flash; HPF is the IO/latency product) (Redburn "Conor O'Mara Asia Trip Takeaways", 2026-06-23).
  - **NAND a notch below DRAM; some 2028 LTA pricing *down* (MS, 2026-06-22):** MS rates NAND fundamentals "8 out of 10" vs DRAM's "10/10," and — while SanDisk has become "everyone's preferred investment" and is consistently the price-setter ("SanDisk is being the bad guy," with Koreans pricing just below) — MS still prefers Micron because **SanDisk looks more expensive on 2028 numbers** after its big move. The caution: some NAND **2028 LTA lock-in prices are *down* from 2027, in some cases "materially so"** (esp. enterprise-grade TLC), though built-in **price escalators** mean the realized price rises if the market is up. Q3 NAND pricing seen up ~20-30% QoQ (MS "Memory & semicap", 2026-06-22).
- **Where the sell-side/buy-side stands:** Bullish and getting more so. **MS OW PT $1,750** (2026-06-03), **GS bullish** (2026-06-01); SNDK structurally featured in hyperscaler-capex supply maps. The debate, as with MU, is no longer direction but **duration** — bulls underwrite multi-year tightness + LTA-locked bits; bears see a price-only blowoff in a commodity that always mean-reverts. No bear-side broker PT on disk; the bear case is voiced via FinTwit and the memory-theme read-through.
- **How the call evolved (early-coverage record):** worth anchoring how fast the Street re-rated — the original bull thesis pre-dates the price spike. **MS (Joe Moore) elevated SNDK to Top Pick, PT $96 (from $70), bull $150 / bear $40** (MS AI NAND overview, 2025-09-10) — base 12x through-cycle EPS $8; bull 15x through-cycle EPS $10 (50%+ GM precedent in prior peaks); bear 8x through-cycle EPS $5, ~book value. The MS framing then was eSSD-driven, not LTA-driven: hyperscalers placing "10s of exabytes" of eSSD orders into a ~250-300EB CY25 market, SNDK raising channel/consumer prices ~10%, BiCS 8 as the eSSD share catalyst. **Bernstein (Mark Newman) initiated OW, PT $120** (Bernstein initiation, 2025-09-15) on a deep-value / replacement-cost case — fab replacement value ~$25B, stock at a 54% discount; valued 10x CY27 EPS (≈13.4x est. $1.3B FY27 FCF); explicit M&A optionality (Micron — whose CEO Mehrotra co-founded SanDisk — and SK Hynix as potential acquirers, cheaper than greenfield in a down-cycle); HBF treated as unmodeled call-option upside. Both PTs ($96 / $120) are now far below the $1,750 / Q3-FY26 print and are superseded — but the structural eSSD / HBF / replacement-cost logic is the same thesis that later re-rated on the LTA layer.

## Catalysts / what to watch
- **Q4 FY26 print** (fiscal year-end June; reported ~Aug-2026) — vs guide rev $7.75–8.25B / GM 79–81% / EPS $30–33. Watch GM past 80%, bit-shipment trajectory, and any further NBM signings / backlog disclosure.
- **NBM / LTA cadence** — incremental multiyear agreements and how much of FY27/FY28 bits get locked (and at fixed vs variable pricing) is the durability catalyst.
- **BiCS 8 ramp** — to majority of bit production exiting FY26 (Q1 FY26, 2025-11-06); QLC "Stargate" hyperscaler revenue ramp. BiCS 8 + controller investment is also the lever to close SNDK's eSSD share gap vs Micron / Hynix-Solidigm (MS AI NAND overview, 2025-09-10). The Bernstein expert (ex-WDC NAND VP, 2026-05-04) flagged that essentially **all high-capacity AI drives (128/256TB) are QLC**, a "very hard" technology where SanDisk + Kioxia "have done quite well" (Samsung struggling, Micron early lead then questions) — so QLC mix is both the growth lever and a real differentiator on the latest node.
- **High Bandwidth Flash (HBF)** — the bull-case optionality on AI accelerator-attached flash; introduced at the Feb-2025 Investor Day, "Best of Show" at FMS. HBF stacks 3D-NAND with TSVs on a base interposer (HBM-like) to give the GPU large NAND capacity on-package, targeting AI inference: **8-16x the capacity of HBM at similar bandwidth (~1.2TB/s)**, ~288-576GB per stack vs ~36GB for HBM3E, enabling up to 4TB of VRAM on a GPU (gen 1). **SK Hynix signed an MOU with SanDisk (Aug-2025)** to standardize the spec and build an ecosystem. **First HBF samples 2H26; first AI-inference devices with HBF early 2027.** Caveat: not "drop-in" compatible with HBM (minor protocol changes, requires GPU-level redesign), and CSP/fabless adoption is still early — watch for design-win commentary from fabless accelerator players first (MS AI NAND overview, 2025-09-10). Bernstein flags HBF as large but un-diligence-able upside, deliberately not in its numbers (Bernstein initiation, 2025-09-15). The ex-WDC NAND VP corroborated HBF as "a direct drop-in replacement for HBM" exploiting NAND's fast *streaming* read for AI-inference coefficient loading — "very promising" but "still a bit early," needing power/performance/reliability proof; he also sees broader AI demand for performance/capacity NAND (KV-cache, high-perf cache tiers à la VAST) (Bernstein "HDD/NAND", 2026-05-04).
- **WDC stake unwind** — UBS (Arcuri) tracked WDC exchanging SNDK shares for WDC stock toward full redemption by Sep-Q (briefings 2026-05-06/07); residual overhang/technical.
- **$6B buyback** execution against the elevated multiple.
- **M&A optionality** — Bernstein flagged SNDK as a plausible target (Micron/SK Hynix) at the pre-spike discount-to-replacement valuation (Bernstein initiation, 2025-09-15); largely moot at the current multiple but still a structural feature of a sub-scale NAND pure-play.

## Risks
- **Commodity cyclicality** — NAND ASPs remain volatile; current results are price-driven with declining bits, the canonical setup for a sharp reversal when capacity normalizes (theme read-through; Micron ex-VP, 2026-06-10).
- **Cannibalization tailwind maturing** — the eSSD-displaces-HDD vector that underwrites the AI-NAND TAM may be later-cycle than bulls assume: Bernstein puts the NAND/HDD ASP gap at a stabilized ~6-7x and peak datacenter NAND penetration likely behind us (Bernstein on HDD, 2025-10-23).
- **Competition** — Samsung, SK Hynix, YMTC and assemblers; YMTC (China) pressure on the low end (FY25 10-K). In eSSD specifically SNDK lags Micron and Hynix/Solidigm on share (MS AI NAND overview, 2025-09-10).
- **Kioxia JV dependence** — bit supply, cost and capex are tied to Flash Ventures; the $1.165B 2026–29 manufacturing payment + JV terms flow through COGS (Q2 FY26, 2026-01-29).
- **Customer concentration** — top-ten customers a large share of revenue (FY25 10-K); hyperscaler demand is lumpy.
- **Short standalone history** — limited as a public company since Feb-2025; thin comparables, evolving capital structure, residual WDC-separation items. Bernstein also flags SNDK's investor communications/disclosures as "confusing," a dissuader for quality-focused investors (Bernstein initiation, 2025-09-15).
- **China / geopolitics** — Unis Venture (China JV) restructuring; export/tariff exposure (FY25 10-K).
- **LTA execution risk** — backlog ($42B) and guarantees are only as good as counterparty follow-through if pricing rolls over.
- **HBF may disappoint** — early-stage, not HBM-drop-in, requires GPU redesign and ecosystem standardization; CSP adoption still tentative (MS AI NAND overview, 2025-09-10).
