## Snapshot
SanDisk is a pure-play **NAND flash** maker and storage-solutions vendor — flash components plus SSDs, memory cards and drives sold under the SanDisk and WD brands (FY25 10-K, 2025-08-21). It is the **standalone flash business spun out of Western Digital**, which separated on **Feb 21, 2025**; trades on Nasdaq as SNDK with only a short post-spin filing history (one 10-K, six 10-Qs as of Jun-2026). It runs a **single reportable segment** but reports three end-markets: **Data Center, Edge (PC/mobile), and Consumer** (10-K; Q3 FY26 transcript, 2026-04-30). Bits come from the long-standing **Kioxia "Flash Ventures" JV** (BiCS NAND fabs in Yokkaichi & Kitakami, Japan; JV extended to Dec-2034). It competes with Samsung, SK Hynix and YMTC in flash (FY25 10-K). The thesis: NAND is being repriced from a commodity into a strategic AI-storage input — enterprise/QLC SSD for the data-center capacity tier and KV-cache, plus on-device-AI premium storage at the edge. The demand math that underpinned the re-rate: MS sizes an **incremental ~US$29bn AI-NAND TAM by 2029**, with AI NAND reaching **34% of the global NAND market / 431EB (~20% of bit shipments) by 2029**, driven by CSP AI-inference storage (MS AI NAND overview, 2025-09-10).

## Position in the value chain
SanDisk sits in the **middle** of the flash chain: it co-develops and co-manufactures BiCS NAND with **Kioxia** (Flash Ventures JV) plus buys equipment/wafers, turns it into flash + SSD/storage solutions, and sells to **datacenter/enterprise (hyperscalers), client OEMs and consumer** channels. Short post-spin history (standalone since Feb-2025) means limited standalone comparables — model off the WDC-era flash segment plus six quarters of SNDK filings. In NAND, **QLC eSSD** is the structural growth vector: it is "the most optimal storage solution" for the randomized-I/O needs of AI training/inference (pre-trained models, checkpoints, KV-cache off-loading), displacing both TLC NAND and nearline HDD (MS AI NAND overview, 2025-09-10). SNDK still trails Micron and Hynix/Solidigm in eSSD share; the **BiCS 8** transition + controller investment is management's path to close that gap.

## Current state (latest quarter)
**Q3 FY26 (reported 2026-04-30, quarter ended Apr 3, 2026) — a blowout.** Revenue **$5.95B**, +97% q/q, +251% y/y, well past the $4.4–4.8B guide. **Non-GAAP gross margin 78.4%** (vs 51.1% prior q; guide 65–67%); **non-GAAP EPS $23.41** (vs $6.20). Adjusted FCF $2.955B (49.7% margin); cash $3.735B. Crucially, **bits were flat y/y and down high-teens q/q** — the move is entirely **price/mix**, not volume (Q3 FY26 transcript). **Data center $1.467B, +233% q/q** (TLC enterprise SSD + QLC "Stargate" readiness); Edge $3.163B, +118%; Consumer $820M, −10%.

**Business-model shift:** five multiyear supply agreements (NBMs) signed — 3 in Q3, 2 in Q4 — with **$42B minimum contractual revenue** (Q3 contracts) and **>$11B financial guarantees** (incl. ~$400M prepayments), covering **>1/3 of FY27 bit supply** (Goeckeler/Visoso, 2026-04-30). Kioxia JV extended to **Dec-2034**; $1B Nanya investment for DRAM supply. JPM corroborated the LTA layer at its TMC conference and added that SanDisk **extended its undersupply call from end-CY26 to end-CY27**, with the three signed LTAs carrying **floor-and-ceiling pricing and pre-pay-style walkaways with financial guarantees**, and that BiCS 8 (200L+, TLC+QLC) is executing well (JPM "Conf feedback", 2026-05-22). The NBM enforceability mechanics (Bernstein expert, ex-WDC NAND VP Rob, 2026-05-04): the ~$11B guarantee on ~$42B obligations is ~25% — so in a sharp downturn a customer tearing up the deal still leaves SanDisk capturing ~25%, i.e. **a −50% market price would translate to only a ~−25% effective ASP decline** for SanDisk; in practice "tearing up" means renegotiating for more years at a lower price, not walking (Bernstein "HDD/NAND", 2026-05-04).

**Guidance — Q4 FY26:** revenue **$7.75–8.25B**, GM **79–81%**, **EPS $30–33** (158M shares). New **$6B buyback** authorized; remaining $650M term loan repaid; capex held to ~4% of revenue. CY26 data-center growth raised to **mid-70s%** (from 60s%). The trajectory off the spin is steep: Q4 FY25 GM 26.4% / EPS $0.29 (2025-08-14) → Q3 FY26 GM 78.4% / EPS $23.41 in three quarters.

**Stock-price rally arc (May–June 2026):** the tape itself became the story. Barron's "Inside Scoop" (2026-05-18) had SNDK **+465% YTD vs +15% for the Nasdaq 100**, "driven higher by strong fiscal third-quarter earnings and the sustained demand for memory chips" from the AI datacenter buildout, with WDC and Seagate "more than doubled in 2026" and Micron cited as moving in lockstep (Barron's, 2026-05-18). Two weeks later Barron's "Up & Down Wall Street" (2026-06-01) had it at **+523% YTD**, explicitly framing SNDK as a **1990s-tech-boom echo**: "a memory chip maker, [that] got up to $81 in 2000, then plummeted to below $5... In 2016, it was bought by Western Digital, then spun out again just last year" — "ghosts of tech runs past... they're baaaaack" (Barron's, 2026-06-01). ## Debate / thesis
- **Bull:** AI is turning NAND structural. The disk transcripts frame NAND as "the only economically viable solution" for trillion-parameter model capacity (Goeckeler, 2026-04-30), with KV-cache alone an estimated **75–100 incremental exabytes in 2027, doubling thereafter** (Q2 FY26, 2026-01-29). The NBM/LTA layer ($42B backlog, >$11B guarantees, >1/3 of FY27 bits locked) is the re-rating mechanism — management argues it makes earnings "durable, structurally higher… less cyclical." Sell-side validation: **MS (Joseph Moore, OW) raised PT to $1,750 from $1,100** on 2026-06-02/03 ("+59% raise… match published estimates"; briefing 2026-06-03). **GS** went "full bullish mode on memory stocks," citing MU + SNDK (briefing 2026-06-01). SNDK is repeatedly named in the GOOG $80B AI-supply-chain map for NAND (ParadisLabs, briefings 2026-06-03). Tape: hit ATHs alongside the memory complex through May (briefings 2026-05-07).
- **Bear:** This is still NAND, and the print is running entirely on price while bits shrink — the classic late-cycle tell. The cross-read from the [HBM/memory theme](themes/hbm-memory.md) and the on-disk Micron ex-VP call (2026-06-10) is that astronomical memory margins normalize hard once new fab capacity lands ~a year out; the AI buildout is a bull-whip several layers from end demand. A second structural worry: the **eSSD-vs-HDD cannibalization that powers the AI-NAND TAM is maturing** — Bernstein argues peak NAND penetration in datacenter storage has likely passed, with the NAND/HDD ASP ($/GB) premium having stabilized at ~6-7x for business-critical HDD (down from 8.8x but no longer compressing), so the easy share-gain leg is mostly behind (Bernstein on HDD, 2025-10-23). FinTwit flagged the crowding: JEF called SNDK the template for "an extremely crowded stock running into the print with high expectations" (briefing 2026-05-07), and Burry/Krinsky's "Party Like It's 1999" analog cited **SNDK +581% as 1999's 2nd-best NDX name** (briefing 2026-05-07) — i.e., a momentum-blowoff comp. Hedgeye's "$25K → $1.06M in 12 months (43x)" is celebrated as a top-signal, not a fundamental (briefings 2026-05-06/07).

  - **Bernstein part 2 — case studies show the LTAs have "real teeth"; PT reiterated $3,000 (2026-07-09):** Newman's follow-up (part 2 of the New Memory LTA series) benchmarks SanDisk's contracts against two *failed* legacy LTA structures — Microchip's **Preferred Supply Program** (12-mo rolling non-cancelable/non-reschedulable backlog, no cash, relaxed to 6mo Aug-2023, discontinued for new orders Feb-2024 — reputational, never enforced) and **Hemlock's polysilicon take-or-pay** (legally bulletproof but uncollectible against insolvent solar buyers) — to argue the New Memory LTAs are categorically stronger on **three structural axes**: (1) **upfront, back-end-weighted financial commitment** (cash deposits / letters of credit / third-party guarantees, with the coverage ratio of remaining collateral to remaining obligations rising toward **75-100% of remaining RPO in the outer years**, exactly when downturn risk is highest — vs Hemlock's static, sue-to-collect terms); (2) **better counterparty quality** (investment-grade, revenue-diversified hyperscalers vs thinly-capitalized/diffuse customer bases); (3) **structural AI demand** (high-end memory sold out through 2026 into 2027) vs Microchip's shortage-driven double-order glut. The load-bearing SNDK figure, restated cleanly: **SanDisk backs an estimated $69B of remaining performance obligations with >$11B of guarantees, most sitting on (escrow-like) third-party balance sheets** — the same ~$69.3bn RPO base modeled in the 06-29 note, and *distinct* from the **$42B minimum contractual revenue** (3 Q3 NBMs) management discloses in Current state (a narrower metric, not a contradiction). BofA's desk (Fenske) called the 40-50% FY28 EPS revision across SNDK/STX/WDC an unusually large one-day raise — yet Jefferies (Favuzza) flagged SNDK trading -3.8% pre-market the same morning (07-01) as part of a broader leader/laggard rotation, even after being the single best-performing >$5bn stock in 1H26 (+765%) and a top-6 YTD contributor to both the Nasdaq 100 and S&P 500. Net: fundamental estimate revisions and price action are diverging session-to-session — the durability thesis is being re-underwritten by the sell-side even as the tape itself rotates away from the name. **Into the 2Q print (07-03/07-05) the re-underwriting continued:** UBS (Gaudois, Memory Semis Monthly, 07-03) raised its NAND contract-pricing path to +30%/+12% QoQ 3Q/4Q26 (3Q up from +17%) after +67% in 2Q and pushed the NAND upcycle out to at least 4Q27 — a direct supply-side corroboration of SNDK's undersupply thesis (though UBS's NAND Buys are the Asian names, not SNDK). GS (Schneider, 07-05) then raised its SNDK PT to $2,200 (from $1,200) at 20x normalized EPS $110 (from $55), with CY26 EPS >~30% above Street — making GS the fourth distinct bank in a two-week window to lift numbers, and reinforcing that the debate is duration (NAND tight potentially longer than DRAM), not direction. **Bernstein's part-2 note (Newman, 07-09)** then reframes the durability case historically: by benchmarking the New Memory LTAs against two *failed* legacy structures (Microchip's no-cash PSP that collapsed into an analog glut; Hemlock's legally-airtight-but-uncollectible polysilicon take-or-pay), it argues SNDK's contracts differ on three structural axes — upfront back-end-weighted collateral (coverage → 75-100% of RPO in the outer years), investment-grade counterparties, and structural AI demand — restating SNDK's ~$69B RPO / >$11B guarantees and reiterating PT $3,000 with the "meaningful but not infinite" downside-protection framing. It is the clearest articulation yet of *why* the LTA layer should carry a higher multiple, but explicitly stops short of claiming a floor at any price.
