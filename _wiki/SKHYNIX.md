## Snapshot
SK hynix is the world's #1 high-bandwidth memory (HBM) maker and the #2 DRAM/NAND producer overall, and is **NVIDIA's primary HBM supplier**. Near-monopoly HBM3E history — its HBM tool/process share into NVDA was ~100% "until last fall" (SemiAnalysis HBM). UBS models 2026 industry HBM share at **SK Hynix / Samsung / Micron = 56% / 22% / 22%**, with SK hynix "already shipping HBM4" and larger volumes by 2Q26 (UBS 2026 overview, Dec-2025) — i.e. share normalizing off the historical ~90% as Samsung/Micron qualify. FY2025 revenue 97.1tn KRW (+47% YoY), operating profit 47.2tn (+101%), 49% OP margin (SK hynix IR FY25, 2026-01-29). Mix: DRAM (incl. HBM) is the profit engine; NAND/enterprise SSD via wholly-owned **Solidigm**, where the group's eSSD share reached 30.2% in Q4-2025 (search digest, 2026-06); SK Hynix+Solidigm is modeled at ~36% of 2026e QLC eSSD output, the largest single bloc (MS AI NAND overview). It sits at the demand epicentre of the AI capex cycle: HBM has been "sold out since 2023," 2026 DRAM/HBM volume is contracted out (Q3 2025 call, 2025-10-29), and vendors reaffirm the 2026 sold-out/allocation tone (DeepResearch TSM, citing SK hynix/Micron commentary).

## Position in the value chain
SK hynix converts silicon wafers and litho/etch/deposition tools (ASML EUV, AMAT, LRCX) into DRAM + HBM stacks and NAND/eSSD, then ships to NVIDIA (primary HBM customer, into the Rubin platform), other accelerator/hyperscaler buyers, and mobile/PC OEMs. Its moat is HBM process + advanced packaging lead (HBM3E 12-high, first-to-mass-production HBM4) plus a net-cash balance sheet (35tn KRW at Q1-2026) funding the M15X / Yongin / Indiana packaging build-out. On DRAM node mix it also leads Samsung: ~40-50% of SK hynix DRAM bits on 1a-nm-or-more-advanced nodes vs Samsung ~30% (Micron 70%+) — a key reason for Samsung's relative de-rating (Bernstein Semis deck, May-2025). HBM packaging hinges on a few specialist tools: SK hynix's thermocompression (TC) bonding for HBM3E 12-high has run almost entirely on Hanmi tools; a Q2-2025 dispute (Hanmi pulled field service) showed how concentrated this dependency is, with ASMPT/Besi/Hanwha alternatives not yet qualified (SemiAnalysis HBM).

HBM4 demand "far exceeding current supply capacity," supplying HBM3E + HBM4 simultaneously. **Capacity build-out — narrowing the DRAM gap, dominant in HBM (StoneX/TrendForce "Memory expert call — Avril", 2026-06-23):** SK Hynix exits 2026 the #2 DRAM maker at **~610K wafers/month** (vs Samsung ~740K) but reaches **710K by end-2027, narrowing the gap to Samsung** (which adds only ~45K). It remains the **dominant HBM supplier with the largest HBM wafer cap at ~200K end-2026** (vs Samsung 180K, Micron 100K). **Yongin FAB1 (200-250K, geometry-dependent)** is expected ready end-2027, but chip output only lands in 2028 given equipment install (~1-1.5 quarters) plus the 2-quarter wafer-in-to-wafer-out cycle; **Yongin FAB2 was just announced** and is "far along." Clean-room space — not equipment or demand — is the binding constraint through end-2028. **Risk:** Samsung's improving HBM4 raises a real possibility it overtakes SK Hynix as #1 HBM supplier in 2027 (same source; cross-ref SAMSUNG). **Anthropic** is pursuing a deal with SK Hynix (server DRAM + eSSD 2027 / HBM 2028), implying an in-house Anthropic ASIC (same source).

**HBM4 cadence / pricing (expert detail, Jefferies Techknowledge, 2025-12-17):** >70% of SK hynix's 2026 HBM bits are still HBM3 (12-high crossover hit in April); **HBM4 does not cross over in 2026 — blended ≤50% of mix**, ramping in 2H26. HBM pricing "finalized" and tracking at start-of-year expectations, with SK hynix guiding **HBM ASP +40-50% YoY in 2026**; cannot renegotiate HBM3E prices and there is "more upside in demand but limited capacity." Blended HBM margin can dip in 1H26 (less HBM4 in the mix), then hold once HBM4 is mainstream. SK hynix frames **~70% OP margins as the level "needed to maintain R&D," and "not high enough"** to fund the greenfield/hybrid-bonding capex step-up — pushing back on the peak-margin bear narrative. It is capacity-rationing non-NVDA: with limited M15X output it prioritizes HBM4 and "cannot increase non-NVIDIA above 20%" in 2026, having declined ASIC/AMD volume (Jefferies Techknowledge, 2025-12-17).

## Risks
- **Commodity cyclicality / ASP reversal** — peak ~72% OP margin is not a through-cycle level; capacity additions (own + China CXMT/YMTC) normalize pricing. CXMT aggressively expanding HBM (HBM2 8-high mass production from 1H25, TSV capacity nearing Micron's by YE); Huawei building its own HBM supply chain (SemiAnalysis HBM).
- **HBM share loss / customer concentration** — UBS 2026 HBM share of 56% (vs ~90% historically); NVDA encouraging Samsung to ramp; Samsung's HBM4 self-described as best-in-class (Jefferies Techknowledge, 2025-12-17; UBS 2026 overview).
- **HBM tool dependency** — concentrated TC-bonding reliance on Hanmi for HBM3E 12-high; ASMPT/Besi/Hanwha not yet fully qualified (SemiAnalysis HBM).
- **Demand destruction** — elevated DRAM/NAND prices crimp PC/mobile bit demand.
- **Capex intensity / funding** — large greenfield fab + packaging build-out (Yongin, M15X, Indiana) into a possible cycle peak; rising hybrid-bonding/R&D cost; Korea construction delays (Daifuku via Jefferies Techknowledge, 2025-12-17). Funding mechanism corroborated tool-side: ASML's DRAM customers only placed (prepaid) EUV orders **after receiving hyperscaler prepayments** — i.e. the LTA/deposit chain is what de-risked the capex (BofA ASML callback, 2026-04-15).
- **Litho/tool intensity & EUV bottleneck risk** — Hynix's DRAM expansion is increasingly EUV-layer-intensive (1C ~6-7 EUV layers, a prospective 1D ~10 layers ~2028), but legacy capacity adds still pull **immersion**: e.g. **SK Hynix's Wuxi (China) fab runs no EUV**, so capacity growth there is immersion-driven — a China-exposure/tool-mix nuance (BofA ASML callback, 2026-04-15).
- **Korea/macro & FX** — KRW reporting, geopolitical/export-control exposure, China memory entrants.
- **Solidigm/NAND** — fragmented, structurally less profitable than DRAM, though MS sees Solidigm as a key NAND-upcycle beneficiary on high QLC eSSD exposure and fully-depreciated, low-cost fabs (MS AI NAND overview); HBM-driven DRAM mix-shift squeezing NAND wafers cuts both ways.
