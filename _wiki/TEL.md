## Snapshot
TE Connectivity is a global leader in **connectivity and sensor technologies** — engineered connectors, terminals, sensors, relays, heat-shrink, wire/cable and antennas that connect and distribute **power, data and signal** in harsh environments. Post-FY24 reorg it runs **two segments**: **Transportation Solutions 54%** of sales (auto 75% of segment, commercial transport 15%, sensors 10%) and **Industrial Solutions 46%** (digital data networks 28%, automation & connected living 27%, aerospace/defense/marine 19%, energy 17%) (10-K FY25, 2025-11-10). FY2025 revenue **$17.4bn, +8.9% y/y** (+6.4% organic); ~93,000 employees, ~55,000 in manufacturing (10-K FY25). The live story for a tech/semis book is the **AI datacenter ramp inside Industrial** — DDN/AI revenue going from ~$300m (FY24) to >$800m (FY25) to **approaching $2.4bn in FY26** (FQ2 FY26 call, 2026-04-22) — layered on a grid/energy build-out and a still-soft auto base. Connector/interconnect peer set: APH, Molex, Aptiv, Sumitomo, Yazaki, Sensata; on AI power/connectors it's read alongside APH and FLEX.

## Position in the value chain
TE sits in the **middle of the electronics chain**: it buys raw materials (metals — its largest purchase category — plus oil-based resins/plastics) and components, then designs and manufactures the connectors, terminals and sensors that physically move **power and signal** through a system, and sells into auto, AI datacenter, industrial/automation and energy/grid customers. The AI-relevant content is **rack power distribution and high-speed signal interconnect** — including busbars, power connectors and cabling tied to the shift to **800V / HVDC** rack architectures, where TE is cited (with APH/FLEX) as a connector-content beneficiary (JPM "Watts Up!", briefing 2026-06-09; Citi, briefing 2026-06-15). JPM scopes TE's datacenter offer as **end-to-end** — high-speed data cables, backplane connectors, over-the-board interconnects, power interconnect and **liquid-cooled busbars** — translating to **at least ~$870 of content per chip**, with signal content rising on the **224G → 448G per-lane** transition (JPM, 2025-12-15). TE's VP/Head of ICT framed **HVDC (400V→800V)** as cutting energy loss 2-5% per conversion stage with liquid cooling mainstream by 2030 (expert call, briefing 2026-06-18).

## Debate / thesis
- **Bull:** TE is a direct, lower-beta way to own AI datacenter power and signal interconnect with a self-funding capital-return profile. The AI ramp is steep and visible — **~$300m (FY24) → >$800m (FY25) → ~$2.4bn (FY26)**, raised twice in the last two prints, with DDN orders +60% and book-to-bill 1.12 building 2027 backlog (FQ1/FQ2 FY26 calls). The ramp was already tracking ahead before that: Jefferies noted TE **tripled AI sales to ~$900m in FY25** on hyperscaler ramps and guided **+$600m of AI growth in FY26 (~65%+ y/y)** (Jefferies, 2025-11-10) — the company later lifted the FY26 target to ~$2.4bn. JPM frames the DC opportunity around **content per chip of at least ~$870**, with TE one of the **top-3 connector companies** addressing datacenter via end-to-end high-speed data cables, backplane connectors, over-the-board interconnects, power interconnect and **liquid-cooled busbars**; AI/Cloud guided **$1.4bn (2025) → implied ~$2.2bn (2026)** with a history of over-delivery vs guide, plus tailwinds from rising compute density, the **224G → 448G per-lane** step-up, and higher rack wattage (JPM, 2025-12-15). JPM also flags **NVDA share-gain optionality** — TE is a minority player at NVDA today vs ~one-third share in the market outside NVDA — and that TE trades at **~22x vs APH ~34x**, a wider-than-historical 4-10x discount that could compress on AI delivery (JPM, 2025-12-15). JPM (Samik Chatterjee) "Watts Up!" names **APH, FLEX, TEL, WOLF best positioned** on the 800V AI-DC power-stack rewiring (briefing 2026-06-09). Industrial margins are inflecting hard (+260-500 bps y/y in recent quarters) and capex is being deployed into demand, not speculatively.
- **Bear:** AI/DDN is still only ~$2.4bn of a $17-18bn company — **Transportation (54% of sales) is the majority and auto is soft** (auto -4% organic, NA production "a little worse," auto outperformance at the low end of the 4-6% target; FQ2 FY26 call). JPM's bear case: **EV demand headwinds** limit near-term catalysts — roughly two-thirds of the targeted growth outlook is EV-linked (powertrain plus rich data-connectivity content) and global EV penetration for 2027 has been cut to **~25% (from ~30% late-2024, ~35% late-2023)**; and the **portfolio mix tilts more cyclical than secular** (high auto/commercial-truck weight), capping the valuation multiple vs peers (JPM, 2025-12-15). The 800V opportunity is real but timing is contested — @bubbleboi channel checks argue rack-level GaN/SiC/connector content is "2-3 years before hitting earnings" with no near-term DC-construction pull (briefing 2026-06-15, on the broader 800V basket). Input-cost inflation (metals, resins, freight) and clean-energy project "pauses" are live margin watch items (FQ1/FQ2 FY26 calls). It's a connector beneficiary, not the design-win owner of the AI silicon socket.
- **Where the sell-side/buy-side stands:** **JPM = Neutral, ref price $230.16** (Samik Chatterjee, 2025-12-15) — long-term AI leverage and content growth across multiple end-markets balanced by the **2025 re-rating**; JPM models TE AI revenue at **6% of sales (2025) → 8% (2026)** (JPM coverage scorecard via Jefferies Techknowledge, 2025-12-17). **Jefferies = BUY, ref price $247.76** (2025-11-10). Both houses are constructive on the AI-DC power/signal content story; JPM's hold is a valuation/cyclical-mix call, not a demand call. JPM also constructive on the 800V infrastructure read-through and underappreciated optical optionality (briefing 2026-06-09). Citi groups TEL with APH on connector content into the 800V/Rubin Ultra ramp (briefings 2026-06-15).

## Catalysts / what to watch
- **FQ3 FY26 print (~late Jul 2026):** delivery vs the ~$5.0bn / $2.83 guide; whether FY26 AI revenue tracks/exceeds the ~$2.4bn target and 2027 backlog keeps building.
- **800V / HVDC rack adoption curve:** NVDA 800V "Rubin Ultra" ramp (2H27 per Citi/desk reads) — the content-per-rack and timing debate is the swing for TEL/APH connector revenue (briefings 2026-06-15/18).
- **NVDA share gains:** TE is a minority player at NVDA today vs ~one-third share outside NVDA — closing that gap is a key upside driver to the AI top-line (JPM, 2025-12-15).
- **Copper-vs-optical mix & RampPhotonics integration:** how much scale-out optical TE captures alongside the copper "workhorse" (FQ2 FY26 call).
- **Auto / EV inflection:** NA production stabilization and a return toward the 4-6% content-outperformance target; ~two-thirds of the growth outlook is EV-linked, so the cut to ~25% 2027 EV penetration is the main drag (FQ2 FY26 call; JPM, 2025-12-15).
- **Investor Day follow-through:** product showcase / mid-term framing from the Nov 20, 2025 event and any updated AI-content TAM (FQ4 FY25 call).

## Risks
- **End-market cyclicality / auto weight:** Transportation is 54% of sales and auto is the largest single end market; NA auto production softness and the EV/charging mix shift drive Transportation results. JPM views the portfolio as tilting more cyclical than secular vs peers, capping the multiple (10-K FY25; FQ2 FY26 call; JPM, 2025-12-15).
- **AI concentration & timing:** the growth narrative leans on hyperscale DDN; demand can "fluctuate significantly" (10-K FY25, DDN end-market language) and the 800V content ramp timing is contested (briefing 2026-06-15).
- **Input-cost inflation:** metals (largest purchase category), oil-based resins and freight; spot-market exposure pressures margin (FQ1/FQ2 FY26 calls).
- **FX:** non-USD revenue meaningful — FX added ~4.4% to FQ2 reported growth and can reverse (10-Q FQ2 FY26).
- **Energy project pauses:** clean-energy slippage within the otherwise strong grid/energy book (FQ2 FY26 call).
- **Tariffs / trade & tax:** Irish-domiciled (plc) with global manufacturing — trade policy, tariffs and tax-regime changes are structural exposures (10-K FY25).
