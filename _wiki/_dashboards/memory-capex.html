Basis traps & caveats — read before quoting numbers from this page
$/Gb vs $/GB (8×). HBM quoted per-GB (Redburn $30-32, Bernstein $53) and per-Gb (TrendForce HBM4 $2.05→$5/Gb ≈ $16→$40/GB). Server DDR5 spot "~$2/Gb" = ~$16/GB. Never mix.
Demand ≠ shipments. All UBS bit inputs are labeled demand; in a sold-out market supplier $ built on demand bits biases up (HBM 2027: UBS demand 58 vs Redburn allocation-cut supply 50 bn Gb — knob provided).
Baskets. Top-4 capex (MSFT/AMZN/GOOGL/META) ≠ all-buyer DC capex (adds ORCL, neoclouds, OpenAI, sovereign, enterprise). Model memory is all-buyer; the top-4 denominator overstates share and is kept only as a memo. All-buyer 2027 $1,450bn is PARTIAL/EST — skeptic argues up to ~$1,650bn (knob).
Supplier vs embedded. Hyperscalers buy DDR5/SSD/HDD ~direct (1.0×) but HBM arrives inside NVDA/AVGO silicon at a markup (Redburn: NVDA ~75% margin on HBM = 4×; ASIC pass-through ~1.1-1.2×; base blend 3×). BOM-share claims (MS 25-30% of VR200; Aletheia 45→70%+) sit between the two bases — underdetermined, both shown.
Attribution flag. Conor O'Mara is tagged Jefferies on the 2026-04-07 call but Redburn on the 2026-06-23 note in the corpus — unresolved inconsistency, numbers unaffected.
