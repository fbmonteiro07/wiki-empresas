The core thesis: “degross, not deterioration”

Every high-conviction semis voice (Serenity, Tae Kim, Paradis) reads the move as forced cross-name liquidation, not a fundamental break. The tell is the indiscriminacy — and the tape itself ($NVDA green while $MU/$LITE/$INTC bleed = “a pod is blowing up,” Tae Kim).

Against the prices, the fundamental datapoints are almost all positive: SK Hynix's US$28bn IPO multiple-times oversubscribed + US$51bn new fab; Samsung record operating profit (+19x y/y); HBM $/rack quintupling into Vera Rubin; foundry/packaging demand at 130–150% of capacity; TSMC set to guide up (3Q +13% vs. cons. +9%). The bearish friction is narrative (Kyber/Rubin-Ultra) + rotation, not the demand curve.

Macro overlay (the wildcard): Hormuz re-escalation, Iran's oil waiver revoked, Brent back into backwardation with speculative positioning at its net-short of the year and the SPR at a 43-year low → asymmetric upside risk in oil. FOMC minutes today, 2:00pm ET. This is what could turn an orderly degross into a broad risk-off.
