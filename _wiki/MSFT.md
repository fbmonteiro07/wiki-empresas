## Snapshot
Mega-cap software + hyperscale cloud, three reporting segments: Productivity & Business Processes (M365/Office, LinkedIn, Dynamics), Intelligent Cloud (Azure, server, GitHub, enterprise services) and More Personal Computing (Windows OEM, Search/Bing, Gaming/Xbox). In the AI stack MSFT sits across every layer it can monetize: own datacenters + first-party silicon (Maia 200 accelerator, Cobalt 200 CPU), the agent app platform (Foundry, Agent 365, IQ/Work IQ context layer), and high-value agentic apps (M365 Copilot, GitHub Copilot, Security Copilot, Dragon). Anchor relationship with OpenAI (equity stake + frontier-model IP rights to 2032 + Azure compute customer), plus Anthropic as both a model on Foundry and an Azure compute customer. Microsoft Cloud cleared $54.5B in revenue in the latest quarter; AI ARR >$37B, +123% y/y (Q3 FY26, 2026-04-29). OpenAI is the spine of the Azure AI story: UBS estimates ~half of Azure's 39% cc growth came from AI workloads (16pts disclosed in 3Q/Mar) and pegged run-rate Azure AI revenue at ~$18.6B as of 4Q/Jun-25, with substantially all of it OpenAI-tied (UBS OpenAI deep-dive, 2025-09-02); MS models OpenAI explaining 49% of Azure AI growth in CY25 and 100%+ in CY26 (MS MSFT/OpenAI, 2025-05-21). OpenAI's training supercluster spend is off-P&L (netted against MSFT's equity stake, not in Azure revs/COGS/capex), while inference compute and the rev-share land in reported Azure (UBS, 2025-09-02).

## Current state (latest quarter)
**Q3 FY26 (reported 2026-04-29):** Revenue $82.9B, +18% (+15% cc); EPS $4.27, +21% y/y (ex-OpenAI impact); operating margin 46%; company GM 68%, down y/y on AI infra. Microsoft Cloud $54.5B, +29% (GM 66%).
- **Azure +40% cc**, ahead of consensus, capacity delivered earlier than planned (Fairwater Wisconsin online 6 weeks early). Q4 FY26 guide: Azure +39–40% cc, with management expecting **modest acceleration in 2H CY26** vs 1H; supply expected to remain **constrained at least through 2026** (Q3 FY26). JPM corroborates the supply backdrop — MSFT "expects to be capacity-constrained through at least the end of the fiscal year," demand "significantly ahead of capacity" (JPM AI Capex & Financing, 2025-11-10).
- **Azure AI economics:** MS estimates Azure AI revenue $18.2B/$30.5B in CY25/CY26, with OpenAI-related mix climbing from ~27% of Azure AI revenue (3Q24) toward ~62% (4Q26e); on MS math, OpenAI explains 100%+ of CY26 Azure AI growth, implying upside to the Street's ~27.5% CY26 Azure growth est. if non-OpenAI ("Other") rev holds (MS MSFT/OpenAI, 2025-05-21). UBS: the early-2025 renegotiation gives MSFT a ROFR on new OpenAI capacity, but there's "no evidence it's been exercised" as OpenAI signed $100B+ with ORCL/CRWV/Google — ROFR "may not have teeth" in practice (UBS, 2025-09-02).
- **M365 Copilot:** record seat adds, +250% y/y; **>20M paid seats** (vs 15M in Q2 FY26, 2026-01-28); customers >50k seats quadrupled y/y; Accenture >740k seats. Weekly engagement now "at the same level as Outlook" (Satya, Q3 FY26). Broader Copilot umbrella (Sales/Finance/Service/Security + GitHub) >100M MAU (SemiAnalysis MSFT AI Strategy, 2025-11-12).
- **Capex $31.9B** in Q3 (down sequentially from $37.5B in Q2 FY26); ~2/3 short-lived assets (GPU/CPU). **CY26 capex guided ~$190B, of which ~$25B is higher component pricing (memory)**; Q4 capex steps to >$40B (Q3 FY26).
- **OpenAI:** ~45% of $625B commercial RPO was OpenAI-related as of Q2 FY26; ex-OpenAI RPO grew 28%. Restructured deal — MSFT keeps royalty-free frontier-model IP to 2032, **no longer pays rev-share to OpenAI**, while retaining rev-share *from* OpenAI through 2030 (margin-accretive; Q3 FY26 + JEF NDR recap, 2026-06-12). Equity-method drag: MSFT books its share of OpenAI losses in "Other, net" — $2.9B of equity-method losses 3FQ25 YTD; MS flags EPS growth accelerates in CY27 as cumulative OpenAI losses hit the cap at MSFT's initial investment (~$13B) and the drag rolls off — a tailwind not in the multiple, with MSFT at ~28x CY26 GAAP EPS (~2.0x PEG) (MS MSFT/OpenAI, 2025-05-21).
- FY27 framing: "another year of double-digit revenue and operating income growth"; headcount down y/y; opex growth mid-to-high single digits (Q3 FY26).
- **AI ARR $37B, +123% y/y**; Bernstein estimates the majority of SaaS AI ARR (~$6-7B) is M365 Copilot, with paid seats +33% q/q to 20M and >5M adds guided next quarter; flags >11% of total MSFT revenue is now growing triple digits — AI a growth driver, not a headwind (Bernstein "Cloud in the Quarter", 2026-06-22). Commercial RPO $627B, +26% ex-OpenAI / +99% incl. OpenAI's $250B+ commitment; weighted-avg duration 2.5yrs (Bernstein "Cloud in the Quarter", 2026-06-22).
- **GitHub usage exploding** — code commits ran from ~1B to ≥14B/run-rate as of Apr-2026 on AI coding tools, straining traditional CPU datacenter capacity; MSFT plans to rent AWS capacity for the GitHub CPU demand rather than divert from Azure (Bernstein "Cloud in the Quarter", 2026-06-22).
- **Funding curve — the laggard pre-funder (JPM "AI Capex 2.0", 2026-06-16):** alone among the five hyperscalers, MSFT has funded the buildout **entirely from internal cash** — **no IG bond or equity issuance in 5+ years**, a net debt repayer (~$46bn gross debt). Instead it leans on **finance leases (~$63bn at F3Q) plus ~$197bn of data-center leases not yet commenced through FY31**. JPM models capex **~$88bn FY25 → ~$140bn FY26 → ≥$240bn FY27 → ~$300bn FY28** (the $190bn CY26 outlook implies the FY27 run-rate), with the funding gap widening to ~$90bn FY27 / ~$130bn FY28 once dividends/buybacks are counted. JPM sees MSFT "~1 year behind Alphabet and Amazon on the funding curve" and points to "sizable new supply from MSFT in C2H26 and beyond" — testing AAA territory the market has long treated as risk-free (JPM "AI Capex 2.0", 2026-06-16).
- **Token-budget exhaustion (JPM "AI Capex 2.0", 2026-06-16):** JPM cites Microsoft (with Uber) as an enterprise that **exhausted its full-year 2026 token budget within months** as agents entered production — a demand-side datapoint for agentic-inference compute intensity. Maia 200 reiterated at **~30% better perf/$** vs the latest hardware in MSFT's own fleet.

## Debate / thesis
- **Bull:** Bernstein reiterates **Outperform, TP $646**, calling MSFT strongly positioned in an AI-bubble case — first-party capacity usage ramping via a diversified app base, third-party contracts beyond OpenAI, and contract duration well-aligned with equipment useful life; Azure stable→accelerating with GAAP margins in the mid-40s, and current valuation an "attractive entry point" (Bernstein "Cloud in the Quarter", 2026-06-22). Bernstein estimates Azure GAAP gross margin ~56.4% in 3FQ26 (vs ~61.5% y/y) and frames AI-vs-non-AI margins converging as MSFT manages opex to flat FY26 op margin (Bernstein "Cloud in the Quarter", 2026-06-22). AI is a real, large engine, not eating SaaS — Copilot is sold separately, not bundled/discounted, seat growth accelerating into E7 launch (Bernstein hyperscaler review, 2026-04-30). Margins expanded despite record capex; Bernstein's read is this "rerates" exactly like the prior cloud transition — opex grows slower than revenue, and capex eventually grows slower than cloud revenue, inflecting AI/Azure margins up (Bernstein, 2026-04-30). First-party silicon (Maia 200 >30% better tokens/$, Cobalt in ~half of regions) and first-party MAI models lower COGS; FinTwit flags MSFT at ~16.8x 2028E as "stupid cheap," targeting ~50% of AI compute on own silicon by 2028 (@ParadisLabs, 2026-06-15). Ackman built a new core MSFT position at ~21x fwd, M365+Azure ~70% of profits (13F, 2026-05-15/18). GS a "Buyer" post-Build (Borges, 2026-06-07). MS reiterates **OW $482**, durable mid-teens EPS CAGR, and sees the GAAP PEG gap (1.7x ex-OpenAI loss vs ~1.9x large-cap avg on CY27) as closeable — plus any OpenAI equity value as free upside (MS MSFT/OpenAI, 2025-05-21).
- **Bear:** Capex is the battleground — MS/Altimeter revised CY27 capex est. from $178B to **$276B**, the single biggest hyperscaler revision (@Midnight_Captl, 2026-06-13); 1-yr return ~ -18% with the "lowest ROI on AI capex to-date" critique (@ParadisLabs, 2026-06-12). SemiAnalysis quantifies the ROIC problem: MSFT's own AI ROIC is "not much higher than ORCL's ~20%" once you strip the OpenAI rev-share (set to end 2030–2032), vs 35–40% blended MSFT today (SemiAnalysis MSFT AI Strategy, 2025-11-12). The "Big Pause" (CY24 datacenter freeze) ceded share: MSFT paused >3.5GW that would have been built by 2028, dropped out of multi-GW of LOIs (Phoenix, Chicago, UK, Nordics, Japan, India, LatAm) that went to ORCL/Meta/CRWV/Google, and lost the $100B Stargate contract to Oracle — effectively stepping away from ~$150B of OpenAI gross-profit dollars (~$30B/yr, which would have added >18% to FY25's $194B GP) (SemiAnalysis, 2025-11-12). Now back in the market but "running out of options," MSFT is renting GPUs from neoclouds and reselling — structurally lower Azure margin (SemiAnalysis, 2025-11-12). UBS adds the disclosure nuance: the ORCL/CRWV subcontractor workloads report as gross Azure revenue (their revs = MSFT COGS), and the biggest medium-term Azure risk is whether OpenAI *inference* (not just training) migrates off Azure (UBS, 2025-09-02). OpenAI concentration in Azure backlog (~45% RPO; TCI flagged single customer >40% of Azure backlog before cutting its $8B stake, 2026-05-11). DeepSeek/"tokenmaxxing" substitution risk — MSFT reportedly weighing hosted DeepSeek V4 + usage pricing on Copilot Cowork (Axios via briefing, 2026-06-17; @zerohedge, 2026-06-18). Stratechery's read sharpens the strategic logic: under usage-based pricing (E7 bundles Anthropic+OpenAI for $99/employee/month), MSFT is incentivized to offer a "significantly higher level of usage" via a cheap open-source model rather than ceding margin to Anthropic/OpenAI on every token — MSFT confirmed to Axios it is exploring a fine-tuned DeepSeek V4 (or another open-source model) as a lower-cost alternative powering Copilot Cowork, expecting to make it available "in the coming weeks"; Thompson expects Cowork best-with-Claude and Copilot best-with-GPT, with DeepSeek the bundle's cost-efficient default, and thinks MSFT goes this route — framing American software cos as the biggest beneficiaries of a cheaper Chinese model (Stratechery, 2026-06-23). Barclays desk: investors see MSFT "stuck in the mud" near-term, used as a funding long vs SaaS, with Azure growth decelerating relative to GCP/AWS accelerating (Rand, 2026-06-03); positioning moved from +2 to -1 post-Q2 (Barclays, 2026-05-29). HE flags "Growing MSFT Risk" thematically (HE Global Technology BB, 2025-12-04). Sell-side spec-sales desks corroborate the positioning bear: BofA called MSFT the **"most hated Mag7," "dead for sale"** in the long-only community (reflexive selling as the underweight grows), caught in the "software is going to die" thesis — the core gripe being that an Anthropic Excel plugin works better than native Copilot despite MSFT's head start, and that Azure is capped near-term because GPUs are diverted to internal R&D rather than chasing a mid-40s Azure number (BofA/Wigg, 2026-05-06; BofA/Fenske, 2026-04-16). A Jefferies CIO check echoed it: enthusiasm "tempered not on Azure but on applications" (Power Apps vs custom LLM/Snowflake/Databricks builds), with ~5 cases of ~$5M each that may not renew at SaaS vendors (Jefferies conf, 2026-06-01); a cloud-MSP expert ranked **Google — not AWS — as MSFT's biggest competitive threat** (most "resilient" post-outages, not a competitor in many verticals) and said Copilot was "sold the wrong way" as a silver bullet (up to 96% adoption when positioned as an assistant, but loses to Claude Code/Cursor on coding) (WFC cloud/MSFT expert, 2026-04-14). ## Catalysts / what to watch
- **Q4 FY26 earnings (late Jul 2026):** Azure cc growth + the 2H acceleration claim; CY26 $190B capex confirmation; M365 Copilot net seat adds (guided up sequentially). Watch the OpenAI vs non-OpenAI Azure AI split — MS's thesis hinges on "Other" Azure AI (GitHub Copilot, Foundry, GPUs) re-accelerating, since OpenAI alone explains 100%+ of CY26 Azure AI growth on its model (MS, 2025-05-21).
- **E7 launch (eff. May 31 2026):** first time Office carries a variable consumption component on top of seats — read on per-user + usage monetization.
- **GitHub Copilot usage-based pricing** (eff. June 1 2026) — watch consumption-driven top-line acceleration vs near-term cloud GM dilution (guided ~64% Q4). Competitive overhang: SemiAnalysis argues Cursor/Claude Code/Codex are out-executing GitHub Copilot, and MSFT's "ecosystem bet" (Agent HQ plugging in third-party labs) is less profitable than owning the agent (SemiAnalysis, 2025-11-12).
- **Capex revision watch:** any move toward the $276B CY27 number (MS/Altimeter) vs the ~$190B CY26 base. JPM frames hyperscaler aggregate capex at ~$364B CY25 (Top-4), funded largely from ~$700B/yr OCF, with AI/DC issuers now ~14.5% of the JULI IG index — a financing backdrop to monitor (JPM AI Capex & Financing, 2025-11-10).
- **OpenAI/DeepSeek model-economics:** hosted DeepSeek/MAI substitution and Copilot Cowork usage pricing. Inference-cost backdrop for any hosted-DeepSeek economics: SemiAnalysis measured **DeepSeek V4 serving cost at $0.156 per 1M output tokens on GB300 NVL72** (50 tok/s/user, 8k in/1k out, MTP on), and B200 token throughput rising ~1.7x (~300k→~500k tok/s/MW) on software alone in ~5 weeks — i.e. open-model inference costs falling fast, sharpening the substitution case against premium OpenAI/Anthropic APIs (SemiAnalysis "DeepSeek V4 Day 0→43", 2026-06-09). MSFT is listed among the InferenceX supporters.
- **OpenAI inference migration:** whether OpenAI keeps *inference* on Azure (the high-margin reported piece) or follows training to ORCL/CRWV/Google — UBS's key medium-term Azure swing factor (UBS, 2025-09-02).
- **CY27 EPS inflection:** OpenAI equity-method losses roll off once cumulative losses hit the ~$13B initial-investment cap, mechanically accelerating GAAP EPS (MS, 2025-05-21).
- Datacenter/power: CVX-MSFT CCGT (GE Vernova turbines) FID expected later 2026 (UBS, 2026-06-11); self-build campuses (Conover NC, Chase City VA, Castroville TX) reactivating. Fairwater scale: each Wisconsin GPU building ~300MW (~150k GB200/building), with a planned 3rd phase of two >600MW buildings taking the campus to >2GW (SemiAnalysis, 2025-11-12).

## Risks
- Capex/ROI mismatch: capex compounding far faster than Azure revenue; component (memory) inflation adds ~$25B to CY26 capex and will pressure cloud GM as 6-yr depreciation builds. Ex-OpenAI-rev-share AI ROIC sits near ORCL's ~20%, well below MSFT's 35–40% blended (SemiAnalysis, 2025-11-12).
- Margin mix from neocloud reselling: MSFT renting GPUs from neoclouds and reselling (gross-basis ORCL/CRWV subcontracting where their revs = MSFT COGS) structurally dilutes Azure margin vs first-party build (SemiAnalysis 2025-11-12; UBS 2025-09-02).
- Depreciation/useful-life debate: Burry-style claim that hyperscalers (incl. MSFT) flatter earnings by extending IT useful life from 3-5 to 5-6 yrs; SemiAnalysis pushes back (real operational life of GPUs exceeds the 2-3yr Nvidia product cycle), but it remains a headline risk (SemiAnalysis, 2025-11-12).
- Customer concentration: ~45% of commercial RPO tied to OpenAI; partnership economics and OpenAI's ability to multi-cloud post-April-2026 restructuring. UBS flags MSFT's ROFR may lack teeth, and that loss of OpenAI *inference* (vs just training) would force tempering of medium-term Azure growth (UBS, 2025-09-02).
- AI cost discipline / model commoditization: enterprises substituting cheaper models (DeepSeek/open source) erodes frontier-model monetization; "tokenmaxxing" pullback. MAI not at the frontier; under-investment risks no top-5 lab post-2032 (SemiAnalysis, 2025-11-12). The cheaper-model dynamic now cuts toward MSFT's *own* sourcing: usage-based pricing makes MSFT itself the incentivized adopter of a fine-tuned DeepSeek V4 / open-source model to protect bundle margins (Copilot Cowork), i.e. American software cos as the prime beneficiaries of cheaper Chinese models (Stratechery, 2026-06-23).
- Custom silicon execution: Maia 200 internally judged a failure; Maia 300 (2nm) not viable near internal targets until late 2027 vs Nvidia Vera Rubin; MSFT shipping far less AI silicon (CoWoS) than GOOG/AMZN/Meta — leaves MSFT reliant on renting Nvidia GPUs and competing against ORCL/CRWV/Nebius rather than capturing merchant-ASIC margin like GOOG/AMZN (SemiAnalysis, 2025-11-12).
- Competitive: Azure cc growth trailing GCP/AWS acceleration; Anthropic Office-suite apps (Claude for Excel/Word GA) pressuring Copilot; Cursor/Claude Code/Codex out-executing GitHub Copilot (SemiAnalysis, 2025-11-12).
- Consumer drag: Windows OEM guided down high-teens (memory-price PC headwind, inventory normalization); Gaming/Xbox declining; impairment charges in gaming.
- Standard 10-K factors (FY25 10-K): intense competition, cybersecurity, AI execution/legal/IP (incl. NYT v. OpenAI/Microsoft copyright case), datacenter capacity & power, and government regulation/antitrust.
