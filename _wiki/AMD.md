## Snapshot
Fabless designer of x86 CPUs, GPUs and adaptive/embedded silicon, fabbed at TSMC (2nm/5nm) with some GF, UMC, Samsung capacity. Four segments: **Data Center** (EPYC server CPUs + Instinct AI GPUs — ~56% of revenue and the entire growth story), **Client & Gaming** (Ryzen PCs + Radeon + semi-custom consoles), and **Embedded** (Xilinx FPGA + adaptive SoC, highest-margin business at ~38-40% segment OM). Competes head-to-head with Intel (x86), Nvidia (AI GPU), Arm-based merchant/custom CPUs, and the custom-ASIC complex (Broadcom/Marvell). The thesis is fully an AI-infrastructure story: AMD is the credible #2 merchant GPU and the share-gaining merchant server CPU into an agentic-AI demand pull (10-K FY25, 2026-02-04).

- **Verano (2027) + MI500 rack cycle:** MI500 AI racks launch 2027 paired with a new EPYC "Verano" CPU, just one year after Venice. SemiAnalysis's view (2026-02-09) is that the Zen7 core won't be ready in time (AMD's core-design team runs on a ~2-year cadence) — so Verano will likely be a Venice variant, still on Zen6 cores, probably the same 256-core count. The key differentiator is a new I/O die on 3nm adding PCIe Gen7 + 200G Ethernet SerDes, enabling faster Infinity Fabric links to MI500 GPUs and supporting UALoE (SemiAnalysis "CPUs are Back," 2026-02-09).
- **Florence (2028) — the "true" next-gen core:** AMD's next real core redesign, "a true next generation Zen 7 EPYC," debuts 2028 on TSMC A16 (backside power); the alternative path is waiting for A14 (2029) if AMD skips backside power at the A16 node. SemiAnalysis's own estimate (explicitly not AMD guidance) is "around 320 or so" cores (SemiAnalysis "CPUs are Back," 2026-02-09).
- **SP8 platform filling Intel's vacated enterprise gap:** new 8-channel Venice SP8 platform (successor to EPYC 8004 "Siena," up to 128 dense Zen6c cores) launches directly into the gap left by Intel's cancellation of its Diamond Rapids-SP mainstream platform — watch for AMD enterprise/mainstream share gains through 2026-27 as this lands (SemiAnalysis "CPUs are Back," 2026-02-09).

## Risks
- **Intense competition** vs Intel (improving x86 supply / Diamond Rapids, Coral Rapids pulled to mid-2027) and Nvidia (Vera Rubin), plus merchant/custom Arm and ASIC scale-up (10-K FY25).
- **Foundry concentration on TSMC** for leading-edge HPC/AI wafers + advanced packaging (CoWoS-L) — supply tightness in HBM/wafers a gating risk to the ramp; AMD raised 2027 CoWoS bookings to ~120K wafers but realization is Helios-execution dependent (Jefferies / Q425 Techknowledge, 2025-12-17).
- **Customer concentration** in Data Center AI (OpenAI/Meta) and uncertain hyperscaler allocation of 2027-28 GPU share.
- **Export-control / China** revenue volatility (MI308/MI325 licensing "dynamic").
- **Memory/component cost inflation** dragging 2H Client + Gaming demand; console cycle in year 7 (semi-custom down significant double-digits in 2026).
- Warrant-related share dilution; GPU gross margin dilutive during ramp.
- **Memory-supply lock-up dynamic — flagged as a reader/community comment, NOT SemiAnalysis analyst body content:** a comment from Drew Van Pelt on SemiAnalysis's "Memory Mania" piece (comment section, p.35, 2026-02-06) speculates that AMD (and Meta) could be incentivized to lock up disproportionate long-term memory supply if they perceive a risk of being squeezed out as laggards in the memory-shortage environment — i.e. a fear-driven over-ordering dynamic that could tighten HBM/DRAM allocation further or strand AMD with excess commitments if demand undershoots. This is explicitly a third-party comment, not part of SemiAnalysis's own analysis, and should be weighted accordingly.
