## Snapshot
Wolfspeed is a US-listed, **vertically integrated, pure-play silicon-carbide (SiC) company** — it grows its own SiC crystal/substrates and sells **(1) SiC + GaN materials** (wafers/substrates) and **(2) SiC power devices** (MOSFETs/modules). Two product lines: **Power Products ~67% of revenue, Materials Products ~33%** (10-Q, 2026-05-07; Power $218.4M vs Materials $100.3M over the Successor period 9/30/25–3/29/26). End-markets are **Automotive/EV + fast charging** and **Industrial & Energy** (AI data centers, grid modernization, renewables/storage) (10-Q, 2026-05-07). **The headline is the balance sheet, not the end-markets:** Wolfspeed filed a **prepackaged Chapter 11** (RSA 2025-06-22, confirmed 2025-09-08) and **emerged 2025-09-29**, cutting total debt ~70% and adopting **fresh-start accounting** — old equity was nearly wiped (existing holders kept ~1.3M of 25.8M new shares, ~5%; convertible noteholders took the rest) (10-Q, 2026-05-07). It is a sub-$200M-quarter, gross-loss, cash-burning turnaround with a SiC-800V optionality call layered on top — own the restructuring before you own the theme.

## Position in the value chain
Wolfspeed is **vertically integrated in SiC** — it grows its own SiC boules/crystal and substrates (assuring supply, controlling cost), then fabs/packages SiC power devices, and also sells bare SiC/GaN materials to third parties (10-Q, 2026-05-07). It sells into **EV/automotive, industrial & energy (grid, renewables/storage), and the emerging 800V AI-datacenter power** stack — the latter the only real growth vector right now (datacenter rev +50% QoQ in Q2 FY26, +30% QoQ in Q3 FY26; 10kV MOSFET launched; customers migrating 400V→800V) (Q3/Q2 FY26 calls). **But the operative fact is the post-Chapter-11 balance sheet**: ~70% debt cut, fresh-start basis, near-total equity wipeout, still running negative gross margin on Mohawk Valley underutilization. SiC sits at infrastructure voltages (>1,000V) in the 800V debate; JPM names WOLF among 800V beneficiaries but is **Underweight the equity** (JPM, 2025-12-15) — a small-cap, levered, going-concern-recovered name with a distressed-equity profile, not a clean compounder. See [800v-dc-power](themes/800v-dc-power.md).

## Debate / thesis
- **Bull:** The balance sheet is fixed — Chapter 11 cut total debt ~70%, interest expense ~60%, pushed first maturity to 2030, and left ~$1.2B liquidity, removing the going-concern overhang that defined the 2024–25 story (10-Q, 2026-05-07; Q2/Q3 FY26 calls). On a clean basis it is a **vertically integrated, US-made, pure-play SiC** franchise with the only fully-ramped 200mm device fab (Mohawk Valley) and a **10kV MOSFET / 300mm crystal** tech edge. The call option is **800V AI-datacenter power**: datacenter rev +50% then +30% QoQ, customers migrating 400V→800V, and JPM names WOLF among best-positioned 800V beneficiaries ("Watts Up!" 800V AI-DC stack, JPM/Samik Chatterjee, briefing 2026-06-09). FinTwit treats WOLF as the levered 800V power-semi torque play — "+300% or -50%" (@insane_analyst, briefing 2026-05-16); Serenity: "very stupid to be a bear… your WOLF power-semi basket should go brrr from 800VDC acceleration" (briefing 2026-06-15). 800V was pulled *ahead* to Q3 2026 (NVDA Vera Rubin, briefing 2026-06-15).
- **Bear:** It is still a **gross-loss, cash-burning, sub-$200M-quarter** business — Q3 FY26 GM -20.6%, op loss -$114M, OCF -$84M, revenue down YoY and guided flat — with the core **EV/automotive demand "uncertain"** and Mohawk Valley badly underutilized (~$46M/qtr drag) (10-Q + call, 2026-05-05). Old equity was essentially wiped in the restructuring (~5% retained), so the new equity carries the recovery story but at a **distressed multiple/float** and a sky-high cost of capital on the next refi (CFO: rate steps up mid-CY26; "very high cost of capital… something to be looked at," Q2 FY26 call). On the theme itself, SiC is contested: Citi's 800V framing favors **GaN at the rack / SiC at >1,000V infrastructure** and crowns TXN/IFX/ON, not WOLF; **China can flood SiC/GaN** and Chinese vendors are challenging incumbents incl. The desk consensus this spring was **short-biased WOLF** (briefing 2026-05-31). The one formal sell-side rating now on file is bearish: **JPM rates WOLF Underweight, PT $18.56**, classified in its ADAS/AV/EV bucket — "sizable end-market opportunity in EVs and emerging opportunity in datacenter; however, execution, profitability and balance sheet remain concerns" (JPM/Samik Chatterjee, 2025-12-15).

## Risks
- **Post-bankruptcy / distressed-equity profile:** emerged from Chapter 11 2025-09-29; old equity ~wiped (~5% retained); new equity is a recovery instrument with a high cost of capital on refi (10-Q 2026-05-07; Q2 FY26 call).
- **Still loss-making & cash-burning:** Q3 FY26 GM -20.6%, op loss -$114M, OCF -$84M; profitability hinges entirely on Mohawk Valley utilization ramping (10-Q 2026-05-07).
- **Going concern history:** FY25 10-K (filed pre-confirmation) carried explicit **substantial-doubt going-concern** language and NYSE delisting risk; the latest 10-Q now asserts viable going-concern post-emergence — but the model is unproven on a Successor basis (10-K 2025-08-26; 10-Q 2026-05-07).
- **End-market cyclicality:** EV/automotive demand "uncertain"/soft; concentrated SiC exposure to one cyclical vertical historically (Q3 FY26 call; 10-K). JPM bins WOLF in its ADAS/AV/EV coverage, i.e. an auto-leveraged name first, datacenter optionality second (JPM, 2025-12-15).
- **800V is a share game, contested for SiC:** GaN-at-rack vs SiC-at-infrastructure split favors others (Citi); China SiC/GaN flood risk; "SiC garbage" skepticism (briefings 2026-06-15, 06-17).
- **Fresh-start non-comparability + intangible amortization:** Successor financials carry large intangible amort (~+$60M/qtr in Q2) and are not comparable to history; reported EPS noisy (Q2/Q3 FY26 calls).
- **Renesas overhang/governance:** Renesas holds convertible 2L notes + warrants + board/governance rights (capped at 39.9% voting, 9.9% voting limitation) — dilution/control considerations (10-Q 2026-05-07).
- **Litigation:** consolidated securities class action re: Mohawk Valley operational disclosures Aug 2023–Nov 2024 (10-Q 2026-05-07).
