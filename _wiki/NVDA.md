## Snapshot
The accelerated-computing platform that sits at the center of the AI buildout — GPUs, CPUs (Grace/Vera), NVLink + Spectrum-X/InfiniBand networking, and the CUDA software moat sold as full-stack "AI factories." Data Center is ~90%+ of revenue ($75B of $82B in Q1 FY27); the rest is Gaming, Pro Viz, and Automotive/physical AI. From Q1 FY27 NVDA re-segmented Data Center into **Hyperscale** (~50%) and **ACIE** (AI clouds, industrial, enterprise, sovereign, ~50%) plus a new **Edge Computing** platform. Whole thesis is leverage to hyperscaler + sovereign + neocloud capex, where NVDA captures the compute, networking, and increasingly the CPU. The scale-up network is core to the moat: NVLink is ~the entire scale-up market today and NVDA is expected to remain the majority of a market growing **$4B (2024) → ~$17B by 2029, 34% CAGR** (MS Scale-up primer); on compute NVDA is modeled holding **~70% share into C28**, with per-GW revenue economics nearly 2x its closest peer (UBS 2026 overview).

## Risks
- **Demand sustainability / AI-capex ROI** — hyperscaler + neocloud FCF and financing dependence (Crisafulli, Chanos).
- **Customer concentration** — top-5 cloud/hyperscalers ~50%+ of DC revenue; large direct/indirect customer exposure.
- **Supply concentration** — TSMC (N3/N2 + CoWoS), HBM (SK Hynix/Samsung/Micron), advanced packaging are gating; "supply-constrained throughout life of Vera Rubin."
- **Competition** — custom ASIC the sharpest structural threat: GOOG TPUv7, **AWS Trainium3/4** (open-sourcing software to attack CUDA; AWS Trainium3 Deep Dive, 2025-12-04), AVGO/MRVL merchant ASIC, AMD MI400/MI450X (OpenAI ~26GW deal + equity rebate), inference-specialist silicon. JPM ASIC volume CAGR 32% vs GPU 7% (2025-28); UBS ASIC ~25-30% of accelerator TAM long-term.
- **Scale-up roadmap gap for Rubin Ultra (new, 2026-07-06)** — with the **Kyber NVL144 delayed 12+ months to 2028** (PCB-midplane manufacturability), **NVL576 delayed/small-volume**, **NVL72x2 cancelled**, and **CPO NVSwitch not available until Feynman**, NVDA currently has no proven solution to expand the scale-up world size for Rubin Ultra — a window for **AMD MI500X** and **GOOGL TPUv8i 'Broadfly'** to gain scale-up advantages (SemiAnalysis, via CNBC & MS TMT, 2026-07-06). Bear-ish nuance for the scale-up leg of the moat, distinct from the merchant-ASIC-units debate above.
- **China / export controls** — China DC compute zeroed in guidance; geopolitical and licensing risk.
- **Valuation/sentiment** — "funding short" status; UBS view that multiple expansion is exhausted (~20x buy-side out-year EPS); correlation to decelerating y/y growth as comps peak ~FQ2.
- **"AI bubble" search-trend / sentiment risk** — Google searches for "AI bubble" up ~400% y/y (peaked November); stock trailed the SOX index by >70pp since start of 2025 despite normally trading at a 40% premium to it; bar to beat (~80% sales growth) stays high vs an average ~3% beat over the prior 3 quarters (Barron's/Al Root, 2026-05-18).
- **Hyperscaler-growth-mix optics** — hyperscaler DC revenue (+115% y/y) now outpacing the ACIE/smaller-customer segment (+74% y/y) even as management told the Street NVDA "should be growing faster than hyperscale capex" — stock fell ~3% over two days post-Q1-print despite the beat on this specific tension (Barron's/Adam Levine, 2026-05-25).
- **HBM/memory-wall structural dependency** — Nvidia commands the "lion's share" of HBM demand through 2027 (Rubin Ultra pushes per-GPU HBM to 1TB), and is likely the most profitable HBM vendor in the world by capturing margin on HBM shipped inside its accelerators — but that same concentration is the supply chain's single point of stress (SK Hynix/Samsung/Micron allocation, HBM4 base-die-first risk, pricing step-ups) already flagged above (SemiAnalysis "Scaling the Memory Wall", 2025-08-11).
