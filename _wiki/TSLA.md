## Snapshot
Tesla is an EV maker, energy-storage company (Megapack), and autonomous-driving / robotics platform. The Street narrative is rotating from "EV volume story" toward an autonomy-and-robotics re-rate: FSD cumulative miles crossed 10bn and daily usage accelerated to 26.7mn/day (vs. 18.5mn/day in April 2026), with Morgan Stanley framing it as a "rate-of-change story." Alongside autonomy, Tesla is pursuing Optimus humanoid robots and is a co-anchor customer for the Terafab US chip-fab JV with SpaceX and [INTC](INTC.md), a project that has grown in scope from $55bn to up to $119bn.

## Debate / thesis
- **Autonomy re-rate — real or priced in?**
  - **Bull:** FSD daily miles accelerating sharply; FSD crossed **10bn cumulative miles in early May** (MS/Percoco, 2026-07-06); Austin robotaxi launch tracked by NHTSA; **Miami launched July 3 (incl. unsupervised vehicles)**, with MS expecting Phoenix/Orlando/Tampa/Las Vegas by year-end; Musk described as "very serious" about all tech bets (per ASML CEO). MS PT $415 (EW).
  - **Neutral read on Miami (MS/Percoco + Bernstein, 2026-07-06):** MS keeps EW/$415 and Bernstein TMT rates the Miami launch **neutral (=)** with limited expected stock reaction — the geographic expansion is on track vs the 1Q26 deck but the market is looking for the unsupervised-Cybercab ramp (still gated on FSD v15, ~5-6mo out per JPM 06-24) rather than city-by-city supervised launches.
  - **Bear:** JPM only just upgraded from Underweight to Neutral (2026-06-08), with no published PT in the corpus — not yet a bull call. No robotaxi revenue or unit data in the ported corpus.
  - **Industry framing — MS "Robotaxi Diffusion" (Tim Hsiao / Brian Nowak et al., 2026-07-03, Industry View In-Line):** sizes a **US$1 trillion global robotaxi opportunity by 2040**, with unit economics broadly reaching break-even by **2028**, scaled ops by **2030**, and a **~2.5mn-vehicle global fleet by 2035**. Converting the first 1% of the global taxi fleet is "a multiplier," handing an outsized advantage to first movers ("a handful of EV incumbents look especially well placed for the L4 shift on technology and cost"). Supportive of the TSLA autonomy re-rate at the industry-TAM level (US + China lead), though the note is sector-wide and carries no TSLA-specific PT. (MS "Global Autos & Shared Mobility: Robotaxi Diffusion", 2026-07-03)
- **Terafab / SpaceX JV — strategic asset, distraction, or prelude to a full merger?**
  - **Bull:** Terafab (SpaceX + TSLA JV) confirmed by ASML CEO; total scope now up to $119bn; SpaceX providing initial capital; solves [INTC](INTC.md) 14A capacity utilization. SemiAnalysis frames Tesla as an anchor customer alongside SpaceX.
  - **Bear:** Capital requirements are massive; Tesla's own contribution not disclosed; the JV ties TSLA capital allocation to Intel Foundry execution risk.
  - **NEW — merger speculation (The Information · Grace Kay, 2026-07-01):** Growing Street expectation that Elon Musk will propose a **SpaceX-Tesla merger**; Terafab (the $55bn semiconductor manufacturing project) is described directly as a **Tesla-SpaceX collaboration**, not just a JV/customer relationship. Org-chart analysis shows the two firms **"already operating as one entity in key ways"**: shared executives include **Charlie Kuehmann** (materials engineering) across both, plus cross-over of Tesla AI/software execs — **Ashok Elluswamy** and **Silvio Brugada** — into SpaceX's **xAI** unit. SpaceX employees make up most of Terafab's leadership. This reframes Terafab from "JV with a separate strategic partner" toward "internal reorg of a de facto combined Musk entity" — a materially different capital-structure/governance read than the prior JV framing (article: "How Elon Musk Is Building the Terafab Team Inside Tesla," The Information · Grace Kay, 2026-07-01).
  - **NEW — JPM on a SPCX-Tesla combination (JPM · Rajat Gupta, 2026-07-07; TSLA rated Neutral, TSLA price 07-06 $419.77):** JPM ("SPCX-TSLA Combination Thoughts") calls a SpaceX-Tesla combination **"strategically coherent on paper"** — it would let Musk unify vision/mission/engineering across AI, robotics, energy, transportation and space, anchored by Terafab + a combined **~$28.5T TAM** narrative (per JPM's same-morning SPCX initiation), with SPCX's record IPO furnishing a **high-value acquisition currency**. BUT JPM thinks press reports **understate the practical regulatory bottleneck**: multi-jurisdictional approvals — **China a particular hurdle** (SPCX defense/US-gov ties = national-security sensitivity; Starlink not approved in China; TSLA's large, profitable China manufacturing footprint — dynamics at odds). Other hurdles: **governance/voting asymmetry** (Musk ~85% of SPCX voting power vs only ~20% of TSLA) and the perception it would be an **SPCX-led acquisition rather than a merger of equals** — especially given signs of a **turnaround in TSLA fundamentals** (strong 2Q26 deliveries; FSD approvals/robotaxi roll-outs gathering pace). Full detail on the SPCX side: [SPCX.md](SPCX.md). (JPM · Rajat Gupta, 2026-07-07)
  - **Terafab hiring (The Information · Grace Kay, 2026-07-01):** first major outside (non-Tesla/SpaceX) hire is **Gary Jiang**, an 18-year Intel veteran and former **Intel Arizona factory manager**, brought on as foundry manager — Terafab has also hired several other ex-Intel employees, per the same org-chart reporting.
- **Megapack / Energy vs. EV volume:** BofA flagged Ford as an emerging battery-storage competitor to Megapack (MISO expert call, 2026-05-23). No Tesla energy-segment numbers in the corpus.
- **US-China dual presence (Physical AI) — Gavekal (2026-06-29):** "Tesla's Two Way Bet In Physical AI" (Gavekal Technologies / Daniel Grozdea fwd, 2026-06-29). Gavekal frames the Shanghai Gigafactory precedent as evidence that Tesla's physical AI ambitions — Optimus, Cybercab, robotics supply chain — require operating at scale in both the US *and* China simultaneously. Shanghai gives Tesla access to the Chinese EV supply chain (lower BOM, faster iteration) while US fabs (Giga Texas + Terafab/SpaceX) provide the AI chip and energy-storage layer. Gavekal reads this dual-presence as a structural advantage vs. pure-US or pure-China competitors — but flags that it also creates a political risk surface if US-China tech decoupling accelerates (China's leverage over TSLA = a hold-up risk). Separately: Barron's flagged deeper ties between Tesla and SpaceX (Terafab + shared AI chip roadmaps) as an underappreciated asset in TSLA's valuation. (Gavekal Technologies, forwarded by Daniel Grozdea, 2026-06-29)
- **Where the sell-side stands:** MS (Percoco) — Equal-Weight, PT $415 (autonomy "rate-of-change story," 2026-05-28). **JPMorgan (Rajat Gupta) — Neutral, PT $475 (Dec-27), price $381.61 (23-Jun-26)** (JPM "Tesla Robotaxi Deep-Dive", 2026-06-24); upgraded from Underweight 2026-06-08. Valuation flag: TSLA at 16x CY25 revenues vs. SPCX at ~90x (DanielTNiles, 2026-06-13).

- Autonomy thesis priced in ahead of robotaxi revenue evidence.
- Terafab capital commitment tied to Intel Foundry execution risk.
- Energy-segment competition (Ford, FLNC).
- High valuation / Mag-7 beta; Musk-attention/key-man overhang.
