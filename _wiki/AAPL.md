## Snapshot
Consumer-hardware franchise (iPhone ~50%+ of revenue) plus a high-margin Services annuity layered on an installed base of **over 2.5bn active devices** (Q2 FY26, 2026-04-30). Revenue mix: iPhone, Mac, iPad, Wearables/Home/Accessories, and Services (App Store, advertising, iCloud, payments, Apple TV+, music). In the semis/AI chain, Apple is the largest TSMC customer (~17% of TSMC sales per BofA, 2026-05-11) — though Bernstein estimates NVIDIA rose to ~20% of TSMC revenue in 2025, "about as big a customer as Apple" (Bernstein Semis deck, 2025-05-05) — a leading-edge-node and DRAM/NAND buyer, and a hyperscaler-divergent AI player: capex/revenue has fallen ~3%→2% over Q1'20→Q1'26 (@TheTranscript_, briefing 2026-05-11) vs. 40–60% for peers (Vital Knowledge, 2026-06-09). AI strategy is capex-light and partner-led — a multi-year Google/Gemini collaboration powers next-gen Apple Foundation Models behind a privacy-first, on-device + Private Cloud Compute architecture; Bernstein frames the resolved Google DOJ remedies as not only clearing a downside risk but opening the path for Apple to leverage Gemini's AI (Bernstein initiation/Newman, 2025-09-15). CEO transition: Tim Cook → John Ternus effective 2026-09-01.

## Risks
- **Memory (DRAM/NAND) cost inflation** — "unlike anything in 40+ years" (Cook via Vital Knowledge, 2026-06-17); largest near-term GM and pricing risk; NAND going "parabolic" (MS, 2026-06-16). New from 06-27: (1) **CXMT lobbying + $263B market-cap loss** — the largest single-day dollar decline on Apple's history, triggered by the revelation that Apple petitioned Commerce to source DRAM from a blacklisted Chinese company; this adds a regulatory overhang to the memory-cost story and shows how acute the LPDDR5 shortage is; (2) **iOS 27 A20 9GB DRAM** (Ming-Chi Kuo) — AI workloads driving DRAM content expansion across the full iPhone lineup, not just Pro models — bull signal for the upgrade cycle; (3) **Vision Pro chief exits to OpenAI** — spatial computing de-emphasized, but talent signals OpenAI's hardware ambitions are credible. **Close-out (06-29):** the price-hike thesis crossed from "analysts flag it" to "market prices it" — Apple's actual Mac/iPad hikes triggered a **6.1% one-day drop**, the stock's worst in over a year, closing the week at **$283.78** (-4.8% w/w, superseding the $295.95 reference from 06-18). This is the real-world payoff of SemiAnalysis's 2026-02-06 forecast (~15% Apple-specific memory pass-through vs. ~20% industry-wide, 2H26-weighted) — a rare case of a quantified forecast landing almost exactly as flagged, 4+ months ahead of the event. It also sharpens the AI debate: Barron's/Mohan's "AI laggard vs. trusted interface" framing (06-08) is now the explicit frame the Street uses heading into the Cook→Ternus handoff (Sept. 2026) — the 33x-vs-27.5x-avg multiple is a bet that Siri 2.0 + WWDC's Google/Gemini-grounded architecture converts distribution into monetizable AI before the "laggard" label starts to bite on fundamentals rather than just sentiment. **Extension (06-30/07-01):** the memory-cost story keeps compounding from multiple independent desks — MS sales notes (Amerian: price hikes risk demand-destruction; Risom via FT: Apple lobbying for CXMT access, a second sourcing line on the weakening-leverage read) and Culpium/Culpan's "Chipflation Dilemma" (memory now Apple's single-largest cost item) all point the same direction as the 06-25 CXMT story and SemiAnalysis's 06-02 forecast. Against that, UBS's 07-01 Evidence Lab survey is the freshest demand read and it's genuinely mixed: US purchase intent improving (+300bps YoY) while China deteriorates (-100bps YoY) and Apple Intelligence still isn't moving upgrade decisions (only ~24% cite AI as an upgrade driver, down 500bps HoH) — but the Apple-branded-foldable favorability spread widened sharply (+600bps HoH to ~48%) ahead of the Sept-26 launch, the one demand data point moving in the bulls' favor. UBS held its Sept-26Q and FY26 iPhone rev/unit estimates and PT/EPS unchanged, so this is confirmation rather than a re-rating. **Extension (07-06):** four fresh datapoints, all confirming existing threads rather than re-rating. UBS's App Store tracker (Vogt, Neutral/$296 unchanged) shows App Store +3% YoY June-Q but **US -6% YoY** with GenAI-app growth decelerating (~79%, off harder comps) — a mild negative for the Services/AI-monetization narrative, offset by ROW +9%. The foldable build ramp firms up: Nikkei/Kuo (via MS TMT) now has Apple prepping **~10M foldable / ~80M total 2026 iPhones** at a ~$2,299-2,499 foldable start price (with <1M fold units in 3Q26 pushing sales to C4Q), corroborating the 07-03 Jefferies/Nikkei firm-order signal and reinforcing the FY27 ASP-lever bull case. On memory, Bloomberg (via MS TMT) reports Apple courting **CXMT (DRAM) and YMTC (NAND)** for China-market devices — extending the 06-25/06-30 CXMT-lobbying thread with a second blacklisted Chinese source and underscoring how acute the shortage is. Finally, JPM (Harlan Sur) adds a content positive: the **Broadcom partnership extended through 2031** across a range of ASICs (power-management ASIC for client products next year, plus AVGO co-designing an AI/datacenter server CPU with Apple) — a new supplier/content angle alongside the modem-insourcing story.
