## Snapshot
Consumer-hardware franchise (iPhone ~50%+ of revenue) plus a high-margin Services annuity layered on an installed base of **over 2.5bn active devices** (Q2 FY26, 2026-04-30). Revenue mix: iPhone, Mac, iPad, Wearables/Home/Accessories, and Services (App Store, advertising, iCloud, payments, Apple TV+, music). In the semis/AI chain, Apple is the largest TSMC customer (~17% of TSMC sales per BofA, 2026-05-11) — though Bernstein estimates NVIDIA rose to ~20% of TSMC revenue in 2025, "about as big a customer as Apple" (Bernstein Semis deck, 2025-05-05) — a leading-edge-node and DRAM/NAND buyer, and a hyperscaler-divergent AI player: capex/revenue has fallen ~3%→2% over Q1'20→Q1'26 (@TheTranscript_, briefing 2026-05-11) vs. 40–60% for peers (Vital Knowledge, 2026-06-09). AI strategy is capex-light and partner-led — a multi-year Google/Gemini collaboration powers next-gen Apple Foundation Models behind a privacy-first, on-device + Private Cloud Compute architecture; Bernstein frames the resolved Google DOJ remedies as not only clearing a downside risk but opening the path for Apple to leverage Gemini's AI (Bernstein initiation/Newman, 2025-09-15). CEO transition: Tim Cook → John Ternus effective 2026-09-01.

## Current state (latest quarter)
**Q2 FY26 (March qtr, reported 2026-04-30):** Revenue **$111.2bn, +17% YoY** (March record, above high end of guide despite supply constraints). EPS **$2.10, +22%**. iPhone **$57bn, +22%**; Services all-time record **$31bn, +16%**; Mac $8.4bn (+6%); iPad $6.9bn (+8%); Wearables $7.9bn (+5%). Company gross margin **49.3%**, +110bps QoQ; products GM 38.7% (−200bps QoQ on seasonal de-leverage + higher memory cost); Services GM 76.7%. Greater China **+28% YoY** (1H grew 33%) — quarterly revenue record. FX was a ~2.5pt tailwind. Returned $15bn ($3.8bn div + $11bn buyback / 42m shares); board added **$100bn buyback authorization** and **dropped the net-cash-neutral target** (Parekh: now evaluating cash and debt independently — not a constraint signal, framework "no longer relevant"; IR call 2026-05-12).

**Guidance (June qtr):** total revenue **+14% to +17% YoY**; gross margin **47.5%–48.5%**; opex $18.8–19.1bn; tax ~17%. Services to grow ~in line with March ex-FX. **Memory is the swing factor:** Cook flagged "significantly higher memory costs" in June (partly offset by carry-in inventory) and warned of an **increasing impact beyond June** — "we will look at a range of options" (read: pricing). June constraints shift to **Mac (mini, Studio, MacBook Neo)** — advanced-node (3nm SoC) capacity gating, not memory; Mac mini/Studio may take "several months" to reach supply-demand balance (Q2 FY26 call; IR call 2026-05-12). Q1 FY26 (Dec, 2026-01-29) was the best quarter ever: $143.8bn (+16%), iPhone $85.3bn (+23%), Greater China +38%, GM 48.2%.

**Memory checks (StoneX/TrendForce + Conor, 2026-06-23):** Apple has **already agreed to a ~+20% mobile DRAM price increase in Q3** — among the few "special deals" running above the ~8-13% Q3 base — and is in the **first LTA wave alongside US CSPs and NVIDIA** (3-yr 2026-28 contracts, floor price + in some cases a ~15% QoQ ceiling) (StoneX/TrendForce "Memory expert call — Avril", 2026-06-23). On the supply side, Avril reads Apple's "vocal" memory-cost complaints as Apple **not getting all the LPDDR5 output it wants** (it is fighting NVIDIA for LPDDR5 as it pushes AI-PC + iPhone share) rather than a pricing-only story (StoneX/TrendForce "Memory expert call — Avril", 2026-06-23). On wafers, Conor corroborates the Fubon read — TSMC raised Apple's wafer price only **low-single-digit in 2026, then +≥10% in 2027** (the first time Apple is included in the hike) (Redburn "Conor O'Mara Asia Trip Takeaways", 2026-06-23).

## Debate / thesis
- **Capstone model (house view):** revenue $416B / $480B / $539B / $585B (2025-28E, +6/+15/+12/+9%); adj EPS $8.35 (2025E, +22% off $6.82 in 2024); GM ~47%, op margin ~32%. Drivers: iPhone units 240M→267M→280M with ASP $874→$946→$1,021; Services $109B→$126B→$145B→$164B. (Capstone official model — WIP 2Q26, 2026-06-16)
- **Bull:** iPhone 17 family is "the most popular lineup in our history through March," 99% US satisfaction (451 Research); 4 consecutive quarters of ~20%+ iPhone growth despite supply constraints (Cook, Q2 FY26). **Installed-base + cycle thesis:** Bernstein initiated AAPL **Outperform** as "the gateway to the Intelligence Revolution," strong EPS growth from user-base expansion + buybacks, with Google DOJ remedies resolved favorably — clearing a significant downside overhang and opening the path to leverage Gemini's AI (Bernstein initiation/Newman, 2025-09-15, PT $290). JPM frames a "much more favorable set up with investors than prior years" — lengthening lifecycle drives iPhone 17 volume despite feature upgrades perceived as modest vs. 16; FY26 iPhone 17-series volume **246m vs. 232m FY25** (bull case to 248m with the foldable), ~10% rev CAGR FY25→27 → ~13% EPS growth on buybacks (JPM/Chatterjee, OW, 2025-12-15). **Memory dislocation is a relative win for Apple** — scale, LTAs and supplier relationships let it pass through cost while Android OEMs struggle; Apple's product GM shows the *weakest* historical correlation to NAND/DRAM ASPs in JPM's coverage (lowest memory % of BOM + scale; JPM, 2025-12-15); Citi concurs Apple/Samsung are "the least impacted given a higher-end product mix and supply-chain priority" (Citi China Tech, 2026-01-20). Citi/Counterpoint sees Apple **flat shipments YoY vs. global −12% sell-through / −14% shipments** (briefing 2026-06-16). Margin held via pass-through despite memory (BofA/Mohan Reit. Buy, **PT $380** on 37x C27 EPS, 2026-06-18). **Foldable = the next ASP catalyst:** Woodring (MS, equity call 2026-06-16) frames FY27 as an "iPhone X-like cycle saved by pricing" — ~20m foldable units, $2,400 start, ~10pts ASP growth from foldable mix alone; MS OW **PT $360**, FY27E EPS $10.30 (~8% above consensus). Citi models industry foldable units **20m/29m/45m (2025/26E/27E)**, driven by the foldable iPhone, with China smartphone ASP +5–7% even as global units fall ~5% in 2026 (Citi China Tech, 2026-01-20). Services annuity compounding on 2.5bn devices, plus an expanding **ad franchise**: the Apple Search Ads → "Apple Ads" rebrand and unification of app + web advertising under AAK (SKAdNetwork merged with the web framework, view-through reporting added) point to a broader ads push into Maps, Podcasts and Apple TV+ — a Services lever that is structurally immune to elongated hardware replacement cycles (Stratechery/Seufert, 2025-04-24); plus JPM Apple Pay penetration ramp.

Installed-base AI gating: ~850m iPhones can't run Apple Intelligence, ~1.3bn can't run advanced Siri (MS, 2026-06-09); most-intense Siri needs iPhone 17 Pro/Air+ (~150m of ~1.5bn base, Woodring). ## Catalysts / what to watch
- **FQ3'26 (June qtr) print:** ~late-Jul/early-Aug — memory GM impact + Mac supply commentary.
- **Cook→Ternus CEO transition** effective **2026-09-01**; first earnings under Ternus is the Sept-qtr call (Oct).
- **Fall product cycle (mid-Sept):** iPhone 18 Pro/Pro Max + **foldable iPhone** (~20m units, ~$2,400; iPhone 18 base reportedly C1Q27 per JPM); Citi supply checks point to a 3-SKU 2H26 lineup (foldable, 18 Pro, Pro Max) on an 18 Air delay (Citi China Tech, 2026-01-20). Foldable content winners in the Apple chain: Lens Tech (UTG cover glass), FII (titanium casing/assembly), DSBJ (FPC/PCB), Luxshare, BYDE (Citi, 2026-01-20). Touch-screen Mac, Mac mini US production (Houston) ramps later this year.
- **Apple Intelligence / Siri 2.0 relaunch** (English beta, Fall 2026); third-party app/agent integration and iCloud-monetized image tools to watch.
- **Memory pricing trajectory** through mid-2027 — and whether Apple takes the **iPhone 18 price hike** (~$50 reported, JPM 2026-06-08; Cook confirmed "forced to raise pricing" in WSJ, BofA 2026-06-18).
- **iPhone SoC packaging transition** — Bernstein supply checks see the A20 (iPhone 18 Pro, 2026) move from InFO to **CoW (Chip-on-Wafer)** with DRAM placed side-by-side, plus a new DRAM package for wider AI bandwidth — drives advanced-packaging equipment demand (DISCO, Ebara, Towa) (Bernstein Semis deck, 2025-05-05).
- **Intel foundry** preliminary agreement (M7 on 18A-P, end-2027 reportedly) — supply diversification vs. TSMC. Fubon is skeptical: "we don't think Apple will use Intel even in some legacy products" — reads the Intel/second-source noise as **Apple leveraging TSMC for 2027 wafer-capacity allocation** amid a widening **15–20% TSMC supply-demand gap**, not a genuine foundry switch (Fubon "Semi 2027 outlook", 2026-06-05).
- **TSMC wafer-price pass-through:** TSMC raised Apple's wafer price only **1–2% in FY26** (vs ~10% for AI/HPC, ~5% for other consumer) — Apple's scale/priority cushioning it. But Fubon expects the **FY27 N2/N3 wafer-price increase to start at ~10%, *including* Apple** — a forward COGS headwind into the A-series/foldable cycle (Fubon, 2026-06-05).

## WWDC 2026 / smartphone market (Citi/Counterpoint, 2026-06-15)
- **Demand backdrop:** Counterpoint sell-through **−5% y/y in Q1** (Apple's first negative since Q4'23) and **−10% y/y total market in April**; FY26 global shipments revised to **−14%** (gradual recovery through 2030), but Apple seen **~flat** as iPhone 17 base stays resilient. The base **iPhone 18 shifts to ~March 2027 (Q1)** — so 2H26 is lighter and the foldable alone won't offset the calendar shift. Apple holding prices flat + carrier promo dollars positions it to grab switchers as Android OEMs raise prices on memory inflation; under-$600 tier (legacy models) is the share-gain opportunity, premiumization a tailwind.
- **WWDC takeaways:** **Siri got its own app** (key stepping-stone for agentic features + Home integration), with **Google/Gemini grounding world knowledge** behind the privacy-first on-device + Private Cloud Compute split — a conservative but "calculated" evolution. Monetization likely folds AI pricing into **existing Services tiers** (not a standalone AI subscription), and third-party apps could charge a premium for Siri/Apple Intelligence pro features. Survey work shows consumers use AI features but are **not short-circuiting upgrade cycles** to get them — validating Apple's slow-roll. Unresolved: **Apple Intelligence in China and the EU** (DMA July deadline on third-party sensor access hangs over both Apple and Google).
- **Foldable:** ~**6M units in 2026** (book-style), September launch on track but inventory/availability TBD — possibly a Vision Pro-style limited rollout if hinge/hardware issues persist; ~4% of the market by 2030; Apple ASP just under $1,000 in 2026/27, accelerating by 2028. Memory: LPDDR5 +10–15% in Q3, +15–20% in Q2 — accelerating on-device-AI penetration (>60% AI-capable by 2029). _(Citi/Counterpoint WWDC, 2026-06-15.)_ [Source](../relat%C3%B3rios%20bons/2026_06_15_aapl_wwdc_citi_15_jun_26.html)

## Risks
- **Memory (DRAM/NAND) cost inflation** — "unlike anything in 40+ years" (Cook via Vital Knowledge, 2026-06-17); largest near-term GM and pricing risk; NAND going "parabolic" (MS, 2026-06-16). Note relative mitigant: lowest memory BOM exposure + supply-chain priority in JPM/Citi coverage (JPM, 2025-12-15; Citi, 2026-01-20). A TSM/Asia-supply-chain expert added color on Apple's supply-chain leverage: Apple reportedly **traded a wide-angle camera module to Samsung in exchange for stable iPhone-18 DRAM allocation**, and TSMC raised Apple's advanced-node wafer pricing only modestly (Apple is TSMC's #2 customer at ~17% of sales — note OCR in the source garbled this as "70%") — i.e. Apple is among the least memory-impacted OEMs (BofA best-ideas expert, 2026-04-09). [Source](../relat%C3%B3rios%20bons/2026_04_09_baml_best_ideas_9_apr_2026.html)
- **Unit-demand elasticity** if price hikes pass through into a soft global smartphone market (−5% to −10%+; Citi sees global smartphone units −5% in 2026E, IDC −10%+).
- **AI execution / monetization** — repeated timeline slips (Apple Intelligence China, global personalized Siri), AI-team talent exodus (JPM, 2025-12-15), narrow device eligibility, no third-party agent integration yet, China/EU regulatory (DMA) overhang; "funding short vs. semis."
- **Supply concentration & advanced-node availability** (3nm SoC gating; outsourced Asia manufacturing — 10-K risk factors: highly competitive markets, dependence on single/limited-source suppliers, supplier concentration). RF supply highly concentrated to AAPL (QRVO/SWKS content volatile gen/gen) and Apple is in-housing the modem (~$5bn QCOM revenue at risk) (UBS 2026 overview). Modem-insourcing corroborated by two independent reads: Qualcomm flagged "more insourcing at Apple with their modem" (Citi C1Q26 recap, 2026-04-30), and SiTime (clock/timing supplier, ~50c–$1 of content per Apple modem) guided its MIC vertical +30%+ for 2026 / >20% of revenue in 2H on Apple internal-modem pull-through — i.e. Apple's modem ramp is real and accelerating into 2H26 (SiTime expert call, 2026-05-07). [Citi](../relat%C3%B3rios%20bons/2026_04_30_msft_wfc_c1q26_recap_30_apr_26.html) · [SiTime](../relat%C3%B3rios%20bons/2026_05_07_sitm_arm_7_apr_2026.html)
- **Tariffs** — temporary consumer-electronics exemption is a key swing; if it expires, Apple, suppliers and US consumers "all likely feel the pain"; reshoring is hard (Bernstein Semis deck, 2025-05-05). Apple has announced a **$600bn US investment over four years**, with Corning (3x KY glass), Coherent (TX VCSELs) and Lumentum scaling US supply for Apple (JPM, 2025-12-15).
- **Glass substrate (supply-chain read-through):** SEMCO (Samsung Electro-Mechanics) is supplying next-gen glass-substrate samples to major tech players "including Apple" — early signal of the glass-core packaging roadmap for Apple silicon (MS "MLCC Super Cycle", 2026-06-22).
- **China exposure** (~strong but cyclically/geopolitically sensitive; Greater China is a swing segment) — in 3Q25 China smartphones, only Apple and Oppo posted positive YoY shipment growth among the top 5 (Citi China Tech, 2026-01-20).
- **OpenAI relationship friction** (potential legal action over 2024 ChatGPT terms) — low-probability, high-flag (briefing 2026-05-18).
