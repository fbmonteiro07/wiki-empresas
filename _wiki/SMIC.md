## Snapshot
China's largest pure-play foundry (0981 HK / 688981 SH), the national champion for domestic semiconductor manufacturing. Predominantly a **mature/legacy-node** house — 12-inch is ~76-77% of revenue, 8-inch ~23-24% (Q1 2026, 2026-05-15) — serving consumer electronics (~43-46%), smartphone (~19-25%), computer/tablet (~14-15%), industrial/automotive (~11-14%) and connectivity/IoT (~7-8%). Revenue is overwhelmingly China-facing (~85-89% domestic), making SMIC the cleanest listed proxy for the **China semiconductor self-sufficiency / import-substitution** theme. It also runs the only credible advanced-node line inside China — pushing 7nm (N+1/N+2, reportedly 5nm-class) via **DUV multi-patterning** since it is cut off from EUV under US export controls — which makes it Huawei's de facto leading-edge fab and the production backbone for domestic AI silicon (Huawei Ascend 910B/910C are made on SMIC 7nm; MS China DataCenter, 2025-04-29). In the chain: TSMC owns the leading edge globally; SMIC is the China-localized substitute pulling mature-node demand onshore and the bottleneck for any domestic AI/advanced-logic ambition. The sell-side now bifurcates sharply: Bernstein rates SMIC **Outperform** as the chief beneficiary of Huawei's AI scale-up (Bernstein Huawei roadmap, 2025-09-22), while MS sits **Equal-weight** flagging the mature-node downcycle (MS AI supply chain, 2025-08-18) — see thesis.

## Current state (latest quarter)
**Q1 2026 (2026-05-15):** Revenue $2,505m, +0.7% q/q / ~+11% y/y. Gross margin **20.1%**, +0.9pt q/q (ahead of consensus). Operating profit $248m; EBITDA $1,435m (57.3% margin); net profit attributable $197m, +14% q/q but below cons on lower government subsidies. Utilization **93.1%**, -2.6pt q/q (off the near-full Q4 levels). Shipments -0.2% q/q but blended **ASP +2.5% q/q** — pricing, not volume, carried the quarter. Cash $13.9bn vs total debt $14.5bn; D/E 40.5%.
- **Guide (Q2 2026):** revenue **+14% to +16% q/q** — a clear upside surprise vs Street/JPM at +2-7% (briefing 2026-05-17). Split is roughly shipment +~10% / ASP +~5%, with **5-10% price hikes** in BCD/analog/PMIC/storage (JPM Gokul, briefing 2026-05-17). GM guided 20-22%.
- **Demand drivers (Q1 2026 call):** "strong demand for supporting chips from AI" lifting power-management; capacity constraints in NOR/NAND flash; traction in automotive-grade, ToF sensors and Micro OLED. Domestic-substitution / "industrial chain reshuffling" still the structural tailwind.
- **No 2025 dividend** — capital intensity of capacity expansion + R&D cited (Q1 2026 call). FY2025 (2026-02-11): record revenue **$9,327m, +16.2% y/y**, GM 21% (+3pt y/y), net profit $685m, **capex $8.1bn**, year-end capacity 1,059k 8-inch-equiv wafers/month, annual utilization 93.5% (+8pt y/y). FY2026 framing: revenue growth "higher than industry average," capex roughly flat vs $8.1bn, +~40k 12-inch-equiv wafers/month of new capacity, and **depreciation +~30% y/y** on new-fab ramp — a margin headwind to underwrite.

## Advanced-node / Huawei supply line (the policy-driven optionality)
- **SMIC is Huawei's wafer provider for advanced logic.** Huawei Ascend 910B/910C run on SMIC 7nm (N+1/N+2-class); foreign fabs cannot manufacture Huawei advanced-node chips under US restrictions, so SMIC remains the sole supplier (MS China DataCenter, 2025-04-29). MS wafer math: 910B die ~666mm², ~70-75 good dies per 12-inch wafer → **10kwpm could yield ~200k 910B units/month**; the 910C die has roughly doubled, lowering yield, so MS estimates **53-106k 910C units/month** at 15-30% yield on the same 10wpm — the mid-to-high end of which it judges enough to meet Chinese hyperscaler demand if H20 stays unavailable.
- **Capacity expansion is the swing factor.** Jefferies channel checks (Q425 Techknowledge, 2025-12-17) put SMIC N7 capacity at **~15kwpm going to ~25kwpm**, allocated roughly: ~10k mobile (Huawei Qilin/Kirin, 110-120mm² die, 50-60% yield supports ~50m smartphone units), ~2k CPU (Kunpeng/Loongson/Feiteng/Hygon), and an AI ramp of Ascend ~5k + Cambricon ~0.5k + others ~3.5k wpm; at 20-30% yield on large dies that implies ~900k–2m AI chips/year. FT (via Bernstein Huawei roadmap, 2025-09-22) reported **local AI production capacity to triple in 2026**, corroborated by Bernstein's WFE-import tracker showing stronger-than-expected China capex.
- **Huawei roadmap is the demand pull.** Bernstein's Huawei roadmap (2025-09-22): Ascend 950DT/960/970 launching 4Q26/4Q27/4Q28; Huawei's single chip is only ~6% of Nvidia (VR200) performance, so it competes through scale — putting ~114x more chips into a SuperPoD via its UB networking protocol. That "more chips, less-advanced node" strategy requires **~16x more chips**, which Bernstein calls **inflationary for foundry/WFE** and the core of its Outperform — SMIC as the leading provider of advanced-logic capacity directly supporting Huawei accelerator production. Huawei publicly articulating the roadmap signals confidence its **local foundry supply is secured** (though Bernstein notes the chain is not yet fully localized).
- **Onshoring is real but costly.** Bernstein's foundry-decoupling work (Semis deck, 2025-05-05): Chinese IC local-sourcing rose from 30-35% (2017-20) to 40-46% (2021-24); Huawei and other Chinese customers shifted **~$1-3bn/yr more advanced-node production onshore to SMIC**. But SMIC spent **>$10bn of extra capex over 2020-24** to bring advanced-node production home — a heavy cost burden — and Bernstein stays conservative on Huawei smartphone units because SMIC is **struggling to improve advanced-node yield** without EUV. Decoupling is happening mostly in advanced nodes; in mature nodes localization barely moved (high-40s% → low-50s%).

## Debate / thesis
- **Bull:** The Q2 +14-16% guide blew past a low Street bar (JPM, briefing 2026-05-17), and the lever is **pricing power** — 5-10% hikes across BCD/analog/PMIC/storage plus ASP +2.5% q/q in Q1 — evidence the mature-node price war is easing as memory-driven supply tightness (NOR/NAND constraints, Q1 2026 call) lets SMIC reallocate capacity to higher-value parts. Domestic localization is secular: China stayed ~85-89% of revenue through 2025, with industrial/automotive +60% y/y and consumer +30% y/y in FY2025 (2026-02-11 call). Q3 2025 (2025-11-14) management was running 95.8% utilization and explicitly **supply-constrained** ("shipment volumes unable to fully satisfy customer demand"). As China's only advanced-node foundry, SMIC captures any onshore AI/logic build it can supply via DUV — and the Huawei AI roadmap is structurally inflationary for foundry demand: the "scale via more chips" approach needs ~16x more silicon, with local AI capacity set to triple in 2026 (Bernstein Huawei roadmap, 2025-09-22, **Outperform**). MS frames the prize as China local-GPU revenue growing to **Rmb287bn by 2027**, with domestic supply almost fully meeting China AI demand by 2027 (MS AI supply chain, 2025-08-18).
- **Bear:** Margins are structurally pressured — gross margin sits ~19-22% (well below TSMC's 50%+), and **depreciation +~30% y/y** from the new-fab ramp (FY2026 outlook, 2026-02-11) eats into incremental volume. The mature-node **capacity glut / price war** is the core risk: MS flags a **prolonged downcycle in mature-node foundry and niche memory** driven by the China fab supply ramp (MS AI supply chain, 2025-08-18); GM fell to 18-20% guides through 2H 2025 (Q3/Q4 guides) and ASP swung -6.4% q/q in Q2 2025 (2025-08-08) before the recent recovery — pricing is volatile and China is adding capacity industry-wide. Management itself flags **"2H consumer risk"** (JPM, briefing 2026-05-17). Net profit leans on government subsidies (Q1 2026 miss). No EUV access caps the advanced-node roadmap at DUV multi-patterning — higher cost, lower yield, hard to scale economically; Bernstein notes SMIC absorbed **>$10bn extra capex 2020-24** to onshore advanced node and is still struggling to lift advanced-node yield (Bernstein Semis deck, 2025-05-05). No dividend; capital sink.
- **Where the sell-side stands:** Coverage is now **split, not uniformly cautious**. **Bernstein (Outperform, PT HK$30 / CNY 110)** is the bull, framing SMIC as the leading-edge beneficiary of Huawei's AI scale-up (Bernstein Huawei roadmap, 2025-09-22). **MS upgraded SMIC to Equal-weight on 2025-04-08** but kept it **EW with PT HK$40** (vs HK$53.5, ~-25% downside) at the 2025-08-18 cut — bullish on the domestic-GPU production story but unwilling to underwrite mature-node margins (MS AI supply chain, 2025-08-18). **JPM (Gokul Hariharan) upgraded UW → Neutral, PT HK$57 → HK$67** on 2026-05-17 (briefing) on the +14-16% Q2 guide and 5-10% price hikes, but kept it Neutral on 2H consumer risk. Net: the policy/Huawei optionality is what separates the bull (Bernstein) from the margin skeptics (MS/JPM).

## Catalysts / what to watch
- **Q2 2026 print** (next earnings; mid-Aug cadence) — does the +14-16% guide convert, and does GM hold the 20-22% guide as depreciation steps up. Watch ASP trajectory (pricing-power durability) vs shipment volume.
- **2H 2026 consumer demand** — the explicit bear flag (JPM, briefing 2026-05-17); whether domestic consumer/smartphone holds after the H1 restock.
- **Capacity/capex pace** — FY2026 capex ~flat at ~$8bn and +~40k 12-inch wafers/month; pace of mature-node adds across China (glut vs discipline) drives the price-war debate. Kokusai's 2026 WFE outlook sees **China foundry/logic (SMIC, Hua Hong) flattish-to-up** (Jefferies Q425 Techknowledge, 2025-12-17).
- **Advanced-node progress** — N7 capacity ramp from ~15k → ~25kwpm (Jefferies, 2025-12-17), DUV-based 7nm / N+2 yields, and Huawei Ascend volume against the 950/960/970 roadmap; US export-control changes (tighter DUV/SME restrictions, or Entity List actions) are the key exogenous swing.
- **US/China policy** — tariff and export-control headlines; in Q2 2025 (2025-08-08) management noted tariff fears "did not happen, or at least have not happened yet" — that remains a live tail. H20 export status is the binary: a no-H20 regime forces Chinese hyperscalers onto SMIC-made domestic GPUs (MS China DataCenter, 2025-04-29). The proposed US **MATCH Act explicitly names SMIC** (with Hua Hong, Huawei, CXMT, YMTC) as firms ASML would be barred from selling/servicing advanced DUV immersion tools to — a direct hit to SMIC's leading-edge tooling path if enacted (AlphaSense "DR ASML primer", 2026-06-22).
- **China CapEx splurge — a demand tailwind read-through** — Jefferies' supply-chain checks (Daifuku/clean-room build-out) flag a "gigantic" China CapEx wave: the China client base has consolidated to ~10 real spenders — **SMIC, CXMT, YMTC, the Huawei complex** — spending heavily; Daifuku raised its China revenue forecast to ~¥110bn (from ¥98bn), and Tokyo Electron lifted its China-WFE view to +10% (potentially more on the CXMT/YMTC IPOs). Bullish for SMIC's own fab build-out, channeled through China local tools (NAURA/AMEC) and Japanese vendors (TEL/Kokusai/AMAT) (Jefferies "Asia tech", 2026-04-07).

## Risks
- **Mature-node price war / capacity glut** — China-wide foundry capacity additions; MS flags a prolonged mature-node downcycle (MS AI supply chain, 2025-08-18); ASP volatility (-6.4% q/q in Q2 2025).
- **Margin compression** — GM structurally ~19-22%; depreciation +~30% y/y on new-fab ramp; subsidy-dependent net profit.
- **Advanced-node yield / cost burden** — no EUV access caps the roadmap at DUV multi-patterning; SMIC absorbed >$10bn extra capex 2020-24 to onshore advanced node and still struggles on yield (Bernstein Semis deck, 2025-05-05); risk of tighter DUV/SME or Entity List restrictions impairing the leading-edge effort and Huawei relationship.
- **China-concentration / demand** — ~85-89% domestic revenue; exposed to China consumer/smartphone cycle and any 2H 2026 consumer softness.
- **Capital intensity / no return of capital** — ~$8bn/yr capex, no dividend; FCF negative on the build-out (FY2025 investing -$6.5bn).
- **Geopolitical tail** — US/China tariffs and sanctions; secondary-customer / supply-chain disruption.
