## Snapshot
Alphabet is the full-stack AI franchise: Google Services (Search, YouTube, subscriptions/devices — ~$90B/qtr, the cash engine), Google Cloud (GCP + Workspace + Wiz security, now the marginal growth driver), and Other Bets (Waymo, etc.). The differentiated edge vs. other hyperscalers is owning the *entire* vertical stack — frontier models (Gemini), custom silicon (TPU, Axion CPU), networking/OCS, data centers, and apps/agents — which management frames as the source of leading power/perf efficiency and ROIC (Q1 FY26, 2026-04-29). Sits at the center of the AI capex cycle as both a demand anchor (its own ~$180-190B C26 capex) and an emerging merchant-silicon supplier (external TPU sales begin late C26). The TPU franchise is the swing variable: Asia supply-chain checks now size total TPU production at ~3.1-3.2mn units in 2026, potentially doubling to 5-6mn in 2027 (~5mn via Broadcom + ~1mn via MediaTek) — alongside AWS Trainium one of the two dominant merchant-ASIC programs (Jefferies Techknowledge, 2025-12-17). On the model side, Gemini 2.5 Pro is tied #1 on LM Arena and recognized as a leading cost-effective frontier model that has displaced OpenAI on coding/overall leaderboards (JPM OpenAI initiation; JPM Anthropic deep-dive, 2025-09-18).

## TPU / merchant-silicon (Asia supply-chain, Jefferies Techknowledge, 2025-12-17)
- **Volumes inflecting:** total TPU production ~3.1-3.2mn units in 2026 (some checks as high as 4mn, viewed as a stretch), potentially **doubling to 5-6mn in 2027** — ~5mn from Broadcom + ~1mn from MediaTek. With AWS Trainium, TPU is one of the two dominant merchant ASICs; total ASIC + ASIC-server units both seen +50%+.
- **CoWoS allocation reset higher** for TPU 2027 projects (TSMC AP7/AP8 fully loaded); slot estimates raised to ~130k from a prior 110k. Broadcom 2nm TPUs cited as a large 2027 TSMC driver.
- **v8 generation:** "Zebrafish"/"Sunfish" v8 on 2nm, larger than Ironwood (v7), pricing roughly **$23-34K** (≈2x prior gen). MediaTek's v8e ("Zebrafish") taped out with good performance; MediaTek reiterates ~$1bn 2026 TPU revenue, ~1mn units across 2026-27 (~15% of 2027 TPU shipments), ~$4K dollar-content/chip; follow-on v9 work with Google still early-stage. EMC gaining CCL share toward 50-70% on V6/V7.
- **Packaging:** Google evaluating Intel **EMIB-T** for TPU v9, citing CoWoS capacity constraints and power-delivery advantages in the HBM4 era (more visible EMIB ramp 2H26).
- **HBM procurement shifting Broadcom→MediaTek (StoneX/TrendForce "Memory expert call — Avril", 2026-06-23):** in prior TPU generations Google bought only conventional DRAM itself and **relied entirely on Broadcom for HBM** procurement; now **MediaTek (TPU V8/V9) is increasingly handling Google's HBM directly** and is in talks with Samsung on HBM3E/HBM4E. Google **attempted to procure HBM directly itself but found it too challenging**, so for the next ~3 years HBM procurement stays split between **Broadcom and MediaTek** (the cited ~20% Google figure includes both, all flowing to Google). The driver is cost: Broadcom charges Google for HBM in the BOM, MediaTek does not — pushing share toward MediaTek (Conor corroborates: rising HBM cost is "the key reason" Google wants MediaTek) (StoneX/TrendForce "Memory expert call — Avril", 2026-06-23; Redburn "Conor O'Mara Asia Trip Takeaways", 2026-06-23).
- **Memory pull:** Google's 2026 HBM + DRAM demand reportedly **double** prior plan; vendors flag TPU demand "much stronger than original plan," with Google repeatedly raising forecasts — a key reason memory checks revised 2026 ASIC+GPU growth to ~40% (from 25-30%), though prior cycles saw later downward revisions (a risk flag).
- **Rack-level build-out:** Hon Hai (Foxconn) "aggressively" expanding US capacity for Google, moving from L6 components to full L10-11 compute trays/racks; Google also adopting BBU in next TPU server and shifting toward ±400VDC/HVDC power architecture (OCP).

  - **Regulatory** — UK CMA ordering Search-ranking disclosure (6mo) + data portability (3mo); ongoing US antitrust on ad-tech, Search distribution, Android/Play (10-K FY25). YouTube exposed to UK under-16 social ban.
- **Where the sell-side stands:** Broadly constructive-to-bullish; PTs cluster $405-450 (Barclays $405 OW, Citi $447 Buy, GS $450). MS OW but relative-underweight within MAG on valuation/IPO-supply. Rothschild & Redburn Buy. No bear-rated desk in the inbox.
- **Chris Wood (Jefferies) removes Alphabet from two global portfolios (2026-06-25).** Christopher Wood removes Alphabet from both his global long-only and international long-only portfolios, rotating the capital into SK Hynix and Samsung Electronics (hardware / "picks and shovels"). The rationale is NOT a fundamental bear call on Google's business but a *Jevons Paradox portfolio rotation*: cheaper AI tokens (GLM-5.2, open-source Chinese models) → more compute demand → the hardware layer (DRAM, GPU) outperforms the model/software layer. Worth noting as a contra-indicator: Chris Wood is historically a buy-and-hold macro/tech investor who rarely rotates out of core long-term holdings. The removal reflects a view that GOOG's AI optionality is already partially captured and that the near-term marginal alpha is in semiconductor hardware. Neutral-to-negative on positioning; fundamental thesis intact (Jefferies / Christopher Wood "GREED & fear: The mother of all cycles", 2026-06-25). [../relat%C3%B3rios%20bons/Chris_Wood.html](../relat%C3%B3rios%20bons/Chris_Wood.html)
- **MS (Stephen Byrd) sees Google Cloud as a hyperscaler winner of the model-routing era (2026-06-26).** MS's thematic research head argues that hyperscalers building sophisticated model-routing/orchestration capabilities — including Google Cloud — are net beneficiaries if "orchestration layers can cobble together multiple lower-cost models to achieve the same result as a single frontier model." However, the same argument is also the bear case for Google's first-party Gemini models if orchestration commoditizes frontier-level output. Net: positive for Google Cloud, ambiguous for DeepMind/Gemini business specifically (Morgan Stanley / Stephen Byrd "Tokenmaxxing discussion", 2026-06-26). [../relat%C3%B3rios%20bons/2026_06_26_stephen_byrd_on_tokenmaxxing.html](../relat%C3%B3rios%20bons/2026_06_26_stephen_byrd_on_tokenmaxxing.html)
- **Token-optimization read-through — neutral:** UBS is **neutral** on hyperscalers re: token optimization — enterprise down-tiering to cheaper SLMs/open-source Chinese models (Qwen, Z.ai, Kimi, DeepSeek) could weigh on frontier-model API revenue growth and AI price sensitivity could limit near-term GPU pricing, but the hyperscalers are already multi-model and face a wall of AI demand. For GOOG the down-tiering risk is within the frontier suite (cheaper Gemini tiers vs the premium model) rather than a platform threat, and being multi-model is framed as an edge "similar to the advantage secured by the likes of Snowflake and MongoDB that were multi-cloud while AWS and Microsoft were pushing their native databases" (UBS "The Ramp in Token Optimization" / Keirstead-Arcuri, 2026-06-23).

## Risks
- **Search ad concentration** — bulk of revenue/profit from advertising; AI-driven query-behavior shift could pressure CTR/CPC mix even as volume grows (10-K FY25; "intense competition"). ChatGPT substitution is the headline overhang (UBS OpenAI deep-dive).
- **Antitrust / regulatory remedies** — active US suits on ad-tech, Search distribution, Android/Play Store; ad-tech guilty finding with remedy uncertainty (Stratechery — Eric/ADS); Search-distribution DOJ remedies appear benign (Bernstein, 2025-09-15). UK CMA Search/data-portability orders.
- **Capex digestion / depreciation drag** — accelerating depreciation + energy/DC opex pressure P&L; ROIC depends on backlog converting and AI monetization scaling.
- **Compute supply chain** — power, land, advanced-node (TSMC CoWoS), HBM/DRAM and packaging (ABF, T-glass) constraints; TPU upside is gated by whether the supply chain can meet doubled memory/CoWoS demand (Jefferies Techknowledge, 2025-12-17).
- **Frontier-model / talent leakage** — model leadership not guaranteed (Shazeer departure; DeepMind a net talent donor to Anthropic, JPM, 2025-09-18; MoE-vs-TPU debate); model commoditization risk (JPM OpenAI initiation).
- **YouTube brand-ad softness** and FX (tailwind moderating to ~1pt in Q2 from 3pt in Q1).
