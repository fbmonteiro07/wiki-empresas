## Snapshot
Fabless data-infrastructure semiconductor supplier "spanning the data center core to the network edge" — core IP in complex SoCs integrating analog, mixed-signal, DSP, **electro-optics and silicon photonics** (FY26 10-K, 2026-03-11). One reportable segment; the **data center end market is 74% of revenue** ($6.10B of $8.19B in FY26, +46% y/y) with comms/enterprise/carrier the rest after the August-2025 divestiture of the automotive-Ethernet unit to Infineon for $2.5B (FY26 10-K). Three AI franchises drive the story: **custom compute ASICs/XPUs** for hyperscalers (the #2 merchant custom-silicon partner behind AVGO), **electro-optics** (1.6T PAM DSPs, 2nm coherent DCI, now CPO via Celestial AI), and **data-center switching/interconnect** (51.2T/100T, AEC/retimers, CXL via XConn). Highly concentrated: top-10 customers = 82% of FY26 revenue; one direct customer 14%, one distributor 37% (FY26 10-K).

## Position in the value chain
MRVL is a fabless designer sitting between the foundry/IP/memory supply base and the hyperscalers. It owns no fabs — it co-designs custom ASICs and electro-optic/DSP/switching silicon, manufactured at TSMC, and sells the bulk of its data-center output to the four US hyperscalers (AWS Trainium, Microsoft Maia roadmaps it owns that AVGO does not; per JPM Sur, 2026-06-17). It is the **#2 merchant custom-silicon partner behind Broadcom**, levered to (a) hyperscaler ASIC insourcing and (b) the copper-to-optical interconnect transition.

Marvell (with Broadcom) is also flagged as a go-to source of **high-speed SerDes IP** for hyperscalers (p.20), and is named as **first out of the gate** as an HBM base-die design partner (p.40-41). (SemiAnalysis, 2025-08-11)
- **ECTC 2026 — custom HBM package-level detail + optical-on-package (SemiAnalysis "EMIB-T Roadmap / ECTC 2026", 2026-07-02):** at ECTC Marvell finally gave the package-level data for **custom HBM4E**. By moving interface logic off the accelerator into a **custom base die** (fabbed on advanced logic), it cuts the **host ASIC footprint dedicated to HBM PHYs/logic by ~60%** — freeing area for compute/cache/I/O — and shortens the **interposer channel from 6.5 mm → 1.5 mm** (keeping 9 routing layers / 2-2 µm L/S while raising bandwidth; example: 1024 ch @ 32 Gb/s = 4.1 TB/s ≈ a 2048-bit JEDEC HBM4E interface at 16 Gb/s). Notably Marvell's example uses an **organic RDL interposer** (not silicon), lowering packaging cost. Nvidia's **Feynman also adopts custom HBM** (SemiAnalysis estimates ~16% of Rubin GPU die area today is HBM logic/PHY), validating the architecture as an industry direction. On optics, Marvell brought **OMIB (Optical Multi-Chip Interconnect Bridge)** and **Photonic Fabric** (both from the Celestial AI acquisition) onto the package — embedding a PIC in the organic RDL interposer where optics are needed and electrical bridges elsewhere; claims **1.8 Tbps/mm²** bandwidth density; tested an optical engine on a 5 nm (likely TSMC N5) EIC with 4× 56 Gb/s TX-RX pairs (224 Gb/s/direction) using **EAMs** rather than micro-ring modulators (better thermal stability / wider wavelength range, though SemiAnalysis thinks EAMs will be hard to scale). Read: reinforces both the custom-HBM technical lead and the Celestial-AI-driven scale-up CPO leg already on this page. (SemiAnalysis "EMIB-T Roadmap / ECTC 2026", 2026-07-02)

## Risks
- **Customer concentration** — significant revenue from a few customers; top-10 = 82%, with sales heavily concentrated in the **data center end market** (FY26 10-K). Loss/reduction at a major customer "would likely harm" results.
- **Design-win dependence** — revenue swings on timing/number of design wins; custom programs are multi-year and binary.
- **Foundry/supply dependence** — fabless; relies on TSMC and third-party manufacturers/substrates/HBM; $1B FY27 capacity prepayments to secure supply (10-Q).
- **Merchant-silicon competition** — AVGO (custom + DSP share gains at 1.6T), captive hyperscaler/COT teams, NVDA captive DSP; pricing/share pressure as customers diversify suppliers. CPO long-term (30%+ of market, Jefferies) is a scale-out transceiver/DSP headwind.
- **Margin mix** — ASIC growth dilutive to gross margin vs optics/switching.
- **Macro / AI-capex cyclicality & geopolitics** — China/export-policy exposure; substantial non-US operations (FY26 10-K).
- **Governance/optics note (not a red flag) — new CFO's first open-market sale amid the selloff:** per Barron's "Inside Scoop" 13D/insider column (Barron's staff, sourced to Verity Data/Form 4 filings, June 29, 2026 issue), newly appointed CFO **Dan Durn sold 2,250 shares at ~$281.01 (~$632,272)** — his **first open-market sale** since joining MRVL's board in 2024 — days after being named CFO on **2026-06-15** (succeeding Willem Meintjes, who stays as an advisor through April 2027; Durn resigned his board seat on becoming CFO). **Quarter synthesis:** the flow confirms the two strategic legs — Celestial AI as the entry into scale-up CPO ("going to explode") and the ecosystem (not rival) position alongside NVDA/TSMC. The nuance that shifted this quarter is the **erosion of optical-DSP leadership** (1.6T in lockstep with AVGO) and the '27 custom contribution gap vs supply. From 06-27: **Teralynx T100 (102.4 Tbps, 25% lower power)** — industry's first switch breaking the 100 Tbps barrier, extends MRVL's switching capability into the ultra-high-bandwidth AI DC tier. New 06-29: **UBS raised MRVL to a $340 PT on a re-rated CXL market** ($7–10B 2030E attach TAM; MRVL leading share, CXL ~$1B C2027E → ~$2B C2028E) — turning the Structera/CXL memory-pooling story from an optionality footnote into a quantified $1–2B revenue leg, and materially resetting the top sell-side PT (was $120). Popular-press flow this quarter bookends the move: **Barron's (06-08)** ties a **+33% single-day pop** to Jensen Huang's "next $1 trillion company" comment, while **Barron's (06-29)** confirms the **S&P 500 addition effective 06-22** landed right as the stock fell **-14.1%** in an AI-capex-fear selloff — and flags the new CFO's small first open-market sale as an optics item, not a fundamental one. SemiAnalysis' HBM-wall piece (dated 2025-08-11, carried forward) reinforces the technical underpinning of the custom-silicon bull case: MRVL was first to reveal a working custom **HBM base-die** design. New 07-02: **SemiAnalysis's ECTC 2026 roundup** adds the package-level proof for that lead — custom HBM cuts the host-ASIC HBM-PHY/logic footprint ~60% and shortens the interposer channel 6.5→1.5 mm on a cheaper organic RDL interposer (Nvidia's Feynman adopting custom HBM validates it as an industry direction) — and shows Marvell bringing **optical interconnects onto the package** via OMIB + Photonic Fabric (from Celestial AI), a further data point for both the custom-HBM technical lead and the scale-up CPO leg.
