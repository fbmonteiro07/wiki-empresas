## Snapshot
Infineon is the world's **#1 power-semiconductor** vendor and **#1 automotive-semiconductor** vendor — an IDM (own fabs) spanning silicon MOSFETs/IGBTs, SiC and GaN power devices, automotive microcontrollers (AURIX), sensors and security controllers. FY2025 revenue EUR 14.66bn (Q4 FY25 call, 2025-11-12). Four segments today: **Automotive (ATV, ~50% of revenue)**, **Green Industrial Power (GIP, ~11%)**, **Power & Sensor Systems (PSS, ~29%)**, **Connected Secure Systems (CSS, ~10%)** (segment-split per IR / Bristlemoon, FY25). Re-orgs into 3 divisions — Automotive, Power Systems, Edge Systems — effective 2026-07-01 (Q2 FY26 call, 2026-05-06). For a tech/semis book the live story is the pivot of a legacy auto/industrial power franchise into the **AI data-center power** supply chain (800V DC), which is re-rating the whole analog/power group.

## Position in the value chain
IFX is a vertically-integrated (IDM) power & systems semi player: it buys Si/SiC/GaN wafers and uses some foundry partners upstream, fabs and packages its own power devices, MCUs, sensors and security chips, and sells into auto OEMs/Tier-1s, industrial, renewables/EV, and — increasingly — **data-center power (800V DC)**. The AI-datacenter-power read-through is the key incremental driver: IFX guides AI-power revenue EUR 1.5bn FY26 → EUR 2.5bn FY27, on ~$175 of semi content per kW (Q2 FY26 call, 2026-05-06), via its NVIDIA 800V centralized-DC collaboration.

**Where IFX content sits in the 800V HVDC stack (Jefferies, 2026-02-10):** NVIDIA is moving AI-DC power distribution to 800V HVDC, expected to anchor on the Kyber rack architecture (launch H2'27), with rack power rising toward 1MW from 2029. By conversion stage: 800V→54V favours **SiC MOSFETs** replacing Si IGBTs (SiC cost de-risked by China substrate oversupply; GaN still unproven >600V today and higher-cost); 54V→12V is the **GaN** opportunity (likely GaN-input/Si-output initially, full-GaN later); 12V→sub-12V VRMs stay a pure-Si MOSFET market with ~3x content uplift on the vertical-power shift. Jefferies estimates ~60% of power-semi value sits in this final Si-MOSFET stage and expects IFX to remain market leader there. IFX offers both 2-stage and 3-stage 800V conversion topologies. IFX is named in NVIDIA's 800V silicon-partner list alongside ADI, AOS, EPC, Innoscience, MPS, Navitas, OnSemi, Power Integrations, Renesas, Richtek, ROHM, STM and TI (Jefferies Q425 Techknowledge, 2025-12-17). Content benchmarks: **>$100k semi content in a >1MW rack (FY29); EUR 8–12bn DC-power SAM by 2030** (IFX co. data via UBS, 2025-12-22). IFX is building **300mm GaN production in Villach** — the move to 300mm GaN is a cost-down lever ahead of the 800V ramp (Jefferies AIXA note, 2026-02-10). JPM's sector primer frames IFX as **"the most comprehensively positioned company for this theme"** — the only vendor spanning the entire power chain (AC-DC front-end → DC-DC intermediate bus → point-of-load VRM), a direct supplier to all major GPU makers/hyperscalers, **qualified by NVIDIA for the 800V architecture** and engaged in "dozens of SST customer projects" (incl. a Nov-2025 SolarEdge collaboration on next-gen medium-voltage SiC solid-state transformers); on Gartner data IFX is the **#1 discrete power transistor supplier for computing (26% share, 2025)** and its voltage-regulator/reference computing share leapt **2%→7% (+363% YoY)** as it expands from discretes into integrated power-stage modules (JPM "First Principles — AI Power," 2026-06-25). JPM also flags a **CPU-power opportunity separate from the AI-power numbers (~€500m market already in FY25)**, expected to grow with inference/agentic demand (JPM "First Principles," 2026-06-25). BofA's full "Watts to Tokens" industry model quantifies IFX as **the biggest share-gainer in the AI analog-semi market — revenue $904m (CY25) → $4,630m (CY30), share 11.5% → 17.3%** (from #4 to the #2 AI-analog vendor behind TXN), on the broadest portfolio spanning Si/SiC/GaN across the full grid-to-core power tree; IFX is named as a lead supplier in **multi-phase VRMs, IBCs, protection/hot-swap (400/800V, an ~$800m SAM per IFX), ESS, SSTs and SSCBs** and its own data (SST ~40x-lighter/14x-smaller vs a conventional transformer; ~31% ESS-shipment CAGR) anchors several of BofA's power-infra sizings (BofA "Watts to Tokens" full model, 2026-05-25).
