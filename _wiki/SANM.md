## Snapshot
Sanmina is a US-HQ electronics-manufacturing-services (EMS) / contract manufacturer serving communications, cloud, industrial, defence, and medical end markets, competing with [FLEX](FLEX.md) and Celestica. The acquisition of ZT Systems (AI rack assembly) became the primary revenue driver and re-rating catalyst: ZT contributed $1.88B in F2Q26, well ahead of plan, driven by AMD MI300/MI450 accelerated-compute solutions. Management guides FY27 total revenue to $16B+ (~2x FY25). The Street is re-rating SANM from a legacy-EMS discount toward a ZT-enabled AI-infra play, though all three covering analysts (JPM, BofA, SIG) hold Neutral on ZT-margin-sustainability and rack-ramp-timing uncertainty. SANM and SMTC were added to the S&P MidCap 400 (2026-06-08).

## Debate / thesis
- **Bull — ZT + AMD rack integration = structural re-rating:** ZT is executing ahead of plan on AMD MI300/MI450 rack assembly; FY27 $16B+ implies a near-doubling within two years. Book-to-bill >1.1x; next-gen MI450 wins announced with multiple customers ramping FY27. Longer term, PCB vertical integration is the next margin vector beyond MI450/MI500.
- **Bear — ZT margin risk + pull-forward distortion:** F2Q26 ZT revenue was a pull-forward from F3Q/F4Q; the F3Q guide reflects normalization. ZT operating margins are only in-line with core SANM (~6.4%), not a premium. As more competitors build AMD Helios racks, SANM may face price competition; management characterized current MI300 business as "project-based."
- **AMD opportunity cadence:** MI300 (minimal, late-cycle/project-based) → MI400/MI450 (full rack integration, early involvement, FY27 ramp) → PCB vertical integration (next vector).
- **Where the sell-side stands (all Neutral):** **JPM (Samik Chatterjee) — Neutral, PT $210** (raised $145→$210 post-F2Q26). **BofA (Ruplu Bhattacharya / Wamsi Mohan) — Neutral, PT $234** (raised $195→$234, 18x C27E EPS; cautious on ZT margins — mix, competition, consignment; MI500 flagged as next vertical-integration tailwind). **SIG/Susquehanna (Mehdi Hosseini) — Neutral, PT $185** (raised $155→$185; ZT shipments entirely MI450, no Blackwell/Rubin; upside risk $250). UBS (Robert Ruple) and Jefferies (Rankie Wong) cover via sales commentary only (no formal rating).

## Catalysts / what to watch
- **F3Q26 (Jun) print** vs. the $3.2B–$3.5B guide.
- **ZT ramp cadence** from test units to MI450 production; AMD MI450 full-rack revenue contribution in FY27.
- **ZT margin sustainability** (~6.4% OM) as AMD Helios competition increases.
- **PCB vertical integration** timing and incremental margin (beyond MI450, into MI500).
- Any S&P MidCap 400 rebalancing flow / re-initiation.

## Risks
- ZT margin compression from mix, competition, and potential consignment.
- Pull-forward distortion in quarterly ZT revenue.
- AMD rack-ramp timing and concentration on a single key customer.
- Memory (DDR4/5) availability cited as a F2Q26 Communications-segment limiter.
