## Snapshot
Two-segment infrastructure-tech compounder. **Semiconductor Solutions** (~68% of rev in Q2 FY26) is now dominated by **AI semis** — custom XPUs/ASICs co-designed for hyperscalers/frontier labs plus the merchant AI-networking franchise (Tomahawk switches, Jericho fabric, 200/400G SerDes, 1.6T DSPs, CPO/optics). **Infrastructure Software** (~32%, GM ~93%) is VMware/VCF + legacy CA/Symantec, a sticky ARR annuity. The investment case is almost entirely AI-ASIC: AVGO is the de-facto primary silicon partner to **Google TPU** (12-yr relationship, 14 ASICs to date), plus Anthropic, OpenAI, Meta MTIA, and two unnamed customers (JPM believes ByteDance + Alibaba; per Harlan Sur, 2026-06-05). It also designs custom XPUs at TSMC and depends on outsourced foundry/HBM/substrate supply (FY25 10-K). Bench-marked as a structural AI-networking winner: UBS pegs AVGO at ~29% of the merchant AI-networking TAM by 2028 (UBS 2026 overview, 2025-12-22).

- **Back-end offload:** AVGO's lower-value **networking processors moving to ASE FoCoS** (~10–15% of its CoWoS-equivalent capacity); all AI accelerators (TPU/ASICs) stay on TSMC CoWoS (Fubon/Jefferies). AVGO's AMD-style scale-up packaging "may have a faster ramp-up" than AMD's UALink (Fubon).
- **2027 CoWoS share revised *down* / V9 pushed out:** in its 2027 sector outlook Fubon now cuts AVGO's TSMC CoWoS allocation to **24–25% for 2027** (≈459.6k of ~1.89M wafers; #2 behind NVDA's 50%) on limited volume from its 2nd/3rd ASIC projects (OpenAI/Meta — no samples seen yet, so MP slips to 2H27). **V9 is NOT cancelled** ("remains on track but pushed out") — **V8i MP late 3Q26, V9 MP late 3Q27** → V9i volume very limited in 2027; total AVGO TPU ~6.2M units in 2027 (mix V8i/V9i). AVGO's growth momentum "more meaningful by 2028" (Fubon "Semi 2027 outlook", 2026-06-05).
- **V9/V10 design spec (Fubon):** **V9** = two 3D-stacking chips, four compute dies but only **two on N2 + two on N3** (Google lowering inference cost), moving to **SoIC**; **V10** = four 3D-stacking chips / **8 N2 compute dies**, migrating to **400G SerDes**. AVGO also wins **AWS T4 design service** (front- and back-end; one of two T4 SKUs) — read as share-additive into 2028 via its networking edge (Fubon, 2026-06-05).
- **Full ASIC-by-customer roadmap (Jefferies "Revisiting on Pullback", 2026-06-29):** Jefferies' customer map has AVGO designing Google **TPU v6e Trillium (N4N/HBM3), TPU7x Ironwood (N3/HBM3E/192GB), TPU 8i Sunfish (N3/HBM3E/288GB), TPU 9i Pumafish (N2/3, HBM4/384GB, SoIC), TPU 10i (N2/HBM4/576GB)** — with MediaTek holding the *training* variants (8t Zebrafish, 9t Tigerfish); **Meta MTIA 300/400/500** (MTIA 450 goes to MRVL); **OpenAI Jalapeño (N3/HBM3E/216GB) + V2 (A16/HBM4)**; **Apple Baltra (N3/HBM3E/288GB)**; **SoftBank Izanagi (N3/HBM3E)** — the "two additional customers" AVGO cites are believed to be **Apple** + one other; Jefferies believes **AWS Trainium and xAI are NOT on AVGO's roadmap**. TPU v9 seen **on track (April-27 launch), N2/3 + 400G SerDes (co-packaged copper) + 3D-stacked logic**, with **v10 in late-C27 (~8 logic die, 4-stacked-on-4)** pushing the lead further; MediaTek's Zebrafish only **~60k units in C26** (vs Street 400-450k) deflates the Google-share-loss bear case. (Jefferies / Blayne Curtis, 2026-06-29)

## Networking, scale-up & CPO (new)
- **Scale-up TAM:** MS sizes the scale-up network at **$4B (2024) → $17B by 2029 (34% CAGR)**; NVLink stays the majority near-term, but XPU proliferation opens a greenfield where **AVGO, ALAB, MRVL** all participate (MS Scale-up primer). MS models AVGO **AI-networking revenue >$9B by 2026** (MS Scale-up primer).
- **Scale-up Ethernet (SUE) push:** AVGO strongly advocates SUE as the hyperscalers' preferred scale-up path, citing 5–10x higher switch density and **250ns Ethernet latency**, arguing Ethernet's lack of a specified latency "allows for innovation" (vs AMD's UALink, which specifies latency). MS notes both camps stress ecosystem/interoperability/TCO; SUE/UALink ramp mostly 2027/2028 (MS Scale-up primer; MS Asia on AI Factories).
- **Tomahawk 6** supports both scale-out and scale-up; "multiple large-scale deployments planned with XPUs using Tomahawk 6 for scale-up" (MS Scale-up primer).
- **CPO:** AVGO is a proponent of optics/CPO in the scale-up domain; scale-out CPO from NVDA & AVGO is "garnering more attention" with end customers, and CPO becomes the **main driver of bandwidth increases in scale-up for the latter part of the decade** (CPO Primer — SemiAnalysis). AVGO can also OEM CPO photonic switches into NVDA's Quantum 800 line (StoneX/John Su, 2025-08-20) — a non-competing supplier angle. MS still flags scale-up optics/CPO as a 2028+ event for AVGO (MS Scale-up primer). Redburn corroborates the timing from the equipment side: recent CPO reliability improvements "tip the balance" toward wider adoption into **scale-up networking by 2028/29**, enabled by hybrid-bonding the optical engine (TSMC COUPE/SoIC); it models a cumulative ~50 BESI bonders for CPO by 2030, accelerating from 2H28 (Redburn, 2026-06-15) — a read-through that AVGO's scale-up CPO push is an equipment-gated 2028+ ramp.

## Debate / thesis
- **Capstone model (house view):** total revenue $68B→$115B→$190B→$315B (2025-28E); AI semis $24B→$63B→$132B→**$251B** (ASIC $18→52→90→180B; AI networking $8→17→40→70B), AI rising to ~80% of revenue by 2028. Pro-forma EPS $7.18 (2025E) → $12.89 (2026E). GW deployed by client (2026/27/28E): Google 2.5/4.0/4.8, Anthropic 1.4/3.2/7.0, OpenAI 0/1.3/4.3, Meta 0/1.0/2.9 → total 4.1/9.9/19.6 GW; total AI rev $57B/$139B/$274B. (Capstone official model, 2026-06-10)
- **Bull:** FY28 is the real story. JPM Harlan Sur (2026-06-05 / 2026-06-17 calls): ~20–22GW deploy in FY28 → **$250–300B AI rev**, ~**$35–38 EPS** vs cons ~$25. TPU V8i "Sunfish" = ~70% of Google's FY27 TPU demand, ~6.5M units ≈ **~$100B** alone; +~$45B networking. The April **5-yr (through 2031) Google LTA** carries *rising guaranteed annual revenues* and spans next 4 TPU gens (V8–V11); V9 2nm (4 compute die / 16 HBM) "**no delay**," ramps C28. Citi (Malik) #1 semis pick 2026, PT $500, AI →~81% of sales by F4Q28 (briefing 2026-05-12); reaffirmed AVGO as #2 mega-cap in its 2026-06-29 memory-supply ranking (TPUs assumed ~85% memory-fulfilled). MS keeps AVGO OW within its top AI-semi picks (NVDA/AVGO/ALAB; MS Asia on AI Factories). Internal Capstone model (Felipe, 2026-06-08): 2026E rev $115B / 2027E $190B / 2028E $315B; Anthropic largest AI client by GW in 2028.
- **Bear:** (1) **FY27 not raised** despite better visibility → the binary debate (Street $116B vs buyside $150B+). (2) **GM compression** — ASIC mix drags consolidated GM toward low-70s; HBM pass-through pricing an added headwind, now quantified at ~$2.5k of a ~$12k TPU ASP (Jefferies Curtis, 2026-06-04; Fubon/Jefferies). (3) **Google diversification / TPU8 share loss** — Hock conceded "diversity of sources"; StoneX flags **TPU8 went to Alchip on 2nm/A16**, while AVGO retains TPU v1–7 (StoneX/John Su, 2025-08-20); the CLT/MediaTek "Zerafish" training chip also exists, though Sur argues AVGO keeps the higher-value inferencing roadmap and the COT chip "still needs optimization/respins." (4) **Power/datacenter readiness** is now the binding constraint on multi-GW greenfield ramps (SIG/Susquehanna callback, 2026-06-03) — Sur reads this as *why* Hock won't raise FY27. (5) Independent caution: EnerTuition "Competitive Risk Starting To Materialize" (2026-06-07). (6) **Crowding:** AVGO is the most crowded name in the AI/networking set (UBS 2026 overview, 2025-12-22). (7) **Multiple still de-rating + lumpier book (UBS Tim Rohde, 2026-06-04):** if forced to choose, Rohde **buys NVDA over AVGO** — AVGO's multiple "still has more to come down" (partly the Google/MediaTek share drumbeat that "only gets louder"), its book is lumpier and more concentrated (Google + a few labs), and it shows backend/substrate/packaging constraints NVDA doesn't ("Jensen drops $20B Vera out of thin air and gets supply"). (8) **Asia checks** flag the Broadcom chip still in development → V8 a 2026 event, the Anthropic rack order cut to ~$2-3B (internalized at GCP), and AVGO as a near-term "funding short" (Citi/Jefferies, 2026-05-22). (9) **New merchant-ASIC entrants:** LG Electronics is launching an external ASIC design-service business explicitly modeled on Broadcom and Marvell (@jukan05, 2026-06-30) — early-stage but a directional read that the design-service field is drawing more scaled entrants.
- **Bear (HBM-in-BOM = the Google→MediaTek share driver; Redburn "Conor O'Mara Asia Trip Takeaways", 2026-06-23):** the key reason Google is shifting TPU share to MediaTek is that **Broadcom charges the hyperscaler for HBM inside the BOM whereas MediaTek does not** — so Google can cut a "gigantic part of the bill of materials" by moving to MediaTek, as long as MediaTek stays as good as Broadcom (so far it is). Conor frames Broadcom's Google market share going **6% (2026) → 30% (2028)** [i.e. MediaTek's gain at Broadcom's expense], with **400G switch timing (V9 vs V10 at Google) the key swing factor** — Broadcom is currently the only 400G supplier, so if Google adopts 400G at V9 it could cap MediaTek's gains; Conor/Sherman's checks lean toward Google adopting 400G only at **V10** (late). Separately, Sherman **brought down some Broadcom CoWoS** on delays to the OpenAI and Meta projects (mass production slips to 2H, small '27 contribution) (Redburn "Conor O'Mara Asia Trip Takeaways", 2026-06-23). Corroborated by StoneX/TrendForce: **Google previously relied entirely on Broadcom for HBM** procurement, but MediaTek (TPU V8/V9) is now increasingly handling Google's HBM directly and is in talks with Samsung — confirming the HBM-procurement leakage away from Broadcom (StoneX/TrendForce "Memory expert call — Avril", 2026-06-23).
- **Bear (MediaTek wins Google TPU v9 primary orders on SerDes timing; FinTwit, 2026-06-24):** MediaTek won primary production orders for Google's **TPU v9** chips by delivering a custom **336G SerDes** solution after Broadcom's more ambitious **448G SerDes missed the production deadline**. This is a concrete, supply-chain-verified data point that Broadcom failed to meet Google's timeline on a key technical deliverable — reinforcing the Conor/Redburn HBM-in-BOM narrative with a second dimension (SerDes execution). A GOOGL employee interview (via @RihardJarc) argued that the **real value of ASIC designers like AVGO is now their TSM CoWoS allocation, not the co-design** — a narratively bearish reframe for AVGO's "design premium = higher ASP" thesis (@dnystedt; @RihardJarc, 2026-06-24).

## Risks
- **Customer concentration:** top-five end customers a significant share of net revenue (FY25 10-K); AI rev increasingly tied to a handful of frontier labs — Sur flags AVGO is *more* exposed to OpenAI/Anthropic frontier-lab risk than NVDA (2026-06-17).
- **Supply chain:** dependence on a limited number of contract manufacturers/foundries (TSMC), HBM and substrate (FY25 10-K); back-end packaging cited as the FY27 supply constraint (UBS Arcuri, 2026-06-04). AVGO's stickiness as a quasi-foundry to hyperscalers cuts both ways — exposes it to price pressure once CoWoS capacity eases (StoneX/John Su, 2025-08-20). Citi's "DRAM Supply Is the New Ranking Metric" (2026-06-29) assumes TPUs ~85% memory-fulfilled — memory allocation an emerging gate even for the #2-ranked mega-cap.
- **Margin mix:** structural GM dilution as low-margin ASICs scale faster than software/networking; HBM pass-through (~$2.5k/TPU) a quantified drag (Fubon/Jefferies).
- **Share loss / COT:** Google CLT-team (MediaTek) optionality; TPU8 routed to Alchip (StoneX/John Su, 2025-08-20); longer-term COT-model bypass risk (Jukan, briefing 2026-05-18).
- **Macro/AI-capex cyclicality & power availability** gating multi-GW deployments; geopolitical/China (InP, export controls).
