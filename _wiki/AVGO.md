## Snapshot
Two-segment infrastructure-tech compounder. **Semiconductor Solutions** (~68% of rev in Q2 FY26) is now dominated by **AI semis** — custom XPUs/ASICs co-designed for hyperscalers/frontier labs plus the merchant AI-networking franchise (Tomahawk switches, Jericho fabric, 200/400G SerDes, 1.6T DSPs, CPO/optics). **Infrastructure Software** (~32%, GM ~93%) is VMware/VCF + legacy CA/Symantec, a sticky ARR annuity. The investment case is almost entirely AI-ASIC: AVGO is the de-facto primary silicon partner to **Google TPU** (12-yr relationship, 14 ASICs to date), plus Anthropic, OpenAI, Meta MTIA, and two unnamed customers (JPM believes ByteDance + Alibaba; per Harlan Sur, 2026-06-05). It also designs custom XPUs at TSMC and depends on outsourced foundry/HBM/substrate supply (FY25 10-K). Bench-marked as a structural AI-networking winner: UBS pegs AVGO at ~29% of the merchant AI-networking TAM by 2028 (UBS 2026 overview, 2025-12-22).

## Networking, scale-up & CPO (new)
- **Scale-up TAM:** MS sizes the scale-up network at **$4B (2024) → $17B by 2029 (34% CAGR)**; NVLink stays the majority near-term, but XPU proliferation opens a greenfield where **AVGO, ALAB, MRVL** all participate (MS Scale-up primer). MS models AVGO **AI-networking revenue >$9B by 2026** (MS Scale-up primer).
- **Scale-up Ethernet (SUE) push:** AVGO strongly advocates SUE as the hyperscalers' preferred scale-up path, citing 5–10x higher switch density and **250ns Ethernet latency**, arguing Ethernet's lack of a specified latency "allows for innovation" (vs AMD's UALink, which specifies latency). MS notes both camps stress ecosystem/interoperability/TCO; SUE/UALink ramp mostly 2027/2028 (MS Scale-up primer; MS Asia on AI Factories).
- **Tomahawk 6** supports both scale-out and scale-up; "multiple large-scale deployments planned with XPUs using Tomahawk 6 for scale-up" (MS Scale-up primer).
- **CPO:** AVGO is a proponent of optics/CPO in the scale-up domain; scale-out CPO from NVDA & AVGO is "garnering more attention" with end customers, and CPO becomes the **main driver of bandwidth increases in scale-up for the latter part of the decade** (CPO Primer — SemiAnalysis). AVGO can also OEM CPO photonic switches into NVDA's Quantum 800 line (StoneX/John Su, 2025-08-20) — a non-competing supplier angle. MS still flags scale-up optics/CPO as a 2028+ event for AVGO (MS Scale-up primer). Redburn corroborates the timing from the equipment side: recent CPO reliability improvements "tip the balance" toward wider adoption into **scale-up networking by 2028/29**, enabled by hybrid-bonding the optical engine (TSMC COUPE/SoIC); it models a cumulative ~50 BESI bonders for CPO by 2030, accelerating from 2H28 (Redburn, 2026-06-15) — a read-through that AVGO's scale-up CPO push is an equipment-gated 2028+ ramp.

- **Macro/AI-capex cyclicality & power availability** gating multi-GW deployments; geopolitical/China (InP, export controls).
