## Snapshot
MP Materials operates the Mountain Pass rare-earth mine and processing facility in California — the only scaled rare-earth mining and processing site in the Western Hemisphere — and is building toward domestic finished neodymium-iron-boron (NdFeB) magnet production. The investment narrative positions MP as a critical-materials beneficiary of the de-China supply-chain push: Chinese rare-earth export controls and a ~80% drop in Chinese Nd-magnet imports into Japan have created urgency for ex-China sourcing. Coatue flagged MP as an AI-infrastructure adjacency under its "follow the gigawatt" thesis, linking rare-earth magnets to EV motors, robotics actuators, and defense.

No quarterly financials in the ported corpus (no Q1 2026 revenue/EBITDA/magnet-volume figures). **China's Ministry of Commerce added MP Materials to its export-control list (2026-06-23)** — alongside USA Rare Earth and 8 US defense contractors (10 total) — in response to the US expanding its list of Chinese companies with military ties.

## Debate / thesis
- **Bull:** Structural supply gap — China dominates rare-earth processing and MP is the only at-scale US option. Chinese Nd/Dy/Tb import volumes into Japan fell ~80%, forcing allied nations to fund ex-China supply chains (2026-06-12). AI/robotics demand driver — NdFeB magnets are critical for EV traction motors and humanoid-robot actuators; Coatue's "follow the gigawatt" framework flags MP as an AI-infra adjacency, with a Cooperman position disclosed at $184M (Coatue / Jaimin Rangwalla, 2026-05-16). Government backstop — Shin-Etsu's ¥35B rare-earth smelting investment in Japan (half government-subsidized) signals allied willingness to fund diversification (@QQ_Timmy, 2026-06-12). Japan also exploring Greenland rare-earth mining (Nikkei, 2026-06-14).
- **Bear:** China policy cuts both ways — any US-China trade deal could include a WFE-for-rare-earths swap (Edison Lee / Jefferies, non-consensus, 2026-05-14), reopening Chinese supply and compressing MP's pricing power. Vertical-integration execution risk — downstream magnet manufacturing (Fort Worth) is unproven at scale. Sector discount — MP trades in Materials, not Tech, limiting the natural TMT-fund buyer base.
- **Where the sell-side stands:** **BofA (Lawson Winder) — Buy** maintained on the China export-control listing, calling the impact "little to no impact on MP": MP's supply chain is already being de-Chinaed, all inputs can ultimately be sourced from Western sources, and the US DoW supply agreement reinforces China's view of MP as a strategic US asset. Positive second-order read — strengthens US-government incentive to fund domestic rare-earth mining (BofA Mining & Steel Global Daily / Felicity Robson, Lawson Winder, 2026-06-23).
