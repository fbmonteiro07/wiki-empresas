## Snapshot
Oracle is now a full-stack enterprise tech platform spanning (1) cloud infrastructure — OCI, including the GPU/AI "AI infrastructure" buildout; (2) cloud applications — Fusion ERP/SCM/HCM/CX, NetSuite, plus vertical/industry suites; and (3) the Oracle Database franchise, increasingly sold cross-cloud ("multicloud" on Azure/Google/AWS). The story has shifted decisively from a seasonal license business to a hyperscaler-scale, capital-intensive AI infrastructure provider riding committed customer demand. FY26 total revenue surpassed $67B (+16% non-GAAP op income), but the equity is now valued — and debated — almost entirely on the OCI/AI backlog ramp. Oracle co-CEOs Clay Magouyrk (infrastructure) and Mike Sicilia (apps/database) run the business; Larry Ellison remains Chairman/CTO; Hilary Maxson took over as CFO ~June 2026.

## The OpenAI deal — economics & concentration
The single contract that re-rated the stock. The OpenAI–Oracle commitment is framed as a **$300B / 5-year compute deal starting 2027 (~$60B/yr)** (Barclays on OpenAI, Nov25). Oracle's late-June disclosure that one large customer (assumed OpenAI) could spend **$30B/yr by FY28** anchors the bull/bear split — UBS sizes it at ~35% of OpenAI's projected FY28 revs ($86B) and ~2/3 of OpenAI's total compute spend if OpenAI runs ~50% of revenue on compute; a multi-year commitment to reach $30B by FY28 "could easily be $50-100B" (UBS OpenAI deep-dive). Roots: OpenAI already ran on OCI GPU racks via Microsoft's Azure off-loading, so OCI was the natural overflow once the Jan-2025 "Stargate" break in Azure compute exclusivity hit; the swing to Oracle "exceeded the most bullish investor expectations" (UBS OpenAI deep-dive).
- **Backlog/obligations context:** Of OpenAI's ~$650B in 10-yr compute obligations, **OCI is the single largest at $300B**, vs MSFT Azure $250B, GOOGL GCP $40B, AWS $38B, plus CRWV (Barclays on OpenAI, Nov25). Barclays' contract schedule has OCI's OpenAI revenue ramping $24B (2027) → $54B → $60B → $60B. Each ~1 GW cluster ≈ 400k GPUs; each GW generates ~$10B/yr of GPU rental revenue but costs $50–60B of capex to build (split compute vs power/shell) — the core capex-vs-opex mismatch driving the FCF strain.
- **Concentration risk, quantified:** OpenAI is a small slice of each hyperscaler's capacity except Oracle — Barclays models **OpenAI = 76% of OCI's '28 AI capacity** (vs 23% MSFT, 5% GOOGL, 3% AWS); OCI capacity 2.4 GW ('25 year-end) → 7.1 GW ('27). Oracle's offset is large books with ByteDance/TikTok, Meta and xAI (Grok inference) on top of OpenAI (Barclays on OpenAI, Nov25; UBS OpenAI deep-dive). UBS flags two live risks: (1) OpenAI building its own infra ("dig the dirt," OpenAI CFO) could relegate Oracle to a lower-value role; (2) the legacy Microsoft→Oracle sub-contract revenue could wind down as the direct OpenAI deal winds up, pressuring both Azure and OCI optics.
- **Unit economics (third-party view):** SemiAnalysis estimates Oracle signed **>$420B of OpenAI contract value over the trailing twelve months → ~$150B of gross profit**, but at an **AI ROIC of ~20%**, materially below Oracle's historical cloud profile and roughly in line with Microsoft's own AI ROIC once the OpenAI rev-share (ending ~2030–32) is stripped out (SemiAnalysis, Microsoft AI Strategy Deconstructed).

BYOH/prepaid model lets Oracle scale capacity without proportional cash capex or margin loss (Magouyrk, Q4 FY26). "Halo effect" — AI infra training colocated with apps/database drives multiproduct deals, sovereign/Alloy and multicloud DB at 60–80% margins (Sicilia, Q3 FY26). ## Capex / financing / FCF strain
The balance-sheet story is now central, not incidental (JPM AI Capex & Financing, 2025-11-10):
- **FCF turns deeply negative through the build:** FCF –$0.4B FY25A → **–$9.2B FY26E → –$14.0B FY27E → –$19.8B FY28E** (FCF conversion ~–35% to –40%), driven by capex ramping $21B FY25 → $36B FY26E → $50B FY27E → $73B FY28E even as op cash flow grows to ~$52B FY28E.
- **Most-indebted IG tech issuer:** the $18B six-tranche senior-note deal (Sept-25, ~$88B order book) lifted total debt above $100B, making Oracle the most indebted IG-rated Technology issuer and 5th-largest in IG TMT. JPM models gross debt $121B FY26E → $151B FY27E → $171B FY28E; net leverage ~3.1x near-term before deleveraging to ~2.5x FY28E as EBITDA scales. JPM reads the strong $18B order book as scarcity of AI-related IG paper, not a vote on Oracle credit quality; ORCL spreads widened ~30-40bps post-issuance.
- **Forced into private/structured credit:** with traditional capacity constrained by negative FCF and elevated leverage, JPM expects Oracle to pursue Beignet-/Vantage-style off-balance-sheet structures and possibly hybrids (perpetual subs / preferred) for partial equity credit. Caveat: Oracle's mid-BBB ratings with Negative outlooks (S&P, Moody's) make a Meta-Beignet-like notched structure harder; sustained reliance on private credit "could signal funding flexibility is eroding."
- **Off-balance-sheet lease load — "funding relief, not resolution":** the FY26 10-K (year ending 5/31/26) confirms ORCL's **not-yet-commenced lease commitments plateaued at ~$260bn** (MS expected the plateau), while the total across the five IG players rose ~$675bn → ~$820bn. On-balance-sheet lease liabilities grew **>$10bn net in FY4Q** with treatment still skewed operating (not finance). QoQ ALSO: deferred revenues +$4.2bn, unpaid capex +$0.8bn, purchase obligations (primarily DC power) +$2.3bn, short-term lease guarantees +$1.1bn. Post-FY26, Oracle added **$19bn of cloud-infrastructure purchase commitments (FY27-start, 5-year) that appear NOT to meet lease treatment** — i.e. more obligation building outside the lease line (MS TMT Credit, 2026-06-22). DB's read of the same 10-K: balance-sheet lease liabilities +$10bn q/q to **~$38bn**, total lease commitments incl. not-yet-commenced +$9bn q/q to **~$298bn**, with F4Q lease opex ~$1.0bn = ~17% of PaaS/IaaS revenue, in line with peers (DB/Zelnick FY26 10-K review, 2026-06-23).

## Datacenter leasing & power footprint
- **Leasing leadership:** in 3Q25 Oracle led all hyperscalers, adding **+3,200 MW of new (non-commenced) leases**, vs ~600 MW MSFT / +1,000 MW GOOGL / +300 MW META; ORCL projected to ~5.4 GW of under-construction capacity, expansion explicitly tied to OpenAI agreements. Jefferies attributes the ~5x QoQ surge in industry leasing largely to "a single hyperscaler securing capacity for a major AI tenant, likely Oracle supporting OpenAI" (Jefferies 3Q25 Data Center Leasing). Aggregate non-commenced hyperscaler backlog ~15 GW across MSFT/ORCL/META/GOOGL.
- **Stargate site map (Barclays Powering AI):** Stargate 1 / Abilene TX (1.2–1.6 GW, Crusoe/Lancium, fully contracted by ORCL; ~$13mm/MW ex-IT); Frontier / Shackelford County TX (1.4 GW, Vantage, ~$18mm/MW ex-IT, 210 Jenbacher gas engines); Lighthouse / Port Washington WI (1 GW, Vantage, ~$17mm/MW ex-IT, WEC We Energies dedicated rate); Project Jupiter / Doña Ana NM (1.5 GW). July OpenAI/Oracle agreement to develop up to 4.5 GW of additional Stargate capacity under the $300B/5yr deal (a 600-MW Stargate-1 expansion + ~4 GW across three new sites). Full Stargate pipeline ~7 GW / >$400B planned (~$57mm/MW incl. IT).
- **Energy-as-a-service:** VoltaGrid to deploy **2.3 GW of power infrastructure for Oracle data centers** (INNIO Jenbacher engines + ABB), under a long-term, primarily fixed-price PPA — evidence distributed/behind-the-meter power is a structural, not bridge, solution for Oracle's grid-constrained sites (Barclays Powering AI).

- **Oracle Investor Day: October 28, 2026, Las Vegas** (AI World) — reaffirmation/update of FY30 +31% rev / +28% EPS CAGRs; new CFO Maxson's first framing of ROIC and funding.
- Capacity ramps at the five flagship sites (Abilene remainder; Shackelford/Doña Ana/Saline/Port Washington deliveries through 1H–2H CY27).
- Funding execution: ~$40B FY27 raise incl. $20B ATM equity; first private-credit / structured or hybrid issuance as a tell on balance-sheet flexibility; investment-grade rating preservation.
- Component-cost / memory-price trajectory vs. the pass-through contract mechanics; BYOH/prepaid mix shift (only ~12% of RPO is non-BYOH/prepaid per Materne/Evercore Q&A).
- OpenAI signals: whether OpenAI self-builds (relegating OCI) and the cadence at which the legacy Microsoft→Oracle sub-contract winds down vs. the direct OpenAI deal winding up.

## Risks
- **Customer concentration — OpenAI:** Barclays models OpenAI at **~76% of OCI's '28 AI capacity** and $300B/5yr; gives OpenAI material pricing leverage over Oracle and ties OCI's ramp to a still-unprofitable, privately-funded counterparty (UBS OpenAI deep-dive; Barclays on OpenAI). 4 customers each >$8B in Q4 FY26 (Magouyrk).
- **Capex intensity / cash burn** — reported capex well above the ~$70B net outlay; FCF –$9B/–$14B/–$20B FY26-28E; capex intensity to ~61% FY27E; funding via debt+equity + ATM dilution; capex-to-sales at dotcom-peak levels (MS, 2026-06-09; JPM AI Capex & Financing, 2025-11-10).
- **Credit / leverage** — most-indebted IG tech issuer (>$100B debt), mid-BBB with Negative outlooks; growing reliance on private/structured credit and hybrids; spread-widening risk (JPM, 2025-11-10).
- **Margin compression / low AI ROIC** through the build phase before contracted revenue ramps; gross margin down ~5pts FY26, further step-down guided FY27 (Maxson); third-party AI ROIC ~20% vs historical cloud profile (SemiAnalysis).
- **Component/supply-chain cost inflation** (memory/SSD/HDD) — mitigated by fixed-price-only-when-costs-known + pass-through clauses, but a live concern (Magouyrk, Q4 FY26).
- **Competition & overcapacity** — Neoclouds, hyperscaler self-build, OpenAI in-housing infra, new entrants pressuring price/share (Q4 FY26 Q&A; UBS OpenAI deep-dive).
- **Power/grid, construction labor and data-center delays** — industry-wide gating constraints; partial mitigation via VoltaGrid/Jenbacher EaaS (Jefferies/GEV power call, 2026-05-08; Barclays Powering AI).
- **FX** — large international mix; ~$2.4B earnings sensitivity to a 10% FX move (10-K FY25, Item 7A).
