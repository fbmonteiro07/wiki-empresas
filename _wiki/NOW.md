## Snapshot
ServiceNow is a cloud-based enterprise workflow platform, positioned as an "AI control tower" / platform consolidator — not a single-feature SaaS (McDermott, Q4-25, 2026-01-28). Its historical core is ITSM (the "CIO's system of record"), expanded into employee experience, AI-native CRM, AI security and workflow data fabric. The model is ~100% subscription. The FY25 10-K (2026-01-29) describes the mission as helping organizations "govern, secure and manage artificial intelligence" and digitize workflows on top of the ServiceNow AI Platform. McDermott frames a TAM of $600bn (Q1-26). Recent M&A (Moveworks, Armis, Veza, Pyramid Analytics) broadened employee works, AI security/identity and analytics.

## Current state (Q1 FY26, call 2026-04-22)
Beat-and-raise. Subscription revenue $3.671bn (+19% y/y); cRPO +21% y/y (~100bps above the guide); RPO $27.7bn (+23.5%); FCF margin 44% (Mastantuono, Q1-26). McDermott cited a ~75bps headwind from delayed on-prem deals — "beat and raise, not an excuse."

- **FY26 guidance raised:** subscription revenue lifted $205m to $15.735–$15.775bn (+20.5%–21%) (Mastantuono, Q1-26). Compare with the initial guide given at Q4-25 of $15.53–$15.57bn (+19.5%–20% cc) and an op margin target of 32% / FCF margin of 36% (Mastantuono, 2026-01-28).
- **AI monetization / pricing shift:** the target for AI-specific commitments for 2026 was raised from $1bn to "$1.5 billion" — +50% (Mastantuono/McDermott, Q1-26). **"50% of net new business now comes from non-seat-based pricing"** (McDermott, Q1-26). Now Assist surpassed "$600,000,000 in ACV" with 35 deals >$1m in Q4 (McDermott, Q4-25). The model is **hybrid** (seats + assist packs / tokens), not pure consumption — customers want "flexibility and predictability"; pure consumption only where it makes sense (storage, workflow data fabric tokens) (Zavery, Q4-25). The Street's agentic framework cites NOW as the canonical **hybrid** pricing exemplar — "combines seats, subscriptions, and consumption" — as the sector gradually shifts off pure seat-based pricing toward consumption/outcome models retaining a flat subscription floor; evolved pricing is framed as the lever to lift ARPU and offset seat erosion (JPM, 2025-09-03).
- **Agentic product surface:** beyond IT/employee workflows, NOW has rolled out **SecOps expert AI Agents** that automate security operations end-to-end — incident handling from threat detection to remediation — while maintaining governance standards; the Street flags IT & business operations (e.g. NOW's AI Agents) as a domain where agentic ROI lands earliest, with COOs/senior business managers as buyers alongside the CIO (JPM, 2025-09-03).
- **Margins / LLM cost:** the gross margin headwind comes from migrating more to hyperscalers, with initially lower margin — "margin business you'd want me to take every single day" (Mastantuono, Q4-25). Inferencing is still "very low as a percentage of cost or usage-wise"; frontier models are expected to dominate inference over the long term, with ServiceNow LLMs serving sovereign/on-prem (Zavery, Q4-25). Q4-25 op margin 31% (+1pt vs guide), FCF margin 57% (+950bps y/y).
- **Quality metrics (Q4-25):** 244 deals >$1m NNACV; 98% renewal rate; 603 customers >$5m ACV; customers >$20m ACV +30% y/y; MAU +25%; global gov "+80% y/y" in EMEA/Asia.
- **M&A:** Moveworks (acquired 2025 for **$2.85bn** to fold IT-helpdesk agents into Agent Studio — JPM, 2025-09-03) integrated in 3 weeks, already closing deals >$1m NN ACV and "quintupling" the Q1 business vs the entire prior year (McDermott, Q1-26). Krishna Gidwani took over M&A integration as "#1 priority" (Mastantuono, Q1-26). Armis ~1pt of contribution to subscription, up to 50bps of op margin headwind (Mastantuono, Q4-25). McDermott: "not have a large-scale M&A on the roadmap" going forward (Q4-25). Capital: new $5bn buyback authorization + immediate $2bn ASR (Q4-25).

**Prior long-term algo & segment economics — 2024 Financial Analyst Day baseline** (read direct from deck; useful as the trajectory the 2026 FAD's $30bn/2030 superseded — Source: NOW Financial Analyst Day, 2024-05-06):
- **Old LT target:** **$15bn+ subscription revenue by 2026E** at a **20%+ cc CAGR through 2026** (targets exclude material M&A) — i.e. the framework the page's current $30bn-by-2030 guide replaced. 2024E was guided at **$10.6bn sub-rev (~22% growth)** off **$8.68bn in 2023**.
- **Margin glide path then guided:** non-GAAP op margin **30%(2023)→31%(2024E)**, "average 50bps+ expansion/yr" despite tax headwinds (~100bps in 2024 & 2025, ~200bps in 2026); FCF margin "average 100bps+ expansion/yr"; **SBC down 19%(2022)→18%(2023)→<15% by 2026**; annual dilution **1.1%(2023)→<1.0%** target.
- **TAM trajectory disclosed:** **$39bn(2015) → $110bn(2018) → $165bn(2023) → $200bn(2024) → $220bn(2025E) → $275bn(2026E forecast)** — the $275bn 2026 "GenAI TAM" predates McDermott's current **$600bn** framing (Q1-26), and was split Technology $108bn / Creator & FSC $84bn / Customer & Industry $68bn / Employee $15bn. TAM penetration then **<5% across all geographies, <7% across top 6 industries**.
- **Cohort / land-and-expand proof:** 2010 cohort grew initial ACV at **224% annual growth** (a $100k land → $3.236M ACV by Q1-24); **98% of 2023 NNACV from multi-product deals, 82% from 5+-product deals**; renewal rate **98.5%**; ~70% of customers grew ACV in 2023; time-to-$1M-ACV compressed from **8 years → 2 years** for newer cohorts.
- **Pricing-uplift precedent (key for the seat→usage debate):** **~25% realized price uplift ITSM Pro vs Standard**, **>30% realized uplift Pro Plus vs Pro** — the historical evidence base behind the 2026 FAD's 20–30% uplift claim. Then-flagged Gen-AI upsell pools: **$1bn+** from existing Pro customers and **$2.5bn** if all ITSM/CSM/HRSD service-desk customers upgrade to Pro Plus.
- **Segment scale (2023 ACV):** Creator Workflows **$1.1bn+**, Customer & Industry **$1.05bn+** (from ~$20M in 2016), Employee Workflows **$850M+**; emerging products (IRM, SecOps, ITAM) **~50% 2019–23 CAGR**; OT a **$5bn+ TAM** opportunity. Sales efficiency **3.8x — #1 vs ADBE 3.4x / WDAY 3.0x / CRM 2.3x** (peer avg 2.9x), 2023.

## Debate / thesis
- **Bull:** context/data moat — 22 years, "95 billion workflows and more than 7 trillion transactions"; one CIO said it would need to be "at least 10x more expensive" to replace (McDermott, Q1-26, 2026-04-22). AI reinvigorates the core (ITSM gains importance as AI complexity rises), it does not cannibalize (McDermott responding to Borges/GS, Q1-26). A platform open to any model "aligned to customers' policies, permissions and rules" wins in an environment of AI confusion (McDermott, Q1-26). **TAM expansion**: rather than charging a slice of the IT budget per seat, agentic pricing lets vendors tap the far larger pool of white-collar labour spend — NOW's CFO Mastantuono: "GenAI and our new product launches continue to expand our massive TAM"; Gartner sees global software TAM growing from ~$1.1tn (2024) to ~$2tn (2029), a 13% CAGR (JPM, 2025-09-03). **Incumbent advantage**: J.P. Morgan's 2025 CIO Survey shows enterprises leaning to established vendors for AI needs, with NOW among the named incumbents (alongside MSFT, AMZN, GOOGL, SAP) — though only 1.2% of respondents named it top-of-mind vs MSFT 32.7% / OpenAI 22.0% (JPM, 2025-09-03). BofA reinstated at **Buy, PO $130**, thesis "AI beneficiary, entrenched workflow position" (morning briefing, 2026-05-19). Bernstein's 247-page Gen-AI Handbook reinforces the incumbent-moat case structurally: it argues AI-native SaaS challengers face the same multi-year ramp the original SaaS leaders did — "one need only look at how long it took Salesforce, Workday, or **ServiceNow** to ramp their businesses" — so disruption of entrenched, multi-product platforms is slow, not sudden, and well-run incumbents survive/thrive rather than being displaced (Bernstein "Scaling Intelligence", 2026-06-22; thematic framework, not a fresh datapoint).
- **Bear:** (1) Risk of core pricing cannibalization as AI reduces seats — a premise Borges/GS raised and management rejected (Q1-26); the Street acknowledges seat-based disruption as a real sector risk that the move to consumption/outcome pricing is meant to offset, not eliminate (JPM, 2025-09-03). (2) Negative stock reaction post-print despite good numbers; Keith Weiss/MS questioned when NOW would show acceleration comparable to "AI platforms" (Q1-26) — the "AI tax" debate on application software. (3) M&A cadence (Moveworks, Armis, Veza) generated "investor concern" about dependence on acquisitions vs organic growth (Samana/Jefferies, Q4-25); Veza/Pyramid contribution was minimal in Q1. (4) Structural gross-margin headwind from hyperscalers/LLM cost (Mastantuono, Q4-25). (5) Deals "more complex... longer, more expensive sales cycles, increased pricing pressure" (Risk Factors, 10-K FY25).
- **Financial Analyst Day (2026-05-04) — third-party reads (UBS/Keirstead):** likely a positive catalyst — **$30bn 2030 subscription revenue (~17.5% four-year CAGR; in line with UBS's $29.6bn est.), with $32bn offered as an upside case**, and a **"Rule of 60" 2030 guide** (growth + FCF margin) implying better op-margin gains than the ~50bps/yr UBS modeled. The growth algo leans heavily on a **pricing pivot: 20–30% average pricing uplift** from a combination of straight price increases, usage-based meters customers exceed, and outcome/productivity-share deals — SKUs renamed **foundation / advanced / prime** with AI embedded across all. Mgmt (Gina) confirmed the **path to $30bn is non-linear** — the real ACV inflection from seat→agent substitution lands ~two years out (~2028), leaving 2027 growth potentially "a little more modest." NOW also flagged a move down-market via a **new fully AI-native ITSM product priced 100% consumption-based** (a watch-item for Atlassian JSM / Freshworks). Keirstead stays "measured" on three worries: Bill McDermott's unhedged optimism (zero acknowledged disruption risk, vs Palantir on the same day declaring "the death of legacy software"); uncertainty the coding-style seat→usage uplift translates to non-coding use cases (Salesforce backed off a similar outcome-pricing pitch a year ago); and the non-linear trajectory (UBS "NOW investor day / PLTR" call, 2026-05-05). McDermott's own framing of who gets disrupted: software firms that lack NOW's portfolio breadth ("Workday in HR fits that description"), have lower usage intensity, or bolt AI on as a side-car SKU rather than embedding it (UBS, 2026-05-05). [../relat%C3%B3rios%20bons/2026_05_05_now_pltr_ubs_review_5_may_26.html](../relat%C3%B3rios%20bons/2026_05_05_now_pltr_ubs_review_5_may_26.html)
- **Token-optimization read-through — mixed (third-party):** UBS names ServiceNow (with Salesforce and Workday) as a "larger and well-penetrated seat-based SaaS firm" that is NOT helped as enterprises cut headcount growth / optimize seats / trim app-software budget growth to fund rising AI token costs — and warns the seat-plus-usage pricing transition "may now be met with even greater resistance" (directly relevant to NOW's 50%-non-seat mix and 20-30% pricing uplift). The offset: model-neutral software firms (don't own 1P models) could gain by playing an independent "token optimizer" role like Palantir's Evolve (UBS "The Ramp in Token Optimization" / Keirstead, 2026-06-23).
- **Renewal-pause read-through (third-party):** UBS/Keirstead flagged Microsoft citing weaker Dynamics contract renewals as enterprises paused to evaluate "new AI scenarios, including new usage-based pricing models" — a negative read to the broader SaaS apps space that "is likely to show up in pending SaaS prints" and "may have already weighed on the recent results and guide of ServiceNow and SAP" (UBS MSFT print recap, 2026-04-30). ## Catalysts / what to watch
- **Q2 FY26 print** (~Jul/2026, by historical cadence — Q2-25 was 2025-07-23). Watch: traction of the $1.5bn AI commitments target and the non-seat-based mix (already at 50% of net new).
- **Financial Analyst Day 2026-05-04** — now covered via UBS read-through (see Debate): $30bn FY30 sub-rev guide (~17.5% CAGR) / $32bn upside / Rule of 60; 20–30% pricing uplift; non-linear path inflecting ~2028; new 100%-consumption AI-native down-market ITSM SKU.
- Integration/revenue contribution of Moveworks, Armis (closed early 2026), Veza, Pyramid.
- Pricing evolution: proof of agentic monetization (Now Assist / autonomous workforce — "Level 1 specialist resolves in <20 min vs 2 days", Zavery Q1-26) without erosion of the core seat-based business.
- Gross margin trajectory as workloads migrate to hyperscalers.

## Risks
- Incorporating AI brings "operational, legal, regulatory, ethical" challenges (Risk Factors, 10-K FY25, 2026-01-29).
- Failure to innovate in an "intensely competitive" market could hurt competitive position.
- Growing dependence on a partner network for part of revenue.
- More complex deals → longer/more expensive sales cycles + pricing pressure.
- Exposure to the public sector / regulated entities (procurement, contracting) and to trade/sanctions/anti-corruption laws.
- M&A: risk of failing to realize expected benefits, management distraction, dilution.
- Cybersecurity / data-localization and regulatory expectations around data use and movement.
