## Snapshot
Seagate (Ireland-domiciled, "Seagate Technology Holdings plc") is one of three scaled hard-disk-drive makers (with Western Digital and Toshiba) and a **vertically integrated** one — it designs and manufactures substantially all of its own recording heads and media (FY25 10-K, 2025-08-01). The business is now overwhelmingly a hyperscaler mass-storage story: the **Mass Capacity** segment (high-capacity nearline drives + systems for cloud/enterprise) is the majority of revenue, and **data center was 80% of revenue / 88% of exabytes** in the March 2026 quarter (Q3 FY26 call, 2026-04-28). The thesis runs on **areal density**: Seagate's **Mozaic** platform is "the industry's first implementation of HAMR" (heat-assisted magnetic recording) (FY25 10-K), and per IDC hard drives store **87% of exabytes in large data-center deployments** (FY25 10-K). It also sells SSDs and Lyve edge-to-cloud systems (Edge IoT, ~20% of revenue), but the equity is a play on nearline HDD demand from AI data creation. Acquired Intevac (thin-film processing systems) on 2025-03-31, deepening the upstream toolset (FY25 10-K).

## Position in the value chain
Seagate is the vertically integrated manufacturer in the HDD chain: it builds its own recording heads and media in-house (a limited number of independent head/media suppliers exist; vertical integration lowers unit cost in up-cycles but raises underutilization charges in down-cycles — FY25 10-K), assembles drives largely in China/Thailand/Malaysia/Singapore/N. Ireland, and sells nearline/mass-capacity HDDs to hyperscalers and enterprise. Its differentiator is being **ahead of WDC on HAMR**: Seagate is already shipping Mozaic 3/4 HAMR for revenue and ramping 40TB-class drives in 2H CY26, whereas WDC has not yet qualified HAMR and ramps 44TB only in H1 CY27 (MS/Woodring equity call, 2026-06-16). HDDs are the lowest-TCO medium for the cold/warm petabyte-to-exabyte tier vs the [NAND complex](themes/hbm-memory.md).

**Oligopoly structure / share (Bernstein HDD, 2025-10-23).** Three scaled players on revenue share: WDC ~44-45%, STX ~41-43%, Toshiba ~13-14%. The industry collapsed 62% over nine years as NAND cannibalized 2.5" PC-grade and mission-critical drives, but those vulnerable segments are now largely gone — the NAND-vs-HDD shipment-CAGR gap narrows from 23% ('13-'24) to ~2% ('24-'30), i.e. the most intense cannibalization is behind. Business-critical/nearline HDD revenue grows ~15-17% '24-'30 CAGR; HDD bit shipment ~20% CAGR. The cozy structure dates to the 2011 Thai floods (units -32%, ASP/drive +41%), which entrenched profitability-over-growth discipline. Bernstein models STX share recovering **41.7% → ~46% (FY25-30)** as HAMR scales (STX had ceded share by going early on HAMR with a weaker legacy portfolio). **HAMR moat = Intevac.** STX's Apr-2025 Intevac buy locked up the only HAMR-proven media-deposition toolset (Intevac's 200 Lean platform; ~65% of worldwide media capacity, >90% of Intevac revenue was STX); WDC must either license from STX or build a HAMR system from scratch with Canon Anelva, deepening the catch-up gap (Bernstein HDD, 2025-10-23). Supply-chain checks corroborate the HAMR difficulty: Toshiba/TDK view WDC's 2027 HAMR target as **"too optimistic"** ("Seagate took a long time to get HAMR right... not easy to build HAMR in high volumes"); Hoya glass-substrate utilization <80% leaves room for STX/WDC to squeeze out ~10% unit CAGR (Q425 Techknowledge tech takeaways).

## Current state (latest quarter)
**Q3 FY26 (March 2026 quarter, reported 2026-04-28) — beat-and-raise.** Revenue **$3.1B, +44% y/y / +10% q/q**; non-GAAP gross margin **47%** (+180bps q/q, +480bps y/y); non-GAAP operating margin **37.5%**; non-GAAP EPS **$4.10** (+115% y/y). GAAP figures from the 10-Q (period ended 2026-04-03): revenue $3,112M, net income $748M, diluted EPS $3.27, GAAP gross margin 46.5%, operating cash flow $1.1B (10-Q, 2026-04-29). FCF **$953M** (~31% margin), highest in over a decade; capex $151M (~4% of revenue). **Exabytes shipped 199 EB, +39% y/y**, on flat units — growth is areal-density, not capacity-add ("increasing areal density over unit volumes"). Data center 175 EB (88% of exabytes, 80% of revenue), data center revenue $2.5B (+55% y/y); revenue/TB up mid-single-digits y/y. Balance sheet de-levered: gross debt $3.9B, **net leverage 0.7x**, and **Fitch upgraded STX to investment grade** (all Q3 FY26 call, 2026-04-28).

**Technology / contracts:** Mozaic 4 (4+TB/disk HAMR) began shipping for revenue late March, expected to be the majority of HAMR exabytes by end CY26; **75% of leading global cloud customers** now on Mozaic for revenue; two largest CSPs qualified on 4+TB/disk. Mozaic 5 (50TB) "well to plan," qual shipments late 2027. **Nearline almost fully allocated through CY2027**; top-3 CSP remaining performance obligations nearly doubled to **$1.1T** (Q3 FY26 call, 2026-04-28). **HAMR mix roadmap (Bernstein HDD, 2025-10-23):** STX guides HAMR/Mozaic to **40% of nearline exabytes by end FY26 → 50% by 1H FY27 → 70% by end FY27 → 80% by end FY28**; near-term capacity roadmap 36TB (CY26) → 40TB (2027) → 50TB beyond. Capacity roadmap: HAMR delivers density with fewer platters (STX hit a 32TB drive on 10 platters via HAMR vs Toshiba's 31TB on 11 platters via MAMR + shingling).

**Guidance — Q4 FY26 (June quarter):** revenue **$3.45B ±$100M** (+41% y/y midpoint), op margin "lower 40% range," non-GAAP EPS **$5.00 ±$0.20** (Q3 FY26 call). **Long-term annual revenue growth target RAISED to "at least 20%"** (from low-to-mid-teens at Q4 FY25), on demand acceleration + CY27 visibility. Trajectory in context: EPS has gone Q4 FY25 $2.59 → Q1 $2.61 → Q2 $3.11 → Q3 $4.10, GM 37.9% → 40.1% → 42.2% → 47% (transcripts 2025-07-29, 2025-10-28, 2026-01-27, 2026-04-28). **Build-to-order is structural:** both STX and WDC have shifted hyperscalers onto build-to-order/long-term-agreement models that incentivize forward planning and keep unit capacity disciplined — nearline EB growth comes from areal density, not factory builds (UBS 2026 overview, 2025-12-22; Q425 Techknowledge).

Slippage extends STX's window; on-time WDC + ePMR "bridge" (UBS's view) compresses STX's share-gain premium.
- Exabyte demand breadth — confirmation AI training/inference/physical-AI sustains the cycle, and that EB shipments don't snap back to trendline (UBS double-ordering risk). Read-through in [hbm-memory](themes/hbm-memory.md) and [ai-datacenter-power](themes/ai-datacenter-power.md).

## Risks
- **Technology-transition execution** — HAMR/Mozaic areal-density ramp must stay cost-effective; the FY25 10-K flags failure to advance areal density / technology transitions as a key competitive risk. STX itself ran multiple years of HAMR delays (originally targeted 2018-20, slipped to 2024-25) — execution is not a given (Bernstein HDD, 2025-10-23).
- **Customer concentration** — one customer was ~10% of consolidated revenue in FY25; demand is driven by hyperscaler capex (FY25 10-K). Top-3 CSP RPO of $1.1T concentrates both upside and risk.
- **Double-ordering / above-trendline shipments** — TTM EB shipments running above the long-term demand trendline raise the odds of "shadow" orders that don't convert; the clearest bear vector if AI-storage demand normalizes (UBS 2026 overview, 2025-12-22).
- **ASP / demand cyclicality** — "intense competition" and demand variations; vertical integration amplifies underutilization charges when systems demand falls (FY25 10-K). Peak-pricing optics if exabyte growth decelerates (Woodring, 2026-06-16; UBS out-year EPS declines).
- **Flash / SSD substitution** — NAND-based solutions complement and compete; QLC nearline SSD launching from late-2025/2026 and ramping into the HDD shortage, with substitution economics improving as eSSD pricing falls toward ~3x HDD (vs ~6-8x today) (MS NAND, 2025-09-10; Q425 Techknowledge).
- **China / tariffs / geopolitics** — most assembly and substantial sales are ex-US (China, Thailand, Malaysia, Singapore, N. Ireland); changes in US trade policy/tariffs may materially impact operations (FY25 10-K, 10-Q 2026-04-29 "Recent Developments").
