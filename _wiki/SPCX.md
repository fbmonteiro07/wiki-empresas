## Snapshot
Space Exploration Technologies Corp. (SpaceX, **Nasdaq: SPCX**, Texas-incorporated) is Elon Musk's vertically-integrated space-and-AI conglomerate. Post the **February 2026 acquisition of xAI**, it reports in **three segments** (424B4 2026-06-12):
- **Connectivity** — Starlink LEO broadband + direct-to-cell: **~10.3M Starlink subscribers** across 164 countries; **~7.4M monthly unique devices** for direct-to-cell/messaging across ~30 countries.
- **Launch** — Falcon 9 / Falcon Heavy / Dragon (operational, reusable) + **Starship** (Super Heavy + upper stage, in development); the launch backbone that uniquely enables the orbital-compute ambition.
- **AI** (ex-xAI) — **Grok** frontier models, **X** (social/advertising), and **AI compute** — the COLOSSUS / COLOSSUS II training clusters.

**FY2025 total revenue $17.2bn** (FY24 $12.5bn, FY23 $9.3bn); pro-forma (incl. xAI) **net loss attributable to common shareholders ~$(4.9)bn** in FY2025, driven by AI-segment compute scaling (424B4). It sits at the intersection of three of this wiki's biggest themes — AI compute/datacenter power, satellite connectivity, and reusable launch.

## The IPO (424B4, 2026-06-12 · Reg. No. 333-296070)
- **555,555,555 Class A shares at $135.00** → **~$75.0bn gross** (the largest IPO on record); underwriting discount $0.90/sh (~$500M); net proceeds ~$74.5bn before expenses. Over-allotment: up to 83,333,333 additional shares.
- **Listing:** Nasdaq + **Nasdaq Texas** under **SPCX**.
- **Dual-class control:** Class A = 1 vote, Class B = 10 votes. **Musk holds ~82.4% of voting power** post-IPO → SPCX is a **"controlled company"** (relies on Nasdaq governance exemptions). Musk's Class B vests against extreme milestones — market-cap tranches **plus a permanent Mars colony (≥1M people)** and **non-Earth-based datacenters delivering 100 Tbps** (424B4).
- **Shares outstanding post-IPO:** ~7.38bn Class A + ~5.70bn Class B ≈ **~13.1bn total** → implied equity value very roughly **~$1.7–1.8tn** at the $135 IPO price (computed; SPCX did not print an explicit market cap — treat as ESTIMATE).
- **Use of proceeds:** fund growth — **expansion of AI compute infrastructure**, launch infrastructure/vehicles, and scale/capacity (424B4).

## AI compute — the neocloud / datacenter story (the reason this name is here)
- **COLOSSUS (Memphis, TN) + COLOSSUS II (Memphis + Southaven, MS)** — described as the **largest AI training data center clusters on Earth**; collectively **~1.0 GW of compute power** today (424B4). SpaceX claims it was **first to deploy a coherent gigawatt-scale AI training cluster (2026)**; built COLOSSUS's first cluster in **122 days**, COLOSSUS II's in **91 days**, at per-MW construction cost "considerably below industry benchmarks."
- **~325,000 NVIDIA GPUs** across COLOSSUS / COLOSSUS II (disclosed via the Cursor compute agreement), backed by hyperscale CPUs, exabyte storage and high-speed interconnect.
- **GPU sourcing:** all GPUs procured **purchase-order basis, no long-term supplier contracts** — flagged as a supply-concentration risk (depends on a concentrated set of advanced fabs).
- **Terafab** — chip-manufacturing initiative **with Tesla and Intel** (framework announced Mar 2026); long-term goal **~1 terawatt of compute hardware per year** → vertical integration into chip design/manufacturing.
  - **NEW — SpaceX-Tesla merger speculation (The Information · Grace Kay, 2026-07-01):** Growing expectation Musk will propose a formal **SpaceX-Tesla merger**; Terafab (Tesla's $55bn semiconductor manufacturing project) framed as a **Tesla-SpaceX collaboration** rather than an arm's-length JV. Org-chart analysis: the two firms are **"already operating as one entity in key ways"**; **SpaceX employees make up most of Terafab's leadership**; shared executives include **Charlie Kuehmann** (materials engineering), and Tesla AI/software execs **Ashok Elluswamy** and **Silvio Brugada** have crossed over into SpaceX's **xAI** unit. Terafab has also hired several **ex-Intel** employees, including its first major outside hire, **Gary Jiang** (18-year Intel veteran, former Intel Arizona factory manager) as foundry manager. See [TSLA.md](TSLA.md) for the Tesla-side framing of this same reporting.
- **Cursor (Anysphere) — Apr 2026 compute + option agreement:** SPCX provides Cursor GPU-cluster capacity and gets a **right (not obligation) to acquire Cursor** at a predetermined price; tied to an implied Cursor equity value of ~$60bn (424B4, "Recent Developments"). Drives inference demand + developer-interaction data.
- **Orbital compute ambition:** stated goal to **launch ~100 GW of compute to space per year** via satellites carrying >100 kW compute/metric ton — "AI compute satellites" / "non-Earth-based datacenters." This is the moonshot the launch business is meant to enable (and is explicitly flagged as unproven/uncertain timeline).
- **NEW — xAI reportedly reselling compute externally, dialing back internal AI tools (Vital Knowledge · Adam Crisafulli, 2026-07-01):** xAI reportedly **dialing back expectations for internal AI tools** (Grok, Cursor) and has **started selling compute capacity to external customers, including Google and Anthropic** — Crisafulli draws a direct parallel to **Meta's new "Meta Compute" external-cloud pivot**, announced the same day. This is a notable shift from the IPO-prospectus framing of COLOSSUS/COLOSSUS II capacity as being built to scale SpaceX's own AI ambitions (Grok training, Cursor option, orbital-compute roadmap) — now reads more like a neocloud/compute-reseller pivot, at least at the margin.
- **JPM (Wei Zheng Chan desk, 2026-06-30):** reports **xAI sold its Colossus 1 compute capacity to Anthropic for $1.25bn/month**, covering **220,000+ NVIDIA GPUs (H100, H200, GB200)**; note flags **Anthropic's ARR ceiling as reportedly compute-constrained** — i.e., Anthropic's willingness to pay up for guaranteed capacity is itself a signal of compute scarcity. This $1.25bn/month figure is a HARD external-revenue data point for the AI segment that is not in the 424B4 (which pre-dates the deal) — flag as the single largest disclosed external compute-sale so far.

## Debate / thesis
- **Bull:** Unique vertical integration — the only entity pairing frontier AI (Grok/xAI), gigawatt-scale terrestrial compute (COLOSSUS), a dominant satellite-connectivity franchise (Starlink ~10.3M subs), reusable launch (Falcon/Starship) and a chip ambition (Terafab). Fast, low-cost datacenter construction (91–122 days/cluster, below-benchmark per-MW cost). Starlink is the cash-generative anchor; the orbital-compute angle is asymmetric optionality. New incremental AI-compute demand node for the supply chain (Jeff Pu CRDO Buy, 2026-06-29). **NEW:** external compute monetization is now real and large — xAI's Colossus 1 sold to Anthropic for **$1.25bn/month** covering 220k+ GPUs (JPM, 2026-06-30) — a concrete external-revenue proof point that de-risks the "deeply loss-making AI segment" bear case, if the deal is representative of a broader neocloud pivot (Vital Knowledge parallel to Meta's "Meta Compute," 2026-07-01). A Tesla-SpaceX merger, if it happens, would also unlock Tesla's Terafab/AI-chip roadmap and balance sheet alongside SpaceX's (The Information, 2026-07-01).
- **Bear:** **Deep losses** — pro-forma net loss ~$(4.9)bn FY2025, AI-segment Adj. EBITDA **$(1,237)M in 2025** (from +$347M in 2024) and **$(609)M in Q1-26** as it scales compute. **Extreme key-man / governance risk** — Musk ~82.4% voting, controlled-company exemptions, Class B vesting tied to a literal Mars colony. **GPU supply on purchase-orders only** (no long-term contracts). The 100 GW-to-orbit goal is explicitly unproven. Valuation (~$1.7tn+ implied) prices in enormous execution. **NEW:** xAI reportedly **dialing back internal AI-tool ambitions** (Grok, Cursor) just as it pivots to selling compute externally (Vital Knowledge, 2026-07-01) — could read as a tacit admission that in-house frontier-model economics aren't working as well as the neocloud/reseller model, undercutting the "unique vertical integration" bull framing. The **AI-handset prototype** (WSJ via Vital Knowledge, 2026-07-01) and **TMUS-merger rumor** (Jefferies/Handelsblatt, 2026-07-01) both read as scope-creep speculation with no confirmed strategic rationale; Crisafulli explicitly skeptical SpaceX can scale a consumer device platform, and reiterates Starlink stays complementary to AT&T/Verizon/T-Mobile rather than displacing them.
- **Where the sell-side stands:** brand-new issue (priced 2026-06-12) — formal coverage is just initiating. Jeff Pu's connectivity read-through (CRDO new Buy) is the first in-corpus datapoint; no SPCX consensus/PT set yet. Jefferies (Favuzza, 2026-07-01) flags SPCX as the heaviest pre-market dollar-volume mover in its tech basket ($573.1mn notional) amid the cluster of Tesla-merger / phone / TMUS / xAI-compute headlines.

## Risks
- **Profitability / cash burn** — multi-billion net losses; AI segment deeply EBITDA-negative while scaling.
- **Key-man & governance** — Musk control (~82.4% vote), controlled-company exemptions, Mars/Tbps milestone vesting; concentrated decision-making.
- **GPU / supply concentration** — purchase-order-only GPU procurement; dependence on a concentrated set of advanced fabs and on power availability.
- **Execution / unproven moonshots** — 100 GW orbital compute, Terafab (1 TW/yr), Starship — all explicitly uncertain in the prospectus.
- **Valuation** — ~$1.7tn+ implied equity value on a still loss-making, capex-heavy combination.
- **Regulatory** — spectrum, launch licensing, AI/chatbot safety (Grok), cross-border satellite operations.
