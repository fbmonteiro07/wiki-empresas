# FSLY — Fastly, Inc.

_Wiki · generated 2026-06-25 · sources: `E:\Wiki Felipe empresas\CDNs\_imported_from_E_root\` (FSLY filings + transcripts + decks). Master index: [00_INDEX.md](00_INDEX.md)._

## Snapshot
Fastly is an edge-cloud platform — a modern, software-defined CDN whose thesis is diversification away from commoditizing delivery toward higher-value **Security** and **Compute@Edge**. It reports three lines: **Network Services** (delivery/CDN, the legacy core), **Security** (Next-Gen WAF plus a fast-expanded suite — DDoS Protection, Bot Management, API Discovery/Inventory, ContentGuard), and **Other** (Compute@Edge). FY2025 revenue was **$624.0M, +15% YoY** with the company's **first profitable fiscal year** on a non-GAAP basis (FSLY Q4 2025 call, 2026-02-11); GAAP still loss-making — net loss $121.7M in FY2025 vs $158.1M in FY2024 (FSLY 10-K FY2025). In Q1 2026 the mix was Network Services ~73% ($126.2M), Security 22% ($38.8M), Other (Compute) ~5% ($8.0M) (FSLY Q1 2026 call, 2026-05-06). The story: a CEO-led (Kip Compton, ~1yr in seat) turnaround — re-accelerating top line, record gross margins, swing to non-GAAP profit and positive FCF — set against heavy customer concentration, consumption-revenue volatility, and competition from Cloudflare, Akamai and hyperscalers.

## Current state (latest quarter — Q1 2026, call 2026-05-06)
Beat near the high end and a guidance raise, led by Security/Compute. Q1 2026 revenue **$173M, +20% YoY** (guide $168–174M); non-GAAP **gross margin 65.1%, a record** (110 bps above the 64% guide midpoint, +780 bps YoY from 57.3%; ~190 bps was a one-time benefit from a server useful-life accounting change); non-GAAP **operating income $19.1M (11% margin)** vs negative 4% a year ago, ~1,500 bps YoY expansion; non-GAAP **net profit $22.9M / $0.13 diluted EPS** (vs −$0.05); adj. EBITDA $29.5M (17% margin) (FSLY Q1 2026 call, 2026-05-06).
- **Diversification working.** Security accelerated to **+47% YoY** (fourth straight quarter of accelerating security revenue), now 22% of total; nearly half of security products sold to new customers in the quarter were the *newer* products (DDoS, Bot Management, API Discovery/Inventory), not just the WAF. Compute ("Other") **+67% YoY**, the largest sequential dollar step-up ever in that line (+$1.6M QoQ). Combined Security + Other grew ~50% YoY and management expects them to exceed a **$200M annual run-rate by late 2026** (FSLY Q1 2026 call, 2026-05-06).
- **Network Services +11% YoY** ($126.2M) — roughly 2x market growth via share gains, but decelerated from Q4's +19% on seasonality (Q4 had a one-off gaming download + holiday e-commerce strength). Price erosion in delivery "mid-single digits" YoY, same as Q4; applies only to Network Services, not Security/Compute. Management chose to honor existing contracts rather than push memory-driven price increases (contrasting Akamai), citing a more capital-efficient platform (FSLY Q1 2026 call, 2026-05-06).
- **Customer metrics.** LTM **NRR 113%** (up from 110% prior quarter, 100% a year ago), now broadening from largest customers into mid-market. Record **RPO $369M, +63% YoY** (4th consecutive quarter of accelerating RPO); current RPO 75% of total (~$275M next-12-month commit), +77% YoY. Top-10 = 34% of revenue, grew 25% YoY; no single customer >10%. **Large-customer (>$100k ARR) count = 634** — and the company has **stopped disclosing total customer count** as of Q1 2026 (>90% of revenue from large customers) (FSLY Q1 2026 call, 2026-05-06).
- **Cash / capital.** ~$330M cash & investments (down $31M QoQ on retirement of $39M current debt due Mar-2026); CFO $28.9M; **FCF +$4.1M** (down YoY on higher infra spend). 7.75% convert outstanding (due Jun-2028) plus 0% converts due 2030; CFO comfortable with liquidity (FSLY Q1 2026 call, 2026-05-06).
- **Guidance raised.** Q2 2026 revenue $170–176M (+16% midpoint), GM ~64% ±50 bps, op profit $12–16M (8% margin), EPS $0.05–0.08. **FY2026 raised to revenue $710–725M (+15% midpoint)** (from $700–720M / +14% at Q4), GM 64% ±50 bps, op profit $58–68M (9% margin, vs 4% in 2025), EPS $0.27–0.33, **FCF $40–50M**. Infra capex 10–12% of revenue (front-loaded; vs 5% in 2025), with possible H2 increase. Management said **>half of FY2026 incremental revenue will come from Security + Other** (FSLY Q1 2026 call, 2026-05-06).

## Debate / thesis
- **AI-software read-through — "CDN for tokens" (Vercel CEO Guillermo Rauch, Stanford MS&E435, 2026-06-29):** Rauch's "AI Gateway = CDN for tokens" thesis extends edge-CDN primitives (routing/caching/observability/security) to inference traffic — directionally a Compute@Edge opportunity for Fastly, but the category is being defined at scale by Vercel/Cloudflare; Fastly's subscale compute leg makes this a TAM watch-item, not yet a win. See [tokenmaxxing theme](themes/tokenmaxxing.md).
- **Bull:** The turnaround is real and self-funding. FY2025 was the first profitable year (non-GAAP), Q4 2025 re-accelerated to +23% YoY (highest in 3+ years) and Q1 2026 sustained +20% with record 65.1% gross margin and ~1,500 bps op-margin expansion (FSLY Q4 2025 call, 2026-02-11; Q1 2026 call, 2026-05-06). The mix shift to Security (+47%) and Compute (+67%) de-risks the commoditizing delivery base; management guides Security to ~25–30% and Network Services 9–11% for FY2026 (FSLY Q1 2026 call, 2026-05-06). AI is framed as a tailwind across all three lines — agentic traffic volume in delivery, security attach (privacy, API governance, ContentGuard anti-AI-scraping), and inference-driven compute. CEO: workloads "shifting from training to inference… may very well open up a bigger opportunity for us given the way our network is built" (FSLY Q1 2026 call, 2026-05-06). Cross-name context: among the three CDNs, Fastly "recovered growth from 12% to 23% YoY (Q4) and is in operational transformation," rebuilding go-to-market and a 5+ product security portfolio (CDN_Evolucao_Q2-25_Q1-26.docx, 2026-05-11).
- **Bear:** Network Services (~73% of revenue) is a low-growth, price-eroding, consumption-volatile core; +11% in Q1 leaned on share gains and an easy decel off a one-off-boosted Q4. Memory/component cost inflation (2–3x on memory) is a margin and capex risk, and Fastly's choice not to pass through price could compress margin if delivery reaccelerates the wrong way (FSLY Q1 2026 call, 2026-05-06). GAAP still deeply loss-making (FY2025 net loss $121.7M) (FSLY 10-K FY2025). Heavy customer concentration (top-10 = 32–34% of revenue) plus a TikTok/ByteDance dependency whose US restructuring leaves traffic levels uncertain (FSLY 10-K FY2025). The newly raised FY26 op margin is still only 9%, well below scaled peers, and the company just stopped disclosing total customer count — reducing transparency on the funnel.

## Risks
- **Customer concentration:** 10 largest customers = 32% (33% in 2024) of TTM revenue; media & entertainment heavy; low minimum commitments make it easy for large customers to reallocate or switch usage (FSLY 10-K FY2025).
- **TikTok / ByteDance dependency:** TikTok was a top customer in 2025 and remains one; the Jan-2026 US restructuring (TikTok USDS JV) leaves future traffic levels and the relationship uncertain (FSLY 10-K FY2025).
- **Consumption-based revenue volatility:** usage-based model + long, variable sales cycles (months to >1 year) make revenue hard to predict (FSLY 10-K FY2025).
- **History of net losses (GAAP):** net losses since 2011 inception; FY2025 GAAP net loss $121.7M (FSLY 10-K FY2025).
- **Intense competition:** legacy CDN (Akamai); app/API security vendors (Akamai, Cloudflare, F5, Thales/Imperva); point CDNs (Bunny, CDNetworks, CDN77, Qwilt); serverless-edge/compute and hyperscalers; large customers building their own CDNs (FSLY 10-K FY2025).
- **Component cost / supply chain:** memory price inflation (2–3x) and tariff/trade-dispute risk to the hardware supply chain (FSLY Q1 2026 call, 2026-05-06; FSLY 10-K FY2025).
