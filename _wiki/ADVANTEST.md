## Snapshot
Advantest is the **#1 global automated-test-equipment (ATE) vendor** — a back-end semiconductor name levered to **test intensity**, not wafer starts — in a duopoly with Teradyne. Two reportable segments (restated to two from Q1 FY2025): **Test System Business** (~90% of revenue: SoC test systems + memory test systems incl. high-performance DRAM/HBM and NAND, plus device handlers/probers) and **Services & Others** (~10%: support, consumables/test-interface boards, system-level test) (Advantest FY2025 results release, 2026-04-27). The structural driver is the swing of the SoC mix to AI/HPC — **~80% of SoC business is high-performance compute / AI** — plus rising **HBM/high-performance DRAM** test intensity (Advantest Q3 FY2025 call, 2026-01-28). It is effectively the **sole SoC tester at the GPU/CoWoS leaders** today — CoWoS device testing "comes from Advantest," and it is "better for HPC because of very large dies" while Teradyne fits mobile/InFO (Apple); a TSMC second-source qualification of Teradyne for AI test is "very difficult" and unlikely to displace it near-term (StoneX/John Su, 2025-08-20). Customers are foundries, OSATs, IDMs and memory makers; ~98% of sales are overseas; China is ~20–25% of revenue (Q3 FY2025 call; FY2025 release).

## Position in the value chain
Back-end test. Advantest buys test instruments, ATE components and handler/prober parts, integrates them into test platforms (V93000 SoC, T2000, memory testers, SLT), and sells to foundries, OSATs, IDMs and memory makers. It is levered to the **rising test intensity of complex AI chips, custom ASICs and HBM/high-performance DRAM stacks** — more chiplets, more die-stacking and tighter latent-defect tolerance mean more test seconds and more test insertions per device, the core AI/HBM tailwind (Advantest Q4 FY2025 call, 2026-04-29). The intensity loop is reinforced by chip economics: high-ASP advanced packaging (CoWoS) makes scrapping a finished device prohibitive, so customers raise test content per device to protect yield — and NVDA's annual product cadence is pulling SoC-tester deliveries earlier than expected (Jefferies/Techknowledge, 2025-12-17).

## Current state (latest quarter)
**FY2025 (year ended Mar 31 2026; results released 2026-04-27)** — a record year. Net sales **¥1,128.6bn, +44.7% y/y**; operating income **¥499.1bn, +118.8%** (op margin **44.2%**, up from 29.3%); net income **¥375.4bn, +132.9%**; basic EPS **¥515.15**; ROE 57.6% (Advantest FY2025 release, 2026-04-27). Segment split: **Test System ¥1,019.4bn (+49.3%)**, segment income **¥518.8bn (+97.9%)** — SoC test up sharply on HPC/AI complexity + volumes (mature auto/industrial soft), memory test up on high-performance DRAM and non-volatile; **Services & Others ¥109.2bn (+12.7%)**, segment income ¥8.8bn (vs ¥-16.1bn). The **Q4 print (call 2026-04-29)** beat: revenue ¥328.07bn vs ¥281.48bn forecast, EPS ¥174.55 vs ¥138.67 — but the stock fell **-5.56% after-hours**: management called Q4 margins "super high due to a favorable product mix… not indicative of future consistency" and guided **flat FY2026 operating margin** on BOM inflation + memory supply-chain constraints + macro (Q4 FY2025 call). **FY2026 guidance (ending Mar 2027):** net sales **¥1,420.0bn (+25.8%)**, operating income **¥627.5bn (+25.7%)**, net income ¥465.5bn (+24.0%), EPS ¥641.61, FX 1USD=¥150 (FY2025 release). SoC capacity ramp: **5,000 systems/yr by end-FY2026 → 10,000/yr by 2028–2029** (Q4 FY2025 call) — Jefferies/Techknowledge (2025-12-17) frames the same ramp as V93000 production >3,000/yr inside ~5,000 total capacity by end-FY2026, then 7,500 → 10,000 long-term (incl. buffer inventory), with **NVDA the single biggest FY-Mar-26 opportunity** and demand strong enough that customers are asking Advantest to pre-build buffer inventory. SoC tester share in the **high-60% range in 2025** (Q3 FY2025 call). China ~20–25% of revenue (Q3 FY2025 call).

## Debate / thesis
- **Bull (Jefferies):** Advantest is a top WFE/test pick — JEF **BUY, PT ¥30,000** going into the June 16 IR webinar (briefing, 2026-06-03; 2026-06-08). The story: AI test intensity (test seconds = unit volume x test content) structurally lifts the ATE TAM; SoC ~80% AI/HPC, custom-ASIC programs moving to high-volume manufacturing in 2026, and an HBM/high-performance-DRAM memory-test leg layered on top (Q3/Q4 FY2025 calls). JEF channel work (Techknowledge, 2025-12-17) adds three points: (i) a **new hyperscaler ASIC customer designs AI silicon next year, contribution starting FY26**, with Advantest leveraging its NVDA franchise — its edge is the **tester-handler interface** — to win the ASIC deals; (ii) DRAM/HBM memory-test and SoC compute both higher next year with "no downside at least," and visibility into demand has improved vs three months prior on the next-gen-device migration, so the 2H digestion phase looks more moderate than the July projection; (iii) NVDA's faster product cadence + costly packaging keeps pulling test content and tester deliveries forward.
- **Bull (memory-test / HBF leg — MS):** MS is **OW Advantest** (with Disco) and prefers it among Japan SPE names for high-bandwidth-flash (HBF) exposure — expansion of the high-speed flash-memory market would drive demand for the high-speed flash-memory testers Advantest is developing (MS AI NAND overview). MS also tags Advantest an **OW AI semi / AI-semi-enabler** alongside TSMC, Samsung, Alchip, MediaTek, Aspeed and KYEC (MS Asia on AI Factories), and on the AI long list (briefing, 2026-05-21).
- **Bull (sector read-through):** JEF Japan raised 2026/27E WFE to **$149bn / $165bn (+10%/+9%)**, still likely below buy-side (>25% for CY27) — Advantest a top pick into the up-cycle (briefing, 2026-06-08). NVDA Computex framing (hyperscaler capex >$1tn in 2027; CPU pull-through from agentic/inference) reads straight into more test content per system (briefing, 2026-06-03).
- **Bear / risks to the thesis:** (1) **Margin normalization** — FY2026 op-margin guided flat despite +26% sales growth; Q4's mix-driven margin is not repeatable; BOM inflation + memory supply constraints (Q4 FY2025 call). (2) **China** ~20–25% of revenue with decoupling/regulatory overhang and "growing domestic competition" (Q3/Q4 FY2025 calls). (3) **Competitive entry at GPU customers** — an entrant targeting 15–20% GPU test share; mgmt "comfortable" but declined customer specifics (Q4 FY2025 call). The supply-chain read is more sanguine: Teradyne is "best for mobile" / Apple InFO and Advantest "better for HPC," with a TSMC second-source qualification of Teradyne for AI test seen as "very difficult" and Teradyne not going to AI testing in 1–2 years (StoneX/John Su, 2025-08-20). **Sell-side read-through (GS, via its Teradyne note):** in its 2Q Semis Preview, GS frames **Advantest's incumbency in Nvidia merchant-GPU test** as the very share Teradyne is positioned to take — GS is bullish on TER's merchant-GPU test share gains, and calls the **durability of Advantest's GPU-test share the key debate** into the print (Goldman Sachs "2Q Preview: Expect broad upside...", 2026-07-05; no GS rating on Advantest itself). This is the sell-side articulation of the same GPU-test-share risk management flagged (the 15–20%-share entrant), now naming Teradyne as the challenger. (4) ATE cyclicality / lumpy orders gated by foundry + packaging capacity availability (Q4 FY2025 call). (5) New-entrant CPO test (TER/FORM/KEYS named alongside Advantest) is an unsettled share fight (briefing, 2026-05-29). (6) **SLT/burn-in not yet shipping** — Advantest's system-level test is under evaluation with possible 2026 ramp but no shipments yet, so KYEC stays dominant in burn-in near-term (JEF/Techknowledge, 2025-12-17).
- **Where the Street stands:** Buy/long skew. Jefferies BUY ¥30,000 (2026-06-03); MS OW (MS AI NAND; MS Asia on AI Factories; 2026-05-14 / 2026-05-21). No fresh in-house Outlook PT pulled this run (see Sources).
