# OpenAI — AI lab (private)

_Reference page · generated 2026-06-19 · **Private company — no public financials** (no ticker, no SEC filing, no BBG consensus). ## Snapshot
OpenAI is the frontier AI lab behind the **GPT** and **o-series** models, **ChatGPT** (the consumer/enterprise app), and a developer **API**. It sits at the top of the model layer: it consumes enormous amounts of compute (GPUs/accelerators + power), trains frontier models, and monetizes them through (i) ChatGPT subscriptions (consumer Plus/Pro + enterprise/Team), (ii) API/platform usage, and (iii) large one-off licensing/infrastructure deals with enterprises and governments. Flagship model is **GPT-5.5** ("Spud"), released 2026-04-23, positioned as the frontier model for complex professional/agentic work ([OpenAI, "Introducing GPT-5.5"](https://openai.com/index/introducing-gpt-5-5/); [Wikipedia: GPT-5.5](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GPT-5.5)). ChatGPT reported **230M+ weekly users** mid-June 2026 (@OpenAI, per `2026-06-19-company-specific.md`); sell-side tracks a broader WAU base of **~920M** as of early 2026, short of the 1B year-end-2025 goal but with a path to 2.75B by 2030 (Barclays Framework, Mar-2026). It is the most prominent — but no longer the dominant-by-share — player in a market it created, now contested by Google Gemini and Anthropic Claude. Barclays judges OpenAI **~6-12 months ahead of competitors** on user growth, monetization and model capability (Barclays OpenAI, Nov-2025).

## Position in the value chain
OpenAI is the **model/application layer** sitting between compute suppliers and end demand. Historically Microsoft Azure was the exclusive compute backbone; OpenAI has since **diversified** — adding AWS, NVIDIA, custom silicon with Broadcom, and Stargate (SoftBank/Oracle) capacity — while Microsoft remains the largest single backer (~26.8% economic interest) and a primary distribution channel via Copilot ([Alpha Spread](https://www.alphaspread.com/market-news/mergers-acquisitions/openai-restructures-as-public-benefit-corporation-microsoft-takes-27-stake)).

## State of play
- **Revenue trajectory.** Company forecasts have repeatedly been revised up. ARR went from ~$6bn early-2025 to **$25bn by Mar-2026** (Barclays Framework, Mar-2026). FY25 closed at **$13.1bn** (vs UBS's ~$13-15bn 2025 estimate and MS's $12.7bn CY25 — UBS OpenAI deep-dive, Sep-2025; MS MSFT/OpenAI, May-2025). Forward, Barclays lays out OpenAI's own (Information-sourced) plan: **2026E $30.1bn → 2027E $62bn → 2028E $113bn (raised 13% vs prior) → 2029E $184bn (up 27% vs prior) → 2030E $284bn** (Barclays Framework, Mar-2026). This compares to UBS's earlier, lower path of $29bn(26)/$54bn(27)/$86bn(28)/$125bn(29) (UBS, Sep-2025) and MS's $33.6bn CY26 (MS, May-2025) — the numbers have inflected meaningfully higher in <12 months. Mix is ~50%+ ChatGPT consumer (incl. ads), ~27% enterprise agents, ~17% API.
- **Burn.** ~$34bn 2025 spend vs ~$28bn ARR (FT via @C_Barraud, `2026-06-18`). Sell-side frames it bigger: UBS modeled an ~$8bn loss on ~$15bn 2025 revenue (~$23bn cost base — UBS, Sep-2025); JPM flagged The Information's estimate of ~$46bn cash burn over four years (JPM initiation, Jul-2025); the latest plan implies **$111bn more cash burn through 2030** (Barclays Framework, Mar-2026, citing The Information 2/20/26). Friar has repeatedly framed the company as "constantly under compute" — compute, not demand, is the binding constraint (CFO interview, 2026-06-03).

## Debate / thesis
- **Bull:** Category creator with the strongest consumer brand and largest user base (~920M WAU per sell-side tracking, 230M+ on OpenAI's own weekly-active metric); ~6-12 months ahead of competitors (Barclays OpenAI, Nov-2025); revenue is not just scaling but **beating internal plan and being revised up** — FY25 ~9% ahead, 2029 raised 27% to $184B (Barclays Framework, Mar-2026); frontier model leadership re-asserted with GPT-5.5; TAM seen at **$700bn+ by 2030** across consumer/hardware/ads/enterprise (JPM initiation, Jul-2025); deep capital base (~$852bn round) and diversified compute; Microsoft distribution via Copilot. The frontier-token revenue accrual thesis — value concentrates at the frontier even if open-source wins token volume — favors OpenAI (BG2Pod, 2026-06-12). The whole AI capex super-cycle is effectively a leveraged bet on OpenAI hitting these numbers (Barclays OpenAI, Nov-2025; UBS, Sep-2025).
- **Morgan Stanley (Byrd): frontier-model quality commands durable enterprise premium; orchestration the key risk to watch (2026-06-26).** MS stays positive on OpenAI as a developer of leading AI models, with the enterprise ROI case intact (coding/legal/mission-critical tasks: quality far outweighs marginal cost savings of cheaper alternatives). The geopolitical/security preference for Western models underpins demand in US and key Western markets. Key risk flagged: orchestration layers ("Open Router Fusion"-type tools) that can "seamlessly combine multiple lower-cost models to achieve the same result as a single frontier model" — if effective, this leads to commoditization and "a value transfer" away from frontier labs toward hyperscalers and orchestration-platform providers. "The jury is out." MS also flags an emerging market split: high-value Western enterprise stays on frontier American models; Global South / parts of Europe lean toward open-source / Chinese alternatives — an "Apple vs Android" market bifurcation. Net: bull for the high-value enterprise segment, bearish risk flag on orchestration commoditization (Morgan Stanley / Stephen Byrd "Discussion on tokenmaxxing", 2026-06-26). [../relat%C3%B3rios%20bons/2026_06_26_stephen_byrd_on_tokenmaxxing.html](../relat%C3%B3rios%20bons/2026_06_26_stephen_byrd_on_tokenmaxxing.html)
- **Bear (token-optimization read-through):** UBS frames the **model layer as the biggest casualty** of enterprise token optimization — frontier API revenue growth could be pressured as cost-conscious enterprises **down-tier** to cheaper frontier models, SLMs, and open-source Chinese models (Alibaba Qwen, Moonshot Kimi, DeepSeek, Z.ai/GLM), "especially for non-coding use cases," in a model market that "is now more competitive and is bifurcating." A direct read on OpenAI's frontier API growth durability as enterprises put guardrails on token spend (UBS "The Ramp in Token Optimization" / Arcuri-Keirstead, 2026-06-23).
- **Bear:** **Cash burn / capital intensity** — ~$34bn 2025 spend, an implied **$111bn more cash burn through 2030** (Barclays Framework, Mar-2026), ~$650bn of signed compute obligations, and >$600bn of partner-borne capex to stand up the needed ~11GW (Barclays OpenAI, Nov-2025); structurally compute-short (CFO Friar 2026-06-03). Profitability not expected until ~2029 even on the bull plan (JPM initiation, Jul-2025). **Share erosion** — ChatGPT below 50% overall, already behind Anthropic on enterprise API (32% vs 21%, JPM Anthropic deep-dive, 2025-09-18). **Moat fragility** — JPM views the inability of any single lab to hold a durable edge as a commoditization signal (JPM initiation, Jul-2025). **Enterprise pricing power risk** — MSFT reportedly weighing DeepSeek to replace "expensive OpenAI/Anthropic APIs" (@zerohedge `2026-06-18`); MS notes inference cost runs ~49% of OpenAI revenue, pressuring unit economics (MS MSFT/OpenAI, May-2025). **Microsoft overhang** — IP rights through 2030, a reported ~20% revenue share and the equity-stake renegotiation remain unresolved variables (UBS, Sep-2025). **Bubble linkage** — Barclays' explicit framing: figuring out what would cause OpenAI to miss is the key to assessing what could pop the AI bubble (Barclays OpenAI, Nov-2025).
