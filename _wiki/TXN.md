## Snapshot
The world's largest **catalog analog + embedded-processing** semiconductor IDM — ~100,000 products, >80% direct-to-customer (incl. TI.com), longevity/diversity of sockets the moat. FY2025 revenue **$17.68bn** across two reportable segments: **Analog (~81% of revenue)** and **Embedded Processing (~15%)**, plus Other (~4%) (10-K, 2026-02-06). End markets: **Industrial 33%, Automotive 33%, Personal electronics 21%, Data center compute 9%, Communications equipment 3%, calculators ~1%** (10-K, 2026-02-06). Industrial + automotive + data center now ~75% of revenue, up from 43% in 2013 (Q4 FY25 call, 2026-01-27). The objective management manages to is **free-cash-flow-per-share growth over the long term**; the live debate is whether the just-completed 300mm internal-fab capex super-cycle now converts to a steep FCF/share inflection as capex rolls over.

## Position in the value chain
TXN is a fully internalized IDM: it runs its **own 300mm wafer fabs** (Richardson & Sherman TX, Lehi UT) — an unpackaged 300mm chip costs ~40% less than 200mm — plus internal assembly/test, selectively supplementing with foundries; it designs and builds catalog **analog + embedded-processing** chips and sells direct into **industrial, automotive, personal electronics, and enterprise/data center** (10-K, 2026-02-06). The capital story sits squarely in the middle box: ~$109bn allocated 2016–2025, the 300mm build now largely done, and capex guided down to **$2–3bn for 2026** (Q1 FY26 call, 2026-04-22) from a ~$5bn peak — the swing factor behind the **$8 FCF/share** target (Ilan, Q1 FY26 call). UBS models the roll-off steeper still: **$2.35bn 2026E → $2.0bn 2027E** (UBS/Arcuri, 2025-12-22).

## Debate / thesis
- **Bull:** The favorite long in analog — earlier in the recovery than ADI, so more positive revisions ahead, with strong GM torque if the cycle is real (Barclays/Rand desk, briefing 2026-06-03; 2026-05-20). The completed 300mm super-cycle is the FCF story: capex $5bn → $2–3bn while revenue inflects = path to **~$8 FCF/share** (Ilan, Q1 FY26 call). UBS (Arcuri) makes TXN its **top analog pick at Buy, PT $245**, arguing the Street underappreciates how fast capex is rolling off in 2026 — UBS models capex **$2.35bn 2026E → $2.0bn 2027E** vs the ~$5bn 2025 peak, which paired with a share/revenue recovery sets up "very efficient capex" and a sharp FCF inflection. UBS sits well above consensus: **2026E rev $20.5bn / EPS $7.07** (cons $19.1bn / $6.22) and **2027E rev $24.5bn / EPS $10.14** (cons $21.0bn / $8.05) — ~6%/~14% above Street on '26 rev/EPS, ~17%/~26% on '27 (UBS, 2025-12-22). Data center is the new growth leg: ~$1.2bn run-rate in 2025 (Q3 FY25 call), +~90% YoY in Q1 FY26 — and TXN claims it can fill "almost every analog socket on the rack." BofA's Vivek Arya makes TXN a **Buy** with AI sales potentially **>3x to $4.5bn LT** and calls it best-positioned analog share gainer in the **800V data-center era** (BofA, briefing 2026-05-26). BofA's underlying "Watts to Tokens" note quantifies it: at **~$1.5bn CY25 sales (+64% YoY)** TXN already has the **largest data-center business in analog**, but that book is **still mainly general-purpose amps/clocking/references and does not yet include the multi-phase VRMs and GaN sockets** BofA expects it to win as 800V ramps — so AI could reach **~$4.5bn by CY28, up to ~18% of TXN sales (from ~12% today)**; BofA **raised its PO to $370 (from $320) on 40x CY27E P/E** and models TXN **retaining leading AI-analog share (expanding to ~21% by CY30)** (BofA "Watts to Tokens," 2026-05-25). JPM's primer reinforces the "supply is share" argument: management attributed Q1 strength partly to **"ability to supply"** — capacity availability converting into share while peers are constrained — and TXN reclassified historically-industrial **PSU content into the data-center end market**, with meaningful application-specific 800V-socket revenue (Stage-1 conversion, GaN power stages, GPU VRM/V-core sampling) building through **2H26 into 2027** (JPM "First Principles," 2026-06-25). Citi reiterates TXN **top pick** on the 800V transition, expecting **DC-power share gains in 2H26** vs Infineon/MPWR/Renesas; internal high-voltage GaN capacity quadrupled since Oct'24 (Citi/Atif Malik, briefing 2026-06-15). Citi formally raised the **PT $280→$345 (30x 2028E EPS, up from 28x)** and lifted C26/C27/C28 EPS **+1%/+1%/+6% to $8.03/$10.10/$11.42** on price hikes + DC share gains; among Citi's coverage TXN has the **largest GM-expansion opportunity (>10pp)** alongside ON given it manufactures **~85% of wafers internally**, and Citi flags TXN among the few high-voltage (>900V) GaN vendors and a key CPU-VRM supplier alongside IFX/MPWR (Citi "800V power", 2026-06-15). An automotive Tier-1 expert rated **TI as winning vs ADI** — "TI in general capturing the market much higher, much better than ADI, especially because of their involvement in the automotive sector," and named TI (with NXP) as customizing to OEM requirements vs Qualcomm's standardized higher-margin parts (Expert call, 2026-06-08). Redburn **Buy** on broadening 5–15% mature-node price rises and AI-DC supply tightness (R&Co Redburn, briefings 2026-05-12 / 2026-05-20). Jefferies' analog-upcycle thesis reinforces the pricing leg — **TI is raising prices again in July, its second (possibly third) increase this cycle**, far faster than 2021 when the first hike came ~10 months in, and names TI/ADI/Infineon as sitting in a good competitive position in China because **analog/MCUs/MOSFETs see "very little" Chinese investment** (China only attacks high-growth "exciting" categories like SiC/GaN/GPUs) — i.e. the catalog franchise is structurally insulated from China indigenization (Jefferies "Analog Semis," 2026-06-03). JPM (Harlan Sur) keeps TXN an **overweight top pick in the broad-based segment** (with ADI/MCHP), flagging **CY27 EPS already revised +23% across ADI/TXN/MCHP over the past two earnings seasons** and 2H26 pricing actions potentially firing (JPM "Semis spring update," 2026-05-29). The AlphaSense Infineon primer's peer benchmark quantifies why TXN is the margin leader: **FY25 gross margin 57.0%, EBIT margin 34.1%, ROIC 17.4% — highest in the analog/power peer set** (vs IFX 39%/10%, STM 34%/1.5%, ON 33%/1.4%, NXP 55%/24.8%), on its differentiated portfolio + low-cost 300mm base, and notes US analog giants (TI/NXP/ADI) return **80-100% of net income** via dividends + buybacks vs IFX's ~25% (AlphaSense "IFX Primer", 2026-06-22).

## Risks
- **Cyclicality:** rapid demand swings in analog/embedded; large-customer concentration in the top 50 (10-K, 2026-02-06). Recovery was slow through most of 2025 with auto lagging (Jefferies, 2025-09-26).
- **Underutilization / depreciation:** new 300mm fabs lift depreciation; idle/under-loaded capacity is expensed as incurred — direct GM drag in soft quarters (10-K; Lizardi, Q3/Q4 FY25 calls).
- **China:** ~20% of revenue from China; ~50% of revenue from products shipped into China — tariff/export-control/embargo exposure (10-K, 2026-02-06). China automotive weak in Q1 FY26.
- **Tariffs / trade:** tariffs, export restrictions, sanctions and trade barriers specific to semis; demand pull-in/normalization risk (10-K; Q2 FY25 call).
- **800V/DC-power timing:** adoption may lag hype 2–3 years; potential China GaN/SiC oversupply (@bubbleboi, briefing 2026-06-15).
- **Valuation:** trading at 12m relative highs; GS Sell, PT $200 (25x normalized EPS $8.00), GM lagging peers in the recovery on inventory + D&A, FCF/share the swing factor (GS "2Q Preview," 2026-07-05; earlier briefings 2026-05-12 / 2026-05-20). UBS's Buy explicitly flags it may be overestimating TXN's share recovery and revenue growth (UBS, 2025-12-22).
