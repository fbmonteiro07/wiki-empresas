## Snapshot
AIXTRON makes **MOCVD / epitaxy deposition equipment** (metal-organic chemical vapour deposition) for **compound semiconductors** — the tools that grow the III-V and wide-bandgap layers on wafers. It does not make chips; it sells the capital equipment to chipmakers. Four demand pillars: **optoelectronics** (lasers/EML/PIC, VCSEL, photonics for AI-datacenter optics), **GaN power**, **SiC power**, and **LED/MicroLED**. FY2025 revenue EUR 557m, -12% yoy, GM 40%, EBIT EUR 100m (Aixtron FY25 call, 2026-02-26). HQ Herzogenrath, Germany. Flagship platform: **G10** family (G10-AsP for lasers/photonics, G10-GaN, G10-SiC). Structurally a small-cap semicap with **near-monopoly niche share** — historically >90% in InP/laser epitaxy, >85% in GaN epitaxy (Q3/25 call, 2025-10-30).

## Position in the value chain
AIXTRON sits one step upstream of the compound-semi fabs: it buys precision mechanical, optical and gas-handling components and integrates them into epitaxy reactors, which it sells to makers of lasers, power devices and LEDs. The investable read-through runs two ways — **AI-datacenter optical** (800G→1600G transceivers, EML/PIC lasers; the current order driver) and **800V DC power** (GaN + SiC for the NVIDIA-style grid-to-chip power chain). See theme: [800V DC power](themes/800v-dc-power.md) and [optical / CPO](themes/optical-cpo.md).

## Current state (latest quarter — Q1 FY2026, reported 2026-04-30)
A textbook "weak P&L, strong bookings" quarter. **Revenue EUR 59m, -47% yoy** (within EUR 65m ±10m guide) on seasonality + soft power electronics; **gross margin 18%** (-12pp) on negative operating leverage plus a mid-single-digit EUR m one-off personnel charge; **EBIT EUR -22m** (Q1/26 IR call, 2026-04-30). The signal is in orders: **intake EUR 171m, +~30% yoy, >2/3 from optoelectronics** — what the CEO called "a clear inflection point," driven by laser-related Datacom for AI data centers as the architecture shifts copper→optical at 800G/1600G. G10-AsP was the majority of volume orders; many multi-tool orders extend beyond 2026. Management **raised FY26 guidance mid-April: revenue EUR 560m ±30m (was 520m), GM ~42%, EBIT margin 17–20%**; Q2/26 revenue guided EUR 110m ±10m. Balance sheet is fortress-grade: cash EUR 273m, equity ratio 85%, and a **EUR 450m 5-yr 0%-coupon convertible** placed in April (accretive day-one). Mix in Q1: opto 52% of equipment rev, LED/MicroLED 31%, GaN+SiC 17%. After-sales (EUR 24m, more stable) was 40% of revenue.

## Debate / thesis
- **The Jefferies upgrade sequence (the call):** Jefferies first **upgraded Hold→Buy on 2026-02-10, lifting PT to EUR 27 from EUR 18.20** (+33%; Bakhda/Menon, off a EUR 20.32 price), framing AIXA as an "underappreciated AI play with an inflection ahead." Thesis: stronger hyperscaler capex + datacom pull-forward derisks the opto outlook, the 800V HVDC/GaN data-centre narrative underpins the mid-term, and a derisked SiC sets up a re-rate. PT then moved to **EUR 73 on 2026-06-19** (Beavington) as the US optical model was built out. **Jefferies numbers (2026-02-10)**: FY26 rev EUR 568.5m / EBITDA EUR 128.6m / EBIT EUR 110.5m (+8% above cons rev), FY27 rev EUR 670m / EBITDA EUR 176.8m / EBIT EUR 156.7m (+5% above cons rev), FY28 rev EUR 710m / EBITDA EUR 196m. PT of EUR 27 derived off FY27E EV/EBITDA of 19.0x (raised from 14.5x) on EUR 177m EBITDA — a slight discount to the 21.6x front-end semi-cap peer average.
- **Bull — multi-year AI-optical capex play with monopoly share.** Jefferies (Beavington) raised PT to **EUR 73** on 2026-06-19, modelling transceiver units ~5x 2025→2028 against AIXA's ~100% InP MOCVD share, +8%/+16% above 2028 rev/EPS consensus, with GM upside on tool volumes + rising G10 mix (E:\briefings\2026\2026-06-19-morning.md). CEO: of the top-10 AI-datacom device makers, "80%, 90%, maybe 100%" are AIXTRON customers, ~90% on G10-AsP (Q3/25 call). The opto signal is corroborated by customer capex: Coherent's and Lumentum's Dec-qtr (FYQ2'26) capex both ran ~35% above expectations, both front-loading into H1'26 — AIXA's two largest opto epi-tool customers, with Lumentum citing a "significant block of additional capacity starting in H2'26" (Jefferies, 2026-02-10). Jefferies modelled opto rev EUR 100m FY25 → **EUR 155m FY26 (+18.5% vs cons)** → EUR 150m FY27. Optionality from 800V DC power (GaN+SiC) into 2027/28 and super-junction SiC favouring the batch reactor.
- **800V HVDC = the GaN growth engine.** NVIDIA's **Kyber rack (NVL576, ~1MW, H2'27 launch)** drives the architectural shift from 230V AC / 48V toward 800V HVDC: at 1MW, low-voltage distribution means impractical currents (thick copper, I²R losses). SiC likely prevails on 800V→54V conversion, GaN on 54V→12V (with some Si initially); below 12V stays Si MOSFET (~60% of power-semi value, server-board level, Infineon-led, with ~3x content uplift on vertical power supply). Jefferies expects power-semi makers to place epi-tool orders **H2'26/H1'27** ahead of the ramp, with the 300mm GaN cost-down shift (e.g. Infineon Villach) adding capex; AIXA's **~90% GaN MOCVD share** is the exposure (Jefferies, 2026-02-10).
- **Bear — concentration on one cyclical wave + soft power core.** Opto strength masks SiC ("down ~20pp of group mix in 2026," CEO at Q3/25 call — Jefferies reads this as ~-70% to ~EUR 40m, implying only ~3 SiC tool shipments/qtr, and views it as cautious given China 8-inch SiC upside) and only-stabilising GaN. Management itself flags opto duration as "the trillion-dollar question…how long the AI bubble will last" (CEO to BofA's Scemama, Q3/25). Optoelectronics is "extremely fragmented" and historically cyclical; FX (USD/JPY) is a recurring 1pp GM drag; Q1/26 GM of 18% shows the operating-leverage downside in trough quarters. Geostrategic risk (Iran/Middle East) explicitly called out. Jefferies' own downside flags GaN+SiC both declining in 2026 on weak EV demand and delayed GaN ramps, plus share loss to **Veeco** and rising **AMEC** competition in China (Jefferies, 2026-02-10).
- **Where the sell-side stands:** Constructive into the AI-optical cycle. **Jefferies Buy, PT EUR 73** (Beavington, 2026-06-19) — upgraded from Hold at EUR 18.20 PT only four months earlier (Bakhda, 2026-02-10). ## Risks
- **Single-cycle dependence:** opto/AI-datacom is now the swing factor; a capex pause hits orders fast. Management can't see beyond near-term visibility.
- **Power core soft:** SiC overcapacity + EV slowdown; GaN digesting installed base. 2026 SiC mix down sharply.
- **Margin cyclicality:** trough-quarter operating deleverage (Q1/26 GM 18%); ~1pp recurring FX (USD/JPY) drag.
- **Customer concentration / China:** large SiC orders skew to China (overcapacity risk); concentrated opto customer base under NDA. Jefferies flags AMEC as a rising Chinese MOCVD competitor.
- **Competition:** share loss to Veeco in SiC/GaN/microLED is the named bear case (Jefferies, 2026-02-10).
- **Geopolitics / trade:** Middle East/Iran flagged; US-tariff exemption assumed but monitored; export-control exposure on advanced tools.
- **No SEC disclosure:** German reporting (half-year detail, lighter quarterly statements) — less granular than US peers; modelling relies on IR decks + transcripts.
