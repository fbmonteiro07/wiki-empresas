## p.5 — Figure 1: This Report In One Picture

Summary infographic ("The Power Play: Resistance Is Futile").

Left text block: AI datacenter infrastructure is increasingly measured in GW, with power the highest operating cost and often gating compute capacity. High voltage architectures are being introduced to deliver power to more dense, power-hungry server racks while increasing efficiency from 75-85% to 92-95%. TD Cowen models a **$13B greenfield TAM (~60% 5-year CAGR)**.

Chart: AI Datacenter Power TAM ($M), stacked bars by year (y-axis $0–$14,000M):

| Year | Approx total TAM ($M) |
|---|---|
| 2025 | ~$800 |
| 2026E | ~$2,300 |
| 2027E | ~$5,200 |
| 2028E | ~$8,200 |
| 2029E | ~$10,300 |
| 2030E | ~$13,000 |

Stack legend: Stage 2 Power, IBC, Smart NICs, PSU/BBU, Protection/Other, SSTs, SSCBs, Energy Storage. (Individual segment values not printed / [illegible].)

800 VDC Architecture chain (icons): Utility & Renewables → Solid State Transformer → Solid State Circuit Breaker → Energy Storage/UPS → Power Supply Unit → Intermediate Bus Converter → Stage 2 Voltage Regulator.

6 Key Takeaways:
1. Increasing accelerator TDP and compute density within racks is driving exponential absolute power increases; workloads drive intense fluctuations in power.
2. Stage 2 power delivery and intermediate bus converters seen as the largest part of the AI datacenter power TAM by 2030, ~60% of the TAM.
3. Compound semiconductors (SiC, GaN) critical to achieving required power density; more meaningful opportunity for GaN — could approach ~50% of the total GaN power market in 2030E.
4. Power semis are critical building blocks for AI; transition to 800 VDC datacenters needed to enable power density required for 1MW+ racks.
5. AI datacenter power market could be ~12% of the $108B total power market by 2030E, up from just 2% in 2025; opportunity could grow to be >10% of total company revenues even for larger broad-based analog companies.
6. Monolithic Power Systems and Infineon seen as best positioned in coverage to capture value, with accretive growth across the space.
