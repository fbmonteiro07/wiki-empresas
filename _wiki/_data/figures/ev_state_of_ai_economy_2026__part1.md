## p.14 — AI has spurred an uptick in a 50-year trend of compute growth

Chart: "Global compute since 1971", FLOPS (log scale), 1971–2027. Y-axis labels: 1 TFLOPS, 1 PFLOPS, 1 EFLOPS, 1 ZFLOPS, 10 ZFLOPS, 100 ZFLOPS. X-axis: 1971, 1985, 1993, 2000, 2008, 2016, 2021, 2024, 2027.

Series: "Total world compute, including AI servers" (purple); "Without AI servers" (green); "Forecast (2026–27)" (dashed).

Annotations: 66% CAGR (1971–2016 segment); 80% CAGR (post-2016, with-AI line); gap between with-AI and without-AI lines at forecast end ≈ 9x. Lines diverge from ~1 ZFLOPS at 2016.

Source: Exponential View analysis. Note: Includes mainframes, minicomputers, PCs, servers, smartphones, IoT and AI compute. AI-server FLOPS derived from the installed base of Nvidia GPUs by generation, using FP8 from Hopper onward.
