## p.50 — "The Material Upgrade Ladder" (infographic)

Subtitle: "How AI interconnect scaling forces upgrades in laminate, copper foil, and glass cloth." Citrini Research logo.

1. **CCL / Laminate:** M7 → M8 → M9 (lower dielectric loss). Benefits: lower Df / lower insertion loss; more stable Dk and Df; supports higher-speed signal transmission. "The laminate gets lower-loss as data rates rise."
2. **Copper Foil:** HVLP3 → HVLP4 → HVLP5 (smoother copper surface). Benefits: lower conductor loss; less surface roughness at high frequency; harder adhesion / processing tradeoff. "The copper gets smoother to reduce high-frequency loss."
3. **Glass Cloth:** Low-Dk → Low-Dk2 → Low-Dk3 / Q-glass (better dielectric control). Benefits: lower dielectric constant and loss; less skew from glass-weave effects; better dimensional stability. "The glass gets more specialized as signal integrity requirements rise."

"Three material upgrades, three problems solved — Laminate: cuts dielectric loss; Copper: cuts conductor loss; Glass cloth: cuts skew and instability."

Bottom callout: "Higher AI interconnect speeds require lower-loss laminate, smoother copper, and better glass cloth — improving signal integrity but raising cost, complexity, and qualification difficulty."

Page text: "Every new AI accelerator asks the substrate to do more. More compute dies, more HBM, more chiplets, more I/O, more power, and more signals moving across a larger package."
